A **deepwalker**, also called **shadow**, is a large multi-legged crustacean-like sea-dwelling predatory creature on [[First of the Sun]], only living in the waters near the [[Pantheon]].{{book ref|iote|2}}{{book ref|iote|24}} The [[ironhull]] class of ship can successfully attack and defend themselves from deepwalker threats; they manage to kill several during their taming of the [[Pantheon]] islands.{{book ref|iote|13}}

## Ecology
### Appearence
A deepwalker is a large crustacean, as wide as six narrowboats tied together. It possesses something like claws or tusks that are sharp enough to gouge rocks. These claws or tusks are as thick as a man's upper arm.{{book ref|iote|4}}{{book ref|iote|24}} Before the arrival of the [[Ones Above]], very few men actually got to see a deepwalker out of the water. Due to this, artists, lacking good descriptions of the beasts, often depicted them as serpentine, like the [[Dakwara]], the [[Eelakin]] god of snakes, who is said to have infinite coils extending into every ocean.{{book ref|iote|24}}

### Behaviour
Deepwalkers only live near the [[Pantheon]] islands, though many Eelakin scientists do not understand why.{{book ref|iote|2}}{{book ref|iote|24}} Like many creatures native to the Pantheon, deepwalkers hunt by sensing the minds of other living creatures, making protection bestowed by mind-shielding [[Aviar]] essential for unarmed boats. They hunt in the open sea but are also capable of exploding out of the water to devour prey on the beach. They only seek larger prey, not bothering themselves with smaller food like [[slimfish]]. If a deepwalker attacks prey on a beach, it will immediately kill them but drag their corpses back into the ocean before feasting on them.{{book ref|iote|8}}{{book ref|iote|4}}
