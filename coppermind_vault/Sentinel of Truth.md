> I print human-interest stories—the tales of people who are ignored by the larger media. Exciting stories, by adventuring celebrities. Cartoons, pictures of funny-shaped vegetables...
> — [[Maraga Dulcet]]

The ***Sentinel of Truth*** is a broadsheet published in [[Bilming]] on [[Scadrial]].

==Offices==
The broadsheet's offices are located at 42-13 on Seventh Street and are housed in an old wooden building, one of the few structures in Bilming that has not been recently renovated. It is one story with a small attic and a large basement, and is presumably a former fishing shack.

==Content==
The *Sentinel of Truth* is run by its editor-in-chief [[Maraga Dulcet]].  As of , Maraga had been the editor-in-chief for at least four years, and likely quite a bit longer. Maraga's philosophy included publishing cartoons, crass pictures, and sensational stories that other broadsheets passed over. [[Moonlight]] believed that it was one of the least reputable broadsheets in Bilming. Maraga had previously worked at a more respected paper; she claimed that they published as many lies as she did, but her broadsheet (and her readers) were simply more candid about it.

The *Sentinel of Truth* stood out among similar broadsheets in Bilming since it had the rights to publish the serialized stories of [[Jak|Allomancer Jak]] (although Jak's former associate [[Handerwym]] was publishing separate stories in *[[The Two Seasons]]*).

==History==
In , [[Wax]], [[Wayne]], [[Marasi]], and Moonlight visited the *Sentinel of Truth*'s offices during their investigation of the [[Set]], suspecting that [[Tobal Copper]] may have tried to contact the media as a whistleblower. The building appeared abandoned, and the broadsheet was being published irregularly, with delays of as much as six weeks between issues. They were able to make contact with Maraga and gain her trust, and learned that she had secretly been meeting with Tobal due to his concerns about a conspiracy in the city. Tobal was collection various documents and ephemera, and Maraga used her journalistic instincts to create extensive notes. They worked together on this research in the office basement, usually writing in metal to ensure that their words could not be altered. After Tobal was killed, Maraga had become exceptionally paranoid. She was visibly stressed and had locked herself in the building.

As Maraga presented the evidence that she and Tobal had collected, [[Telsin]] appeared and confronted Wax and Wayne. Wayne left and Wax and Telsin had a conversation on the roof, leaving Moonlight and Marasi in the basement with Maraga. They collected the evidence, and Moonlight decided to send Maraga to a [[Ghostblood]] safehouse for her protection. The subsequent fate of Maraga and the broadsheet is unknown.
