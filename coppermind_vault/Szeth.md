> I was Truthless. I simply followed orders. Tell me. Do you think that absolves a man?
> — Szeth to [[Taravangian]]

**Szeth-son-Neturo**, known as **Szeth-son-son-Vallano** when he was [[Truthless]], is a [[Shin]] man from [[Roshar]]. He is the infamous **Assassin in White** and is responsible for the death of [[Gavilar Kholin]], which starts the [[War of Reckoning]]. He has had many masters, including [[Taravangian]], [[Nale]], and [[Dalinar Kholin]]. As the Assassin, he wields [[Jezrien]]'s [[Honorblade]]; eventually, he loses it and is gifted [[Nightblood]].

Szeth becomes a [[Skybreaker]] of the Fifth Ideal, but upon achieving it, he immediately leaves the order, as he feels his [[12124|spren]] is not fit for him. He is the candidate [[Ishar]] chooses to replace the missing [[Jezrien|Herald]]. [[Kaladin]] fills the position instead, protecting Szeth from the fighting inherent in being a Herald, as Szeth had previously confessed that he wants to stop killing.

## Appearance and Personality

### Appearance

Szeth is roughly thirty-five years old at the start of the [[True Desolation]]. Like many [[Shin]], he's short and pale-skinned, with large, round eyes lacking the epicanthic fold that most [[Roshar]]ans possess. Normally, his eyes are dark green, but they change to pale sapphire whenever he summons Jezrien's [[Honorblade]], and glow light grey when he holds Stormlight as a Skybreaker. His hair is naturally brown, but he shaves his head often, mentioning that he's unable to grow a full head of hair. 

As the Assassin, Szeth always wears white. While this originally stemmed from [[listener]] customs, [[Taravangian]] had him keep the outfit as a symbol. Even after he stops following his [[Oathstone]], he continues to wear white.  When Szeth, became assassins and bodyguard, of [[Makkek]] meaking him wear black clothes, covered by a long enveloping cloack form his shoulders to his legs and black cloth mask that covered the lower part of his face. This is who Szeth, became call the Shadow a assassin.

Following his death and subsequent revival, his soul was improperly attached to his body, leaving him with a ghostly white afterimage that can be seen by anyone with sufficient [[Investiture]], such as [[Lift]] or the Skybreakers. The afterimage has become less pronounced over time. At the culmination of the [[Cleansing of Shinovar]], Szeth loses his entire right arm and part of the shoulder to [[Nightblood]].

### Personality

> I am not good at being a person. It is … a failing of mine.
> — Szeth

Szeth has been driven to seek and follow what is right since his childhood. He devotes himself to perfecting his skills, even when they go against his fundamental values. He has a profound and agonizing distrust in his own ability to choose rightly, so he looks to authority figures to direct him.

Szeth is bound strictly to honor. He holds to the rules and expectations of his station as [[Truthless]] to an extreme degree, believing it is his place to obey his masters without question. He is accomplished at assassination, but is not a willing killer, always facing inner turmoil when taking a life. His self-loathing eventually sends him spiraling into madness. He hears the voices of those he's killed in darkness and shadows, even after his near death and resurrection. [[Nale]] believes Szeth is hearing trembles from the [[Spiritual Realm]], serving as a constant reminder of his crimes.

Szeth clings to his faith in [[Stone Shamanism]], though he begins to question the teachings he once followed strictly. He tries to silence any such thoughts, considering them dangerous. He finds the idea of eternal damnation far more comforting than the obliteration of the soul. Even after nine years in the East, he maintains his faith.

As a [[Skybreaker]], Szeth is more vocal to his superiors. He starts questioning his orders, and even argues with those above him if he believes they are wrong. He slowly learns to make his own decisions, although he still yearns for someone to follow, as evidenced by his newfound dedication to Dalinar. His [[Szeth's highspren|highspren]] believes he needs to learn to rely on himself more instead of Dalinar.

He's exceedingly serious, and unless severely agitated, highly respectful to others. He also seems deaf to jokes or sarcasm, often finding what others say to be incredibly profound, even when it's nonsensical or silly. He struggles with nuance, wishing that problems he faces had easy, straightforward solutions.

Szeth is distant, reserved, and outwardly emotionless when interacting with his masters, such as [[Taravangian]] or [[Nale]]. In the presence of those with no authority over him, such as [[Skybreaker]] squires or [[Kaladin]], Szeth speaks bluntly. He is honest when sharing his thoughts and experiences, and considers leaving out pieces of personal information to be a form of lying. He is frustrated when made to explain things he sees as obvious, and doesn't repeat himself when clearly stating his intentions. He has a tendency to not respond in conversation, which leads Kaladin to believe that Szeth doesn't know or care about social customs. In reality, Szeth stays quiet out of a hope that he'll be left alone, or because he doesn't want to speak when others have so much to say.

Szeth is careful and observant, assessing his surroundings to arrive at a solution to challenges he faces. Once he decides how to proceed, he acts quickly, even recklessly.

He struggles with feelings of numbness and suicidality, and is resistant to the idea that he could begin to heal from the trauma he has endured. Szeth believes that it is right for him to suffer, and that accepting help is immoral. However, he gradually acknowledges that he is capable of making his own decisions, and that he is not an object. He learns to see the merit in choosing when to obey, and when to resist. He refuses to fight unless he is given a very good reason. Szeth participates in lighthearted conversation, and is able to smile and relax, even when he knows he will soon face a difficult situation.

## Attributes and Abilities
Szeth is an exceptionally skilled combatant; when ordered to fight, he becomes a nigh-on unstoppable killing machine. He's proficient with a sword, as well as the martial art of [[kammar]]. He combines both of those skills with his [[Surgebinding]] abilities to a deadly effect. In one-on-one combat, he can hold his own against, and even defeat, a master swordsman. He's fearless when attacking even people in [[Shardplate]] and wielding [[Shardblade]]s, trusting his greater, [[Stormlight]]-augmented agility to carry him through the battle unharmed. He also shows great cunning, resourcefulness, and determination, which serve him well both in and out of combat. He has past experience as a dancer, and is able to swim.

### Surgebinding

Szeth knows how to use most of the [[Surge]]s, having practiced them with the [[Honorblade]]s the Shin hold. For years, he bears [[Jezrien]]'s Honorblade, which grants him the powers of a [[Windrunner]] and thus the Surges of [[Gravitation]] and [[Adhesion]]. Over the years, he has mastered all three [[Lashing]]s. However, he is unaccustomed to fighting other Windrunners, leveling the playing field somewhat when his opponent can also take to the skies. He no longer carries the Blade in the present.

Szeth is familiar with the names, powers, and capabilities of each Radiant order, as well as strategies to counter all of them. However, his skill in this area varies. He considers himself more adept at facing [[Edgedancers]] and [[Dustbringers]], and less capable when confronted by [[Elsecallers]] or [[Order of Lightweavers|Lightweavers]]. He has an assortment of other, non-supernatural skills from his training, such as ice-skating.

When he becomes a [[skybreaker]], Szeth gains access to the Surges of [[Gravitation]] and [[Division]]. While he has used the former extensively, he has not practiced the latter in years. With Division, he can cause incredible destruction, but also exert precise control. He is able to set the air aflame, though it can be dangerous if done too often.

### Nightblood

After losing Jezrien's Honorblade, Szeth was gifted [[Nightblood]] by [[Nale]]. While Szeth cannot dismiss Nightblood the way he could a regular Shardblade, he is even more dangerous with Nightblood. Lacking [[Breath]], he has to feed it Stormlight to survive the act of using it, which means he cannot draw it very frequently.

Unlike Shardblades, Nightblood is capable of erasing [[Fused]] from existence completely, preventing them from resurrecting in the next [[Everstorm]]. Other unique capabilities include denting an Honorblade, collapsing an open [[perpendicularity]], and destroying [[Connection]]s made by [[Bondsmith]]s.

After learning [[Surgebinding]] from the [[Honorblade]]s, Nightblood is able to use the Surges, as well as grant them to their wielder. Through this method, Szeth has access to all ten Surges. Nightblood is also able to forge Connections to those beside Szeth, to drain Investiture from them so as not to kill him, allowing him to use Nightblood despite no longer being a Skybreaker.

## History
### Early Life (-)

#### Childhood

Szeth was born in the year  to [[Neturo-son-Vallano]] and [[Zeenid-daughter-Beth]], near [[Clearmount]] in southeastern [[Shinovar]]. He was their second child, born three years after his sister, [[Elid-daughter-Zeenid]]. Szeth and his family were shepherds, and grazed their sheep near their homestead in the early part of the year. In the middle months, they took their sheep to graze the untouched grass on the slopes closer to the mountains. One of Szeth’s duties was to carry water.

He was also a skilled dancer. By the time he was eleven, some in the village considered him to be among the best. Szeth preferred the times of year when he and his family brought their flock back to the homestead, so he could dance in worship for the sacred stone on their land. He had an idealistic view of his pastoral life, and believed that the [[Steen]] raiders who periodically attacked the Shin coast were envious of something they could not have for themselves.

Szeth practiced his dancing consistently, pushing himself hard when he did. He believed that he needed to continue improving his skills so long as he made mistakes. He was bothered that his sister didn’t share the same attitude. 

When Szeth was eleven-years-old, his sheep [[Molli]] led him to discover a new stone in the circular path of soil where he danced. He ran to get his father, who sent a carrier parrot with a note for Zeenid. Szeth, Elid, Neturo, and Zeenid discussed what they should do with the new rock. As it had emerged so close to the larger stone on their family land, Neturo explained that they may need to move into the town. Zeenid brought a shovel made of [[Soulcast]] metal, and suggested that they relocate the new stone a distance away so less attention would be focused on the immediate area. Szeth disliked both options. He looked to Neturo for guidance, but was told the ultimate decision was his to make. Szeth expressed a desire to do what was right. Neturo explained the predicament in more detail, and Szeth became uncertain. Sensing his family’s unspoken preference, he decided that the new rock should be moved, though it made him uncomfortable. Zeenid dug up the rock, but before she could carry it away, the raiders attacked. Zeenid hid the stone among the roots of a tree, where it was unlikely to be noticed for the time being.

Szeth and his family, along with many other shepherd families in the region, drove their sheep to a mountain ravine farther inland. While helping with the preparation of a meal, Szeth spoke with a farmer about choices and volition. He was disturbed by the lack of definitive answers to his questions. He brought Elid a plate of food, and learned that three of their sheep were missing. Molli was among those three. Szeth asked after her, searching flocks owned by other shepherds. However, he knew that Molli always returned to the home, even when the rest of the flock wandered. Szeth went alone to find her.

At the homestead, he found three Shin soldiers from the [[Stoneward monastery]] sitting on his family’s stone, eating charred meat and drinking Neturo’s wine. Szeth drew their attention when he accidentally snapped a twig. The soldiers verbally confronted him, and Szeth asked them to leave, expressing his disdain for people who subtracted. [[Sunken Eyes|One of the men]] challenged Szeth’s assertions, while the other two left. Szeth realized that the soldiers had slaughtered and eaten Molli. He ran after the third man as he began to walk away, attacking him. The soldier threw Szeth to the ground, where he collided with the tree where the smaller stone had been hidden. Szeth picked up the rock and approached the soldier, but stopped himself before causing him harm. The man grabbed Szeth by the throat and attempted to strangle him. In his desperation to escape, Szeth picked up the stone again and killed the man with it. Afterword, he heard the [[Ishar|Voice]] for the first time.

#### Joining the Soldiers

The following evening, Szeth overheard his parents and the farmer discussing his actions. The farmer said that Szeth needed to train with the soldiers, as he had subtracted and would be changed by what he had done. Neturo insisted that he would go with Szeth, so he would never be alone. Zeenid and Elid joined them. Both the farmer and Neturo remained firm in their decisions, and so Szeth and his family went to join the soldiers.

At the training yard near the Stoneward monastery, Zeenid gave Szeth a small toy sheep she had made from Molli’s wool. [[Betheth-son-Vetor]] met Szeth and his family, and explained the workings of the encampment, along with the options that would be available to them in the future. Neturo accompanied Szeth to his initial assessment, where Betheth told Szeth to give up the brightly colored handkerchief he wore around his neck, which had marked him as someone who added up to that point. Szeth joined four older boys who were to be assessed. Betheth handed each of them a spear, ordering them to attack the corpses of dead sheep provided for the testing. The other four began attacking with unrestrained aggression, and Szeth nearly joined them. However, the Voice instructed him to be more careful and deliberate, as the assessment was a way to judge whether or not the new recruits could control their urges to kill. Following the Voice’s instructions, Szeth stabbed once at the neck of a carcass. At Betheth’s questioning, Szeth repeated words given to him by the Voice. Betheth took him to the camp’s [[Kinal|General]], proposing that Szeth would be more suited to officer training.

Three years later, Szeth sparred against other officers in training. One of these was [[Jormo-son-Falk]], and the Voice advised Szeth as they sparred. Szeth eventually knocked Jormo down, and he retaliated by tackling Szeth and slamming his head against the ground. Szeth found a stone in the dirt as he struggled, but let go of it and silently swore that he would never lose control again. Jormo was pulled away, and Sergeant [[Szrand]] berated him. Szrand then scolded Szeth, accusing him of being a problem and acting as if he was better than the other soldiers. Szeth said that he was better than them, explaining that he had been training with the monastery’s acolytes, as Szrand’s instructions were inadequate. Szrand ordered him to run laps around the camp, but Szeth resisted. Szrand took a practice sword and shield from another student and clashed with Szeth. Szeth defeated him, and the Voice expressed his approval. Szeth then began running his laps. Zeenid brought him water while he ran, and Szeth paused to speak with her. She pointed out that failed soldiers were sent to other kinds of work. He disliked the idea, as it would mean that he was incompetent. He finished running his laps, and found a note telling him to report to the General’s office. 

There, Szeth encountered Neturo working at a desk. They discussed Szeth’s confrontation with Szrand. Neturo explained that he was working out a retirement plan for him, and intended to promote [[Yago-son-Yargo]] in Szrand’s place. He went on to say that Yago would retire in approximately five years, implying that Szeth would then rise to the position of sergeant. Szeth liked the idea, as it meant that he wouldn’t need to fight or kill. Neturo then took him to meet with the General, who was speaking with a group of visiting Stone Shamans, including [[Pozen-son-Nash|Pozen]] and [[Sivi-daughter-Sivi|Sivi]]. There, Szeth saw one of the [[Honorblade]]s for the first time. The Voice asked if he wanted to hold one, and said that the time may never be right, though he was watching Szeth. The General introduced Neturo to Pozen, who expressed approval of Neturo’s administrative skills. Hoping for firmer direction, Szeth interrupted the conversation to ask Pozen how he knew what was right. Pozen told him to follow his superiors and the chain of command, which ultimately ended at God himself. Sivi asked if Szeth was struggling with a particular moral question, and he explained his difficulty in causing others harm, even while training. Pozen instructed him to take at least one shift at the slaughterhouse each week, so he could accustom himself to death. Pozen then told the General to put Szeth in the back lines of the defense when the next raid occurred. When asked if he would follow Pozen’s orders, Szeth asked if it was right, then complied.

#### Mission on the Ships

At the age of nineteen, Szeth led a group of soldiers on a mission to defend the Shin coast from another wave of raiders. The raiders had begun taking the common people of fishing villages as slaves. Szeth planted the men under his command in a village in place of the evacuated inhabitants, along with weapons hidden with the valuables the raiders took as loot. The Voice approved of the plan Szeth had devised. As he watched the raiders arrive, he noticed a young boy who had stayed behind. Szeth observed the raiders loading slaves and loot onto their ships, and the Voice translated their words for him, as he did not have the training in languages that was common to the Stone Shamans. Then, the men searched the sheds and chicken coops of the village, approaching the place where the boy had hidden. Szeth stood from his own hiding place and caught the attention of the raiders before they reached the child. He feigned fear, surrendered his sword, and allowed himself to be captured.

On one of the three raiding ships, Szeth saw some of the men he had planted being taken belowdecks. He was searched, and the crew members found a stone he had picked up earlier for luck. They grew excited, believing it to be an [[Oathstone]]. Reserves on the coast blew horns, signaling the next part of Szeth’s plan. The Voice explained the captain’s amusement over the Shin people leaving slaves to be captured, along with his belief that he could profit from their weakness. One of the other ships began to burn, and Szeth saw his unarmored soldiers dive into the ocean. They had cut themselves free of their bonds with blades hidden in their mouths, killed watching crew members, and burned oil they had spread. The ship Szeth was on began to burn next, and he broke away from the men holding him and went down into the hold. There, he came upon a sailor fighting [[Lumo-son-Tumo]]. Lumo cut the ropes binding Szeth’s arms, then they and the rest of the strike team onboard returned to the deck to find that most of the crew members had jumped overboard. Szeth instructed his men to return to shore and watch the beach in the case of sailors moving farther inland. Four obeyed, though Lumo stayed by Szeth’s side, ignoring his orders to go. Szeth saw that the third ship was not burning, so he took the coat from a fallen sailor and told Lumo to swim to shore. 

Szeth leapt into the water and swam to the final ship. When he climbed over the rail, he saw his five men tied to the mast. He went to cut them free, but the raiders recognized that he did not belong among them. Szeth fought the Steen men, and defeated eight of them in moments. One stabbed him in the back from behind, and he collapsed to the deck. The Voice spoke to him about the stakes of battle, and how few people got the chance to learn from their mistakes and become true masters of the sword. As Szeth’s senses began to grow dull, the Voice asked if he wanted another chance. He accepted, and one of the lanterns filled with [[Stormlight]] spheres went out. Szeth’s wound healed, and the Voice told him to learn from his mistake. He freed two of his soldiers, who released two others. They fought the raiders, who soon surrendered. Szeth allowed the Steen men to tend their wounded, and ordered his soldiers to gather bows from the ship and prepare a smaller boat to row to shore. The Voice advised Szeth on how to inspire fear in the raiders, though he warned that such a tactic could result in more ships coming in the future, rather than an end to the attacks. Szeth stated that he was willing to do whatever it took to stop the raids. The Voice told him to show the captain his healed wound, and gave him words to repeat. When asked, the Voice told Szeth that he had proclaimed himself to be one of the Stormriders, and had brought immortals to stop the attacks on Shinovar. The Voice then directed him to stab one of the sailors in the stomach, so they would rush home in a futile attempt at saving the wounded. After a moment of hesitation, Szeth stabbed two of the sailors, and with the Voice’s help, warned them that the Shin shores would no longer be easy targets. 

He and his soldiers rowed back to shore, where the rest of his men and the fishermen were celebrating. Szeth did not join them. He stood on the beach and watched the two ships his men had burned sink into the water, until a group of riders, including Neturo, came from the monastery. The General exclaimed that Szeth had gone too far. He defended his actions, increasingly frustrated. The General ordered him to report to the camp, saying that he would need to consult others to determine how to handle Szeth.

Three days later, Szeth stood outside the [[Kinal|General]]’s office, anxiously waiting to be punished for his actions during the mission. The General was in conference with Neturo, the [[Farmer]], [[Rit-daughter-Clutio|Rit]], and three [[Honorbearers]]. [[Elid-daughter-Zeenid|Elid]] came to speak with Szeth about what he did. She encouraged him that he would be able to withstand whatever came next, and left as he was beckoned into the office. When Szeth entered, the General told him that he was inclined to send him to the mines. Szeth accepted the prospect, stating that they were right to send him away for severely misinterpreting his orders. The Farmer approved of his cooperation, and informed Szeth that he had advocated for him to be sent to a watch post in the mountains instead. The Farmer explained that the post would be solitary, but that soldiers in that position kept sheep for food. Szeth found the idea appealing and comforting. Before he could accept, the Voice interrupted. The three Honorbearers showed strong reactions, then [[Pozen-son-Nash|Pozen]] commanded everyone except Szeth from the room.

He was left alone with Pozen, [[Sivi-daughter-Sivi|Sivi]], and [[Vambra-daughter-Skies|Vambra]]. Vambra demanded to know how long Szeth had been hearing the Voice. When he was given permission to reveal him, Szeth realized that the Honorbearers heard the Voice as well. The Voice explained that burning the ships was his idea, and that since there was no Honorbearer in that particular monastery, he worked through other means. Pozen said that Szeth needed training at a monastery, and Vambra suggested giving him a commendation for his bravery, which would explain why Pozen would take a man from another monastery. Szeth explained the Voice’s plan when Vambra asked, and she told him that he’d done well. 

The Honorbearers called for Neturo and the General to return. Pozen told the General that Szeth was a hero, and would be moved to [[Elsecaller monastery|his monastery]] to begin training as a shaman acolyte. Pozen said that Szeth would go alone, but Neturo objected, stating that Szeth would be joined by his family. Szeth was given his commendation, and prepared to leave with the caravan of the Honorbearers and their servants. Neturo and Elid came with him, but [[Zeenid-daughter-Beth|Zeenid]] stayed behind. She told Neturo that she was going to return to her old life, and that he should let go of following Szeth. Zeenid returned to the camp alone, and Szeth, Elid, and Neturo went with the caravan.

#### Among the Honorbearers

After two years of training at the [[Elsecaller monastery]], Szeth dueled against [[Gonda-son-Darias]] for the position of swordmaster. Szeth won five bouts against him in one day, and took his rank. [[Pozen-son-Nash|Pozen]] spoke to Szeth, expressing his approval of everything about him, save his weakness with Elsecalling. Pozen prepared a hunt for Szeth, with clues that he needed to collect. He went into the city of [[Mokdown]], beginning the task immediately. After finding the first clue on the roof of a church dedicated to [[Ishar]], Szeth encountered [[Sivi-daughter-Sivi|Sivi]], who invited him to a nearby tavern to speak about his training. 

They discussed Pozen’s aspirations for Szeth to join the Honorbearers, as well as Szeth’s reasons for his complete obedience. She asked about his questions, and he admitted his concerns with Pozen still holding the [[Elsecaller]] Honorblade, despite the fact that he was no longer one of the best soldiers. Szeth wondered why Sivi and Pozen were eager for him to become an Honorbearer, if there was no problem with an aging man holding the position. Sivi told Szeth that [[Tuko-son-Tuko]] was a problem. When he asked if Tuko stood against the defense of Shinovar, she said that he did. 

Szeth then asked about the identity of the Voice, and if becoming an Honorbearer would lead to an understanding of what was happening. He did not understand why the Honorbearers first rejected him after the mission on the ships, then welcomed him after learning that he heard the Voice. Sivi explained that the context had changed, and Szeth expressed discomfort with the nuance of her reasoning. She alluded to the day Szeth found the new stone on his family’s land, and revealed that she and Neturo had been spending time with one another. Sivi asked if Szeth would go on pilgrimage to challenge Tuko for his Blade. He became frustrated, and Sivi explained that Szeth’s life had never truly been as simple as he believed. He reflected that the Honorbearers were not at fault for using nuance, but that he was for refusing to see it. He accepted Sivi’s offer to go on pilgrimage, stating that he would like to begin at [[Willshaper monastery|her monastery]], once he rose to the rank of warrior elite. Sivi warned Szeth that Pozen was using him, and approved of his stated desire to find answers about the Voice.

On the day Szeth left the Elsecaller monastery to begin his pilgrimage, he spent time praying in the monastery’s rock garden. The Voice spoke to him and said that he had meaning and a purpose. He also warned Szeth that not all of the other Honorbearers shared Pozen’s devotion to him. Elid interrupted the conversation, entering the garden to inform Szeth that Neturo intended to follow him again. She had told the shamans that she was interested in joining the monastery, and had been invited to look around. She took the opportunity to speak with Szeth in private, despite his objections that he was to be alone during his meditation. Elid told Szeth that he could not allow Neturo to go with him, as he was the mayor of Mokdown. She urged him to do the right thing and let their father stay. Szeth and Elid had a heated discussion about Neturo’s relationship with Sivi, as well as the state of their family after Zeenid left. Szeth was conflicted, but ultimately decided to continue along the purposeful path the Voice had laid out for him. Elid said that she was not going to follow Szeth this time, and again implored him to tell Neturo to stay behind. She asked to know why Szeth was their father’s favorite, and expressed hatred of him as he left the rock garden.

Neturo met Szeth as he set out alone. Szeth asked him to stay, saying that he should remain where he had a life. He told Neturo that he could stay with Elid, or return to Zeenid. Neturo revealed that he and Zeenid had been struggling with their relationship for years, and that no one in [[Clearmount]] would rent to them, as he had persuaded her to question things others did not. He assured Szeth that she was happier, according to letters he had received from her sisters. Szeth told Neturo to stay with Elid. Neturo said that he had never known how to speak with her, and that he would leave to give them both distance for a time. He told Szeth he would go with him, as it was the only thing he could do to help.

#### Becoming an Honorbearer

Szeth spent seven years training with the Honorblades, with the exception of [[Talenel]]’s and [[Ishar]]’s Blades. When he finished the first stage of his pilgrimage, at the age of twenty-seven, Szeth fought [[Tuko-son-Tuko]] to win [[Jezrien]]’s Blade. Tuko insisted on fighting to the death, and Szeth defeated him. Before he died, Tuko implored Szeth to reject the Voice and stop following him. Tuko then gave a [[Death Rattle]]. Once the fight was finished, [[Moss-son-Farrier|Moss]] expressed amazement over the fight, and commiserated with Szeth over the necessity of killing his predecessor. Szeth asked him to inform Neturo that he had been successful, then joined the other Honorbearers who had come to watch. Pozen congratulated him on attaining his rightful place. Szeth said that he had not reached it yet, as he hadn’t completed the second stage of his pilgrimage. He was told that the second stage was a formality and a series of celebrations, rather than a fight against each Honorbearer.

Szeth asked Sivi why Tuko had been such a problem, as he saw no signs of heresy while training with him. She confirmed that Tuko had spoken out where Szeth couldn’t hear. Szeth expressed discomfort with how he had been used to stop the trouble, and Sivi revealed that Tuko had mentioned rebellion and civil war. Szeth wondered why he would go to such measures, then asked when he could meet the Voice. Sivi said that it would happen after the second stage of his pilgrimage. Szeth wanted to know how quickly he could finish. The Voice spoke and instructed that Szeth be allowed to fly to him before beginning the second pilgrimage. The Voice told Szeth to come to [[Ayabiza]] and seek him there, where he would find the answers he sought.

#### Meeting the Voice

Szeth traveled to Ayabiza via [[Lashing]]. Before arriving, he took time to fall into the reaches of the planet's upper atmosphere, contemplating his station in the face of the wind and the vastness of the sky. He dropped back toward the ground when he realized that his [[Stormlight]] would eventually run out, and that he would never find the answers he sought if he didn't return. He landed in the city's central square, admired the sights for a time, then went to the [[Bondsmith monastery]] for the first time to meet the Voice.

Upon arrival, Szeth revered the breaks in the stone where the [[Herald]]s had left their Blades at [[Aharietiam]]. He prayed to [[Talenel|Talmut]], thinking of the Shin teaching that the Herald had chosen to stay in [[Braize|Damnation]], rather than allowing any of the other nine to take his place. Szeth asked the Voice for direction on what came next, and admitted that he had to trust him over the other Honorbearers, as he believed nothing would be left otherwise. Szeth followed the Voice's guidance to a cavern set in a cliffside, then questioned the circumstances that had led to Tuko becoming an Honorbearer. The Voice told him to approach, and Szeth obeyed. 

As he drew near, he sensed a darkness to the surroundings, which the Voice dismissed when Szeth asked. He entered a tunnel which led to a room filled with a variety of [[spren]] nailed to the walls with crystal spikes. Szeth was horrified at the sight. The Voice said that he left them there intentionally, so they could learn their true master, change, and be transformed by him into better versions of themselves. Szeth saw a gathering darkness approach as the Voice told him that he had been chosen and prepared for the role he was to fill, just as the other Honorbearers had. Szeth assumed that the Voice, the pinned spren, and the events of his life since his childhood were workings of [[Sja-anat]]. The Voice commanded Szeth to come and worship. After a moment of conflict, Szeth chose to ignore the Voice and flee.

In a panic and holding too much Stormlight, Szeth fled for hours before going to speak with Neturo, who had been waiting for him. Szeth warned him that everything was a lie, and begged him to hide. Neturo reminded him to breathe, encouraging him that the problem could be solved. Szeth said the issue he faced was large as the world, but feared that he was wrong. Neturo embraced him, telling Szeth that he trusted him and had always wanted to find answers for him. Neturo explained his realization that his father didn't have solutions, when the Honorbearers could not come to heal Vallano before he died. Szeth wondered if anyone had answers, but ultimately realized that he was all his people had. He asked Neturo to stay hidden, then left again.

Szeth arrived at the [[Windrunner monastery]] the following morning, filled with a rare feeling of confidence. He went to the Honorbearer's quarters, where he was met by the monastery's ten shamans, led by [[Faraz-daughter-Daraz]]. Szeth asked the group to walk with him, but Faraz explained that they had agreed to travel to local villages rather than staying to maintain the monastery. Szeth asked how the shamans had come to serve under Tuko, where it was revealed that all ten of them had been rejected for their rebellious attitudes, despite each of them being skilled with the sword. Szeth again asked the shamans to walk with him, stating that they should be glad to have him, as the very best fighters would be needed to resist the other Honorbearers and confront the [[Desolation]]. 

Szeth led the group to the monastery's armory, considering whether or not he could recruit Moss or Sivi to his cause. When he asked Faraz if she knew why Tuko planned to rebel, she admitted that she did not, only that she and the other shamans had been instructed to prepare. Szeth then revealed his belief that the other Honorbearers served one of the [[Unmade]]. He stated his intention to declare that the Desolation had arrived, that the [[Voidbringer]]s had returned, and that it was time to fight. The ten shamans were alarmed, but believed him. Szeth instructed them to recruit any willing people, proclaiming that it was time for war.

#### Civil War

On a night six weeks later, Szeth arrived at the [[Elsecaller monastery]], intending to assassinate [[Pozen-son-Nash|Pozen]]. He had spent the intervening time preparing for war. He ignored invitations to go to the other monasteries, but sent letters to [[Sivi-daughter-Sivi|Sivi]] and [[Moss-son-Farrier|Moss]], none of which were answered. Before entering the monastery through a hatch on the roof, Szeth thought of Neturo, who had gone missing the day after he had spoken with him. Szeth moved through the monastery's halls, and wondered why he used stealth rather than engaging in a direct fight which he could win. He considered killing Pozen as a mercy, and thought of using his Honorblade for [[Soulcasting]]. He also anticipated how the other Honorbearers would respond once Pozen was dead. Szeth reached a meditation chamber and collected himself, hoping that Pozen would come to meditate alone so he could be killed quietly. 

Instead, Pozen arrived with Sivi, and Szeth overheard their conversation. They discussed Szeth's actions following his meeting with the Voice. Pozen was confident that Szeth would obey and fall into line, but Sivi thought that Pozen underestimated him. Pozen was displeased with Sivi's questioning. She expressed concern with the level of Szeth's skill, and warned that he had begun gathering forces from the cities nearest [[Windrunner monastery|his monastery]]. Pozen assured her that he had it handled, and that the Voice believed everything was under control, mentioning specific leverage Sivi had obtained. Szeth feared she would enter the room where he listened, but she instead went into the one beside it. Pozen left. Szeth realized that he could take the opportunity to kill Sivi, claim her Blade, and use it along with Pozen's to keep his forces better protected. Szeth hesitated before breaking through the wall, thinking of how Sivi had treated him well, along with Neturo's love for her. Szeth then considered her service of the Unmade, but wavered and wondered if he was wrong. He chose not to kill Sivi immediately. Instead, he went to speak with her and ask questions. 

Szeth startled Sivi when he entered her meditation chamber, but she did not attack or call for help. She asked if he had come to speak with Pozen, and assumed he had questions. He asked how she could have served one of the Unmade. They discussed the captured spren in the cave, and Szeth stated that the world was in danger and that they were not ready to face it. Sivi became uncertain, wondering about a potential deception. Pozen interrupted the conversation, and demanded to know what was happening. He realized what Szeth intended to do. Szeth briefly tried to grapple him, then gained distance before Pozen could transfer their conflict to [[Shadesmar]]. Szeth retreated when Pozen summoned his Honorblade, knowing that he would never bring any of the other Honorbearers to his cause if he killed him. Szeth declared his intention to spread the truth that the Voidbringers had returned. He said he would welcome those who chose to join him, but would fight anyone who did not. He then fled the monastery.

Szeth led an army of acolytes in a battle outside a small town. He used his Honorblade and Lashings to defeat the town’s militia while his forces struggled with killing for the first time. He expressed frustration with his troops, then brought them into the town to secure it and turn the people to his cause.

Szeth spoke with the town butcher, who did not believe his assertions that the Voidbringers had returned. [[Thal-son-Geord]] said it had taken him time to believe, and [[Visk-daughter-Brador]] suggested that they find and display a Voidbringer's corpse. Szeth thought that method would not be effective against the enemy's stealth. He asked why the butcher could not see the corruption in the Shin leadership, then reluctantly accepted the cuts of meat the man offered. Szeth moved through the town, joined by his officers. Visk warned that they would face the same difficulty everywhere they went, as one Honorbearer in conflict against the others would appear to be a power struggle, which was the reason [[Tuko-son-Tuko|Tuko]] never rebelled openly. 

Szeth felt disappointment at the reception he had met thus far, and dismissed his officers. He thought of the struggle of administrating a single town, and worried about how he would respond once Neturo was used against him. He wondered how he would conquer all of Shinovar, and how he would lead them against the Voidbringers when they retaliated. He asked how he would handle it all. The Voice responded, and said that the Honorbearers were weak and hoped that Szeth would lose his courage. Szeth demanded that he stop speaking to him. The Voice told Szeth that the fight he wanted would come, and that he needed to draw the Honorbearers out of hiding and kill them. Szeth realized that he would be ignored if he contained his rebellion to one region, without a stronger army to support him. He found Visk and instructed her to give the orders to break camp and prepare his army to march. He informed her that their destination was [[Clearmount|his home village]], and resolved to bring the army at the [[Stoneward monastery]] beneath his banner.

At the Stoneward monastery, Szeth tried to convince [[Lumo-son-Tumo|Lumo]] to join his cause. Lumo informed Szeth that Pozen had traveled to the camp the previous night via Elsecalling, and warned that Szeth could not be trusted. Szeth challenged the views Lumo and his soldiers held regarding Pozen, pointing out that he never seemed to care for their fights against the raiders. Szeth brought up the moment from years before when he had initially been punished, only to be suddenly rewarded instead. He vowed that change was brought about by the Unmade's influence. Lumo asked about the Voice, and Szeth admitted that he and every other Honorbearer heard it. He assumed he had failed to sway Lumo, then an enemy army, gathered by the other seven Honorbearers, arrived via Elsegate. Szeth knew retreat was impossible, and stated it was time to fight. He told Lumo he would have to watch him be slaughtered when Lumo refused to join him.

Szeth led his army against the enemy forces, but did not kill. He was haunted by his actions in the previous battle, and felt that the souls of the people he had killed watched him from beneath the stones. Szeth's army fought from a defensible position, and he led them, hovering in the air and giving verbal encouragement. The other Honorbearers stayed out of the conflict, as Szeth did not fight directly. He allowed his army to retreat under pressure, placing them in a desperate situation. Forces from the Stoneward monastery came to join Szeth's troops, and he believed he saw the moment that his resistance gathered momentum and began growing into a force that could stand against the Desolation. Szeth landed on the ground, and had a brief conversation with [[Drodli]] about what they would have done if Lumo hadn’t come to their defense. Szeth then flew to speak with Lumo, who said his army would fight, but only because Szeth's was on the defensive. Lumo recognized that something strange was happening, and said that it was time for the Stoneward monastery to be respected. 

The battle continued as Szeth returned to the fray. He challenged the armies opposing him, and Moss and Sivi were the first sent to fight him. Szeth refused to kill either. He fought Moss until he fled, then Sivi faced him next. Szeth and Sivi fought on the soil, where she could not use [[Cohesion]]. They both kept their uses of [[Surgebinding]] to a minimum, and after a fight that lasted for a few minutes, Szeth disarmed Sivi. She expressed amazement at his strength. He said he fought to protect their land. She told him that he had not seen an Unmade, but he refused to be persuaded. Sivi stated that Szeth had at least allowed himself to be distracted by a duel. At the sound of trumpets, he looked to the conflict and saw that Lumo's troops were turning against his, pinning them between two enemy forces. Horrified at the betrayal, Szeth Lashed himself to the place where Lumo's army had turned on his. 

Before he could demand answers, Neturo stepped forward. Szeth still believed his father was a captive, and was shocked. Neturo greeted him, and said that he and the other Honorbearers believed Lumo and his soldiers would listen if Neturo spoke to them. Szeth initially insisted that he was an illusion formed by [[Illumination]]. Neturo summoned [[Ishar]]'s Honorblade, and Szeth recognized the colors he wore as those belonging to the Bondsmith Honorbearer. Neturo stated that he was real. He explained how he'd accepted the offer Sivi and Pozen had given him, as he had been told it may have been the only way to save Szeth. He revealed that he had come to hear the Voice. Szeth tried to insist that the Voice was a Voidbringer, but Neturo stated that he was not one of the enemy. Szeth again denied Neturo's reality, and backed away from him. Szeth realized the truth when Neturo embraced him. Neturo believed there had to be another way to handle what was happening. Szeth said he was, and always had been, trying to do what was right. Neturo said he had realized why he’d followed him for so long. Neturo had thought Szeth would lead him to answers, and claimed he had. Szeth was overwhelmed by his recent actions, horrified by the prospect of being wrong. He wondered why he had ever trusted his own judgment. He implored Neturo to tell him what to do, saying he would do it, so long as Neturo was the one to direct him.

That night, Szeth sat by a fire while the other Honorbearers, including Neturo, discussed his fate. He was upset by the idea of his father becoming a killer and the Bondsmith Honorbearer. Sivi questioned the Voice's motives and wondered what would have happened if Neturo hadn't spoken to Lumo's soldiers. Neturo said that Szeth simply became confused at times, and that he didn't understand why he couldn't share the Voice’s plans with him. The Voice spoke in Szeth's mind, expressing disappointment that he didn't strike down the Honorbearers. He said he needed to harden Szeth, as armies were needed to fight, and armies needed experienced generals. The Voice said that Szeth didn't need to understand, only that he needed to do what he was told. The Voice said he'd see him in a decade, then stopped speaking to Szeth. 

Szeth wondered what it meant if his decisions could not be trusted at all, and overheard Pozen suggesting that he be made [[Truthless]]. Neturo worried the punishment was too harsh, but Pozen said that Szeth would be a threat to no one in that position. [[Vambra-daughter-Skies|Vambra]], [[Gearil-daughter-Gearil|Gearil]], and [[Dulo-son-Tudla|Dulo]] all agreed, with Gearil suggesting that they claim the Voidbringers would never return, and that they shift Truth to mean something else. Neturo objected, but Sivi reminded him of the future he had seen in the stones when holding Ishar's Honorblade. Pozen declared that there were no Voidbringers, that the ancient powers were no more, and that the [[Knights Radiant]] were fallen. He said that they needed to focus on the true threat, which Neturo recognized as war with other worlds. 

Neturo told Szeth to give up his Honorblade, and, in a state of shock, he complied. He believed he heard the dead whispering in the night. Soldiers bound Szeth’s wrists, and he agreed that he was Truthless. He said that either his actions were right and that he would have to kill the Honorbearers, or that he was wrong. He told Neturo to decide for him, and Neturo agreed to the punishment. Szeth asked what it entailed. Sivi answered that he would be given an Oathstone and banished. Szeth agreed, as he no longer wanted to make decisions and believed a stone would know better than he did. Pozen held up the stone and told Szeth to swear before it, the [[spren]], and his father that he would follow whatever its holder commanded of him, with the exception of killing himself. Szeth made the oath. He felt freed from the burden of making choices. His bonds were cut, and Pozen ordered Szeth's Blade to be taken. The Voice interrupted, and Sivi informed Szeth that he was to keep the Blade instead. The Voice said something that caused Neturo to react with distress. Pozen gave Szeth the Oathstone, and told him to add the refusal to give up his Blade to his vow. Szeth did so, then stated that he no longer deserved his father’s name. Neturo disagreed, and Szeth said that neither of them decided, only that they did what they were told. Pozen banished Szeth with the name Szeth-son-son-Vallano. Neturo gave Szeth one last embrace, apologizing and saying he could not follow him this time. Szeth was taken away, and eventually sold to the Thaylen trader [[Vstim]]. He made the resolution to neither look back nor question again.

### Truthless (-)

From the night of his exile onward, whoever owned Szeth's Oathstone owned him, and Szeth was obliged by his honor to do as the owner commanded, excluding the two restrictions he had been given. Just after being exiled from [[Shinovar]], Szeth visited [[Urithiru]] for the first time.

#### Killing King Gavilar

> I am Truthless. I do as my master requires and I require no explanation.
> — Szeth, regarding his oath.

Two years after his banishment, Szeth was purchased by an assassin named [[Liss]]. She believed that Szeth was the best servant she ever had and told [[Jasnah]] that she wanted to show him off to her. However, she later found that Szeth was too good of a servant, so she sold him to slavers in [[Kholinar]]. A few weeks later, Szeth was purchased by [[Klade]], a member of the [[listener]] ruling council, in . Klade knew of him from [[Venli]], who in turn learned of him from Nale. When Klade pressured him, Szeth told him about his Shardblade and the abilities it brought. As a result, in , [[the Five]] ordered him to murder king [[Gavilar Kholin]] of [[Alethkar]] during the treaty-signing celebration. They stipulated that the killing had to be done with live witnesses, and while wearing white.

As Szeth attacked, he was fooled into believing Gavilar was a bodyguard, and decided to chase after the escaping target, instead of fighting a protracted battle. However, soon after he realized the "target" was simply a decoy, since Gavilar would be much safer fighting with his own Shardblade and Plate instead of fleeing for his life. Doubling back, Szeth returned to kill Gavilar, and a fierce battle commenced. Gavilar almost succeeded in killing Szeth with a Shardplate-enhanced punch, but ultimately failed and fell to his death onto a piece of wooden debris, which impaled him in an area where the Shardplate had been broken.

Before dying, Gavilar had two last requests: to tell his brother to "find the most important words a man could say," and to protect a mysterious [[black sphere]] that shone [[anti-Voidlight|dark light]]. Szeth honored both requests, scrawling a message to [[Dalinar]] into the wood with Gavilar's own hand and blood. Eventually he also hid away the black orb somewhere in [[Jah Keved]].

His clothes on the night of the killing led to the general public dubbing him the Assassin in White.

#### Laying Low
After Gavilar's death, the listeners threw away Szeth's Oathstone as they fled [[Kholinar]]. Since the Alethi believed that Szeth had fled with the Parshendi, they did not attempt to track him down and instead initiated the [[Vengeance Pact]], waging war on the [[Shattered Plains]]. Meanwhile the stone, and as a result Szeth, was eventually picked up by [[Avado]], a travelling merchant.

Avado took Szeth with him to [[Jah Keved]] in fear of possible Alethi retribution against strange foreigners in the wake of their king's assassination. He did not keep his slave for long, however; over the course of the next five years, the Oathstone changed possession many times before landing in the hands of [[Took]], a transient worker. Took was robbed and killed after a night of drinking in [[Ironsway]], a town in the [[Bavland]], and Szeth's Oathstone is taken by [[Makkek]], a small-time criminal. With Szeth under his control, Makkek set out to gain wealth, first by killing his fellow robbers and then by establishing a gambling den in [[Bornwater]]. 

On one of Makkek's missions, this time to eliminate a rival who had opened his own gambling place, Szeth infiltrated the local lord's mansion where his target, [[Gavashaw]], resided. After entering Gavashaw's rooms, he met a mysterious stranger who knew of Szeth and his role in Gavilar's death, as well as his powers. After a short confrontation, the man revealed that both Makek and Gavashaw had been killed, and that Szeth's Oathstone was now in the hands of a new master. Thus, Szeth began to unwittingly serve the [[Diagram (group)|Diagram]].

#### Taravangian's Killer

Szeth's new master gave him a list of high-ranking targets, all of whom were to be eliminated with the flashy display of skill seen over five years ago in [[Kholinar]]. Szeth carved a bloody path across the continent, slaying, among many others, a [[Selay]] [[gerontarch]] and two [[Azir|Azish]] Primes. His second-to-last kill was King [[Hanavanar]] of Jah Keved, whom he killed on  along with his two Shardbearer guards and many others who were present. This led directly to the Veden civil war.

King [[Taravangian]], the last name on Szeth's hit list, turned out to be his secret employer. When Szeth arrived in [[Kharbranth]], Taravangian revealed a portion of his schemes, showcasing his "hospitals" in which people were slowly killed in order to draw out [[Death Rattle]]s. Upon showing this to Szeth, he ordered him to kill [[Dalinar Kholin]].

During a [[highstorm]], Szeth attacked King [[Elhokar]]'s palace on the [[Shattered Plains]], entering it through a hole cut in the wall with his Blade. On his way, he killed [[Beld]] and cut through [[Hobber]]'s midriff, crippling him, then proceeded to fight against [[Adolin]], [[Kaladin]], and [[Dalinar]], all of whom believed he was there to assassinate Elhokar. Szeth quickly gained the upper hand, lashing Adolin to the ceiling and cutting through Kaladin's arm. However, he ultimately failed, as Kaladin managed to push him through his entryway. The two of them fell to the ground, both surviving. Szeth panicked when he saw Kaladin healing his arm, and when Kaladin told him that he was a Windrunner, Szeth fled.

After his confrontation with Kaladin, Szeth retreated to the top of Urithiru, shaken by Kaladin's words and wondering if he was wrong the entire time. Ultimately, he decided to confront Taravangian about the matter. He found the king in [[Vedenar]], and, control almost gone, demanded to know if the [[Knights Radiant]] were returning. Taravangian managed to appease him by claiming that an Honorblade with [[Regrowth]] has been stolen. Having heard that, Szeth departed, vowing to kill [[Kaladin]]; however, Taravangian forbade him to approach Kaladin, demanding that Szeth kill only Dalinar.

#### Death and Resurrection

> What I am owed will come to me eventually! Every bit of it. I will drown in it, stonewalker!
> — Szeth to Dalinar

Szeth attacked Dalinar during the climax of the [[Battle of Narak]] on . Dalinar was able to temporarily hold his own before being lashed to the sky, to eventually fall to his death after the Stormlight sustaining the lashing ran out. Moments after his death, Szeth was revived by [[Nale]], who used a fabrial to heal his body before his spirit departed. Though Szeth was initially outraged at being returned to life, Nale promised to give him a new purpose and gifted him with [[Nightblood]].

### Skybreaker Trainee ()
#### Yeddaw
After gifting him with Nightblood, Nale inducted Szeth into the [[Skybreakers]], and took him to [[Yeddaw]] in order to find a potential Radiant. Throughout this time, Szeth attempted to convince Nale of the upcoming [[True Desolation]], but was unable to get through to him. 

During his time there, he met [[Lift]] in the city's [[Grand Indicium]]. He confronted her and drew Nightblood to test her, but left when Nightblood's power found her innocent. Later, he encountered her again, chasing after him and the other Skybreakers. He stopped to inform her that she could not possibly get there before they did, but also shared the identity of the person they were seeking.

After the Everstorm passed over Yeddaw, Nale departed in a hurry, leaving Szeth stranded in Yeddaw. He was picked up by the Herald after a few weeks and taken to join the other Skybreakers. There, master [[Ki]] informed him that his mission with [[Nale]] was his first task, and that after being evaluated, he was found worthy of the First Ideal. This, however, was not enough for him to draw Stormlight, as Skybreaker trainees can only do so after attaining the Second.

#### Second Ideal
After swearing the First Ideal, Szeth was taken to the [[Purelake]] along with other Skybreaker hopefuls and squires to complete the next test. The challenge was based on a message sent via [[spanreed]] by the city's minister, who told them there was a breakout of convicts. The request was to find the guilty and execute them. While most of the squires and hopefuls started immediately to find and execute the convicts, Szeth stayed and questioned their motives, learning more about the local law.

He was able to find and fight one. He found that the convict had a knife, but was thin, dirty and emaciated. Upon questioning him, Szeth also learned that the prison fort had only one guard. Once back, he saw other convicts, confirming that they had all been poorly fed and under a strict regime, leading him to the realization that the prison itself was improperly run. When he confirmed with Ki that the prison administrator was also on the list of the guilty, Szeth drew Nightblood and killed the man. This allowed him to swear the Second Ideal, letting him draw Stormlight. Impressed, Ki chose him as her squire.

> I swear to seek justice, to let it guide me, until I find a more perfect Ideal.
> — The Second Ideal of the [[Skybreakers]], as sworn by Szeth

#### Learning the Truth
The next test was based on the newly acquired ability to Lash by the Skybreaker hopefuls. It involved Lashing oneself through the air and throwing pouches of colored powder at other competitors -- the one with the least spots of color on their outfit would win the game. Szeth originally held the lead, but when the rest of the group realised he would win, he was surrounded and pummeled with pouches. Shortly after, his Stormlight ran out and he fell into the Purelake. Nonetheless, he managed to win, as the water washed away the colors.

Frustrated with the slow progress, Szeth intended to swear his next ideal there and then, but at this point [[Nale]] arrived. The Herald took Szeth and the other full Skybreakers to [[Marat]], where he showed them a village destroyed by the [[Voidbringer]]s before telling them all the truth about the world. Afterwards, Nale declared his intentions: to side with the [[singer]]s, the original inhabitants of Roshar, and oppose humanity.

Initially, Szeth believed he had no choice other than to follow Nale, but in a conversation Nightblood about the topic, he changed his mind, realizing that there was always a choice. He decided to swear himself into service of Dalinar, and joined the [[Battle of Thaylen Field]].

### Serving Dalinar Kholin (-)

During the Battle of Thaylen Field on , Szeth arrived on the field just in time to drop down and slay a [[thunderclast]]. After landing, he met [[Lift]], who let him know that Dalinar asked her to recover the [[King's Drop]], a perfect gemstone. Acknowledging this as Dalinar's will, Szeth joined her in the hunt. Things initially went poorly, especially when Szeth lost Nightblood's sheath to one of the [[Fused]], causing the sword to go out of control and begin devouring him, despite Lift's attempts to help.

Szeth managed to resheathe the blade, and met with Dalinar. After managing to convince the Alethi of his new loyalty, he returned to his search for King's Drop with Lift. 
Together, the two managed to trick the Fused carrying it into letting it go and grabbing a [[Surgebinding#Illumination|Lightwoven]] fake, with Lift bringing the real King's Drop to Dalinar,  who then used it to trap [[Nergaoul]].

He also made it clear that for his Fourth, the Ideal of Crusade, he intended to travel to [[Shinovar]] and cleanse it of the false leaders who made him the Truthless in the first place.

> I swear to follow the will of Dalinar Kholin. This is my oath.

> I will cleanse the Shin of their false leaders, so long as Dalinar Kholin agrees.

During his imprisonment, Navani interviewed him a number of times, asking him about her husband Gavilar's death. #### Guarding Dalinar on the Emuli Front

> I decide now. I **finally** decide. Me. No one else compelling me. Taravangian, know that in killing you, I make it **my choice**.

While watching Dalinar and little [[Gavinor Kholin|Gavinor]], Szeth's highspren told him he must soon complete his Fourth Ideal.

### Cleansing of Shinovar (1175)
#### Leaving Urithiru and Arriving in Shinovar

Before leaving for Shinovar, Szeth and Nightblood waited for [[Kaladin]] in [[Dalinar]]’s office. Szeth overheard Dalinar and Kaladin discussing mental healing for both himself and [[Ishar]], then stayed behind when they went to continue speaking in private. Szeth agreed to meet Kaladin at one of the [[Oathgate]]s, where they would transfer to [[Azimir]] then ride the [[highstorm]] to Shinovar. They arrived in Azimir, and Szeth waited for Kaladin in a separate room. They, their [[spren]], and Nightblood left Azimir with the highstorm.

They spent the night in a town controlled by the [[Coalition of monarchs]], and approached the [[Misted Mountains]] early the following day. Overwhelmed by his first sight of soil in years, Szeth landed, and told Kaladin that there was nothing wrong with a plant that did not move. Szeth's [[12124|spren]] chastised him for his display of emotion, and he obeyed his instruction. When Kaladin refused to crush a plant, Szeth questioned if he had become a coward, and was resistant to the the idea that he'd said something wrong. Szeth explained to Kaladin that Shinovar contained no plants like those which grew in eastern [[Roshar]], then the two passed into Shinovar itself.

#### The Fifth Ideal

Szeth refused to put up a fight until he received further details. ### Masters

#### Dalinar Kholin

> I serve Dalinar Kholin. I cannot know truth, so I follow one who does.
> — Szeth in reference to his change in allegiance.

[[Dalinar]] is initially nothing more than another target to Szeth. Acting under [[Taravangian]]'s orders, he makes two separate attempts at assassinating Dalinar.

At the [[Battle of Thaylen Field]], Szeth chooses to follow Dalinar, as he believes he cannot see what is right any longer. He acts as Dalinar's bodyguard, following him nearly constantly. Szeth devotes himself to Dalinar because of his reputation, and how he lives up to it. That reputation is far more important than the man himself, in Szeth's eyes. Szeth wishes that he wasn't aware of Dalinar's questions or uncertainty. He views him as an unshakable Ideal, someone he needs to trust absolutely. Even during the [[cleansing of Shinovar]], Szeth still wants to be with Dalinar, but acknowledges that he has another task to do.

#### Nale

> I watched you destroy yourself in the name of order, watched you obey your personal code when others would have fled or crumbled. Szeth-son-Neturo, I watched you keep your word with perfection. This is a thing lost to most people--it is the only genuine beauty in the world. I doubt I have ever found a man more worthy of the Skybreakers than you.
> — [[Nale]] to Szeth

At first, Szeth is outraged at [[Nale]] for bringing him back to life after intentionally letting himself die. Szeth is persuaded to accept training as a [[Skybreaker]], and begins following Nale as his master. He continuously tries to convince Nale that the [[Voidbringers]] have returned, but his warnings are ignored. Szeth treats Nale with reverence and respect. Even when [[Nightblood]] insists that Nale is evil, Szeth refuses to draw the weapon and fight him. Szeth agrees with Nale's assertions that people need to rely on a strict, external code to guide their actions. He does not follow Nale's suggestion to swear to the law itself, instead choosing to follow Dalinar. This leads to Szeth and Nale being on opposite sides of the war for over a year.

When Nale arrives in [[Shinovar]], Szeth initially closes himself off to [[Kaladin]] and returns to strictly obeying Nale’s orders. [[Elid-daughter-Zeenid|Elid]] and Nale inform Szeth that he is meant to become a new [[Herald]], and he questions Nale directly, wondering if he’s insane. Szeth later refuses to give Nale an oath that he will accept his place without question. At Nale’s change of heart, Szeth reassures him that he can be helped.

#### Taravangian

> "You are a monster."<br />
"Yes. But I am the monster who will save this world."
> — Szeth and [[Taravangian]]

[[Taravangian]] serves as Szeth's master for a time, ordering him to assassinate various key political figures such as [[Hanavanar]], [[Dalinar]], and two Azish [[Prime_Aqasix|Primes]]. While Taravangian holds Szeth's [[Oathstone]], he obeys him absolutely, even when in great distress.

Once Szeth begins accepting that he was never [[Truthless]], he stops following Taravangian in favor of [[Nale]], and later Dalinar. Szeth sees Taravangian as manipulative and untrustworthy, and warns Dalinar that there is always more behind his words. His distrust of Taravangian is enough that he disregards anything he says as manipulation, even when he is telling the truth. Szeth deeply despises Taravangian. When he learns that his [[Neturo-son-Vallano|father]] is dead, Szeth comes to interrogate Taravangian, eventually killing him and unintentionally bringing about his Ascension to [[Odium]].

#### 12124

> This emotion is unfitting of your station. Do not spoil your dignity with base sentimentality. You serve the law.
> — [[12124]] to Szeth

Szeth initially views his [[12124|spren]] as another of his masters, and obeys his commands, even when his life is on the line. He describes 12124 as always judging his worthiness. He accepts 12124's assertions that emotion is of [[Odium]], and that honor exists in strict obedience, though Szeth profoundly struggles with such restrictions. Szeth begins losing some of his reverence for 12124 while fighting [[Pozen-son-Nash|Pozen]] and the [[Order of Edgedancers|Edgedancer]] [[Honorbearer]] in [[Shadesmar]].

Szeth feels lonely when 12124 returns to speaking with him infrequently, and 12124 begins to acknowledge that he doesn't like to see Szeth hurting. Szeth finds it remarkable that the spren might need time to think about how to proceed. While 12124 is well-meaning when broaching the topic of Szeth becoming a new [[Herald]], he offers little emotional support.

When Szeth attains the Fifth Ideal, 12124 is amazed. Szeth immediately breaks their [[Nahel bond|bond]], expressing disapproval of the methods used by [[Nale]]'s group of [[Skybreakers]] and stating that he and 12124 aren't well-suited to one another.

#### Ki

> It is orderly, and you have explained it well. Why would I complain?
> — Szeth to [[Ki]], after being told that he will have to follow the same path as other [[Skybreaker]] hopefuls.

Szeth is respectful of Ki. When he asks questions about the structure and methods of [[Nale]]'s group of [[Skybreakers]], he accepts her answers without complaint. He becomes Ki's squire after the Skybreaker test at the [[Purelake]]. Szeth defends his actions during the pole fight test to her and [[Warren]], explaining that he never broke the rules of the contest. Ki is impressed by this, not having realized the extent of Szeth's skill. He and Ki are now on opposite sides of the war against [[Odium]], as Szeth split from the bulk of the Skybreakers when he swore himself to [[Dalinar]].

### Nightblood

> I'm sure you can spot [evil]. You seem smart. If increasingly kind of boring.
> — [[Nightblood]] to Szeth

Szeth acquires [[Nightblood]] from Nale after his death and resurrection. He does not react with either bloodlust or nausea, as his desire to only do what he is told align's with Nightblood's [[Intent]] perfectly. He finally learns Nightblood’s name after months of carrying it, and refers to it with the honorific “sword-nimi". Nightblood is sentient and can communicate with Szeth telepathically. The two often have conversations, and throughout them, Nightblood has learned a fair deal about Szeth's past, including references to the [[Ishar|Voice]] he heard when he was younger. In turn, the sword has no sense of time and casually speaks of his creators and wielders, such as [[Vivenna]], [[Shashara]], and [[Vasher]], even though Szeth has no idea who they are. However, Szeth often finds Nightblood's words to be helpful and profound, and when the [[Battle of Thaylen Field]] comes, it's Nightblood that helps him decide which side to take.

Szeth is warned not to draw Nightblood from its sheath except in case of extreme emergency, and only if he carries a great deal of [[Stormlight]], as the sword is extremely dangerous and can feed upon souls. Occasionally, he will draw it partially to see how other people react to it, as Nightblood causes "good" people to feel sudden nausea.

Szeth has fully drawn the sword only a handful of times. The first is while being tested by the [[Skybreakers]] at the [[Purelake]], the second is at the [[Battle of Thaylen Field]], and the third is to strike at the Herald Ishar. The next time he draws Nightblood is while fighting two [[Honorbearers]] in Shadesmar. The final time Szeth uses Nightblood while it’s unsheathed is while freeing his [[Neturo-son-Vallano|father]], [[Elid-daughter-Zeenid|sister]], and the other Honorbearers from [[Ishar]]’s control. In each case, the sword single-mindedly begins screaming into Szeth's mind while quickly consuming any available [[Stormlight]], causing him extreme pain and eventually eating away his right arm. Nightblood insists that it wouldn't deliberately attack Szeth, as it doesn't consider him to be evil. It later comes to question this, and Szeth discusses the morality of his past actions with the sword.

Between Nightblood and Szeth, Nightblood is the first to confess that it no longer wants to kill. It later decides that it doesn’t want to kill its friends, and stops disintegrating Szeth and [[Kaladin]], even while still unsheathed. When Szeth regains consciousness and believes that Kaladin is dead, he reassures Nightblood that it wasn’t the one to kill him, affirming that it stopped before destroying needlessly.

### Lift

Szeth first encounters [[Lift]] in [[Yeddaw]], while accompanying [[Nale]] and two [[Skybreaker]] squires on a mission to hunt down Surgebinders emerging in the city. Shortly afterword, he tells her about the voices of the dead he hears, and gives her information about the presumed [[Lightweaver]] the other Skybreakers are pursuing. He warns her about the danger of trying to save the person on her own, but she goes anyway.

Szeth and Lift next cross paths during the [[Battle of Thaylen Field]], and work together to retrieve the [[King's Drop]] for [[Dalinar]]. When he looses [[Nightblood]]’s sheath and the sword begins feeding on his soul, Szeth tries to warn Lift of the danger, but she still attempts to heal him. He takes the things she says at face value, even when they’re intended to be sarcastic. Szeth thinks that Lift’s way of sliding on her knees while using [[Abrasion]] is silly, but listens to and implements her ideas on how to take the gemstone from the hands of the [[Fused]]. He trusts her to bring the ruby to Dalinar when he is unable to enter the area where Dalinar confronts the [[Thrill]].

### Navani Kholin

During the year Szeth spends imprisoned in [[Urithiru]], he occasionally gets questioned by [[Navani]]. He tells her about the night he assassinated [[Gavilar]], and explains how he was given the [[black sphere]]. He has also given her facts about [[Nightblood]] and mentioned his training with the [[Honorblade]]s. Szeth is aware of Navani's hatred of him, and even thanks her for her negative opinion.

### Family

#### Neturo-son-Vallano

> I don't want you to think you *have* to care for your old father. But I'll go with you, Szeth, so you don't have to go by yourself. It's the only way I know how to help.
> — [[Neturo-son-Vallano|Neturo]] to Szeth

Neturo-son-Vallano is Szeth's father. Throughout the changes in Szeth's early life, Neturo follows him from one place to another. Each time, Szeth is grateful that he isn't alone. Neturo only breaks this pattern when Szeth is named [[Truthless]]. 

After becoming Truthless, Szeth feels too ashamed to associate himself with his father, and thus besmirch his name. This leads him to taking on the name of Szeth-son-son-Vallano, as a way to keep Neturo's name clean. He even goes so far as to try to stop thinking about him entirely.

Szeth and Neturo are separated for close to a decade. Even after so long apart, Szeth still loves him and is deeply shaken by news of his death.

During the [[cleansing of Shinovar]], Szeth begins reclaiming his father's name for himself. He learns that Neturo has become a human [[Fused]]. He sees that Neturo is suffering, and uses [[Nightblood]] to free him from [[Ishar]]'s control. Szeth chooses to keep living, and says that he cannot follow Neturo into death.

#### Elid-daughter-Zeenid
Elid is Szeth's sister. She resents Szeth for tearing their family apart, having kept his father's and mother's side in their times of sorrow. She wanted Szeth to refuse to change residences for fear of further familial separation. She trains to be the Dustbringer Honorbearer to prove that not all would refuse an Honorblade as Szeth did. She falls prey to Ishar's Voice and becomes a human Fused, dissolving in black smoke when killed by Honorblades or Shardblades. She is killed finally by Nightblood.

#### Zeenid-daughter-Beth
Zeenid is Szeth's mother.
She followed Szeth to the Stoneward monastery, where she had the option of being a washer woman or cleaning floors. She did not follow Szeth when he was transferred to the Elsecaller monastery as she believed Szeth to be an adult who could manage himself.
There is evidence, according to Neturo, that he and Zeenid were having disagreements, and their separation has led to Zeenid being happier.

#### Molli

> He bent down next to old Molli, scratching at her ears as she rubbed her head against him. She might lick rocks and eat dirt, but she was always good for a hug. He loved her warmth, the scratchy wool on his cheek, the way she kept him company when the others wandered.
> — Young Szeth

[[Molli]] is Szeth's favorite sheep in his [[Neturo-son-Vallano|father]]'s flock. He appreciates that she always stays close, and enjoys giving her hugs. He finds comfort in petting her wool when he's stressed or upset. He is devastated when he discovers that soldiers from the [[Stoneward]] monastery have killed and eaten her. Szeth's [[Zeenid-daughter-Beth|mother]] gives him a small toy made of Molli's wool, which he keeps for years. He eventually recovers it as an adult.

## Trivia

### Continuity Change
Szeth's death was subject to a continuity change. In the original hardcover edition of *[[Words of Radiance]]*, Szeth was killed by [[Kaladin]] driving [[Syl]] as a [[Shardblade]] into his chest, severing his spine. Brandon felt this was out of character for Kaladin, and changed the text with the release of the paperback edition of the book. Szeth's death now occurred when he unbonded his Blade and fell through the Highstorm.
