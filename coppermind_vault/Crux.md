{{spoilers|iote}}

**Crux** is an [[Iriali]] woman in the [[cosmere]], likely from [[Zidorna]].{{book ref|iote|28}}{{book ref|iote|30}} She is married to [[Nol]], another Iriali, with whom she has a son. She speaks [[Yolen#Ethnicity and Language|Yolish]] and has the golden hair typical of her people.{{book ref|iote|25}}{{book ref|iote|27}}

==History==
Following a map passed down in Nol's family depicting a portion of the [[Iriali#History|Long Trail]] record, Crux and her family sold all of their belongings to purchase a ship to sail through the [[Cognitive_Realm#Emberdark|emberdark]] in the hopes of being the ones to rediscover a hidden [[perpendicularity]] shown thereon. However, due to a character shift in Middle-old Iriali they misinterpreted the coordinates and were searching the wrong part of the emberdark when an electrical fire started in the ship's cockpit.{{book ref|iote|27}}{{book ref|iote|28}}{{book ref|iote|30}} They deployed [[Investiture|Invested]] floats so as to not sink into the [[Cognitive_Realm#Emberdark|unsea]] and turned on an Invested distress beacon before moving on top of the diminutive ship to get out of the smoke and fire inside.{{book ref|iote|25}}{{book ref|iote|27}} While the distress beacon was eventually picked up by the crew of the *[[Dynamic]]*, the Investited signal also attracted a [[Evil|Type 1-6 Threnodite entity]].{{book ref|iote|25}}

[[Illistandrista|Starling]] and the crew of the *Dynamic* were able to distract the [[Evil|Eidolith]] long enough to mount a rescue and brought Crux and her family on board. When asked by [[ZeetZi]] why they were so far outside the regular lanes of travel, Crux lied telling them that they were trying to get home to Zidorna in an inherited ship when their [[Knell]] compass broke and they were attempting to navigate back to [[Silverlight]].{{book ref|iote|28}} The family was then brought to Silverlight where they had relatives at the university.{{book ref|iote|28}}{{book ref|iote|30}} During the voyage, Crux and Nol agreed to give Starling their map both as a token of thanks and to remove the temptation of trying something so foolish again.{{book ref|iote|30}}
