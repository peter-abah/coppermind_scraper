**Marewill flowers** are a type of flowers that existed on [[Scadrial]] before and after the [[Final Empire]]. Marewill flowers are blue-white colored.{{wob ref|7}}{{file ref|Secret_History_US_Hardcover.jpg|Mistborn: Secret History 2022 Hardcover cover art}}

## Mare's flower
After the [[Ascension of the Lord Ruler]], the world had no flowers. However, [[Kelsier]]'s wife, [[Mare]], was fascinated by [[Classical Scadrial]], and collected pictures of the old era. She held a piece of paper that had an image of a flower on it, which perplexed both Kelsier and [[Vin]], who had never seen the strange plants.{{book ref|mb1|17}}

After Mare died, Kelsier kept the paper with the flower and used it as a symbol of hope.

Later, after Kelsier's death, the picture of the flower was passed onto Vin.

During the [[Final Ascension]], [[Sazed]] used the image of the flower, along with other [[Larsta]] descriptions of the plants, to help restore the world from the damage of the Lord Ruler.{{book ref|mb3|82}} He left the original image of Mare's flower to [[Spook]] in one of his books, which would later be called the [[Words of Founding]].{{book ref|mb3|epilogue}}

## After the Final Ascension
Marewill flowers were located in the [[Field of Rebirth]], on a hill leading to the statues and tombs of the [[Elend Venture|Last Emperor]] and the [[Vin|Ascendant Warrior]].{{book ref|mb4|10}} They were named by [[Spook]].{{wob ref|697}} 

The Marewill flower was chosen as the basis of the [[Ghostbloods]]' three overlapping diamonds insignia.{{book ref|sa2|64}}{{book ref|mb7|20}}{{file ref|Shadesmar Spren.jpg|Shallan's Sketchbook - Shadesmar Spren}}{{book ref|mb7|18}}

[[Hoid]] has been known to wear clothing with the Marewill flower in a repeating pattern.{{file ref|BattleOfWits.jpg|The Battle of Wits}}
