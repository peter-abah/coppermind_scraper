**Thunor** is a god in the [[Earth (Frugal Wizard)|alternate medieval dimension]], and he is the son of [[Woden]] and [[Friag]].{{book ref|fwh|11}} Since Friag's death, Thunor punishes any mortal who desecrates her memory.{{book ref|fwh|13}}

==History==
During the [[battle of Badon]], Thunor and the other gods retreated after [[Fenris]], the great wolf, was released by the [[Black Bear]]. Fenris had the implements of [[metodgodas]] with him, which would bring about the end of gods, and the gods did not want to risk the end of the world. However, Thunor's mother, [[Friag]], returned to the battle after she heard the men crying out in pain, death, and desperation. After pushing back the Black Bear with the finest boasts, and binding him to the land as if he were a [[wight]], Friag was weakened.{{book ref|fwh|13}} Fenris still remained unbound, and under the Black Bear's orders,{{book ref|fwh|24}} he took the opportunity to consume the weakened god, and he killed her.{{book ref|fwh|13}} Thunor's father, [[Woden]], forbade men from writing as punishment for Friag's death, as the runes had been her invention.{{book ref|fwh|11}}{{book ref|fwh|13}} From that point on, Thunor and Woden's other sons would punish any mortal who desecrated their mother's memory by writing.{{book ref|fwh|13}}

{{sidequote|Now, Woden's sons punish any mortals who desecrate her memory.|[[Sefawynn]]{{book ref|fwh|13}}|side=left|size=300px}}

It is possible that when [[John West]] first entered the [[Earth (Frugal Wizard)|alternate medieval dimension]], Thunor tried to explode the book that John had with him with a fire blast, as punishment for the writing in it.{{book ref|fwh|15}}

While travelling to rescue [[Wyrm]], [[Sefawynn]] and [[Ealstan]] told John about the gods they worship. John recognized the god, Thunor, as sounding similar to the Norse god, Thor. From that, John realized that the alternate medieval dimension that he was in was likely an Anglo-Saxon dimension, with people who worshipped Norse-like gods.{{book ref|frugal|11}}

Later, while rescuing [[Yazad (Frugal Wizard)|Yazad]] from a [[Hordamen]] ship, John used ink to draw a rune on the deck of the boat.{{book ref|fwh|14}} Thunor sent a warning to John and the Hordamen,{{book ref|fwh|15}} by setting the rune on fire and cracking thunder across the cloudless sky.{{book ref|fwh|14}} John survived the flames by jumping off the side of the boat into the water, but the boat was destroyed by Thunor's fire. Even after the boat had mostly sunk into the water, the flames continued to smolder.{{book ref|fwh|14}}{{book ref|fwh|15}}

John, Ealstan, and Sefawynn accompanied Yazad back to the preserve. Once there, Sefawynn told a group of children the tale of how Yazad was rescued, and how Thunor struck down the Hordaman ship with flames and thunder.{{book ref|fwh|17}}

==Trivia==
*In Old English, "Thunor" was the equivalent of "Thor" from real-world Norse mythology.{{wp ref|Thor}}
*In the modern English language, the day of the week "Tuesday" is derived from the Old English, "thunresdaeġ", roughly translating to "Thunor's day".{{wp ref|Thor}}
