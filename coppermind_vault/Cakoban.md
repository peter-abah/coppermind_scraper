**Cakoban**, also known as **Tenth the Navigator** and the **First trapper of Patji**, is an [[Eelakin]] explorer, and later mythological hero, on [[First of the Sun]].{{book ref|iote|3}}{{book ref|iote|9}} He is an experienced [[Navigator]].{{book ref|iote|52}}

## Appearance and Personality
Cakoban is of Eelakin ethnicity, meaning that he likely has brown skin and black hair.{{book ref|iote|3}}{{book ref|iote|1}} Although the lore says that Cakoban is a large man, he is actually lean and wiry, not fully five and a half feet tall, but with muscles that bulge. He usually wears the traditional wrap and loincloth.{{book ref|iote|52}}

## History
Not much is known of Cakoban's early life, but he was among the explorers who found the [[First of the Sun|Drominad]] [[perpendicularity]]. After dropping most of their people off at the portal, he left again on a long expedition, with three other ships, to search the darkness. They were separated during a strange storm, and when he’d used his senses to find one of the other ships, he found it wrecked on an island with no survivors. While surveying the area though, they were ambushed by an [[Evil|entity]] and fled. They managed to stay just ahead of it for weeks, but they couldn't catch any [[skullsnake]]s for food. At some point, realising that they wouldn't make it back to the perpendicularity and noticing a [[dakwara's island|nearby island]], Cakoban decided to sacrifice himself for the crew. He leapt from the boat and tried to swim to the island. However, before he could make it, the beast picked him up and fatally wounded him. Before dying, Cakoban bound the dakwara to his will, telling it to guard the island and leave his crew alone. Seconds before Cakoban died, [[Frost]], one of Cakoban's friends, arrived. He was the one to bury Cakoban in the nearby cave.{{book ref|iote|52}}

His crew would later return to the perpendicularity, and tell an embellished version of the events. They, at Cakoban's request, told the people that he knew he could defeat the dakwara, and continued his travels through the Cognitive Realm.{{book ref|iote|52}} It seems that during the millennia that followed, Cakoban became a great myth, and the loremothers began telling stories of his great deeds.{{book ref|iote|3}} Some [[trapper]]s also wore medallions with his face on it.{{book ref|iote|9}}
