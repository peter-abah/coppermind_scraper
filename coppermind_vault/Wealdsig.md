> Kill them. Once you do, remember I let you go, and that all we really want is to live.
> — Wealdsig urges [[Wyrm|Wyrm's]] rescuers to kill [[Ulric Stromfin|Ulric]] and his gang

**Wealdsig** is the [[Midfather]] of [[Wellbury]], one of two reeves in the lands ruled by [[Alwin|the earl]]. He works with [[Ulric Stromfin]] and his gang to kidnap and hold [[Wyrm]] captive. However, after discovering [[Runian]] has the ability to heal, Wealdsig allows Wyrm to escape with his rescuers, and encourages them to find Ulric and his gang, and kill them.

==Appearance and Personality==

> Wealdsig is not the type to give outlanders his devotion. He's not that...dependable honestly. He'll do what he finds amusing at the moment.
> — [[Ealstan]] to Runian

Wealdsig is a thin, scraggly-haired older man who has only one eye. He has a grey beard that is also scraggly. He wears a cloak, and carries a knife with a wolf's head on the pommel in a sheath at his side. At times, he also wields an axe. The fingers on his right hand were once curled and couldn't move. However, [[Runian]] used his [[Nanites|medical nanites]] to restore and heal Wealdsig's hand, which is now fully functional. He often has a wild grin on his face, and a matching wild look in his eyes. 

Wealdsig is a lonely man, and he has allowed himself to feel mad with the grief of losing all of his sons. He is known to drink and get tipsy, and he holds nightly feasts and [[Skop|boasts]]. He likes to be entertained, and he appears to enjoy fighting and combat. He is also zealously devoted to [[Woden]]. Wealdsig can seem unpredictable, frantic, bizarre, erratic, and he can give others the impression that he doesn't care about anyone or anything. He can sometimes be intimidating to his soldiers. Wealdsig often makes decisions based on what he finds amusing at the time. However, he takes the protection of his land and people seriously, and is willing to seek outside help to do so. [[Ealstan]] respects the midfather's heart. Wealdsig understands what it's like to be scared of a foreign invader. However, he is not a coward, and is used to bullies and fighting against terrible odds. He, like his people, is cowed by the ability to kill, but is more impressed by the ability to heal and live.

==History==
===Pre Outlander Arrival===
Around forty years ago, before the battle of Far Strength, Wealdsig nailed himself to a tree as a sacrifice to [[Woden]]. In doing so, he believed that Woden would give him strength, and tip the scales of the battle in his favor. Wealdsig had considered sacrificing one of his warriors instead, but ultimately he wanted the honor for himself. As a result, he lost the use of his right hand, as those fingers became curled and unable to move. However, this gained him the respect of his people.

Very little is known about Wealdsig's life after the battle of Far Strength. However, at some point in his younger years, he had seven sons, all of whom later died in battle. The identity of his children's mother, or whether he had any daughters, is unknown.

Sometime within the last ten years, Wealdsig granted care of some land near [[Wellbury]] to [[Yazad (Frugal Wizard)|Yazad]] and his followers.

===Outlanders Arrive===
After the arrival of outlanders in [[Weswara]], the [[Black Bear]] was pushing his fighting forces in the direction of Wealdsig's lands. The midfather asked [[Sefawynn]], a [[Skop|skop]], for bindings to protect a settlement near Wellbury. After she performed her best boasts, Wealdsig paid Sefawynn and her brother, [[Wyrm]], for their service, and the two siblings left. Despite the skop's bindings, the settlement was destroyed by the Bear.

At some point within the past few years, [[Ulric Stromfin]] first arrived in Wellbury. Wealdsig likely witnessed Ulric and his gang using guns to kill some of the people of Wellbury. The midfather's people were cowed by the gang's ability to kill at a distance, and Wealdsig was convinced to ally with Ulric. 

More recently, Wealdsig worked with Ulric on a plan to trap [[Ryan Chu]]. As part of this plan, Ulric [[Bearn-gisel|kidnapped]] Wyrm from [[Stenford]], and took him to Wealdsig in Wellbury. Ulric hoped that Ryan would follow and try to rescue Wyrm. When Ulric and his gang arrived in Wellbury, Wealdsig threw Wyrm into a pit to hold him captive. The gang stayed in Wellbury that night, before heading to [[Maelport]] the next morning. They left Wyrm behind, still in the pit. [[Quinn Jericho]], Ulric's right-hand man, also stayed behind with Wealdsig, expecting Ryan to try and rescue Wyrm. Quinn informed Wealdsig that Ryan would have red hair.

===Rescue of Wyrm===
====The Distraction====
That evening, Wealdsig sat in his manor waiting for Ryan, drinking ale, and having his nightly feast. He was alone except for several guards, and a dark-haired woman who was sitting with him at his table. During the feast, [[Runian]] burst into the manor, declaring himself as a fortune teller and a mind reader. Wealdsig was wildly delighted, but he didn't recognize Runian, and assumed him to be Ryan. Wealdsig sent the dark-haired woman to inform Quinn, under the guise of fetching his best drink. In the meantime, Wealdsig demanded that Runian perform tricks for him.

> Ha ha! You're far more fun than the other outlanders, spindly one.
> — Wealdsig, impressed with Runian's tricks

He was seemingly impressed by the tricks that Runian performed with augments. Wealdsig declared him to be more fun than the other outlanders who had visited him, and he demanded more. However, Runian asked instead about those other outlanders. Wealdsig admitted that Ulric had come through Wellbury, but had left for Maelport. He also told Runian that he had thrown Wyrm into the pit. Runian was about to continue distracting Wealdsig with more tricks, when the dark-haired woman returned. She had brought Quinn with her.

Wealdsig laughed at how surprised both outlanders were to see each other. Sitting back down at his table, he asked Quinn about Runian's lack of red hair, wondering out loud if the intruder had the ability to change his appearance. Quinn informed Wealdsig that this outlander was not Ryan, the red-headed man they had been waiting for. Wealdsig noted that Runian still possessed powers like other outlanders, and asked Quinn whether they should be worried about him. Despite Quinn explaining that Runian wasn't dangerous, Wealdsig responded that all outlanders looked weak to him. He continued to drink ale, and watched eagerly as Quinn explained the trap to Runian.

As Quinn picked up a phone that had been disassembled by [[Logna|Runian's wight]], Wealdsig laughed and warned Quinn that the [[Wight|wights]] don't like him. A moment later, Quinn left for Maelport, and Wealdsig made himself comfortable, putting his feet up on the table. By this point he was moderately drunk, and he asked Runian to perform more tricks. Instead, Runian asked to be taken to the pit where Wyrm was. Wealdsig was in the process of refusing, when the sounds of shouting from outside distracted him. He quickly got up from his seat at the table, and he called his guards to his side.

====Wealdsig Finds Hope====
A tipsy Wealdsig and his hearthmen followed Runian out the front door of the manor and into the courtyard, where they were met with scenes of commotion. [[Ealstan]] and Sefawynn had rescued Wyrm from the pit, and they were cornered against the wall. A small group of soldiers had surrounded Ealstan, who was waving his axe, trying to get the soldiers to back away. Sefawynn was behind him, clinging to Wyrm.

> With this... We could go to battle and not worry that our sons and brothers would die... We could stand against the Hordamen. We could thrive...
> — Wealdsig, amazed at Runian's ability to heal

Runian pleaded with Wealdsig to call off his soldiers. Instead, Wealdsig challenged the rescuers to fight. The soldiers made way for an archer, who shot Ealstan in the gut, and severely wounded him. Wealdsig watched as fighting ensued. Runian rushed in to protect his fellow rescuers, using his augments to dominate in the fight, and in doing so, alerted the soldiers to the fact he was an outlander. The soldiers scattered as they realised they could not beat Runian. Archers across the courtyard and atop the walls hesitated, not wanting to draw the powerful outlander's attention. Runian demanded, with his artificially enhanced voice, that the soldiers and archers put down their weapons, offering to spare them if they did. Wealdsig was clearly amused and laughing wildly, impressed by the display of power.

Runian proposed that they join forces together against Ulric and his gang. Wealdsig though, was not convinced that they could overpower Ulric. He urged his soldiers forward, joining them with his axe out, and together they crowded the small group of rescuers. Runian pleaded for Wealdsig to take him and let his friends go. However, Wealdsig mused that Ealstan was bleeding out and close to dead. This caused Runian to push aside the other rescuers and rip out the arrows still lodged in Ealstan's gut.

Wealdsig watched as Runian then used his [[Nanites|medical nanites]] to heal Ealstan's wounds. In a state of awe and hope, Wealdsig asked if Runian could bring back the dead. Runian admitted that he couldn't, but instead told Wealdsig to cut his palm, which he did eagerly. Runian transferred some of his remaining nanites to Wealdsig by pressing one of his own bloodied wounds to Wealdsig's cut palm. This was enough to repair the old injury to Wealdsig's hand from nailing himself to a tree, as well as the fresh cut. 

After contemplating Runian's ability to heal, and with some encouragement from Ealstan, Wealdsig was convinced to ally with Runian instead of Ulric. He agreed to allow the group of rescuers to leave, so long as they punched him first. This was to give Wealdsig a cover story if Ulric and his gang were to return, and they discovered that Wyrm had escaped. Runian punched Wealdsig hard enough to knock him to the ground and leave a bruise, but not break too much. Without getting up from the ground, Wealdsig reminded the group of their deal, and told them to kill Ulric and his gang. He mused that the gang wouldn't know what was coming for them.
