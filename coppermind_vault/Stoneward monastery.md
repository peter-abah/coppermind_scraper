The **Stoneward monastery**, sometimes referred to as **Talmut's monastery**, is a monastery dedicated to the [[Herald]] [[Taln]], known as Talmut by the locals, and associated with the [[Order of Stonewards]]. It is located in the south of [[Shinovar]],{{book ref|sa5|51}} in the Encilo region.{{book ref|sa5|45}}

## Location
The monastery is located in the south of Shinovar, atop a cliff on a long ridge at the foot of the [[Misted Mountains]]. It overlooks the [[Nirovah Valley]]. The ridge is rocky, lacking much dirt.{{file ref|Map_of_Shin_Monasteries.jpg}}{{book ref|sa5|51}}{{book ref|sa5|40}}

## Structure
The monastery is a tall fortress, designed as a watchtower. Inside the front gates is a vaulted great hall, designed to be conducive to repelling assaults. The walls feature cages full of gemstones, open to the outside, so the [[highstorm]] can refill them. These gemstones cast orange-tan light, possibly indicating that they are all [[Polestone#Topaz|topazes]]. The great hall features a large mural of Talmut on the floor, depicting him chained in Damnation.{{book ref|sa5|40}} The great hall is ruined after [[Szeth]]'s battle with [[Rit]]. The floor is lowered, with new pillars made of floor, including the mural. Szeth takes the gemstones from their cages in the walls.{{book ref|sa5|42}}

The monastery is located directly next to a soldier's camp, an elongated settlement along the ridge it sits on.{{book ref|sa5|51}} They are connected by a long and winding path up the cliff face.{{book ref|sa5|57}}

## History
The monastery was founded long ago, presumably around the same time as the other Shin monasteries.{{cite}} For centuries, it was the only Monastery of Truth without an [[Honorblade]] of its own,{{book ref|sa5|57}} as Talenel was the only Herald who did not abandon his oaths and his Blade at [[Aharietiam]].{{book ref|sa1|part=prelude}}{{book ref|sa5|77}}

Around {{Rosharan date|1149}}, the monastery's soldier camp, under the governance of a particular [[Farmer]], became very lax and its soldiers were rowdy.{{book ref|sa5|48}}{{book ref|sa5|57}} One night, after defending the town of [[Clearmount]] from a raid, several soldiers came to the homestead of [[Neturo-son-Vallano]] and [[Zeenid-daughter-Beth]]. These soldiers robbed them for their wine and slaughtered [[Molli|one of their sheep]]. When Szeth-son-Neturo came looking for the sheep, [[Sunken Eyes|one of the soldiers]] got into an argument with him, which escalated into the soldier attempting to choke Szeth and Szeth bludgeoning the soldier to death.{{book ref|sa5|38}} As a result of this, Szeth was required to enter the monastery and train as a soldier. His parents elected to bring the entire family.{{book ref|sa5|48}}

Neturo quickly rose into administration and became the aide-de-camp, a secretary to the [[Kinal|General]]. Under his guidance, the morale at the camp improved significantly. Neturo leveraged his previous experience in herding sheep and teaching other shepherds to make the camp more efficient and happier. He recommended that [[Szrand]] be replaced as the camp's sergeant over training by [[Yago-son-Yargo]]. [[Pozen-son-Nash]] and [[Sivi-daughter-Sivi]] visited the monastery to congratulate Neturo and the General on the improvements to the camp.{{book ref|sa5|57}}

When Szeth was recruited as an acolyte of the Shamans in {{Rosharan date|1157}}, Neturo went with him, to become an administrator of [[Mokdown]].{{book ref|sa5|68}}

After becoming the [[Order of Windrunners|Windrunner]] Honorbearer, Szeth began to believe that the Shin were under the influence of an [[Unmade]]. He recruited the shamans of the Stoneward monastery and attempted to start a civil war.{{cite}}{{expand}}

At some point between {{Rosharan date|1173}} and {{Rosharan date|1175}}, the Shamans of the monastery were able to retrieve Taln's Honorblade, believing that Taln had broken, starting the [[Desolation]], and thus it was their right to take custody of his Blade.{{book ref|sa5|40}}{{book ref|sa1|epilogue}}{{book ref|sa2|76}}

Around the same time, the soldiers all left the camp and headed northwards, hearing [[Ishar|a voice]]. The people of the nearby towns took up residence in the soldier's camp, believing that the soil would swallow them if walked on during the daytime.{{book ref|sa5|40}}

Rit-daughter-Clutio, bearing Taln's Honorblade, became one of Ishar's [[Fused]].{{cite}} She trained with the Blade, learning its unique combination of [[Surges]] that had historically been unavailable to the Honorbearers. She was instructed to wait for Szeth in her monastery once Szeth declared his intent to cleanse Shinovar. She waited months for him to arrive. Upon his arrival, the two dueled.{{book ref|sa5|40}} Rit used the Surge of [[Cohesion]] to melt the floor with Taln's mural and use it as a weapon against Szeth.{{book ref|sa5|41}} Szeth killed her and took the Blade, leaving the chamber with a lowered floor and many pillars made up of the mural of Taln. He took the gemstones from the walls, cutting open their cages with his Shardblade.{{book ref|sa5|42}}

Rit's death seemed to free the people of the camp from whatever ailment they had, causing them to leave their houses in daytime again. The new Farmer of the region, son of the previous Farmer, was among those who thanked Szeth for freeing them.{{book ref|sa5|42}}

## Politics
The monastery has jurisdiction over and provides protection to the nearby Shin towns in the Encilo region.{{book ref|sa5|45}} The area is principally ruled by its Farmer,{{book ref|sa5|48}}{{book ref|sa5|57}}{{book ref|sa5|59}}{{book ref|sa5|68}} with the General{{book ref|sa5|57}} and the Cook{{book ref|sa5|36}} noted as additional rulers. All of these rulers do the dirty work their titles imply in addition to making decisions about the governance of the area.{{book ref|sa5|36}}{{book ref|sa5|68}}

The monastery also has a head shaman, despite the lack of a Blade. At the time of the [[True Desolation]], this position belongs to Rit-daughter-Clutio.{{book ref|sa5|68}} Because the head shaman is not an [[Honorbearer]], they have less say about national Shin issues than the Honorbearers of the other monasteries do.{{book ref|sa5|68}} The monastery is considered relatively backwater, with no cities in its jurisdiction, only towns and homesteads.{{book ref|sa5|40}} Because of its lack of an Honorbearer, the monastery recruits few, if any, acolytes.{{book ref|sa5|68}} The Honorbearers view it as barely a monastery.{{book ref|sa5|83}}
