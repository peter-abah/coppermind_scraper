The **Iriali** are an ethnicity of people in the [[cosmere]].{{book ref|sa2|i|2}}

## Appearance and Anatomy
The Iriali have metallic golden-blond skin and hair that breeds true.{{book ref|sa1|i|5}}{{book ref|sa2|i|2}}{{book ref|tress|2}} They mostly have yellow eyes.{{book ref|sa2|20}} They have had what most would consider to be inhuman ancestry, depending on what is considered human.{{wob ref|11884}}

## Culture
{{image|Salute by Ernanda Souza.jpg|side=right|width=220px|[[Nomad]] wearing an improvised Iriali wrap made from an old sack{{book ref|tsm|35}}{{book ref|tsm|37}}}}
The Iriali, while on Roshar, are particular about chastity laws, and dislike public nudity.{{book ref|twok|i|5}} They tend to be less quarrelsome and prudish than the [[Vorin]] nations. Many Iriali keep travel packs ready for when they must leave for the next Land on the Long Trail.{{book ref|sa5|i|5}}

### Clothing
Iriali men often wear only waist wraps on warmer days.{{book ref|tsm|35}}{{book ref|tsm|37}} Iriali people are known to paint their skin with colors and patterns.{{book ref|sa1|interlude|5}}

### Religion
The Iriali worship a god they call "One". According to this belief, the One knew everything but had experienced nothing. And so the One became Many in order to experience all things. As each experience is different, it brings completeness to the One. Eventually, all will be gathered back in when the seventh land is attained and they will once again become One.{{book ref|sa2|i|2}} Every person is a different mind of a single being experiencing different lives. As Many, they need ignorance. Each fragment of the One’s mind has its own body with different passions and inclinations. They exist in variety to experience all kinds of thought. That means some people must know and others must not. Just like some people must be rich and others poor.{{book ref|sa2|i|2}} The more religious segments of the Iriali on Roshar would approve of homosexuality, because they believe life is about new experiences. The general view of the Iriali on it, however, is currently unknown.{{wob ref|1443}}

This religion and its philosophy of one becoming many may be slightly influenced by the [[Shattering]] of [[Adonalsium]].{{wob ref|14397}} The philosophy behind the One is that the [[Spiritual Realm]] and the [[Beyond]] are the same thing and that, when a person dies, their soul goes with their [[Investiture]] to the Spiritual Realm. Most [[cosmere]] philosophers disagree with this belief.{{wob ref|1729}}

Some Iriali in [[Kasitor]] have come to see [[Cusicesh]] as a god, though most only see it as a natural part of the city, and use it as a system of timekeeping.{{book ref|sa1|i|5}} The Iriali name for the [[Herald]] [[Pralla]] is Pala.{{rpg ref|slsw|5}}

### Language
The Iriali script represents numbers using the same characters as they do their letters. Due to Middle-old Iriali experiencing a letter shift in the 3rd millennium{{ref|group=fn|text=This date is given by [[Eddlin]], a [[Silverlight]] [[arcanist]], and is assumed to be using the Silverlight calendar, but it is unknown what the reference epoch is of this calendar era and if it is [[cosmere standard]].}} some conversion needs to happen to be correctly understood in modern Iriali.{{book ref|iote|30}} 

They speak a language related to the Riran and Reshi languages.{{wob ref|3977}} On Lumar, and likely other planets on their Path, they developed a written script that was written vertically, from top to bottom.{{book ref|tress|2}} While the language is no longer spoken on Lumar after the exodus of the Iriali, it is still known and taught to noblemen.{{book ref|tress|2}}

### Long Trail Tapestries
During their many travels, the Iriali created various tapestries. These are woven from their golden hair, with ink writing on them. At least one tapestry is from the ‘forty-seventh count’. Some Iriali households are said to have them, but they generally don’t share them with outsiders. All of the Long Trail tapestries have the same information; records of visits to various planets the Iriali investigated as potential homelands. Most of them were deemed unsuitable, and many are thought to be mythological. Navigating using such a tapestry as a guide can be difficult, as while the Iriali used the Current to navigate, they had their own landmarks. Some of the tapestries use Silverlight as a prime reference point however. By Silverlight law, the Iriali tapestries belong to the Iriali themselves unless directly gifted. Any sale or transfer to another is forbidden. By the Space Age, most of the tapestries were missing or destroyed.{{book ref|iote|30}}

## History
As part of their worship of One, the Iriali believe that they are part of something they call the "Long Trail," a series of seven Lands which all must be "attained" before they will become One again. The Fourth Land is [[Roshar]],{{book ref|sa2|i|2}} which they arrived on from somewhere besides [[Ashyn]] and separately from the rest of humanity.{{wob ref|1516}}{{wob ref|9551}} The Iriali traveled the cosmere as early as the third millennium in the Silverlight calendar.{{book ref|iote|30}}

Shortly before the advent of the [[Night of Sorrows]], the Iriali left Roshar, traveling into [[Shadesmar]] through [[perpendicularity|perpendicularities]] created by [[Cusicesh the Protector]].{{book ref|sa5|i|11}} The land subsequently after [[Roshar]] may have been [[Scadrial]], where there is a population of golden haired "fairy people" according to [[Maraga Dulcet]].{{book ref|tlm|32}}

Since the Iriali were also on Roshar for thousands of years prior to the beginning of The Stormlight Archive, the only way the timeline can be arranged is with Lumar coming after Roshar.}} lands is [[Lumar]], which they seemingly vanished from overnight about three hundred years before [[Hoid]] gained access to [[AonDor]] from [[Riina]].{{book ref|tress|2}} Their leaving the system had some kind of connection with the arrival of [[Xisis]] around the same time.{{wob ref|16140}} During the Space Age, Iriali lived all over the cosmere, including in [[Zidorna]] and [[Silverlight]].{{book ref|iote|28}}{{book ref|iote|30}}
