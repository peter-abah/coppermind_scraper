The **assassination of [[Gavilar Kholin]]** was an assassination of the sitting [[Alethi]] king performed by [[Szeth-son-son-Vallano]] on orders from the [[listener]] [[Council of Five]]. It was a major turning point in Alethkar's history, leading to the [[Vengeance Pact]] and the [[War of Reckoning]]. 

The events put in motion by Gavilar Kholin's assassination would ultimately lead to the reforming of the [[Knights Radiant]], the birth of [[Retribution]] and the start of the [[True Desolation]], giving it a lasting impact on [[Roshar]] and the [[cosmere]] itself. Thus, it is an event of utmost historical significance, on the national, planetary, and interplanetary scale alike.

## Background

Before he discovered the Parshendi a number of changes were noted in Gavilar -- he started following the [[Alethi Codes of War]], and he developed a fascination for [[The Way of Kings (in-world)|The Way of Kings]]. Not known by most people these changes were due to him receiving visions from the [[Stormfather]]. 

Gavilar wanted to become a [[Herald]] of the Almighty and therefore immortal. He started to make deals with off world figures such as [[Thaidakar]] to gain power. Striking deals with [[Vasher]] and [[Axindweth]] to create [[Lights#|Anti-light]] and transport [[Voidspren]] from [[Braize]] to Roshar respectively. And eventually making an organization to help him bring the [[Knights Radiant|Radiants]] back, the [[Sons of Honor]]. All to make sure he and his legacy would never die.      

Gavilar encountered the Parshendi on an expedition south of [[Alethkar]], while exploring the [[Eastern Crownlands]]. Gavilar showed an unusual amount of interest in the Parshendi. attempting to discover more and more about them, most likely because he discovered their connection with the [[Voidbringers]]. Eventually, in , he even made a treaty with the Parshendi, so that could use them to bring back the [[Desolation|Desolations]].

In addition, [[Szeth]] was traded around until he landed in the hands of [[Liss]], who sold him to a slave trader in Kholinar for “being too good.”

## Before the Feast

### To watch a vision
While in a vision of [[Aharietiam]], Gavilar talks with the Stormfather about becoming Honor's champion, attempting to find the words to become a Herald. The Stormfather scolds him for trying random words, saying that Gavilar will never be able to find the words by chance. Gavilar awakes in his study, whereupon the Stormfather begins to lecture him on immortality. At this point the Stormfather is not fully aware of Gavilar’s true intentions for becoming a Herald.

[[Tearim]], wearing Gavilar's [[Shardplate]], announces [[Dalinar|Dalinar's]] entry. Dalinar asks to talk to the Parshendi; his brother asks why. Before Gavilar's motives can be revealed, he moves the conversation toward Dalinar's past mistakes, manipulating him to get him drunk. He sends a guard with Dalinar to ensure that he finds liquor. The Stormfather comments on Dalinar's possible usefulness until Gavilar denies it.
  

> Gavilar, take care. You're not immortal yet, but you've begun to play with powers that rip mortals apart by their very axi.
> — Thaidakar to Gavilar

After sending away Dalinar, Thaidakar knocks on the door, appearing behind Gavilar and demanding him to honor their past agreement -- turning Restares in to him. Gavilar tries to soothe him by saying that he still needs to gain Restares's confidence. Thaidakar tires of Gavilar's games took off his cloak revealing himself as just using a [[seon]] as a avatar, and as the seon flies away Thaidakar warns Gavilar that he is playing with power that can rip him apart.

After Gavilar regains his composure, [[Restares]] (whom Gavilar thinks only *pretends* to be the Herald [[Kalak]]) and Nale -- whose name Gavilar doesn't know -- walks in unannounced, questioning why Gavilar has been looking into bringing back the Voidbringers. When Gavilar explains that to return the Radiants he would return the Voidbringers, thus bringing back the powers that once defeated them as well, Restares says that they should no longer work for the return of Radiants if it means another Return. Gavilar then threatens to turn Kalak in to Thaidakar, prying for more information on what Thaidakar wanted. Under pressure Kalak reveals that Thaidakar wants [[Ba-Ado-Mishram]]. Kalak next demands to know what Gavilar had done to get what he wanted, and Gavilar reveals the project’s success with transporting [[Voidlight]] and making [[Lights#anti-Stormlight]] to [[Nale]] and Kalak. Gavilar notices that [[Navani]] has been spying on him through the door; when she realizes that he has spotted her, she enters the room, saying that notable [[lighteyes]] are looking for him. Gavilar sends the Heralds out so he can deal with his wife.

### To Prepare  
Navani deals with the chaos of organizing the feast while the king was nowhere to be found. She notices [[Rushur Kris]] and wants to talk to her, attempting to escape becoming trapped in conversation with her daughter-in-law, [[Aesudan]] Before she can shake her daughter off, a cook comes up to her in a panic, saying that Dalinar had drunk the reserve wine. She tells him to use her personal store before going off to deal with more of the lighteyes, who are demanding an audience with the king.

When she manages to slip away from dealing with lighteyes, she heads to her study -- the one with her husband and the two Heralds -- after hearing voices coming from it.
> You are no Scholar. You merely like being near them. You are no artifabrian. You are merely a woman who likes trinkets.
> — Gavilar to Navani
 The two Heralds leave, and Navani asks who they are, knowing them not to just be ambassadors. He tells her that it's not her business and that she should go back to the feast. They fight about the fact he missed appointments and his duties with the feast. Navani presses him to give her more answers to what he has been doing.

He instead berates her for rumors she spread, which have damaged his legacy, before telling her to talk to [[Jasnah]] about considering Amaram offer at marriage again. She tells him to do it himself. When she threatens to defile his legacy, he berates her further, saying that she didn’t deserve greatness but would instead only stand in its shadow. Deeply hurt, Navani watches as Gavilar left, and burned a [[glyphward]] inscribed with the [[glyphs]] "Death. Gift. Death" as a prayer to the Almighty to kill her husband.

### To Sneak

At the same time, [[Venli]], with [[Ulim|Ulim's]] help, sneaks into the palace, trying to find a delivery from Axindweth, but after making it to where the delivery was meant to be all she finds is a note addressed from Axindweth saying that she’s left the planet. A short time later Venli gets caught by the palace guard and taken to a room. Soon after, Nale walks in, taking Venli into his custody. When Nale demanded to know how many Voidspren have returned, to which Venli says that she had only ever seen Ulim.  Nale realizes that Gavilar's plan to transport the Voidspren had worked, telling Venli Gavilar's plan to betray the listeners. He also gives her information on where to find an assassin to kill Gavilar. Nale sets Venli and Ulim them free to allow them to procure the assassin.

### To Relate 

After Gavilar deals with Navani, he moves into his chamber to talk with the Stormfather about Thaidakar and Ba-Ado-Mishram. After questioning the Stormfather about her, [[Amaram]] enters the room with a group of new recruits for the [[Sons of Honor]]. Gavilar gives a short speech, noting that [[Taravangian]] is one of three new recruits accompanying Amaram and a lieutenant.

When he finishes the speech, Gavilar pulls Amaram aside, telling him that his experiment successfully worked. He then moves to talk with Taravangian. He is pleasantly surprised at Taravangian’s acceptance of bringing the Voidbringers back. The conversation moves towards discussions of the [[Night of Sorrows]] and [[Death Rattle]]s. The Stormfather warns Gavilar that [[Eshonai]] is coming, and he ushers everybody out.

### To Fear

While Eshonai and other listeners carried drums to be brought inside for the feast, they discuss [[Singer#Slaveform|Parshmen]]. After they’re done talking Eshonai wanders the halls of the palace before stumbling into the king in a meeting. Before she could move away Gavilar asks her to come in. When the five people -- Amaram and members of the Sons of Honor -- the king shows Eshonai a heating [[fabrial]]. While Eshonai examines, it Gavilar asks about the listeners' gods, informing her that he knew the [[chasmfiend]]s aren't their actual gods. Gavilar shows how small the listeners are compared to the rest of Roshar then tells her to tell the Five that he would bring their gods back.

Eshonai, once the king leaves, goes running to meet the [[Listener#The_Five|Five]]. She relays what Gavilar has told her. The Five, after a quick debate, decide that killing Gavilar is the only way forward. They will use [[Klade|Klade's]] new slave assassin that he got with Venli.

## The Feast and Assassination

With Gavilar having signed the treaty with the Listeners, the feast properly begins.
 
### To Witness

> This is the tiresome part of the feast, where the conversation grows louder but no smarter, and the company drunken
> — Jasnah to Gavilar

Close to halfway through the feast Jasnah makes her way to a appointment with an assassin, Liss, noticing a [[Shin]] man dressed in white carrying Parshendi drums into the feast. After leaving, she sees her shadow pointing the wrong direction, before bumping into Amaram and Gavilar. A short conversation starts between her and Gavilar who implies that she should consider partying with Amaram before swiftly leaving.

Once again, Jasnah notices that her shadow points towards light sources instead of away from them, and resolving to face the problem. She faces the shadow, which starts to lengthen, taking the shape of a man.  More figures appear, and in a panic Jasnah unintentionally draws upon [[Stormlight]], using the [[Surgebinding#Transportation|Surge of Transportation]], Elsecalling herself into the [[Cognitive Realm]]. Jasnah drops into the ocean of beads, frantically using the beads to create a facsimile of the hall she was standing in a moment ago. The figure from her shadow hops on to her platform, sword drawn, but before he can approach her Jasnah creates a statue of [[Taln|Talenelat’Elin]] to fight the man. The figure hesitates, then bows to Jasnah. To her great relief, she reappears back in the palace in the [[Physical Realm]].

After recovering, she rushes to her appointment with Liss. Jasnah initally wished to hire Liss to assassinate Aesudan, but as the meeting progresses, she decides that doing that would be brash and hasty, and instead just hiring her to watch Aesudan. When the meeting ended, Jasnah started to make her back to the main feast, but before she made it back she overhears Nale and Kalak talking about [[Shalash|Ash]], and somebody with a [[Shardblade]].

As Jasnah hears screams coming from the feast, she starts to run toward them, reaching Gavilar just in time to see the balcony collapse under him. Right as Gavilar dies, three of the Five (Klade, [[Gangnah]], and [[Varnali]]) come up to Jasnah and take full responsibility for the assassination of Gavilar. They are later hanged for their crimes. 

### To Assassinate 

As required by the listeners, Szeth wears only white, so that he will be seen coming when he starts Gavilar's assassination. He waits in the feast hall after being brought in with the listeners’ drums, then moved toward the beggars feast as he starts his rampage, passing a drunk [[Jezrien]] on his way. Two guards stop him on the way to Gavilar's chamber; Szeth kills them with his expert use of [[Surgebinding]], making sure that one stays alive long enough to alert the other guards.

As Szeth fights, killing indiscriminately, Sadeas dresses to look like a king and is ushered out of the king’s chambers; Gavilar puts on Sadeas’s plate in a attempt to fool the assassin into passing him and focusing on Sadeas instead. Szeth quickly sees through their plan, and starts to fight Gavilar. Through Gavilar’s skill with Shards, he manages to push Szeth onto a wooden balcony, the king’s Plate barely holding together. But before Gavilar can finish Szeth off, the assassin lashes the balcony downwards, dropping it to the ground.

### To Die

After the balcony falls, Gavilar lies in the palace grounds, pierced in the side with wood from the balcony. In Gavilar’s final moments he says that he expected Szeth to come, thinking that he had been sent by Thaidakar. Szeth corrects him, denying any knowledge of Thaidakar, and when prodded further by Gavilar, says that his masters are the Parshendi.

Gavilar, confused and realizing that his legacy will fall apart with him dead, tries to give the anti-Voidlight sphere to the Stormfather, saying to the Stormfather to tell Dalinar to find the most important words a man can say. Szeth interprets Gavilar’s last word as a request for him to fulfill, and grabbed the sphere —- which he will later bury —- then wrote Gavilar's final words with Gavilar's blood and hand onto a piece of wood before he left. This later has major implications as it implies the scandal of the king knowing how to read and write.

### To Mourn

Navani doesn’t know about the assassination until a messenger girl brings her to [[Gereh]], who, after she calms him down, manages to tell her that Gavilar had died. Only after seeing the body does the truth of her husband’s death fully set in. Numb, she mourns over his body, realizing she prayed for his death only a few hours before. After mourning she begins to organize the city from the chaos of the king's death.

## Aftereffects

### The Vengeance pact and The War of Reckoning

After the Gavilar’s death, his son [[Elhokar]] is crowned the new king. With the [[Highprince|Highprinces]] he forms the Vengeance pact which is an agreement to avenge Gavilar by going to war against the listeners. This begins the War of Reckoning. The war is meant to last no more then a couple months, but due to the Parshendi moving into the center of the [[Shattered Plains]], [[Narak]], and the discovery of Chasmfiend [[Gemheart|Gemhearts]] the war drags out for years.

Socially, this furthers discriminatory attitudes the listeners, and further ingrains in Alethi society a system of dehumanization towards them. This was due to the fact that they saw the betrayal and assassination of Gavilar on the night a treaty was meant to be signed as unassailable evidence of their "savagery," thus being incapable of truly integrating into Alethi society or even forming peace between their peoples. This narrative of savagery, intermixed with fear and hatred, was present even before the assassination. But these fires are nonetheless stoked by the death of Gavilar Kholin.

### Jasnah's studies of the Voidbringers

After the assassination, Jasnah begins to study everything that had transpired on that night. Eventually she hits a dead end, instead beginning to study everything Gavilar read and did. This leads her to study the Voidbringers and her subsequent discovery that the Parshendi and the Voidbringers are one and the same.

### Dalinar's trip to the Nightwatcher

During Gavilar's funeral, Jasnah reads the Way of Kings to Dalinar, which, along with the words of his dead wife and Gavilar’s writen message to him, inspires Dalinar to change his ways. While on his way to the Shattered Plains he stops at the [[Valley]], home of the [[Nightwatcher]] where he begged for forgiveness. Because she can't give him such a boon she requests he ask for something different before [[Cultivation]] steps in. Dalinar tells her of the voices he hears, and she offers him a way to remove them and to become a better man at a steep cost. he takes the deal, losing the memories of his wife. This would greatly influence his personality and ideologies after his visit to the Nightwatcher, which would come to heavily influence the course of Rosharan history.

### The Restitution, the True Desolation, and the Night of Sorrows

While not the singular cause of the True Desolation and the Night of Sorrows, the assassination of Gavilar Kholin was a major event in the lead up to the True Desolation, as it brings about the War of Reckoning, which will lead to the circumstances that would motivate the singers to invite in Odium and the [[Everstorm]].

Furthermore, Dalinar's visit to the Nightwatcher would shape the events which occurred between the arrival of the Everstorm and the start of the True Desolation, and would bring about the circumstances that led to the birth of Retribution and the start of the True Desolation.

## Trivia
* The prologues of all of the first five books in [[The Stormlight Archive]] take place on the night of Gavilar's assassination, showing the events of the night from the perspectives of different characters, and they are each titled as an infinitive verb.
* Over the course of the evening, Gavilar meets with the head of every major Rosharan secret society: Thaidakar of the [[Ghostbloods]]; Restares of the Sons of Honor; Nale of the [[Skybreakers]]; and Taravangian, who will later found and lead the [[Diagram (group)|Diagram]].
