**Chasmfiends** (Listener: *Ulo mas vara*, lit. *Beast of the Chasms*){{book ref|sa1|45}} are enormous [[greatshell]]s native to the flatlands southeast of the [[Shattered Plains]] on [[Roshar]].{{book ref|sa1|12}}{{book ref|sa5|55}}

## Appearance and Anatomy
{{image|Chasmfiend.jpeg|[[Shallan]]'s sketch of a chasmfiend|width=300px|side=right}}
{{for|/Gallery|more images}}

Chasmfiends are the largest known land animals on Roshar: at least twenty feet wide, a height between thirty-six and thirty-nine feet, and a length easily over seventy feet.{{rpg ref|slwg|192}}{{file ref|Chasmfiend.jpeg|Shallan's Sketchbook - Chasmfiend}} They are long and narrow, with a flattened, split tail reminiscent of that of a crayfish.{{file ref|Chasmfiend.jpeg|Shallan's Sketchbook - Chasmfiend}} Like most Rosharan fauna, they're crustaceans, with a carapace exoskeleton covering their entire body. Said carapace is dark violet (though it's possible that other colors can also occur), and extremely durable, being able to withstand even a hail of arrows.{{book ref|sa1|13}} It covers the beast's back in a series of interlocking, upside-down-V-shaped plates with horn-like protrusions growing on the edges.{{file ref|Chasmfiend.jpeg|Shallan's Sketchbook - Chasmfiend}} Beneath it, the chasmfiend's blood and gore is purple, and smells of mold.{{book ref|sa1|13}} The meat is edible for both humans and native fauna.{{book ref|sa1|15}}

Below the armor, a chasmfiend has eighteen legs. Four foreclaws are set into broad shoulders, with two clawed toes on each of them. They serve as the primary means of attack, both smashing and slicing the chasmfiend's prey.{{book ref|sa1|13}} Behind them, fourteen smaller limbs provide locomotion. A chasmfiend can move extremely fast when it so desires, and has the agility to turn around in the narrow corridors of the chasms.{{book ref|sa2|71}}

The chasmfiend's head is triangular, narrowing down to a point like an arrowhead,{{book ref|sa2|70}} with facial plating that appears like “chunks of broken rock.”{{book ref|sa5|78}} Inside the long jaw, it has a circular mouth, surrounded by several rings of teeth. There are several types of teeth present, from sharp fangs and flat molars to small mandibles that shove the food further down the throat.{{book ref|sa1|15}} Flanking the mouth is a pair of long, jointed foreclaws --"as thick as a singer’s arms"--which the chasmfiend can use to manipulate objects, pick up food, and even carry passengers over obstacles.{{book ref|sa5|78}}{{file ref|Chasmfiend.jpeg|Shallan's Sketchbook - Chasmfiend}}

The eyes, set above the jaw and peering out through gaps in their armor,{{book ref|sa5|78}} are glassy and green.{{book ref|sa2|70}} They also have apparently thick eyelids that close sideways, possibly a [[wikipedia:Nictitating_membrane|nictitating membrane]].{{file ref|Chasmfiend.jpeg|Shallan's Sketchbook - Chasmfiend}} Their four nostrils are located at the very end of the maw,{{rpg ref|slwg|192}} while the ears are bare holes slightly behind the eyes.{{file ref|Chasmfiend.jpeg|Shallan's Sketchbook - Chasmfiend}}

## Behavior
{{quote
|To them, dominance was never about dominating. They want to look strong before each other, probably to attract mates, partially also because it just … feels good, I suppose.
|[[Thude]]{{book ref|sa5|58}}
}}

{{image|Chasmfiend_by_AutomaticMoon.jpg|side=left|height=350px| [[Kaladin]] and [[Shallan]] defeating a chasmfiend}}

Chasmfiends possess a high level of intelligence. They can communicate with one another by projecting images or impressions into each other’s minds, and are able to share these impressions with singers.{{book ref|sa5|58}} They do not communicate via spoken language, instead expressing ideas through mental or emotional “songs.”{{book ref|sa5|58}} In addition, they are capable of vocalizing. Their roars, which sound like four bellowing trumpets--matching the four tones of Roshar--overlap and harmonize with one another, and can be heard over a highstorm.{{book ref|sa1|13}}{{book ref|sa5|60}} According to [[Wit]], chasmfiends have difficulty "tuning their tonic chords and maintaining just intonation".{{book ref|sa2|55}}

Chasmfiends establish a nest in a chasm, hunting and scavenging from it until they ascend a plateau to pupate.{{rpg ref|slwg|192}} They exhibit cooperative behavior during highstorms, forming an inward-facing ring and lifting their carapaces to create sheltered pockets where smaller chasmfiends--or allied [[listener]]s--can wait out a storm.{{book ref|sa5|60}} Their large size and thick plating allow them to stand firm against the winds and debris, effectively shielding those gathered in the center.{{book ref|sa5|60}} Chasmfiends in these rings will roar in unison, seemingly in challenge at the passing [[Spren#Stormstriders|Stormstriders]].{{book ref|sa5|60}} They are also capable of scaling chasms, and will often do so.{{rpg ref|b9|7}}

Chasmfiends typically have little interest in smaller creatures.{{book ref|sa5|58}} Before the [[War of Reckoning]], they only encountered people when traveling to the [[Shattered Plains]] for their final pupation,{{book ref|sa5|58}} and did not consider their presence noteworthy unless provoked.{{book ref|sa4|48}} Once confronted and killed by humans for the first time, chasmfiends began viewing humans and listeners as rivals in a contest of dominance.{{book ref|sa5|58}}

They challenge or steal prey to prove strength, often testing other chasmfiends in non-lethal displays.{{book ref|sa5|58}} Smaller or younger chasmfiends typically yield to larger ones after a demonstration of boldness.{{book ref|sa5|58}} On rare occasions, two equally matched chasmfiends may fight, but such battles seldom end in death, as the loser usually retreats.{{book ref|sa5|58}}

Chasmfiends are known to play with things that act like prey, even if they aren't hungry.{{book ref|sa4|88}} When they are not hunting or challenging one another, chasmfiends spend much of their time lounging or sleeping.{{book ref|sa5|55}}{{book ref|sa5|58}}{{book ref|sa5|118}}

## Ecology
{{image|Chasmfiend Fight by Ellie L..png|A chasmfiend hunting a human|side=right|width=300px}}
### Life Cycle
{{quote
|They must have an interesting life cycle. They haunt these chasms, but I doubt they actually live here.
|[[Shallan]]{{book ref|sa2|49}}
}}

The life-cycle of a chasmfiend is split into at least three stages, including the pupation stage.{{wob ref|124}} The majority of their life cycle is spent in the flatlands southeast of the Shattered Plains.{{book ref|sa5|55}}

Little is known about the reproduction methods of chasmfiends though it is similar to that of chulls;{{wob ref|762}} and they likely lay eggs in their nests.{{book ref|sa3|68}} It involves a spren in some way.{{wob ref|762}} [[Thude]] theorizes that part of the reason they fight each other for dominance is to look strong in order to attract a mate.{{book ref|sa5|58}} [[Rlain]] notes that chasmfiends migrate towards far eastern side of the Shattered Plains after breeding or pupating.{{book ref|sa4|79}}

Young chasmfiends can be at least as small as an [[axehound]],{{book ref|sa5|60}} and throughout their life cycle they pupate many times in order to grow.{{book ref|sa5|58}} The [[highstorm]] is relevant for pupating chasmfiends,{{book ref|sa5|58}}{{book ref|sa5|60}} and they can use it for interim growth cycles throughout their native range{{book ref|sa5|60}} until they are ready for their final pupation, which must take place at the Shattered Plains.{{book ref|sa5|58}}

They are led to the Shattered Plains by the tone of [[Odium]]'s [[Perpendicularity]],{{book ref|sa5|90}} the [[Well of Control]],{{book ref|sa5|41}}{{book ref|sa5|128}} and attempts to undergo a complete pupation elsewhere are apparently unsuccessful.{{book ref|sa5|58}} During this final pupation they take in a large amount of Investiture,{{wob ref|3304}} likely acquired from the passing highstorm and the perpendicularity underneath the Shattered Plains. This Investiture is vital to their growth to the sizes they can attain.{{wob ref|16753}}

Once at the Shattered Plains, they set up a nest, and continue hunting and scavenging across the chasms.{{book ref|sa1|12}} Eventually, prompted by some unknown instinct, they climb up to one of the plateaus and pupate, forming an immobile, rock-colored chrysalis around themselves. This pupation typically happens during the night, with the chrysalis already formed come daytime. From then, the chrysalis awaits a highstorm; what happens anatomically once that occurs in unclear, as it is at this point that people typically harvest them.{{book ref|sa2|49}}

### Feeding Habits
In their original habitat--the wide flatlands southeast of the Shattered Plains{{book ref|sa5|55}}--chasmfiends are the apex predators,{{book ref|sa2|49}} hunting large herbivores such as [[zumble]]s{{book ref|sa5|55}} and likely wild [[chull]]s.{{book ref|sa2|70}} While hunting, they generally stay close to their nest.{{rpg ref|slwg|192}} They operate as “burst predators,”{{book ref|sa5|118}} expending tremendous energy to catch their prey, then using their enormous foreclaws to crush shells and carapaces.{{book ref|sa1|13}}{{book ref|sa2|70}} These bursts of activity are followed by extended periods of rest.{{book ref|sa5|118}}{{book ref|sa5|58}} They also use the terrain to their advantage, backing prey into dead ends before attacking.{{rpg ref|slwg|193}}
<!-- Editors Note: The "burst predator" behavior is only mentioned in relation to a deception when Thude is telling El that the chasmfiends won't be active for a while, however there is no indication that the "burst predator" behavior is actually false, and seems to me to be more of a conveniently placed truth aiding in the overall deception. -->

Since allying with the listeners, they have been happy to share their kills in exchange for people to groom and scratch their carapace.{{book ref|sa5|55}}{{book ref|sa5|58}} Despite their formidable predatory instincts, they do not universally view singers or humans as food; rather, many chasmfiends initially regarded both species as rivals during their first encounters at the Shattered Plains.{{book ref|sa5|58}} Over time, some chasmfiends have accepted that humans and listeners are not prey, particularly among the group of listeners who escaped the Shattered Plains with Thude.{{book ref|sa5|58}}{{book ref|sa5|89}}

While traveling to or dwelling in the Shattered Plains, chasmfiends turn to carrion-eating, devouring the corpses of creatures washed into the chasms from the plateaus above.{{book ref|sa1|43}} They will opportunistically eat dead humans or singers if not discouraged, though they find humans less desirable than larger game such as chulls,{{book ref|sa5|89}} and do not think humans have enough fat to be "truly delicious."{{book ref|sa5|118}} Some chasmfiends can be persuaded by certain singers not to eat humans at all, though this confuses them given their natural inclination toward scavenging.{{book ref|sa5|118}}

### Gemheart and Spren Bonds
{{image|Luckspren by Eccos.jpg|[[Luckspren]] flying around a chasmfiend|width=300px|side=right}}
{{quote
|Bavamar did the calculations on Reshi greatshells, and found they should be crushed by their own weight.
|[[Shallan]]{{book ref|sa3|99}}
}}

Like every greatshell, chasmfiends have a [[gemheart]] growing inside of them. The chasmfiend gemhearts seem to always be emeralds, and are noted as particularly enormous.{{book ref|sa3|35}}{{book ref|sa1|40}} Those gemhearts are likely what allows chasmfiends to bond with [[mandra]]s, a type of [[spren]] connected to gravity.{{book ref|sa3|99}} The bond is a crucial factor in allowing the chasmfiend to grow to its enormous size.{{wob ref|1227}} It reduces the chasmfiend's weight, keeping it from crushing itself; as such, from a mathematical standpoint, chasmfiends fly, or at least float.{{book ref|sa3|99}} Any creature attempting to mimic a chasmfiend, such as a [[kandra]], would require a way to create or substitute that bond to survive.{{wob ref|3794}}

The mandras leave the chasmfiend's body in the hours immediately after its death. They look somewhat unlike themselves as they return to [[Shadesmar]], being often mistaken for tongues of smoke.{{book ref|sa1|15}}

## Economy
During the War of Reckoning, chasmfiend gemhearts--harvested from pupating young on the Shattered Plains--served as the primary catalyst for Alethkar's economic growth, prompting an aggressive expansion onto the plateaus.{{book ref|sa1|12}}{{book ref|sa1|15}} Each gemheart could cover a highprince’s wartime expenses for months, with larger specimens proving vital for Soulcasting because they were less likely to shatter during repeated use.{{book ref|sa1|15}}{{book ref|sa4|15}} By the time of the re-discovery of Urithiru, much of the [[Alethi]] royal emerald reserve consisted of large emeralds likely cut from chasmfiend gemhearts.{{book ref|sa3|35}}

Chasmfiend gemhearts also proved pivotal to the listeners’ economy during their protracted conflict with the Alethi--not for currency or Soulcasting, but as an energy source to grow food on the barren plains.{{book ref|sa4|15}}{{book ref|sa4|68}}

After the re-discovery of Urithiru, the city’s early population remained dependent on plateau runs to claim chasmfiend gemhearts.{{book ref|sa3|50}} However, as the chasmfiend population dwindled, the humans became increasingly reliant on farming and lumber from the warcamps to sustain their armies.{{book ref|sa3|50}}

## History
### Ancient History
Perhaps the earliest known reference to a chasmfiend is an illustration that is believed to have been created two or three generations after [[Aharietiam]].{{book ref|sa1|28}} The illustration is distorted to look more menacing so as to show what the author believed a [[Voidbringer]] must look like, however it is clearly a depiction of a chasmfiend.{{book ref|sa1|28}} It is unknown if chasmfiends inhabited the Shattered Plains and its surrounding lands prior to their shattering, or if they only moved there after the departure of humankind from the area.{{book ref|sa1|36}} After the departure of humans from the Shattered Plains chasmfiends faded in memory and were completely forgotten by most of human kind by the time of king [[Gavilar]]'s reign.{{book ref|sa1|36}}<!---Gavilar says that  they thought the lanceryn were the last source of large gemhearts.--->{{book ref|sa4|48}}<!---Reactions of Gavilar, Dalinar and the others when they see a chasmfiend for the first time indicates they've never heard of anything like a chasmfiend before.---> The listeners eventually began to hunt chasmfiends, and knew the season and location in which to perform these hunts.{{book ref|sa4|48}} Humans re-discovered the chasmfiends alongside the rediscovery of the listeners, and were shocked to discover where the gemstones worn in listener beards came from.{{book ref|sa1|36}}{{book ref|sa4|48}}

### War of Reckoning
<!---c. Ishi 1167 to Ishishach 1173--->
{{image|Chasmfiend Hunt by Randy Vargas.jpg|[[Elhokar]] hunting a chasmfiend|side=right|width=300px}}

Throughout the War of Reckoning, both listeners and humans hunt chasmfiends down for their gemhearts, although meat and carapace are also harvested.{{book ref|sa1|13}} Most of the time, only the chrysalis is "hunted", as it is then that the chasmfiend is at both its most vulnerable and least aggressive.{{book ref|sa1|12}} Once the chrysalis is located, the hunters break it open with mallets and [[Shardblade]]s and extract the gemheart within, killing the chasmfiend in the process. On such "hunts", the biggest danger are the other armies after the same chrysalis.{{book ref|sa1|15}}

==== Chasmfiend Hunts and Overharvesting ====
{{quote
|Animals like the chasmfiends don't normally have predators. Suddenly losing a hundred and fifty or more of their numbers a year could be catastrophic to their population.
|[[Shallan]]{{book ref|sa2|49}}
}}
Though rarely, adult chasmfiends are also hunted, mostly when one wanders into a territory claimed by one of the [[highprince]]s. First, the chasmfiend is lured to climb up onto the plateau by stringing out hog carcasses and pouring hog blood into the chasms, to let the beast smell it. Once the chasmfiend emerges, which can take up to several hours, a flock of wild hogs is let loose for it to pursue. Archers equipped with [[grandbow]]s -- the only bow type capable of harming the chasmfiend -- then pepper the creature with arrows to weaken it. Eventually, [[Shardbearer]]s approach the beast and cut off its legs with their Blades.{{book ref|sa1|12}} Once toppled, the chasmfiend is set up for a killing stroke, delivered with a Blade through the spine. The gemheart can then be harvested from the downed beast.{{book ref|sa1|13}}

During the War of Reckoning, the number of chasmfiends that died before they could produce offspring soared, with over fifteen being hunted down every month for their gemhearts, and the wealth and [[Soulcasting]] ability they provided. As a result, in the waning years of the war, their numbers began to dwindle, with fewer chrysalises appearing. [[Shallan Davar]] theorized that as the species previously lacked any predators, it simply couldn't cope with this new influx of losses, and was thus headed towards extinction.{{book ref|sa2|49}}

Some solutions have been proposed to mitigate this; primarily, reducing the number of hunts and even breeding chasmfiends in captivity for their gemhearts.{{book ref|sa2|49}} However, with the arrival of the [[Everstorm]], those plans, as well as the hunts themselves, have been put on hold as a bigger threat in form of the Voidbringers occupied the attention of the armies that would normally hunt chasmfiends.{{book ref|sa3|50}}

==== Chasmfiends' Perspective ====
{{sidequote|Then raising a nail, made all of steel,<br />A warrior brought hail, and pain to feel.<br />Now our eyes saw true,<br />Our ears now heard trace,<br />Our ways were at fault,<br />These were rivals to fight.|[[Thude]]{{book ref|sa5|58}}|side=right|size=350px}}
Prior to the War of Reckoning, chasmfiends rarely considered smaller creatures at all, only encountering humans or listeners when visiting the Shattered Plains for their final pupation.{{book ref|sa5|58}} However, the day that humans first fought and killed a chasmfiend became a pivotal moment etched in the chasmfiends’ collective memory, marking a shift toward viewing humans and listeners as 'rivals' to be challenged.{{book ref|sa5|58}}

In response, chasmfiends fought, stole prey, and individually challenged both humans and listeners, seeing them as contenders in a competition of dominance rather than food sources.{{book ref|sa5|58}} Initially, the chasmfiends did not realize humans and listeners were hunting their pupating young.{{book ref|sa5|58}} Upon discovering this they decided that the smaller creatures must actually be dominant.{{book ref|sa5|58}}

### Post War of Reckoning
<!---Ishishach 1173 through present day--->
<!---parnership with singers, role in the final 10 days, etc--->
By the end of the War of Reckoning, chasmfiend numbers had dwindled to around one hundred individuals.{{book ref|sa5|70}} After the Everstorm, when a group of listeners led by Thude abandoned the Shattered Plains, roughly fifty chasmfiends, led by one called [[Thundercloud]],{{book ref|sa5|70}} chose to offer a truce and ally with them.{{book ref|sa5|58}}{{book ref|sa4|115}} The chasmfiends shared their kills and offered protection from the storms in exchange for grooming, an arrangement seemingly preferable to fighting.{{book ref|sa5|58}}

This alliance persisted through the final ten days and the conflict over the Shattered Plains. When Venli led an expedition to Narak, five chasmfiends insisted on accompanying them,{{book ref|sa5|70}} and proved pivotal in assisting the singers to navigate the chasms.{{book ref|sa5|78}} The chasmfiends assisted in the lisners' deception during the [[Second Battle of Narak]], even carrying listeners into battle on their backs.{{book ref|sa5|112}}{{book ref|sa5|118}} Though they participated in skirmishes against humans, they refrained from devouring them unless allowed to by the listeners.{{book ref|sa5|118}} By the time of the [[contest of champions]], Venli and the other listeners secured a truce with the humans, and the chasmfiends remained content living alongside their new allies, with more chasmfiends arriving and carrying listeners in from the edge of the plateaus.{{book ref|sa5|137}}{{book ref|sa5|146}}

## Cultural References
{{image|Historical Greatshell.jpg|An ancient depiction of a [[Voidbringer]], based on a chasmfiend|side=right|width=300px}}

Chasmfiends are deservedly said to be the deadliest predators on Roshar by the Alethi. Prior to the War of Reckoning, they were considered legendary, with people rarely venturing out into the Plains to hunt them.{{book ref|sa1|28}} Back then, to kill a chasmfiend was extremely uncommon; king Gavilar had never slain one, and not for lack of trying. As the war progressed, however, much of the mystique surrounding them was stripped away, though they are still understood to be extremely dangerous.{{book ref|sa1|12}}

### Religion
Prior to and during the War of Reckoning, it was a common belief among the Alethi that the listeners considered the chasmfiends their gods.{{book ref|sa1|13}} This was a misinterpretation made by early explorers based on linguistic barriers, and one that was never thoroughly investigated; as such, near the end of the war, people like Shallan came to doubt it.{{book ref|sa2|54}} In fact, even prior to his death, Gavilar was aware that the listeners did not, in fact, worship the chasmfiends; however, with the assassination and the subsequent conflict, there was never the time for that to become common knowledge, and so the misconception persisted.{{book ref|sa3|prologue}}

Chasmfiends have also had their place in the [[Vorin]] religion. Ancient depictions of Voidbringers were often based on chasmfiends, leading some to believe that chasmfiends were either Voidbringers or part of Voidbringer forces. However, [[Jasnah Kholin]]'s research suggested that it was far more likely that the artists who originally depicted Voidbringers, who lived generations after the last [[Desolation]], did not know what said Voidbringers looked like, and so used the most terrifying creature they knew of -- the chasmfiend -- to approximate.{{book ref|sa1|28}}

With the arrival of the [[True Desolation]], it became apparent that chasmfiends being depicted as Voidbringers came from historians conflating them with [[thunderclast]]s -- another type of massive creatures, which actually serve Odium. Thunderclasts and chasmfiends share very few physical characteristics; most notably, their enormous size and the shape of the head. However, they are killed in very similar way, by slicing at their legs with Shardblades.{{book ref|sa3|120}}

## Trivia
* The inspiration for chasmfiends was a [https://wob.coppermind.net/media/sources/255/Ben%20McSweeney's%20landscape%20concept%20art%20for%20The%20Way%20of%20Kings%2C%20November%202008.jpg concept sketch] that [[Ben McSweeney]] created when Brandon was brainstorming Roshar with him. The sketch contained a picture of a crustacean hiding in a narrow passage based on a coral; the creature eventually became the chasmfiend. That same sketch led to the Shattered Plains -- originally part of *[[Dragonsteel Prime]]* -- to be moved to Roshar.{{wob ref|8580}}
* The chasmfiend is Brandon's favorite fictional creature from his own works.{{wob ref|8529}}
* A chasmfiend could be Awakened as a [[Lifeless]]. However, this would require multiple Breaths, and would result in the Lifeless collapsing under its own weight.{{wob ref|16238}}
* If there were to be a Lego set from Brandon Sanderson's books, a Lego Technic chasmfiend would be [[Adam Horne]]'s first pick.{{wob ref|15288}}
