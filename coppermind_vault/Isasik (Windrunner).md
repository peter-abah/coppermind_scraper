**Isasik** is a [[Windrunner]] and member of Bridge Thirteen on [[Roshar]]. He is bonded to an unnamed Honorspren.{{book ref|sa5|31}}

## Attributes and Abilities
### Surgebinding
Isasik is a Windrunner, having bonded an unnamed [[Honorspren]]. The Nahel Bond gives him the ability to manipulate the Surges of Adhesion and Gravitation, as well as breathe in Stormlight,{{book ref|sa5|31}} granting him heightened speed, agility, durability, stamina, and healing.{{book ref|sa1|62}} It is unknown what Oaths Isasik has sworn, but he is no longer a squire. As a Knights Radiant who has access to two Surges, Isasik also has a [[resonance]].{{wob ref|1296}} Like the other Windrunners, this gives him an increased number of [[squire]]s. His squires are also more powerful than those of the other Orders.{{wob ref|915}}{{wob ref|6740}}

## History
### Bridge Thirteen
==== Running bridges for Sadeas ====
It is unknown what Isasik did to end up in the bridge crews, but he was put in Bridge Thirteen by [[Gaz]]. During his last few weeks as a bridgeman, he served under [[Rial]]. He spent his time in the army running bridges and, when he wasn’t on duty for an assault, doing menial labour around the camp. After assaulting the [[Tower (Roshar)|Tower]], which led to the [[Battle of the Tower]], the entirety of Bridge Thirteen, which included Isasik, was freed by Dalinar Kholin.{{book ref|sa1|69}} Furthermore, Isasik was given the offer to join the Kholin military, which he accepted.

==== Freedom and serving Dalinar ====
During the following weeks, Isasik was trained, equipped, and taught to be a soldier and bodyguard. His crew, after [[Kaladin]] deemed them ready, also began patrolling the nearby wilderness.{{book ref|sa2|25}} Bridge Thirteen was also a part of Highprince Kholin’s expedition to the center of the [[Shattered Plains]].{{book ref|sa2|76}} After arriving at Urithiru, Isasik and the rest of his crew acted as guards for the Kholin family.{{book ref|sa3|4}} 

### Knights Radiant
Together with the rest of Bridge Thirteen, Isasik became [[Teft]]’s squire sometime after the [[Battle of Thaylen Field]]. During the year that followed, many of them, including Isasik, gained their own spren, becoming full Radiants.{{book ref|sa3|122}} After hearing of Teft’s death at the hands of [[Moash]], the entirety of Bridge Thirteen swore to get vengeance, and began wearing red glyphwards.{{book ref|sa5|31}}

==== Raid on the Ghostbloods in Narak ====
Isasik was among the dozen Windrunners ordered by Dalinar to aid the [[Unseen Court]] in a raid on a [[Ghostbloods|Ghostblood]] safehouse in [[Narak]]. They stayed close to the safehouse, waiting for Shallan to activate a spanreed, which would be their cue to strike.{{book ref|sa5|27}} [[Erinor]], their [[Order of Stonewards|Stoneward]], spoke to the stones, and got the impression that there was a secret exit nearby. A few of them went to explore it, but while there, they received Shallan’s signal, and they noticed a few Ghostbloods fleeing in their direction. Most of the strike team, having remained near the safehouse, ignored the secret exit and moved into the building,{{book ref|sa5|29}} having [[Erinor]] [[Surgebinding#Surge of Cohesion|melt]] the basement’s ceiling. The Windrunners quickly swept into the room, although the Ghostbloods had already disappeared.{{book ref|sa5|27}} After realizing that many of the Ghostbloods had escaped via that same secret exit, several Windrunners left to chase them. It is unknown if Isasik was among them or if he stayed behind or had originally remained at the exit.{{book ref|Sa5|29}} The strike team took a number of captives, although some Ghostbloods got away.{{book ref|sa5|31}}

==== The Ghostbloods in Shadesmar ====
Seeing that a few Ghostbloods had escaped to Shadesmar and would soon make their way to Urithiru, Shallan sent two Windrunners, Isasik and [[Breteh]] along with three of Breteh’s squires ahead to search for them. The Windrunners spoke with the three guards posted nearby, but came to the conclusion that nobody had arrived yet.{{book ref|sa5|31}} After Shallan’s arrival, they left the squires behind to watch for any approaching Ghostbloods and lashed themselves- and the guards- towards the Bondsmiths. Once there, the group kept watch for any nearby Ghostblood agents until Shallan realised that they would use Lightweavings as a disguise. Immediately, the three guards from before, who were actually three Ghostbloods, turned on them.{{book ref|sa5|31}}

While the two foreigners, [[Lieke]] and [[Iyatil]], leapt for Breteh, Isasik tackled Iyatil. It quickly became clear that he was no match for her, however, as she slit his throat within seconds of their struggle. [[Renarin]] came to Isasik’s aid, healing his wound. Despite this, Isasik was to dazed to fight. He recovered and, after the Ghostbloods had been mostly defeated, he walked over to [[Mraize]], who was stuck on the ceiling, having been lashed there by Breteh. He was confused by Radiant’s appearence in the fight, though he accepted the aid. One of the Ghostbloods, Iyatil, spoke to them, telling the group that the actual guards (who they had impersonated) were being held at the bottom of the tower, and would soon be executed if no one came for them. Breteh and Isasik, along with their Spren, immediately flew down to free them.{{book ref|sa5|32}}

While the two Windrunners were at the base of the tower, busy freeing the guards, a large explosion occurred in the room they had just left. When the Windrunners went to check it out, they found the large room broken and smoking, the floor had been ripped open and the ceiling was a fractured web. They immediately sent for their superior, [[Lopen]], who alerted the [[Navani|Queen]] and went to check it out himself. The three of them confirmed that there was no sign of Shallan or her companions, and resolved that there was nothing they could do to fix the situation. Afterwards, Isasik joined the group of Windrunners who flew [[The Mink]] to [[Herdaz]], and then flew to either Narak or [[Thaylen City]].{{book ref|sa5|33}}
