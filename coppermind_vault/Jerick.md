**Jerick** is the son of a lumberer on [[Yolen]].{{book ref|sa5|127}}{{book ref|sa5|128}}

## History
Jerick was born on Yolen to a lumberman. Eventually he was chosen for his acumen in order to prove the mental capacity and capabilities of the lower classes in a contest. This was put into motion by a king of a city-state that sincerely believed that all people had the capacity for great things if given the chance, and his desire to prove it to those that doubted his idea.{{book ref|sa5|127}}{{book ref|sa5|128}} 

Jerick succeeded in every trial right up until the last, when he fled and went off to war, not showing up for the trial. He would eventually return and a coup would occur where he killed several barons of the city-state. The ultimate result of the coup, however, is unknown.{{book ref|sa5|127}}{{book ref|sa5|128}}

[[Hoid]] -- who witnessed this story, having been selected to judge the final trial -- later told of Jerick to [[Dalinar Kholin]], just before the [[contest of champions]].{{book ref|sa5|127}}{{book ref|sa5|128}}

## Development
{{unpublished|book=Dragonsteel prime}}
Jerick was one of the first characters that [[Brandon Sanderson]] created, appearing in the very first story he wrote in his mid-teens.{{wob ref|7600}} He was originally named "Rick",{{wob ref|7600}} and at one point he was an elf.{{wob ref|11232}} He was a hero character that was falsely accused of murder;{{wob ref|7600}} Brandon later cited him as an example of a "flawless hero" in his writing who did not have internal struggles like those of [[Vin]] or [[Dalinar]].{{wob ref|7809}} In [[Dragonsteel Prime]] he was very different, being the son of a lumberman that was chosen by a king to partake in trials to prove the capacity of the lower class to the upper class, much like the form of him that was later canonized in *[[Wind and Truth]]*.

Brandon has mentioned including Jerick as part of a rewritten [[Dragonsteel (series)|Dragonsteel series]] on several occasions over the years,{{wob ref|7825}}{{wob ref|7600}} but this would not occur until after the completion of *[[The Stormlight Archive]]*.{{wob ref|12313}} Because characters and plot points were cannibalized from *Dragonsteel* Prime to improve *[[The Way of Kings]]*,{{wob ref|8601}}{{wob ref|8838}} Jerick's character arc would need to be significantly changed.{{wob ref|7142}} Brandon had stated that Jerick had been written out of the Cosmere entirely,{{wob ref|15092}} but he appears to have returned as at least a character in [[Hoid]]'s backstory, as Hoid tells part of Jerick's story in *Wind and Truth*.{{book ref|WaT|128}}
