**Jerick** is the son of a lumberer on [[Yolen]].

## History
Jerick was born on Yolen to a lumberman. Eventually he was chosen for his acumen in order to prove the mental capacity and capabilities of the lower classes in a contest. This was put into motion by a king of a city-state that sincerely believed that all people had the capacity for great things if given the chance, and his desire to prove it to those that doubted his idea. 

Jerick succeeded in every trial right up until the last, when he fled and went off to war, not showing up for the trial. He would eventually return and a coup would occur where he killed several barons of the city-state. The ultimate result of the coup, however, is unknown.

[[Hoid]] -- who witnessed this story, having been selected to judge the final trial -- later told of Jerick to [[Dalinar Kholin]], just before the [[contest of champions]].

## Development

Jerick was one of the first characters that [[Brandon Sanderson]] created, appearing in the very first story he wrote in his mid-teens. He was originally named "Rick", and at one point he was an elf. He was a hero character that was falsely accused of murder; Brandon later cited him as an example of a "flawless hero" in his writing who did not have internal struggles like those of [[Vin]] or [[Dalinar]]. In [[Dragonsteel Prime]] he was very different, being the son of a lumberman that was chosen by a king to partake in trials to prove the capacity of the lower class to the upper class, much like the form of him that was later canonized in *[[Wind and Truth]]*.

Brandon has mentioned including Jerick as part of a rewritten [[Dragonsteel (series)|Dragonsteel series]] on several occasions over the years, but this would not occur until after the completion of *[[The Stormlight Archive]]*. Because characters and plot points were cannibalized from *Dragonsteel* Prime to improve *[[The Way of Kings]]*, Jerick's character arc would need to be significantly changed. Brandon had stated that Jerick had been written out of the Cosmere entirely, but he appears to have returned as at least a character in [[Hoid]]'s backstory, as Hoid tells part of Jerick's story in *Wind and Truth*.
