**Thude** is a [[listener]] warrior living on the [[Shattered Plains]] of [[Roshar]]. During the [[War of Reckoning]], he was part of a warpair with [[Bila]], under the command of [[Eshonai]], in her personal division. He was friends with [[Rlain]] as well. After the First [[Battle of Narak]], Thude fled into the chasms along with seventeen hundred other listeners. Consequently, with most of their leaders dead, Thude joined the Five. He also took part in the [[Second Battle of Narak]], and was partly responsible for the foundation of a listener nation.

## Appearence and personality
Like all singers, Thude’s appearence depends on the form he is wearing. As a kid, he wore dullform. After his seventh or eight birthday however, he changed into workform. Furthermore, after Venli’s discovery of warform, Thude was among the dozen listeners who were the first to change into it. While wearing warform, Thude is tall and thick limbed, and due to its strength and endurance, warform is his favorite form. There are some things that stay the same however, regardless of the form he is wearing. He always has a pattern made of large marbled patches. He also tends to wear a long coat, even despite Bila’s tendency to mock it.

> I can respect the humans, but we can never join them.
> — Thude on joining humanity’s war against Odium

For most of his life, Thude was jovial and content with his life. This changed after the [[assassination of Gavilar]] however, with the [[War of Reckoning]] taking its toll on him. He became more and more solemn, and worried for their people’s survival. After the First Battle of Narak, Thude changed much, becoming a true leader. He also feels strongly about Odium, rejecting him fully and hating what has been done to the listeners at the hands of their gods. Thude also loved food, and used eating as an excuse for ending arguments.

## Attributes and Abilities
#### Shardwielding
Thude has trained with both a [[Shardblade]] and a set of [[Shardplate]]. He is capable enough with them that [[Eshonai]] considers him to be the best candidate for them should she die. It is unknown if he ever received Blade or Plate from his Radiant Bond.

#### Willshaper
Thude is also a [[Knights Radiant]] of the [[Order of Willshapers]]. He received his spren shortly after the occupation of [[Urithiru]], when [[Venli]] brought a number of Radiant spren to the listeners. It is unknown how many oaths Thude has sworn. The [[Stone]] has spoken to him at least once.

#### Other
Because of the lack of a [[keeper of songs]], Thude also composed the [[Song of the Beasts]]. He feels that it might be a little uneven and amateur, however [[Jaxlim]], their second to last keeper of songs, believes the song to be amazing. When riding a chasmfiend, Thude likes to stand up straight instead of crouching down. He also loves riding chasmfiends.

## History
### First Rhythm Family
Thude was born into the [[Listener#Families|First-Rhythm]] family. He spent much of his youth working the fields and chopping wood. He also became friends with Eshonai and Bila at an early age. When Venli went into the storm to free [[Ulim]], Thude was convinced that she was becoming a mate, and he disagreed with Eshonai about it. He also admitted to her that he wanted to ask Bila to adopt mateform with him. After the Five agreed to test Venli’s hypothesis that painspren were the key to adopting warform, two dozen listeners, including Thude, were given gemstones. Of these two dozen, only one dozen managed to take the form. Both Thude and Eshonai were among this dozen. Directly after the storm, Thude spotted Eshonai and talked with her about the new form. During their return to the city, they realised that another family was attacking them, and the warforms went to confront them. They first visited their family’s Shardbearer, Sharefel, and then went to the rival family. Thude was present among the warforms when Eshonai told the rival family that they should join the First-Rhythm family. Afterwards, the warforms began training themselves to control the form, although Thude was fairly good at this already.

### War of Reckoning
#### Early years
Thude was among the listeners invited to go to [[Kholinar]], the Alethi capital. During this visit, he wore workform again. After the dire ending of the feast, with the Alethi King dead, Thude and the other listeners fled back to the Plains. Once they arrived, Thude was amongst those who assumed warform again, and he was happy to feel its strength. After the Alethi armies arrived at the Plains, he talked to Eshonai, questioning whether they could even win the war. Later, he was the one to introduce the idea of spying on the Alethi. He even volunteered to go himself, but Eshonai denied him this, stating that she needed his expertise on the battlefield. Sometime either during or shortly before the War of Reckoning he spent two weeks in mateform with [[Bila]].

#### Later years
The war dragged on and Thude fought in many large battles, such as the [[Battle of the Tower]]. He was also present when, back at Narak, Eshonai showed him a captured stormspren, and Thude was left to stop an argument between Bila and Eshonai. He also went to the Alethi by order of the Five and arranged a parley. After Eshonai returned from a highstorm, having taken [[Stormform]], Thude was horrified. He talked to Eshonai and told her that the form was wrong. Similarly, he disagreed with her actions during what was essentially a coup. Eshonai in turn put him in command of the listeners who had refused to take the form as a way of distracting and reassuring him. Thude led the listeners in a successful attempt to escape her, destroying a bridge and using the rope to climb into the chasms. While Eshonai assumed that they would drown during the highstorm, the group was soon approached by a number of chasmfiends, who offered their version of a truce and helped these listeners out of the chasms. They then roamed around on the Plains for a while, avoiding both humans and Fused.

Roughly fourteen months after their escape, they spotted a number of other singers approaching. One of them, who revealed herself to be Venli, stepped forward, wishing to speak with the Five. Thude saw Venli and, at first believing her to be Eshonai, spoke to her. He had trouble trusting her at first, seeing as to how she had led to the near destruction of their people, but allowed her to see her mother. Additionally, Venli told him that she had brought Radiant spren with her. After Venli questioned how they escaped the chasms, Thude showed her one of the chasmfiends. He also allowed the singers, including five Fused who had rejected Odium, to stay with them.

### Journey to Narak
Thude, joined by Jaxlim and Venli, spoke to the head Fused, Leshwi, asking her to reject Odium and join the listeners. Afterwards, he agreed to tell Venli the story of how they had allied themselves with the chasmfiends, and he sung the [[Song of the Beasts]] for her, which he himself had created. With that out of the way, he and Venli discussed the current state of the war, and Venli pointed out that, with the war’s end, Odium would focus on exterminating the listeners. She suggested joining the Coalition of Monarchs, although he, and the rest of the Five, were opposed to this idea. Following this, a highstorm arrived, and Thude was busy preparing the camp. During the storm, Thude heard the [[Stone]] talk to him, telling him to seek the center of Narak.

#### The Pool
The Five decided to follow the instructions and head to the center. With them, they took about fifteen other listeners, several chasmfiends and the Fused. Thude, like the rest of the Five, appointed someone as his replacement should he not return. He and Venli had a conversation while walking, talking about events in their past, such as Eshonai’s death. Venli also told Thude that Eshonai hadn’t been Odium’s when she died, which made Thude somewhat happy. He also reinforced their decision to stay out of the fight at Narak, stating that they couldn’t afford any more losses. As they reached the central plateaus, a few listeners began to doubt their mission, and the Five decided to vote, worried that Odium might be tricking them. Nonetheless, they voted unanimously to continue. After arriving near Narak, the Fused accepted Odium’s offer of power, which he was subconsciously giving to every Fused in the area. Leshwi decided that the Fused should hold back, in case their presence would draw Odium’s eye, and the Five agreed.

After they traveled the last bit, Venli realised that the sound she had been hearing came from underneath the plateau, and she created a tunnel to the source of the sound. Before going down the tunnel, Thude told the others to stay, and he, the rest of the Five, and Venli went down. They found a [[Well of Control|large pool of golden light]], and briefly questioned its nature and origin. Venli explained what her spren told her, and Thude foolishly reached out to touch the liquid, although Bila stopped him. Before they could decide what to do next, a guard came running down the tunnel, telling them that their heavenly ones had been discovered.

#### The Battle
With the Fused and chasmfiends discovered, there was only a little time to leave the pool and return to the surface. Shortly after doing so, and sealing up the tunnel they’d created, the group was spotted by a few Fused. These Fused carried the listeners to the Fused commander of Odium’s forces at Narak, El. He gave them an offer and a threat, stating that Odium would destroy them after the war was over, but if they and their chasmfiends aided him, Odium would let them go unpunished for their rebellion. He gave them time to think and decide on which choice to make. Thude, while realising that refusing the offer would likely mean death, was set on rejecting the offer. After Venli came up with a cunning plan however, Thude agreed.

> Rejecting him is what defines us.
> — Thude to the rest of the Five about Odium

Their plan involved working together with the Alethi. The Five, which Thude is a part of, sent Venli to treat with the Alethi, and, after realising that their plan would not work, she managed to get the Alethi to agree to a new plan. This new plan was to make a treaty. The treaty would give the Shattered Plains to the listeners and give the Alethi, who were slowly losing Narak, a way to keep the Plains out of the hands of their enemy and retain some rights, such as the ability to keep farming their lands. The next morning, the Five informed El that they had (supposedly) decided to take his offer, and they prepared for battle. The listeners climbed atop their chasmfiends and, acting as the second wave, charged towards the human fortress. Their attack was somewhat fake, as they weren’t actually trying to kill any humans, and likewise, the humans weren’t really trying to kill them. It had been agreed upon that the listeners would ‘force’ the humans to retreat, during which the Alethi would discreetly give Venli the signed treaty. This went according to plan, and Thude had a lot of fun during this ‘battle’, riding his chasmfiend and ‘destroying’ as much as possible.

#### Freedom
As the Battle ended, Thude, like the other listeners, retreated and dismounted. El commanded him to move over to another plateau and guard the Oathgate, to make sure that the humans did not manage to take Narak back. Thude told him that they couldn’t do that however, as the Chasmfiends wouldn’t move anymore. He suggested watching the plateau from where they currently were, and El agreed.

> Then, by that contract, we respectfully ask that you withdraw your forces until diplomatic relations between our nations can be normalized.
> — Thude to El, commander of Odium’s forces at Narak

The next morning, after the [[contest of champions]] had begun, effectively freezing borders, the listeners approached El. It was Thude himself who, showing El the treaty they had made with Queen Jasnah of Alethkar the day before, formally asked El to withdraw his forces from the Shattered Plains, which were now in listener possession. El, being bound by Odium’s promise and unable to take it from the listeners, agreed and promptly withdrew. With the establishment and recognition of this nation, Thude became a member of the first Council of Five of the new listener nation. The next day, Thude was among the people who saw Odium’s perpendicularity, which had emptied, refill and turn into [[Retribution]]’s perpendicularity.

## Relationships
### Eshonai
Eshonai was a good friend to Thude. Even before the discovery of the Alethi, the two often spent time together. Thude was with Eshonai when they first took warform, and they trained extensively in the months after. They didn’t always agree with eachother however, and Thude questioned Eshonai’s choice (along with the rest of the Five) to kill King Gavilar. When Eshonai took stormform, Thude was one of the few listeners to realise that Eshonai had changed somewhat. This led to him refusing stormform, and escaping Odium’s grasp. Her death was only confirmed to him months after the First Battle of Narak, when Venli told him. While he already knew she had died, the confirmation was still a lot to take. He was glad to finally know what had happened to her however.

### Venli
Thude has had a very rocky relationship with [[Venli]]. During most of his youth, he liked her, knowing her as the sister of his friend, Eshonai. However, Venli was the one to discover stormform, which led to the near destruction of their people. Thude spent the next couple months hating her for having caused so much pain. When they met again, out on the Shattered Plains, he first thought she was her sister, Eshonai. He allowed Venli to see her mother, but still didn’t trust her yet. The following days, they spent a good amount of time together, and Thude began to see that she had changed, and wasn’t the person who had led to so many deaths anymore. He spent these days, and likely the time after, trying to move on.

### Bila
Thude has a close relationship with [[Bila]]. After Thude asked Bila, they spent two weeks in mateform together, although it didn’t lead to any offspring. The two of them also rejected Stormform, and both of them were elected to join the Five. During the War of Reckoning, the two formed a warpair together.
