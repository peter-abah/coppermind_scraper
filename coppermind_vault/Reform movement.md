The **reform movement** is a popular shift in philosophies concerning the autonomy expected of the [[yoki-hijo]] in [[Torio]], following a schism between those with more liberal opinions and those with traditional, orthodox beliefs on the matter. The schism occurs a few hundred years prior to the activation of the [[father machine]]. At the time of the machine's activation, twelve of the fourteen yoji-hijo are part of the reform movement.

Adherents of the reform movement often eat, bathe, and make other decisions for themselves. They are also known to take days off working for rest or other personal reasons, and insist on retiring at the age of seventy. Opposers of the movement believe that there is danger in abandoning their traditions, and view the more liberal attitude as weak and petulant. Orthodox [[Kihomaban|wardens]] withhold information about the movement from their yoki-hijo.

[[Dwookim]] is a very active yoki-hijo in the reform movement, prior to her retirement. [[Yumi]] is entirely unaware of the movement until she is informed by [[Hwanji]].
