The **Order of Willshapers** is an order of the [[Knights Radiant]] on [[Roshar]]. They form [[Nahel bond]]s with [[Reachers]], also known as lightspren.{{wob ref|9531}}

The Willshapers are [[Surgebinder]]s who use the [[Surge]]s of Transportation and Cohesion, and they are associated with the amethyst [[polestone]].

## Philosophy
{{sidequote
|I will seek freedom.
|The core philosophy of the Willshapers{{rpg ref|slhb|190}}
|side=right|size=250px
}}

Willshapers generally subscribe to a philosophy that all people should be free to make their own choices.{{wob ref|13779}}{{rpg ref|slhb|190}} They focus on personal fulfillment, radical self-expression, and freeing those who are captive.{{wob ref|16467}}{{book ref|sa4|60}} This can and does mean the opposition of injustice, but also the concept that the people need to be able to solve problems, and seek what they dream, themselves. They build society around these ideas, putting up real structures, or equally systems, that allow for freedom. They tend to believe that any law that stops people from seeking what they dream is immoral, often contrary to the [[Skybreakers]] who are more oriented towards the concept that people should not be able to do something unless the law allows.{{wob ref|16467}} Despite this, they understand that some laws are needed to prevent tyrants, and advocate for laws that do so.{{rpg ref|slhb|190}}

The order is said to be quite varied, but members share a love of adventure, novelty, or oddity.{{epigraph ref|sa2|50}}{{wob ref|14995}} They tend to attract builders, craftsmen, and other artisans.{{wob ref|13779}}{{wob ref|16467}} Willshapers tend to teach skills and trades to others, so they can better themselves and their community.{{rpg ref|slhb|190}}

In modern Vorin theology, they are associated with the number eight, the polestone of Amethyst, the Essence of Foil, and the Divine Attributes of Resolute and Builder.{{book ref|sa1|part=ars}}

## Ideals of the Willshapers
The Ideals of the Knights Radiant, also known as the [[Immortal Words]], are a set of rules by which the Radiants live. The First Ideal is shared by all of the orders, and is used as a motto for the Knights Radiant as a whole. Each of the orders then have an additional four Ideals that are unique to that order.{{book ref|sa1|59}} The Ideals of the Order of Willshapers all focus on individual freedom and liberty; the Willshapers champion self-expression and freedom of choice for all.{{wob ref|13779}} Their oaths can be acknowledged by [[Cultivation]] or the [[Stormfather]].{{book ref|sa5|55}}{{book ref|sa4|117}}

### The First Ideal
{{quote
|Life before death, strength before weakness, journey before destination.
|The First Ideal of the Knights Radiant{{book ref|wok|59}}
}}

The Willshapers share the first ideal with all other orders of Radiants, which serves as the motto of the organization as a whole.{{book ref|sa1|59}} The words are broadly interpreted to state the value of life, the importance of strength and service and the importance of the methods by which one accomplishes a task.

### The Second Ideal
{{quote
|I will seek freedom for those in bondage.
|The Second Ideal of the Willshapers, as sworn by [[Venli]]{{book ref|sa4|60}}
}}

This oath is centered around recognizing oppression in all its forms and to help those suffering.{{rpg ref|slhb|191}} This could include opposing slavery, caste systems, rigid gender roles, or the destitution and poverty of nations. In addition, the Willshaper gains the ability to take on squires.{{book ref|sa4|67}}

### The Third Ideal
{{quote
|I will fight oppression…
|The Third Ideal of the Willshaper order{{rpg ref|slhb|191}}
}}

This oath focuses on fighting against oppression despite one's flaws and biases.{{rpg ref|slhb|191}} The Ideal requires finding oppression beyond the individual. It must be spoken before a Willshaper can manifest their Reacher as a [[Shardblade]].{{rpg ref|slhb|194}}

### The Fourth Ideal
The specific words of this Ideal are individualized, but overarching themes are focusing on the people, and if the Willshaper is making a lasting difference in their lives.{{rpg ref|slhb|192}} This Ideal prioritizes giving others the ability to maintain their own freedom. Upon achieving this Ideal, a Willshaper gains their [[Shardplate]], which is formed of [[joyspren]].{{rpg ref|slhb|194}}{{rpg ref|slhb|196}}

## Abilities
The Willshapers use the Surges of **Transportation** and **Cohesion**. Like most Knights Radiant, the Willshapers have squires.{{book ref|sa4|67}}

### Transportation
[[Surgebinding#Transportation|**Transportation**]] allows the transfer of one's physical body into and out of the [[Cognitive Realm]],{{wob ref|1597}} a process sometimes known as Elsecalling.{{wob ref|14921}} It works by creating a miniature [[perpendicularity]], called Elsegates, that the Willshaper can go through.{{wob ref|1525}}{{book ref|sa5|53}} This surge is incredibly difficult to master.{{book ref|sa5|50}}{{book ref|sa5|53}} It is significantly easier to enter the Cognitive Realm using Elsecalling than it is to leave it.{{book ref|sa3|33}} Willshapers are able to use this ability to [[worldhop]].{{wob ref|655}} Willshapers are also capable of looking into Shadesmar without fully Elsecalling.{{book ref|sa5|89}}

### Cohesion
[[Surgebinding#Cohesion|**Cohesion**]] allows them to alter the shape of solid objects in a way called Stoneshaping. This surge allows a Willshaper to make stone more malleable, like clay, or even a liquid.{{book ref|sa4|67}} The Willshaper can then reshape the stone according to their desires. This surge also connects the Willshaper to the spren of the stone in a deep, fundamental way, though the full extent of this is not yet known.{{book ref|sa4|67}}{{book ref|sa4|part=ars}} Through this, they are able to communicate with the stones.{{book ref|sa5|55}}

### Regals
When a Willshaper is also a [[Regal]], additional abilities arise. Though there is a voidspren within the singer's gemheart, the Reacher keeps it trapped and protects their Willshaper from the voidspren's influence.{{book ref|sa4|11}} This allows the Radiant to retain their form's abilities and switch between red and normal eyes at will, letting them blend in with Odium's forces.{{book ref|sa3|120}} Regal Willshapers are able to use Stormlight, Voidlight, or Warlight to fuel their Surgebinding.{{book ref|sa4|11}}{{book ref|sa4|109}}{{book ref|sa5|146}} These Willshapers are also highly resistant to [[suppression fabrial]]s that stop Radiants from Surgebinding.{{book ref|sa4|67}}

### Shardplate Abilities
Willshapers' [[Shardplate]] is formed of [[joyspren]].{{wob ref|16808}}{{rpg ref|slhb|196}}

## History
{{image|WOKLB - KS - Willshapers by Steve Argyle.jpg|width=250px|side=right}}

### The Ancient Radiants
Willshapers worked as aid settlements, constructing buildings and infrastructure, and teaching essential trades.{{rpg ref|slhb|192}} Though the order tended to attract builders, craftsmen and other artisans, their membership tended to be varied in profession.{{wob ref|13779}} They were also varied in personality, but generally enjoyed discovering new, unusual and strange things; they were also considered, by some, to be capricious and unreliable, making them somewhat frustrating to interact with.{{epigraph ref|sa2|50}}{{rpg ref|slhb|192}}

Where the [[Windrunners]] were the protectors and the [[Stonewards]] the soldiers, the Willshapers were the liberators, both freeing those who were physically captive and aiding those who espoused new and radical ideas.{{rpg ref|slhb|192}} They tasked themselves with building and supporting societies; they aided those who resisted tyranny and oppression.{{rpg ref|slhb|192}} They were also literal builders -- in times of peace, they would use theirs powers to aid in constructing buildings and infrastructure, as well as training those who lived there; in times of war, they would fortify towns; and after the battle was over they, like their patron Herald, would travel to teach people of crafts such as bronzeworking or sanitation.{{wob ref|13779}}{{book ref|sa2|i|7}}

Much like the [[Elsecallers]], they were considered to have an affinity for [[Shadesmar]], suggesting that they might've also interacted with the [[spren]] nations on regular basis.{{epigraph ref|sa2|53}}

### True Desolation
In the modern times, no human Willshapers have presented themselves so far; the [[Listener]]s appears to be the only ones alive to have bonded [[Reacher]]s.{{book ref|sa3|120}}{{book ref|sa5|55}}{{rpg ref|slhb|192}}
