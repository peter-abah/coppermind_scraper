The **Order of Willshapers** is an order of the [[Knights Radiant]] on [[Roshar]]. They form [[Nahel bond]]s with [[Reachers]], also known as lightspren.

The Willshapers are [[Surgebinder]]s who use the [[Surge]]s of Transportation and Cohesion, and they are associated with the amethyst [[polestone]].

## Philosophy

> I will seek freedom.
> — The core philosophy of the Willshapers

Willshapers generally subscribe to a philosophy that all people should be free to make their own choices. They focus on personal fulfillment, radical self-expression, and freeing those who are captive. This can and does mean the opposition of injustice, but also the concept that the people need to be able to solve problems, and seek what they dream, themselves. They build society around these ideas, putting up real structures, or equally systems, that allow for freedom. They tend to believe that any law that stops people from seeking what they dream is immoral, often contrary to the [[Skybreakers]] who are more oriented towards the concept that people should not be able to do something unless the law allows. Despite this, they understand that some laws are needed to prevent tyrants, and advocate for laws that do so.

The order is said to be quite varied, but members share a love of adventure, novelty, or oddity. They tend to attract builders, craftsmen, and other artisans. Willshapers tend to teach skills and trades to others, so they can better themselves and their community.

In modern Vorin theology, they are associated with the number eight, the polestone of Amethyst, the Essence of Foil, and the Divine Attributes of Resolute and Builder.

## Ideals of the Willshapers
The Ideals of the Knights Radiant, also known as the [[Immortal Words]], are a set of rules by which the Radiants live. The First Ideal is shared by all of the orders, and is used as a motto for the Knights Radiant as a whole. Each of the orders then have an additional four Ideals that are unique to that order. The Ideals of the Order of Willshapers all focus on individual freedom and liberty; the Willshapers champion self-expression and freedom of choice for all. Their oaths can be acknowledged by [[Cultivation]] or the [[Stormfather]].

### The First Ideal

> Life before death, strength before weakness, journey before destination.
> — The First Ideal of the Knights Radiant

The Willshapers share the first ideal with all other orders of Radiants, which serves as the motto of the organization as a whole. The words are broadly interpreted to state the value of life, the importance of strength and service and the importance of the methods by which one accomplishes a task.

### The Second Ideal

> I will seek freedom for those in bondage.
> — The Second Ideal of the Willshapers, as sworn by [[Venli]]

This oath is centered around recognizing oppression in all its forms and to help those suffering. This could include opposing slavery, caste systems, rigid gender roles, or the destitution and poverty of nations. In addition, the Willshaper gains the ability to take on squires.

### The Third Ideal

> I will fight oppression…
> — The Third Ideal of the Willshaper order

This oath focuses on fighting against oppression despite one's flaws and biases. The Ideal requires finding oppression beyond the individual. It must be spoken before a Willshaper can manifest their Reacher as a [[Shardblade]].

### The Fourth Ideal
The specific words of this Ideal are individualized, but overarching themes are focusing on the people, and if the Willshaper is making a lasting difference in their lives. This Ideal prioritizes giving others the ability to maintain their own freedom. Upon achieving this Ideal, a Willshaper gains their [[Shardplate]], which is formed of [[joyspren]].

## Abilities
The Willshapers use the Surges of **Transportation** and **Cohesion**. Like most Knights Radiant, the Willshapers have squires.

### Transportation
[[Surgebinding#Transportation|**Transportation**]] allows the transfer of one's physical body into and out of the [[Cognitive Realm]], a process sometimes known as Elsecalling. It works by creating a miniature [[perpendicularity]], called Elsegates, that the Willshaper can go through. This surge is incredibly difficult to master. It is significantly easier to enter the Cognitive Realm using Elsecalling than it is to leave it. Willshapers are able to use this ability to [[worldhop]]. Willshapers are also capable of looking into Shadesmar without fully Elsecalling.

### Cohesion
[[Surgebinding#Cohesion|**Cohesion**]] allows them to alter the shape of solid objects in a way called Stoneshaping. This surge allows a Willshaper to make stone more malleable, like clay, or even a liquid. The Willshaper can then reshape the stone according to their desires. This surge also connects the Willshaper to the spren of the stone in a deep, fundamental way, though the full extent of this is not yet known. Through this, they are able to communicate with the stones.

### Regals
When a Willshaper is also a [[Regal]], additional abilities arise. Though there is a voidspren within the singer's gemheart, the Reacher keeps it trapped and protects their Willshaper from the voidspren's influence. This allows the Radiant to retain their form's abilities and switch between red and normal eyes at will, letting them blend in with Odium's forces. Regal Willshapers are able to use Stormlight, Voidlight, or Warlight to fuel their Surgebinding. These Willshapers are also highly resistant to [[suppression fabrial]]s that stop Radiants from Surgebinding.

### Shardplate Abilities
Willshapers' [[Shardplate]] is formed of [[joyspren]].

## History

### The Ancient Radiants
Willshapers worked as aid settlements, constructing buildings and infrastructure, and teaching essential trades. Though the order tended to attract builders, craftsmen and other artisans, their membership tended to be varied in profession. They were also varied in personality, but generally enjoyed discovering new, unusual and strange things; they were also considered, by some, to be capricious and unreliable, making them somewhat frustrating to interact with.

Where the [[Windrunners]] were the protectors and the [[Stonewards]] the soldiers, the Willshapers were the liberators, both freeing those who were physically captive and aiding those who espoused new and radical ideas. They tasked themselves with building and supporting societies; they aided those who resisted tyranny and oppression. They were also literal builders -- in times of peace, they would use theirs powers to aid in constructing buildings and infrastructure, as well as training those who lived there; in times of war, they would fortify towns; and after the battle was over they, like their patron Herald, would travel to teach people of crafts such as bronzeworking or sanitation.

Much like the [[Elsecallers]], they were considered to have an affinity for [[Shadesmar]], suggesting that they might've also interacted with the [[spren]] nations on regular basis.

### True Desolation
In the modern times, no human Willshapers have presented themselves so far; the [[Listener]]s appears to be the only ones alive to have bonded [[Reacher]]s.
