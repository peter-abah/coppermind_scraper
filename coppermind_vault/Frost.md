**Frost** is a [[dragon (cosmere)|dragon]] from [[Yolen]].{{wob ref|2565}} He was present at the [[Shattering]],{{book ref|iote|47}} and has been corresponding with [[Hoid]] through [[letters]].{{wob ref|2682}}

==Appearance and Personality==

When in his human form, Frost is pale-skinned with a long silver beard. He appears to be around sixty years old, and dresses in white and silver robes with a conical headdress. His draconic appearance is currently unknown.

Frost is a caring, empathetic dragon, who teaches his [[Illistandrista|niece]] to always see the best in others. Like most dragons, he dedicates a significant portion of his time to listening to the prayers of his followers. He is also quite playful and teases Starling.{{book ref|iote|prologue}}
 
Frost has lived through significant events in the cosmere and has adopted a non-interference policy.{{wob ref|4214}} He's sarcastic and insulting towards Hoid,{{epigraph ref|sa2|62}}{{epigraph ref|sa2|74}} although it is clear that their personal relationship is complicated.{{epigraph ref|sa2|63}}

==Attributes and Abilities==
Like all dragons, Frost is functionally immortal, meaning that he does not age, but he can be killed.{{wob ref|3074}}{{wob ref|5881}} He is the oldest known character in the cosmere, even older than Hoid.{{wob ref|945}} He has gained a second form of immortality on top of his natural dragon immortality through unknown means.{{wob ref|15673}}

Dragons are also known to have the abilities of flight{{wob ref|11737}} and shapeshifting, including the ability to appear human.{{wob ref|2655}}

While not a [[worldhopper]], he is still highly knowledgeable about the cosmere.{{wob ref|3679}} He is associated with the [[Seventeenth Shard]],{{epigraph ref|sa1|26}} a group that shares his nonintervention policy.{{wob ref|1735}} He is able to keep track of current events in the cosmere through the Seventeenth Shard, correspondence, or other means. Among other facts, he knows that Hoid possesses a bead of [[lerasium]] from [[Scadrial]],{{epigraph ref|sa1|14}}{{wob ref|5483}} that [[Odium]] is trapped in the [[Rosharan system]],{{epigraph ref|sa2|69}} and that a gemstone of Hoid's (presumably the [[First Gem]]) is "dead".{{epigraph ref|sa2|60}} Despite the existence of the [[Silverlight Mercantile]] interplanetary postal service, this was not the method Hoid and Frost used to exchange letters, meaning that they had some other means of contacting one another.{{wob ref|12626}}

Hoid considers Frost to be one of the best contract negotiators he knows, and a person of good judgement.{{book ref|sa5|18}}

### Immortality
Frost's current mortality status is ambiguous. As a dragon, he is naturally ageless; immortal in the weak sense that he could live forever absent outside intervention. However, Hoid says "as you are **now** essentially immortal",{{epigraph ref|sa1|22}} he must be immortal in *some* sense stronger than that.<!--{{wob ref|15673}} but there's no reason to cite for this, it's literally basic logic.--> But contravening that, he still can be killed.{{wob ref|3074}} Frost is both sufficiently difficult to kill that Hoid would consider him "essentially immortal", but still possible to kill. What this means is not clear.

==History==
Little is known of Frost's history even though he is truly ancient. He is slightly older than Hoid,{{wob ref|945}} and both of them are known to have lived on [[Yolen]]. In their correspondence, Frost and Hoid seemed to know each other well, and Frost hinted that they had worked together in the past.{{epigraph ref|sa2|66}} They also exhibited a shared personal knowledge of a number of the original sixteen [[Vessel]]s who perpetrated the [[Shattering of Adonalsium]] on Yolen and took up the power of the [[Shard]]s.{{msh ref|3|2}}{{wob ref|11602}} Specifically, the letters mentioned [[Rayse]], [[Bavadin]], [[Aona]], [[Skai]], [[Ati]], and [[Tanavast]] by name, and Frost referenced "the sixteen".{{epigraph ref|sa2|70}} He was present at the [[Shattering]].{{book ref|iote|47}}

[[Cakoban]] knew Frost in some way, and Frost was there when Cakoban challenged the [[Dakwara]].{{book ref|iote|52}} Frost left a message at Cakoban's grave on the [[Dakwara's island]], stating that he was too late to save Cakoban, but that the man died with honor.{{book ref|iote|61}}

In Hoid's initial letter to Frost, it seemed that the two had not spoken in some time; Hoid was not sure if Frost was fully aware of the [[Splinter]]ing of [[Devotion]] and [[Dominion]] on [[Sel]]{{epigraph ref|sa1|22}} even though several millenia had passed since that event.{{epigraph ref|sa2|70}} Hoid begged Frost for his support, implying that Frost still had clout in the cosmere.{{epigraph ref|sa1|28}} Frost rebuffed Hoid, emphasizing his nonintervention policy, and referred to their past actions as having caused more harm than good.{{epigraph ref|sa2|66}} Although Frost seemed to agree with Hoid's assessment that Odium was dangerous,{{epigraph ref|sa2|67}} he did not understand Hoid's level of concern{{epigraph ref|sa1|17}} since Odium was trapped in the [[Rosharan system]].{{epigraph ref|sa2|69}} The two were diametrically opposed on the best way to save the cosmere, and Frost entreated Hoid to abandon his quest; he felt that this was the only way to ensure that Hoid would not make things worse with his meddling.{{epigraph ref|sa2|60}}

Frost still lived on Yolen during Roshar's [[True Desolation]].{{wob ref|2565}} While he would not assist Hoid with analyzing a Rosharan contract when contacted about it by [[tamukek]], his sister did agree.{{book ref|sa5|18}}

Frost raised his niece, [[Starling]], though it is unclear what exactly happened to her parents.{{book ref|iote|prologue}} His current location is unknown, and many assume him to be dead.{{book ref|iote|32}} Starling and Hoid believe that he is still alive.{{book ref|iote|51}}{{book ref|iote|61}}

==Relationships==
===Starling===
{{quote
|You can never be ruined, child. Never, ever.
|Frost to Starling on the day of her transformation{{book ref|iote|prologue}}
}}
Illistandrista, or Starling, is Frost's niece, most likely by his sister. Starling's parents died when she was young, and she was raised by her uncle. Frost teaches her how to empathise with mortals, in preparation for when she will have followers of her own. The two are very close, and Starling looks up to her uncle a lot. He encouraged her apprenticeship with Hoid. Though Frost does not intervene in cosmere events, he appreciates that Hoid acts with care for those weaker than him.{{book ref|iote|44}} Frost worried that Hoid would get Starling into trouble, but allowed the apprenticeship regardless.{{book ref|iote|51}} 

===Hoid===
Frost and Hoid have known each other for a very long time, and Starling describes the two as friends. Frost and Hoid have very opposing views, but remain in contact. Hoid speaks fondly of Frost, and seeks his assistance when trapped in a vault.{{book ref|iote|51}}
