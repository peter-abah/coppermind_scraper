**Jaddeth** is a deity on [[Sel]] related to rocks and earth. He is the god of the modern [[Shu-Dereth]] religion.{{glossary ref|Elantris|J}}

He is mentioned in *[[Wyrn the King]]*, the oldest text on Sel, as a minor god that cared for the rocks under the earth.{{book ref|Elantris|43}} The originally pantheistic religion later shifted to monotheism through the teachings of [[Keseg]].{{glossary ref|Elantris|K}} [[Dereth]] further interpreted Keseg's doctrine to mean that mankind should serve a single monarch.{{glossary ref|Elantris|D}}{{book ref|Elantris|14}} Dereth's ideas were rejected in his native [[JinDo]], but were later embraced in [[Fjorden]] after the fall of the Old Empire and the adoption of [[Shu-Dereth]] as the official state religion by [[Wulfden the First]].{{glossary ref|Elantris|S}}{{glossary ref|Elantris|W}} Jaddeth was elevated to be the primary god of Shu-Dereth and therefore the entire Fjordell Empire.{{glossary ref|Elantris|J}} Over the next several centuries, Fjordell [[Wyrn (title)|Wyrn]]s and priests altered historical texts to make it seem like Fjorden had always served Jaddeth with holy purpose.{{book ref|elantris|43}}

In the modern Derethi religion, he is often referred to as "Lord Jaddeth"; shortening his name to "Ja" is regarded as vulgar.{{book ref|elantris|9}}{{glossary ref|Elantris|J}} The Fjordell believe Jaddeth to be the only extant god, and consider him to be omnipotent; he is said to have created mankind and remains capable of granting miracles.{{book ref|elantris|36}}{{book ref|elantris|18}}{{book ref|elantris|45}} [[Svrakiss]] are thought to be the souls of the dead who wasted their chance to worship Jaddeth.{{book ref|elantris|6}}

Some of Jaddeth's original characteristics have remained despite the evolution in how he is worshipped, including the fact that he dwells underground rather than in the skies.{{glossary ref|Elantris|J}} He is said to slumber in the earth, waiting for a time when the whole world worships him to return and rule the world.{{book ref|elantris|3}} This is cited as the justification for the Fjordell Empire's intent to conquer all of mankind. The "heathen" nations of [[Arelon]] and [[Teod]] that have historically used [[AonDor]] and resisted the Fjordell are seen as threats to the empire and Jaddeth's will.{{book ref|elantris|9}}{{book ref|elantris|45}} 

The Fjordell Wyrn acts as Jaddeth's prophet, and is the only Derethi that is said to directly serve and speak to him.{{book ref|Elantris|8}} Each Wyrn issues proclamations that are said to be the actual words of Jaddeth.{{book ref|elantris|3}} Followers of Shu-Dereth adhere to a strictly organized hierarchy that technically serves Wyrn rather than Jaddeth himself.{{book ref|elantris|12}} They believe that Jaddeth rewards devotion in his followers, as well as ambition.{{book ref|Elantris|8}}{{book ref|elantris|61}}{{book ref|Elantris|3}}
