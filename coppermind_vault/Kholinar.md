**Kholinar** is the capital city of the [[Kholin princedom]] and of [[Alethkar]], [[Roshar]].{{book ref|twok|60}} It's roughly symmetrical in shape, built around enormous stone formations known as *the windblades* for their unmistakable shape.

It is the place where [[Talenel]], Herald of the Almighty, announced to the world that the [[True Desolation]] had come.{{book ref|twok|epilogue}}

## Geography
{{image|Kholinar city map.jpg|side=right|width=300px|Map of the city}}
Kholinar is located in northern Alethkar. The city is built near the Windrunner river. Kholinar is sheltered from Highstorms by huge rock formations called windblades. It has many gardens and is considered beautiful in part because of the Windblades that jut up in and around the city.{{cite}}

## History
### Heraldic Epochs
Kholinar is one of the [[Dawncities]], the most ancient sites on all of Roshar. As the [[Silver Kingdom|Silver Kingdoms]] emerged Kholinar became [[Alethela]]’s capital and a [[Oathgate|Fabrial]] was built in the city. It was the home of King [[Nohadon]] during one of the [[Desolation]]s.{{book ref|twok|60}} Through the ages, it was rebuilt several times.

### Age of Solitude
Following the [[Sunmaker]]'s death, it fell into the hands of one of his sons, the progenitor of [[House Kholin]].{{book ref|sa3|19}}

During [[Gavilar]] and [[Dalinar Kholin]]'s war of Alethi unification, it was conquered by the two brothers and subsequently became the capital city of [[Alethkar]].{{book ref|sa3|16}}

Near the end of the [[War of Reckoning]], [[Aesudan]]'s negligence and [[Pai]]'s accusations caused the city to erupt in riots.{{book ref|sa2|i|12}} The [[Kholinar Wall Guard]] managed to restore order to the city and exiled all parshmen beyond its limits before the first [[Everstorm]] arrived. 

### The True Desolation
==== The Siege ====
After the arrival of the Everstorm, the Fused gathered the transformed parshmen and began the [[siege of Kholinar]]. With high numbers of refugees coming in from the entire princedom and silence from the palace, the city quickly became overwhelmed, with various services collapsing and gangs roaming the city. The chief forces within Kholinar at the time were the Wall Guard and the Cult of Moments. In addition, the city was under the influence of three [[Unmade]], [[Ashertmarn]] - who influenced the Cult of Moments and caused the breakdown of the Lighteye structures in the city, [[Yelig-nar]], who took control of the queen, his current host at the time, and ended up killing her because she wasn't strong enough to handle the extra power, and [[Sja-anat]], who corrupted the various spren. It was these corrupted spren who tortured Gavinor, the king’s son and heir to the throne of Alethkar.{{cite}}

While the city's defenses and command structure had all but collapsed, the Wall Guard had taken up its defense under the command of Highmarshal [[Azure]]. However, they were focused almost exclusively on the external threats. In the city, spren reacted strongly to any use of Stormlight, including spanreeds and soulcasters. This, along with the threat of Fused attacks, made outside communication impossible, and Soulcasting was only made possible by metal plates provided by [[Hoid|Wit]], seemingly made of aluminum, that prevented detection. The Guard's single Soulcaster was able to keep them and some of the city fed.{{book ref|sa3|81}}

When the leaders of the Voidbringer forces outside the city realised that the strike team, including [[Azure]], were attempting to retake the Oathgate, they ordered a massive assault. A Thunderclast breached the walls and the Voidbringers broke into the city. While the team managed to reach the Oathgate, they were unable to activate it properly, and Kholinar was overrun.{{book ref|sa3|87}}

==== Occupation of Kholinar ====
This was the first time in modern history Kholinar had fallen to an invading force. Under the control of the Voidbringers, tens of thousands of humans were moved out of the city to work on farms, while singers were moved in. Kholinar’s singers also put the humans to work rebuilding the city. However, the most awesome feats of construction, like a redesign of the Kholinar Palace, were completed by the Fused known as the Altered Ones. Afterwards, many new brands of Fused took up residence in Kholinar, such as Masked Ones and Focused Ones. With the presence of so many Fused, singers and humans alike learned to step carefully in Kholinar.{{rpg ref|slwg|152}} Some singers advocated for better treatment of the humans inside Kholinar.{{rpg ref|slwg|154}} The Fused encouraged the singers in Kholinar to explore the ancient ways of their people—their art, food, music, and more, while abandoning everything Alethi that they had absorbed. After each storm, newly blessed Regals gathered in the former dueling arena to train under capricious and volatile Fused, in preparation for rotations to the various fronts.{{rpg ref|slwg|152}} 

Shortly after the occupation began, the Kholinar resistance formed. While most of their members were humans, a few singers, such as [[Melur]], joined the resistance as well.{{rpg ref|ss|21}} At some point, a thriving [[firemoss]] trade sprung up among the resistance. A bullish Focused One was tasked with stopping it however, and he pressed a few people into investigating. The investigation uncovered that a group of humans were processing the addictive moss in an abandoned temple, then using the proceeds to smuggle dissident singers and humans, including one [[Knights Radiant|Radiant]] out of Kholinar.{{rpg ref|slwg|154}} Later, [[Eltharon|another Focused One]], or perhaps the same one, was tasked with destroying the resistance. He was responsible for the deaths of many members and managed to make the resistance leave Kholinar.{{rpg ref|ss|21}}{{rpg ref|ss|23}}

## Landmarks
{{image|Kholinar by Connor Chamberlain.jpg|A view of the city from outside.|side=right|width=350px|artist=Connor Chamberlain}}
### Windblades
The most notable feature of Kholinar are the windblades. They are massive rock formations, akin to giant fins, sticking out of the ground. They appear to be made of red, white, and orange strata, highly similar to the walls of [[Urithiru]]. Over the centuries, tunnels have been cut into them for quick and discrete passage through the walls. The windblades are wide enough for people to live atop some of them.{{book ref|sa3|61}}

### The City Wall
The wall surrounds the entire city. It incorporates the windblades for much of its length, with man-made structures in the spaces between them. It is sixty feet tall at its highest point and ten feet wide, with guard posts every three hundred feet. Each post is large enough to hold a platoon of soldiers.{{cite}}

### The Kholinar Palace
The [[Kholinar Palace]] consists of the main palace complex, a garden, and the Monastery Dais, which is connected to the main building by a passage known as the Sunwalk. The Palace is situated in the northern portion of the city, on a plateau high above the rest of the building. This caused a set of unloading docks to be built at the bottom, to transport cargo to and from the palace.{{book ref|sa3|prologue}}

### The Kholinar Oathgate
The original function of the Kholinar [[Oathgate]] has been lost to time. In the modern day, it is known as the Monastery Dais and serves as the headquarters of the Kholin ardentia. Buildings have been built on the Oathgate platform, including chapels and housing, although the original chamber, with its eleven lanterns and a keyhole, remains unchanged. The Oathgate is currently non-functional, as Odium forced [[Sja-anat]] to corrupt its [[spren]].{{book ref|sa3|84}}

### The Impossible Falls
The [[Impossible Falls]] are waterfalls located in a small park in the south-west portion of the city. It appears to be a private meeting place. It is said that when viewed from the correct angle, the water seems to flow down tiers and then back up to the top. [[Adolin Kholin]] describes them to [[Shallan Davar]], telling her he would like to show them to her in person one day.{{book ref|ob|77}}

### The Lanacin Monument
A monument in the southwestern part of the city.{{file ref|Kholinar.jpg|Map of Kholinar}} Little is known about this monument, but it is likely related to [[Lanacin|Lanacin the Surefooted]].

### Sunmaker Park
A park in the northern part of the city that is named after the [[Sunmaker]].{{file ref|Kholinar.jpg|Map of Kholinar}} Sunmaker park is home to many cultivated [[Stumpweight]] trees.{{rpg ref|slwg|151}}

### Ringington market
The Ringington market is the market closest to [[Yokska]]'s shop.{{book ref|sa3|72}}

### District of Colors
A section of Kholinar under the Fused occupation, potentially existing before then. Nearby, quarried rock is being taken to build houses for the [[Makay-im]].{{book ref|sa4|i|4}}

### Dueling Arena
Kholinar also has a duelling arena. After the occupation by the Fused, the arena ceased to hold tournaments, and instead became a place for new Regals to train under capricious and volatile Fused, as a preparation for rotations to the various fronts.{{rpg ref|slwg|152}}

## Politics
### Royal family
Alethkar’s Royal Family resides in Kholinar, but with the [[War of Reckoning]] the only known members to reside here are [[Aesudan]] and [[Gavinor]]. As the [[Elhokar|King]] and Queen normally reside here, Kholinar is the center of Alethi politics and the residence of the Royal Court.{{cite}}

==== Palace Guard ====
This was the force tasked with the protection of the royal family in the city and the palace. A portion of the Guards resisted the lure of the Unmade and were locked in a room within the palace complex. Upon their release, they claimed time seemed to pass differently for them, feeling that it had only been a few days rather than weeks since the riots.{{cite}}

### Wall Guard
The [[Kholinar Wall Guard]] was tasked with patrolling the city wall and controlling gate access, but was crippled when most of the high ranking lighteyes disappeared into the Palace after the execution of an ardent and the subsequent riots. With no high-ranking lighteyes to take charge, a woman calling herself Highmarshal [[Azure]] seized control of the Guard and organized them to defend the city. With [[Ithi]] and her sister using the single remaining [[Soulcaster]] in a room lined with [[Aluminum]] provided by [[Wit]], Highmarshal [[Azure]] was able to keep the Wall Guard fed and provide a sizeable amount of food for the common people of Kholinar.{{book ref|sa3|81}}

They recruited any able-bodied fighter, using food and redemption to entice volunteers. Their primary focus was defending the wall from Fused raids, letting the city run itself for the most part. They used extra food to stop the other highlords from trying to seize control of the walls.{{cite}}

Kaladin was able to convince High Marshal Azure to lead six hundred of the Wall Guard to assault the palace.{{book ref|sa3|83}}

### City Guard
The City Guard is the main peacekeeping force in the city. During the riots after the execution of [[Pai]], the remaining city guard was recalled to the palace. Like other people who entered, most of them never returned, leaving behind only the personal forces of the highlords.{{cite}}

[[Elhokar]] and [[Adolin]] were able to recruit a few of the highlords, who together contributed around a hundred members of their personal guard to join on the assault.{{book ref|sa3|83}}

### Kholinar Resistance
After the city’s conquest and occupation by the Fused, the Kholinar resistance became a powerful faction. It is made up of both humans and singers,{{rpg ref|ss|21}} and has access to at least one Radiant.{{rpg ref|ss|21}} Its members also have access to a single [[Shardblade]]. After a Fused named [[Eltharon]] was tasked with destroying the resistance, the faction was forced to withdraw and leave the city.{{rpg ref|ss|23}} 

### Cult of Moments
The cult is a new arrival, as it either appeared or revealed itself upon the beginning of the siege. They are divided into two groups. One group roams the street imitating corrupted spren, while the rest of the members hold feasts on the Monastery Dias. [[Ashertmarn]], the Heart of the Revel, compels them to mindless hedonism. The cult takes new members easily, provided that they can bring them food. Shallan is able to join the revelers on the Oathgate platform by stealing and distributing it.{{cite}}

### Fused
After being conquered by singer forces, administration of the city, and much of the nation, was given to the [[Nine]], a group of nine high ranking individuals among the Fused. Under them, singers became more instrumental to the city; Regals were placed in high ranking government positions and the Fused began various building projects, such as transforming the Palace and constructing homes for many different Fused.{{rpg ref|slwg|152}}

## Religion
Kholinar is located in the Vorin country of Alethkar, making the majority of its population Vorin. As the capital of Alethkar the city likely contains temples to each of the [[Heralds]]. The Queen is known to have many ardents in her retinue. Multiple Devotaries have a presence in the city.{{book ref|sa2|i|12}} At least some temples were abandoned after the Fused occupation.{{rpg ref|slwg|154}} The known Devotaries to have a presence in the city are:{{cite}}
* The Devotary of Insight: they have a temple in Kholinar.
* The Order of Tanelenat: they have a presence in the western part of Kholinar, adjacent to the temple of Tanelenat.
