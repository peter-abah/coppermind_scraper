**Bila** is a [[Listener|listener]] on [[Roshar]]. She is [[Thude]]'s mate for two weeks and is later part of a warpair with him under the command of [[Eshonai]], in her personal division.{{book ref|sa2|i|4}}{{book ref|sa2|i|11}} Bila is also a member of the [[Council of Five]],{{book ref|sa5|58}} and went on a mission to Narak during the [[Second Battle of Narak]].{{book ref|sa5|101}} This mission led to the foundation of a listener state recognized by both [[Odium]] and the [[Coalition of monarchs]].{{book ref|sa5|137}}

Bila often mocks Thude’s coat.{{book ref|sa5|56}} After six years of war with the Alethi, Bila hates humans. She also hates Odium for having caused so much death.{{book ref|sa5|58}}

## History
### First Rhythm Family
Bila was born into the [[Listener#Families|First-Rhythm]] family.{{ref|group=fn|text=She knows both Eshonai and Thude, the latter even planning on becoming a mate with her, this suggests that she was born into the same family.}} She became acquainted with both Eshonai and Thude from an early age. During the year {{Rosharan date|1166}}, Thude asked Bila to take [[mateform]] with him, to which she agreed. They spent the next two weeks in the form, and although it didn’t lead to any children, it did create a strong and lasting bond between them.{{book ref|sa4|57}} It is likely that Bila was either a member of the first group that took [[warform]], or took the form shortly after.

{{sidequote|I’m going to do it one of these days, Eshonai. I’m going to see if Bila will go with me. Life is meant to be more then working the fields or chopping wood.|Thude to Eshonai on asking Bila to take mateform with him{{book ref|sa4|57}}|side=right|size=300px}}

### War of Reckoning
As the [[War of Reckoning]] started, Bila, either having already taken warform previously or taking it (again) for this conflict, signed up to fight. She joined together with Thude, who had been her mate sometime before, and was assigned to Eshonai’s personal division. Bila and Thude formed an excellent warpair, and they fought together for the duration of the war. At some point, Thude suggested to Eshonai that they should try sending spies to the warcamps. He volunteered, but Eshonai didn’t want him to go, stating that he was to valuable because of his expertise. After this, Bila volunteered to go, Thude shut this offer down though, telling Bila that he couldn’t imagine losing her.{{book ref|sa5|79}} 

After six years of fighting humans, Bila grew to hate them, lamenting the deaths the war had caused. When Eshonai showed her and Thude a gemstone, cut after human design,{{ref|group=fn|text=Listener gemstones holding spren were cut after a human design previously given to Venli.}} with a stormspren inside, explaining that the spren could lead to a new form, Bila appeared skeptical but also stated that she had no inhibitions against taking Forms of Power if they helped her on the battlefield. Bila and Eshonai argued about this, almost driving Bila to the point of insubordination before Thude intervened by offering food.{{book ref|sa2|i|4}} 

Later, after Eshonai returned from a highstorm, having taken the new form by allowing a stormspren into her gemheart, she picked two hundred soldiers to take the form as well. However, for a reason she herself couldn’t quite place, she didn’t pick members of her personal division. Fifteen days later, when she gathered the listener soldiers and told them to take stormform, the soldiers in her personal division, including Bila and Thude, agreed. However, Thude did approach Eshonai, telling her that he was concerned, and that what they were doing wasn’t right. She put him in charge of her division and told him to Watch over the group of listeners who had refused to take stormform. He agreed and went back to work. A short while later however, he and Bila, along with the rest of their division, organised an escape. They climbed down into the chasms and fled from Narak.{{book ref|sa2|i|11}} Shortly before the next Highstorm, the group was approached by a small group of chasmfiends, who offered their version of a truce and helped these dissenters out of the chasms. They then roamed around on the Plains for a while, avoiding both humans and Fused.{{book ref|sa5|56}} At some point in the near future after their escape, Bila became a member of the Five.{{book ref|sa5|58}}

Roughly fourteen months after their escape, some of the dissenters spotted a number of other singers approaching their camp. One of these singers, who revealed herself to be Venli, stepped forward, wishing to speak with the Five. Thude was the first to speak to her, and, while not trusting her, allowed her to see her mother and talk to the Five. Venli also brought a great number of [[Reachers|Radiant spren]] with her. Additionally, the Five allowed the other singers, including five Fused who had rejected Odium, to stay with them.{{book ref|sa4|115}}

### Journey to Narak
==== The Journey ====
After several of their people heard a voice, belonging to the [[Stone]], tell them to seek the center of the Shattered Plains, the Five decided to head to the center. With them, they took about fifteen other listeners, several chasmfiends and the Fused. Bila, like the rest of the Five, appointed someone as her replacement should he not return. As they neared the central plateaus, a few listeners began to doubt their mission, and the Five decided to vote, worried that Odium might be tricking them. Bila was baffled by the others’ insistence on finding answers. Regardless, the Five voted unanimously to continue. After arriving near Narak, the Fused accepted Odium’s offer of power, which he was subconsciously giving to every Fused in the area. Leshwi decided that the Fused should hold back, in case their presence would draw Odium’s eye, and the Five agreed.{{book ref|sa5|89}}

After they traveled the last bit, Venli realized that the sound that she had been hearing, which was linked to the voice that had spoken to several listeners earlier, came from underneath the central plateau, and she created a tunnel to the source of the sound. Before going down the tunnel, it was decided that the Five and Venli would go down, while the rest stayed back.{{book ref|sa5|89}} At the end of the tunnel was a cavern, in which the Five found a [[Well of Control|large pool of golden light]]. While Venli told the Five what her spren said about the [[perpendicularity]], Bila stopped Thude from touching the liquid. Before they could decide what to do next, a guard came running down the tunnel, telling them that their Fused had been discovered.{{book ref|sa5|90}}

==== The Battle ====
With the Fused and chasmfiends discovered, the Five rushed back up the tunnel, and once above ground, sealed up the tunnel Venli had created. Immediately after this, the group was spotted by a few Fused. These Fused carried the listeners to the Fused commander of Odium’s forces at Narak, [[El]]. He gave them an offer and a threat, stating that Odium would destroy them after the war was over, but if they and their chasmfiends aided him, Odium would let them go unpunished for their rebellion against him. He gave them time to think and decide on which choice to make.{{book ref|sa5|90}} The Five argued over what choice they should make, and Bila realised that joining the battle would mean being used as fodder. However. before the Five could come to a clear conclusion, Venli came up with a cunning plan.{{book ref|sa5|101}}

{{sidequote|If we join this battle they’ll use us as fodder at the front of their final assault. Us and the chasmfiends. We’ll be slaughtered.|Bila to the rest of the Five{{book ref|sa5|101}}|size=300px|side=right}}

Their plan involved working together with the human forces defending Narak. The Five, which Bila was a part of, sent Venli to treat with the Alethi, and, after realising that their plan would not work, she managed to get the Alethi to agree to a new plan.{{book ref|sa5|105}} This new plan was to make a treaty. The treaty would give the Shattered Plains to the listeners and give the Alethi, who were slowly losing Narak, a way to keep the Plains out of the hands of their enemy and retain some rights, such as the ability to keep farming their lands.{{book ref|sa5|137}} The next morning, the Five informed El that they had (supposedly) decided to take his offer, and they prepared for battle. The listeners climbed atop their chasmfiends and, acting as the second wave of forces, charged towards the human fortress.{{book ref|sa5|112}} Their attack was somewhat fake, as they weren’t actually trying to kill any humans, and likewise, the humans weren’t really trying to kill them. It had been agreed upon that the listeners would ‘force’ the humans to retreat, during which the Alethi would discreetly give Venli the signed treaty. This went according to plan.{{book ref|sa5|118}}

==== Freedom ====
As the Battle ended, Bila, like the other listeners, retreated and dismounted. El commanded the listeners to move over to another plateau and guard the Oathgate, to make sure that the humans did not manage to take Narak back. Due to the chasmfiends’ biology, making them tired and incapable of moving, this couldn’t be done however, and El told them to watch the plateau from where they currently were.{{book ref|sa5|118}}

{{sidequote|Why can’t anyone ever just leave us alone?|Bila to Venli upon seeing Retribution’s perpendicularity fill{{book ref|sa5|146}}|side=right|size=300px}}

The next morning, after the [[contest of champions]] had begun, effectively freezing borders, the listeners approached El. Thude, a member of the Five, showed El the treaty they had made with Queen Jasnah of Alethkar the day before, formally asking El to withdraw his forces from the Shattered Plains, which were now in listener possession. El, being bound by Odium’s promise and unable to take the Plains from the listeners, agreed and promptly withdrew. With the establishment and recognition of this nation, Bila became a member of the first Council of Five of the new listener nation.{{book ref|sa5|137}} The next day, Bila was among the people who saw Odium’s perpendicularity, which had emptied, refill and turn into [[Retribution]]’s perpendicularity.{{book ref|sa5|146}}

## Relationships
### Thude
{{sidequote|I can’t lose you|Thude to Bila{{book ref|sa5|79}}|side=right|size=300px}}
Bila and [[Thude]] enjoy a close relationship because of the two week time period they spend together in [[mateform]]; after that, they also fight as a warpair while in [[warform]].{{book ref|sa2|i|4}} The two also fled Narak together,{{book ref|sa2|i|11}} and became members of the Five together.{{book ref|sa5|58}} Additionally, the two were together when they travelled to Narak during the [[Second Battle of Narak]].{{book ref|sa5|101}} It seems that Bila is the more careful one in their relationship, as she stops him from foolishly touching Odium’s perpendicularity.{{book ref|sa5|89}}

### Eshonai
When [[Eshonai]] shows Bila the captured stormspren, she notes that Bila does not care about her people's origins or the origin of the power this new form promises; she is focused only on the war with the Alethi. This frightens Eshonai, who knows there are others who think the same way, discounting the danger of the solution in the face of the immediate threat posed by the humans.{{book ref|sa2|i|4}} After Eshonai demanded that every soldier take stormform, Bila agreed. However, before taking the form, Bila and a number of her friends fled Eshonai.{{book ref|sa2|i|11}}
