**Tiw**, the warrior, is a god in the [[Earth (Frugal Wizard)|alternate medieval dimension]].{{book ref|fwh|11}} 

==History==
{{sidequote|The gods don't fight because they fear the monsters, and the end. They know it should have been Tiw who died.|[[Ealstan]]{{book ref|fwh|24}}|side=left|size=300px}}
Many years ago, the great wolf, [[Fenris]], took off Tiw's hand and tasted his blood. Tiw then bound Fenris to the land, but was fated to eventually be killed by the wolf. Later, during the [[battle of Badon]], the [[Black Bear]] freed Fenris. The wolf was in possession of the implements of [[metodgodas]], which would bring about the end of gods. Tiw and the other gods retreated from the battle, not wanting to risk the end of the world. However, [[Friag]] returned to the battle when she heard the men crying out in pain, death, and desperation. After pushing back the Black Bear with the finest boasts, and binding him to the land as if he were a [[wight]], Friag was weakened.{{book ref|fwh|13}} The wolf still remained unbound, and under the Black Bear's orders,{{book ref|fwh|24}} Fenris took the opportunity to consume the weakened god, and he killed her instead of Tiw.{{book ref|fwh|13}} Because of Friag's sacrifice, Tiw escaped his fate.{{book ref|frugal|13}}{{book ref|fwh|24}}

People in the alternate medieval dimension, such as [[Sefawynn]], sometimes swear by Tiw's holy name.{{book ref|frugal|11}}

==Trivia==
*It is likely that Tiw is the alternate medieval dimension's version of the real-world Norse god, Týr, as "Tiw" is Old English for "Týr".{{wp ref|Týr}}
*In real-world Norse mythology, the monstrous wolf Fenrir bit off Týr's right hand after realising that the gods had bound him.{{wp ref|Týr}}{{wp ref|Fenrir}}
*In modern English, the word "Tuesday" is said to be named after Týr ("Týr's day").{{wp ref|Týr}}
