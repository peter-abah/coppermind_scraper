**Auxiliary**, usually shortened to **Aux** and originally called **12124**, is [[Szeth]]'s former [[highspren]] (until {{Rosharan date|1175|5|2|5}}).{{book ref|sa5|147}} He later bonds [[Nomad]], traveling with him throughout the [[cosmere]] while on the run from the [[Night Brigade]].{{book ref|tsm|20}}{{book ref|tsm|26}}{{book ref|tsm|1}} He is eventually consumed by the [[Dawnshard]] temporarily held by Nomad,{{book ref|tsm|20}} leaving little remaining of his essence beyond fragments of his mind and personality.{{book ref|tsm|30}} After his death,{{book ref|tsm|1}}{{book ref|tsm|10}} Auxiliary continues to travel with Nomad until he sacrifices his remaining essence on [[Canticle]] to save his companion's life and the citizens of [[Beacon]].{{book ref|tsm|11}}{{book ref|tsm|26}}{{book ref|tsm|45}}{{book ref|tsm|47}}

## Appearance and Personality
{{for|/Gallery|more images}}

### Appearance
Prior to his death, Auxiliary appeared as all highspren do, as a black slit in the fabric of reality with a void of stars within. The air seems to warp around him. He was also capable of making himself invisible whenever he wanted.{{book ref|sa4|i|7}}{{book ref|sa3|98}}{{file ref|Shallan's Sketchbook - Highspren.jpg}} He looked like a human-shaped but otherwise similar void in the [[Cognitive Realm]].{{file ref|Shallan's Sketchbook - Highspren.jpg}}

{{sidequote|side=left|size=480px|Auxiliary, you're literally a living manifestation of physical forces—sharing substance with the concepts of gravitation and the interaxial force. You should know about this stuff.|Nomad chides Auxiliary for being bad at physics{{book ref|tsm|19}}}}

After his death, Auxiliary cannot corporally manifest in the physical realm on his own,{{book ref|tsm|13}} and exists instead as a deep monotone voice that rides along in Nomad's head.{{book ref|tsm|29}}{{book ref|tsm|1}} While the [[spren]] cannot use vocal inflection to indicate tone he is still capable of making sounds like sighs and oohs, though the result is distinctively amusing.{{book ref|tsm|6}}{{book ref|tsm|37}} Unlike tone, Auxiliary is still able to modulate the volume of his projected words.{{book ref|tsm|25}}{{book ref|tsm|30}} 

In the cognitive realm, Auxiliary continues to have some kind of form after his death, though the details are unknown.{{book ref|tsm|epilogue}} Auxiliary remarks on multiple occasions that he doesn't have any eyes.{{book ref|tsm|18}}{{book ref|tsm|25}} However, it is unclear if he is speaking generally in regard to his incorporeal physical realm self or rather literally about a lack of eyes in his body in the cognitive realm similar to a [[deadeye]] spren. After his second death, Auxiliary is burned away entirely and has no presence remaining in the cognitive realm.{{book ref|tsm|epilogue}}

Auxiliary is able to manifest a body in the physical realm only when summoned by Nomad as a particular object.{{book ref|tsm|29}} This body can take on any number of forms, limited only by Nomad's understanding of the intended shape.{{book ref|tsm|5}} The object is always made out of a silvery metal,{{book ref|tsm|37}} likely an alloy of the [[God Metal|God metals]] of [[Honor]] and [[Cultivation]].{{wob ref|11949}}{{wob ref|2541}} It forms out of a glowing white mist{{book ref|tsm|1}}{{book ref|tsm|43}} and once manifested can alter its shape by partially transforming back into mist and reforming.{{book ref|tsm|37}}

### Personality
Auxiliary generally has a upbeat and witty personality even when Nomad is in pain or superficial danger.{{book ref|tsm|1}}{{book ref|tsm|18}} He displays a dry and sarcastic sense of humor which is often used at Nomad's expense.{{book ref|tsm|16}}{{book ref|tsm|25}} Though the jokes are usually good natured and meant to guide Nomad toward more moral and self-preserving actions,{{book ref|tsm|24}} they do on occasion go too far and end up coming across as genuinely hurtful.{{book ref|tsm|25}} Auxiliary's humor is also sometimes self-deprecating,{{book ref|tsm|10}}{{book ref|tsm|14}} though he much prefers self-aggrandizement.{{book ref|tsm|19}}{{book ref|tsm|21}} Despite this predilection for boastfulness, Auxiliary actively rejects being referred to as a god despite his [[Splinter|divine origin]];{{book ref|tsm|14}} a reaction at odds with some other [[Spren#True Spren|sapient spren]] such as [[Sylphrena]].{{book ref|sa4|16}}

{{sidequote|side=right|size=400px|I love showing off, the knight exclaims. It feels so good. Why do mortals have taboos against it?|Auxiliary on showing off{{book ref|tsm|21}}}}

While with Szeth, Auxiliary acted very reserved towards his Radiant, keeping his tone formal and addressing him as acolyte.{{book ref|sa4|i|7}} He did not talk to him or show himself to him often.{{book ref|sa4|i|7}}{{book ref|sa3|121}} Auxiliary was initially hesitant to show his true self to Nomad but his expressiveness grew over their decades together until the day he died.{{book ref|tsm|6}} Though he is still able to feel emotions after his death, he has a difficult time conveying them because of his monotone voice.{{book ref|tsm|19}}{{book ref|tsm|37}} He is self-conscious about his monotone and adds third-person commentary and dialogue tags to his speech to help convey meaning, tone, and intent.{{book ref|tsm|1}} To Nomad's great irritation, he sometimes overuses adverbs in an effort to convey emotion,{{book ref|tsm|12}}{{book ref|tsm|16}} and also occasionally needs to clarify the intended connotation of a word or phrase with multiple meanings.{{book ref|tsm|13}}{{book ref|tsm|14}} When he curses, Auxiliary uses the [[Roshar|Rosharan]] "storms/storming" and "[[Damnation|damnation]]" though it is unknown if he knew of and used such words before bonding with Nomad.{{book ref|tsm|14}}{{book ref|tsm|30}}{{book ref|tsm|34}} He is usually blunt and direct in his speech and doesn't fully understand why humans, particularly Nomad, speak circumspectly rather than doing the same.{{book ref|tsm|29}}

{{image|The Sunlit Man Front Endpaper by Ernanda Souza.jpg|side=left|width=400px|"That's a sight, the knight says with awe. Just water and stones, but on such a scale as to be beautiful. Amazing. Why is it we hate traveling these worlds again?…I do wish we could pause a little more often and just enjoy the view." -Auxiliary{{book ref|tsm|26}}}}

Auxiliary is extremely intelligent,{{book ref|tsm|19}} and is particularly adept at making connections between things that he learns{{book ref|tsm|7}}{{book ref|tsm|24}} and extrapolating meaningful information from a small sample size.{{book ref|tsm|2}}{{book ref|tsm|3}}{{book ref|tsm|5}} He also has some amount of medical knowledge, being able to diagnose broken ribs, severe whiplash, imminent severe bruising before it forms, and potential concussion upon a cursory examination of the patient.{{book ref|tsm|42}} Auxiliary is also sometimes quite philosophical, posing questions about the nature of reality and how it is shaped by perception.{{book ref|tsm|4}}{{book ref|tsm|28}}{{book ref|tsm|33}} Despite being a "living manifestation of physical forces", Auxiliary does not have an inherent understanding of those concepts and seems to hold physics and math in disdain, preferring art instead.{{book ref|tsm|19}} One of Auxiliary's favorite forms of aesthetic beauty are grand natural vistas as the views fill him with awe and make him wish he and Nomad could pause more often to enjoy the views on their travels to many worlds.{{book ref|tsm|26}}

Auxiliary is very interested in understanding the motivations behind people's actions.{{book ref|tsm|17}}{{book ref|tsm|33}} This interest likely stems from not understanding humans very well and a desire to know them better.{{book ref|tsm|23}}{{book ref|tsm|28}}{{book ref|tsm|45}} Though he has trouble understanding motivation, he is able to recognize human emotion through facial expressions and is even able to recognize when a person or group of people are acting strangely.{{book ref|tsm|13}}{{book ref|tsm|15}}{{book ref|tsm|32}} He has a particularly hard time understanding actions that have no logical reasoning behind them such as Nomad's abandonment of his [[Knights Radiant#Immortal Words|oaths]].{{book ref|tsm|45}} 
Though Auxiliary does on occasion understand a person's motivation correctly,{{book ref|tsm|21}} he is not nearly as experienced or proficient in the skill as Nomad.{{book ref|tsm|15}} He is frequently impressed by Nomad's ability to intuit people's actions and is amazed at how this allows Nomad to stay ahead.{{book ref|tsm|14}}{{book ref|tsm|41}} Despite his own directness, Auxiliary gets frustrated when Nomad says something bluntly to another person, thinking that Nomad is supposed to be "good with people"; though Nomad disagrees, thinking that "being good" and speaking kindly are not always the same thing.{{book ref|tsm|23}}

{{sidequote|side=right|size=490px|You should tell her, the knight interjects, that is basically the entire point of math. Explaining stuff everyone already knows.|Auxiliary on the purpose of mathematics{{book ref|tsm|19}}}}

Auxiliary has a strong sense of morality and chides Nomad when he contemplates mistreating someone, even going so far as refusing to participate in Nomad's actions when he considers them immoral.{{book ref|tsm|5}}{{book ref|tsm|11}} He also calls out Nomad when he lies to people,{{book ref|tsm|11}}{{book ref|tsm|17}} though he only pushes so hard, likely believing that it must be the choice of the individual to become better.{{book ref|tsm|11}}
Auxiliary considers himself to still be a [[Knights Radiant|Knight Radiant]] in good standing,{{book ref|tsm|35}}{{book ref|tsm|45}} frequently referring to himself as "the knight" or "the hero" and to Nomad as his squire, valet, or servant.{{book ref|tsm|1}}{{book ref|tsm|14}}{{book ref|tsm|29}} He is particularly empathetic towards the oppressed,{{book ref|tsm|15}} becoming frustrated with Nomad when he considers switching allegiance to the [[Cinder King]] and abandoning the Beaconites.{{book ref|tsm|18}} He likewise feels badly about killing the [[Charred]] despite their apparent hostility as he considers them just as much victims of the Cinder King as anyone else.{{book ref|tsm|37}} Auxiliary is particularly disgusted by the idea of turning people into [[Sunheart|sunhearts]], especially against their will,{{book ref|tsm|13}} likely because of the resonance it has with his own experience.{{book ref|tsm|20}}

## Abilities and Attributes
### Nahel Bond
As a sapient spren, Auxiliary is able to form a [[Nahel bond]] with someone and was most recently bonded with Nomad.{{book ref|tsm|20}} Before being consumed by the Dawnshard,{{book ref|tsm|30}} the bond allowed Auxiliary to exist in the physical realm on his own without losing his mind as well as granting Nomad access to the [[Surgebinding|surges]] of [[Surgebinding#Gravitation|Gravitation]] and [[Surgebinding#Division|Division]].{{book ref|sa2|17}}{{book ref|sa2|60}}{{book ref|sa3|90}}{{book ref|sa5|135}} He could also make himself invisible.{{book ref|sa4|i|7}} Nomad and Auxiliary reached at least the [[Knights Radiant#Immortal Words|fourth ideal]] of the [[Order of Skybreakers]] giving the pair access to Skybreaker [[Shardplate]].{{book ref|sa4|i|7}}

==== Telepathic Communication ====
{{sidequote|side=left|size=440px|Ah, that's right, the hero realizes. You can't speak up here. How special…how delightful. I can talk, and you can't interrupt? You know, for a lowly valet, you certainly do monopolize a great deal of the speaking opportunities.|Auxiliary speaks to Nomad when he is incapable of responding{{book ref|tsm|28}}}}

Their shared bond allows Auxiliary to project words directly into Nomad's mind.{{book ref|tsm|1}} The bond was once strong enough for Nomad to be able to respond in kind but that ability was lost with Auxiliary's death, forcing Nomad to respond to Auxiliary out loud.{{book ref|tsm|30}} Though Auxiliary has become quite adept at anticipating Nomad's thoughts and actions,{{book ref|tsm|15}} he frequently needs to directly ask Nomad what his plan is in the given situation.{{book ref|tsm|16}}{{book ref|tsm|18}}{{book ref|tsm|31}} However, Auxiliary is still able to understand Nomad's emotions allowing for at least a base level of telepathic communication when necessary.{{book ref|tsm|30}}

Auxiliary's ability to speak into Nomad's mind and to hear his spoken words appears to have a range that exceeds that of normal spoken conversation.{{book ref|tsm|4}} However, the ability is not limitless, as communication is impossible when Auxiliary's physical manifestation is a good distance away from Nomad.{{book ref|tsm|8}}{{book ref|tsm|42}}

==== Investiture Quantification ====
Auxiliary intuitively understands how much [[Investiture]] Nomad has and how much performing a given action will consume.{{book ref|tsm|1}}{{book ref|tsm|5}} He often informs Nomad of these values as a number of [[Investiture#Measurement|Breath Equivalent Units]] or as a percentage of [[Sigzil#Skipping|Skip]] capacity (Skip capacity being approximately 20,000 BEUs).{{book ref|tsm|1}}{{book ref|tsm|4}} He can also numerically determine how quickly Nomad can absorb Investiture from a given source, providing the value in BEUs per hour.{{book ref|tsm|5}} Despite their utility, Nomad dislikes these quantitative readouts of his power level as it makes him feel like a machine with a power source.{{book ref|tsm|25}}{{book ref|tsm|34}} After Auxiliary's second death however, Nomad no longer has access to the specific numerical level of his Investiture and misses the spren's readouts.{{book ref|tsm|49}}{{book ref|tsm|52}} In addition to his intrinsic understanding of Nomad's Investiture level, Auxiliary is also adept at estimating the levels of Investiture in sources he encounters.{{book ref|tsm|4}}{{book ref|tsm|24}} Likely related to these abilities, Auxiliary is able to feel when a portion of Nomad's [[Sigzil#Dawnshard|Torment]] is drawn away by the [[Thaylenah|Thaylen's]] sunheart.{{book ref|tsm|36}}

==== Manifestation ====
Though no longer able to create a body for himself in the physical realm after his death, Auxiliary can manifest in the physical realm when summoned by Nomad in a particular form.{{book ref|tsm|1}}{{book ref|tsm|29}} Once manifested, Auxiliary seems to have some amount of agency in the exact size and even form that he takes.{{book ref|tsm|3}}{{book ref|tsm|39}}{{book ref|tsm|42}} However, most manifestations are controlled entirely by Nomad and are bound by his understanding of the form.{{book ref|tsm|1}}{{book ref|tsm|5}}{{book ref|tsm|35}} Nomad can summon Auxiliary instantly and without speaking aloud despite Auxiliary no longer being able to read Nomad's thoughts.{{book ref|tsm|4}}{{book ref|tsm|30}}

{{image|Elegy wields Auxiliary.jpg|side=right|width=400px|Elegy wields Auxiliary as a [[Shardblade#Radiant Shardblades|Shardspear]] in battle}}

Auxiliary can manifest as virtually any shape as long as Nomad understands the form's construction on a fundamental level.{{book ref|tsm|5}} If a form is novel or particularly complex, there is a chance that the manifestation will form misshapenly and need to be attempted again.{{book ref|tsm|5}} The manifestation can be composed of multiple pieces such as a chain or gauntlet{{book ref|tsm|1}}{{book ref|tsm|6}} or a complex mechanism like a jack or clock{{book ref|tsm|5}} so long as the discontiguous pieces are touching.{{book ref|tsm|5}} The more complex the manifestation is, the longer it takes to create.{{book ref|tsm|5}} However, this time can be shortened with repeated use of a particular form.{{book ref|tsm|5}} All manifestations require some amount of Investiture which is continuously drawn as long as the manifestation is present.{{book ref|tsm|3}}{{book ref|tsm|17}} Larger or heavier manifested objects require a greater amount while most manifestations below about one hundred pounds require a negligible amount of Investiture and are not worth accounting for.{{book ref|tsm|3}}{{book ref|tsm|39}}{{book ref|tsm|47}} The small amount of Investiture required for most manifestations means that it is the last power available to Nomad and Auxiliary when at low levels of Investiture.{{book ref|tsm|34}} There does not seem to be an upper bound for the size or weight Auxiliary can manifest beyond the limitations of Nomad's storage and use of Investiture.{{book ref|tsm|39}}{{book ref|tsm|47}}

All manifestations are made entirely of metal,{{book ref|tsm|3}}{{book ref|tsm|5}} likely an alloy of the God metals of Honor and Cultivation,{{wob ref|11949}}{{wob ref|2541}} and form out of nothingness from a white mist.{{book ref|tsm|1}}{{book ref|tsm|43}} The manifestation persists unless Nomad runs out of Investiture or specifically dismisses or resummons Auxiliary even if Nomad is on another planetary system or falls unconscious.{{book ref|tsm|4}}{{book ref|tsm|epilogue}} Nomad can alter Auxiliary's form even when not in direct physical contact.{{book ref|tsm|42}} He can also adjust Auxiliary's form without entirely dismissing him and can custom-mold the spren to fit a real-world condition perfectly.{{book ref|tsm|1}}{{book ref|tsm|30}} When altering shape or size, the affected portions of the manifestation "fuzz" back into mist before reforming into the new shape.{{book ref|tsm|17}}{{book ref|tsm|37}} The object typically behaves normally once manifested in the physical realm, interacting with forces like gravity and processes like thermodynamics.{{book ref|tsm|2}}{{book ref|tsm|16}} However, Nomad can change certain properties of the manifestation at will such as its opacity and its magnetic field.{{book ref|tsm|6}} Additionally, the natural properties of the metal make it nearly impossible to break or bend under natural circumstances{{book ref|tsm|30}} as well as incredibly resistant to heat,{{book ref|tsm|2}} Investiture,{{book ref|tsm|39}} and even explosive force.{{book ref|tsm|42}}

Nomad is able to summon Auxiliary instantly, not requiring the usual ten heartbeats necessary for summoning a deadeye spren.{{book ref|tsm|37}}{{book ref|sa1|13}}{{book ref|sa2|86}} If Auxiliary is already manifested in the physical realm, he vanishes into glowing mist at his current location and appears at or near Nomad's location.{{book ref|tsm|43}} Summoning the spren in this way has seemingly no limit on distance as Nomad is able to do so when the pair are on different planetary systems.{{book ref|tsm|epilogue}} Nomad can also dismiss Auxiliary, causing the manifestation to vanish without being replaced by another form.{{book ref|tsm|5}}{{book ref|tsm|25}} Other people are able to touch and use Auxiliary while he is manifested as an object,{{book ref|tsm|4}}{{book ref|tsm|5}}{{book ref|tsm|41}} though Nomad retains the ability to summon or dismiss him at any time.{{book ref|tsm|43}}{{book ref|tsm|48}}

{{sidequote|side=left|size=500px|You did that, Nomad. It's not my soul that is cankered, not my oaths that have been broken. You are the one who can't harm anyone. You are the one who can't form a weapon meant only for killing.|Auxiliary explains why Nomad cannot create the form of a weapon{{book ref|tsm|35}}}}

Though Auxiliary himself makes no distinction between manifestations that are weapons or otherwise and is capable of becoming either,{{book ref|tsm|1}}{{book ref|tsm|4}}{{book ref|tsm|35}} by the time the pair arrive on Canticle Nomad's Torment has grown strong enough to prevent him from summoning Auxiliary as a weapon in almost all situations.{{book ref|tsm|1}} Prior to their time on Canticle, Nomad's Radiant oaths partially or entirely overrode that portion of the Dawnshard's corruption of Nomad's [[spiritweb]], allowing him to summon Auxiliary as a weapon.{{book ref|tsm|1}}{{wob ref|16231}}{{wob ref|15596}} This restriction is tied to Nomad's [[Intent]] as he is able to summon Auxiliary in the form of a weapon in emergency situations if he makes a conscious effort to use the form either purely defensively or as a tool.{{book ref|tsm|4}}{{book ref|tsm|30}} Even if he tells himself that he will use the form non-violently, he is not able to do so except in situations that are expressly part of an effort to save others, potentially a remnant of his former oaths overriding the restriction.{{book ref|tsm|5}}{{book ref|tsm|35}}

While in the form of a [[Shardblade]] or [[Shardblade#Radiant Shardblades|Shardspear]] Auxiliary possesses the usual properties of those weapons such as cutting through non-living objects nearly effortlessly including the hull of a [[Scadrian]] [[Scadrian research facility|research vessel]]{{book ref|tsm|41}}{{book ref|tsm|45}} and severing the souls of living beings when cut causing their eyes to burn and smoke.{{book ref|tsm|37}}{{book ref|tsm|42}} These properties make the weapons particularly effective against enemies like the Charred who are used to being able to push through attacks from normal weapons.{{book ref|tsm|42}} Auxiliary can choose to blunt the edge of a manifested blade to prevent the wielder's attacks from being instantly lethal.{{book ref|tsm|42}} Attacks from blunt or bludgeoning weapons do not exhibit the same properties as a bladed weapon.{{book ref|tsm|37}}{{book ref|tsm|42}}

The Shardblade is considered Auxiliary's "truest form" and is the form in which he would appear unbonded at Nomad's side if Nomad were to be killed.{{book ref|tsm|4}}{{book ref|tsm|52}} The blade is between six and six and a half feet long, wavy, ornamented near the hilt, and glitters.{{book ref|tsm|4}}{{book ref|tsm|45}} The head of the Shardspear form has a similar glittering appearance and is etched with Rosharan patterns.{{book ref|tsm|37}}

==== Radiant Powers ====
Auxiliary's bond with Nomad allows the spren to activate and manage a number of powers inside of Nomad. Though Nomad may be able to access these powers on his own, the pair typically delegate Auxiliary to manage the powers so Nomad can focus on the task at hand.

===== Fast Healing =====
Auxiliary can use Nomad's Investiture to instigate the latter's body to rapidly heal.{{book ref|tsm|2}}{{book ref|tsm|18}} The healing effect requires relatively low levels of Investiture, remaining available unless Nomad's stores are almost entirely depleted.{{book ref|tsm|5}}{{book ref|tsm|34}} However, the lower Nomad's Investiture levels are the less efficient and effective the healing capability becomes.{{book ref|tsm|1}} Healing is not an automatic process and Auxiliary can choose to withhold doing so if he is concerned about dropping Nomad's Investiture levels too low. He can also target specific portions of Nomad's body to heal to maximize the use of the limited Investiture.{{book ref|tsm|5}}

As an extension of the healing ability, Auxiliary can adjust Nomad's body to acclimate to a given planet's air pressure.{{book ref|tsm|2}} This adjustment is performed once, usually upon first arriving on a new planet, and is permanent unless later adjusted, remaining in effect even when completely out of Investiture.{{book ref|tsm|2}}{{book ref|tsm|39}} In ultra-low oxygen environments the necessity for breathing can be omitted entirely, the body being sustained by a pressure bubble powered by a small but continuous drain on Investiture.{{book ref|tsm|23}}{{book ref|tsm|28}}

===== Connection =====
{{image|Connection by Nabetse Zitro.jpg|side=right|width=250px|Auxiliary increases Nomad's [[Connection]] with Wit}}

Auxiliary is able to override a portion of Nomad's spiritweb, forming a [[Connection]] with new places and allowing him to understand local languages.{{book ref|tsm|4}}{{book ref|tsm|6}} While Connected in such a way, Nomad's soul believes he was raised on the target planet and the local language replaces his native [[Azir#Language and Writing|Azish]].{{book ref|tsm|6}} Because of this, Nomad typically speaks to Auxiliary in [[Alethkar#Language and Writing|Alethi]] while Connected so as to retain privacy from the locals.{{book ref|tsm|6}} Auxiliary is also able to feel and understand when Nomad becomes naturally Connected to a place on his own.{{book ref|tsm|40}}

Connecting to the planet is typically one of the first things that Auxiliary and Nomad do upon Skipping to a new system.{{book ref|tsm|1}} Forming Connections requires Nomad to have a threshold of 10% Skip capacity (approximately 2,000 BEUs), though once a Connection is formed it is maintained even if Nomad dips below that threshold.{{book ref|tsm|17}} However, Connections are typically not permanent and will slowly fade over time with stronger Connections able to last longer than weaker ones.{{book ref|tsm|10}} Creating the Connection requires several minutes of work during which Auxiliary is generally unavailable.{{book ref|tsm|6}}

Auxiliary is also able to reinforce an existing Connection within Nomad to an individual allowing them to appear as a softly glowing apparition.{{book ref|tsm|10}} This apparition is preceded by a series of physical sensations reminiscent of the [[Highstorm#Centerbeat|centerbeat]] of a [[highstorm]] including a tugging in the gut, a strange warmth, and a slowing or lessening of stormy weather.{{book ref|sa1|35}} Auxiliary is able to reinforce personal Connections seemingly at any distance and it can be done without Nomad's knowledge or consent. As the strength of the Connection wanes, the apparition appears increasingly transparent until it vanishes altogether.{{book ref|tsm|10}}

### Dawnshard
Auxiliary also gained abilities through the Dawnshard held by Nomad or by its partial consumption of his essence.

==== Investiture Absorption ====
Auxiliary is able to absorb Investiture from a wide variety of sources and make it available for Nomad to use,{{book ref|tsm|1}}{{book ref|tsm|5}}{{book ref|tsm|24}} a process Auxiliary thinks of as "recycling" the energy.{{book ref|tsm|4}} Nomad gained this ability due to his holding of a Dawnshard and through him Auxiliary is able to do the same.{{book ref|tsm|4}} Auxiliary is quite adept at estimating how much Investiture a potential source has and if he will be able to absorb it simply from observation,{{book ref|tsm|4}}{{book ref|tsm|24}} though he cannot know for certain until making contact.{{book ref|tsm|6}} Typically, physical contact is necessary in order to draw from a source,{{book ref|tsm|4}} though certain intangible sources such as Invested light can also be absorbed.{{book ref|tsm|5}} To mitigate the potential risks of direct contact with unknown sources of Investiture, Nomad will often employ Auxiliary as an intermediary either by summoning him as a protective piece like a gauntlet or shield{{book ref|tsm|4}}{{book ref|tsm|6}} or incorporating a socket into Auxiliary's form into which Nomad can place the Invested source.{{book ref|tsm|37}}{{book ref|tsm|47}} Sources of Investiture that are particularly strong such as [[Canticle#Sun|Canticle's sun]] however, remain inaccessible as Auxiliary would be destroyed if he opened himself up to absorb it.{{book ref|tsm|2}}{{book ref|tsm|5}} He is not able to draw on the energy of Nomad's Torment at all.{{book ref|tsm|37}}

{{sidequote|side=right|size=500px|Uh, Nomad. I get the feeling that trying to absorb and use Investiture from that is going to be like trying to pick out a snowflake from an avalanche. I…don't think we should let it hit you.|Auxiliary suggests Nomad not get hit by Canticle's sunlight{{book ref|tsm|1}}}}

Auxiliary can detect when a substance Nomad is exposed to is made of or contains Investiture.{{book ref|tsm|2}}{{book ref|tsm|24}} Upon making physical contact, Auxiliary can determine if the source is safe for Nomad to handle directly.{{book ref|tsm|6}} He can also determine or intuit certain properties of the Invested source such as its stability.{{book ref|tsm|6}} This allows him to differentiate sources of Investiture even if they are similar to one another like sunhearts and [[Sunheart#Cinderhearts|cinderhearts]].{{book ref|tsm|6}} He can also feel if a source is made of or contains [[Investiture#Anti-Investiture|anti-Investiture]], though he is not the best at spotting it.{{book ref|tsm|24}}

==== Self Destruction ====
While being consumed by the Dawnshard, Auxiliary is able to selectively protect portions of his own essence to be the last things potentially burned away.{{book ref|tsm|20}} After his first death, Auxiliary is able to voluntarily convert the remaining portions of his essence into Investiture for Nomad to use. He can do so at any time and without Nomad's consent. As with the initial incident, Auxiliary is able to determine what aspects of himself will be consumed and what will remain. It is unclear if this ability is theoretically available to all bonded spren or if it is unique to Auxiliary because of his prior partial-consumption by the Dawnshard.{{book ref|tsm|45}}

### Realmatic and Cosmerological Knowledge
Auxiliary is quite knowledgeable about [[Realmatic Theory]] and about many places in the cosmere. His familiarity with Realmatic Theory includes a deep understanding of Connection,{{book ref|tsm|24}}{{book ref|tsm|40}} the properties of Investiture and its many forms,{{book ref|tsm|24}}{{book ref|tsm|39}} and the power of Intent.{{book ref|tsm|39}} He has also shown an understanding of the properties of souls and corruption{{book ref|tsm|24}} as well as an aptitude for connecting familiar Realmatic concepts to novel ones.{{book ref|tsm|22}}

Due to his extensive travel through the cosmere,{{book ref|tsm|12}} Auxiliary is familiar with many places and cultures including [[Threnody|Threnodite]] culture,{{book ref|tsm|7}} [[Nalthis|Nalthian]] culture,{{book ref|tsm|24}} and some knowledge of [[Dragon (cosmere)|dragons]].{{book ref|tsm|19}} Though he is not as knowledgeable as Nomad on such topics, he is able to follow most of his partner's thinking if given a basic explanation.{{book ref|tsm|21}}

### Connection
Auxiliary can become Connected to places independently of Nomad, allowing him to understand a local language.{{book ref|tsm|2}}{{book ref|tsm|6}} This Connection slowly fades over time,{{book ref|tsm|10}} but he retains some amount of knowledge of the language long after leaving the Connected location.{{book ref|tsm|2}}

### Sensory Perception
{{sidequote|side=left|size=500px|While Aux didn't have a physical shape unless Nomad called him as an object, the spren could use Nomad's body to experience the world. Aux had been keeping watch, even if Nomad hadn't.|Nomad on Auxiliary's use of his senses{{book ref|tsm|13}}}}

Auxiliary is able to perceive the world around him through the senses of his "valet" Nomad.{{book ref|tsm|13}}{{book ref|tsm|34}} Despite sharing the same senses, Auxiliary is able to focus on different things than Nomad, allowing him to call his partner's attention to things he may have otherwise missed.{{book ref|tsm|6}}{{book ref|tsm|13}}{{book ref|tsm|18}} Auxiliary also tends to use Nomad's senses more acutely than Nomad does himself.{{book ref|tsm|21}}{{book ref|tsm|34}} This is especially true when Nomad is unconscious, during which time Auxiliary is still fully able to make use of his partner's senses.{{book ref|tsm|4}} Auxiliary seems to not share Nomad's sense of pain and does not appear to be able to feel pain from his physical manifestations even when it is subject to extreme forces or conditions.{{book ref|tsm|2}}{{book ref|tsm|3}}{{book ref|tsm|4}}

Auxiliary has a great internal sense of direction, being able to direct Nomad's attention to things using a callout of degrees to Nomad's left or right.{{book ref|tsm|13}}{{book ref|tsm|18}}{{book ref|tsm|41}} He also has a very good internal sense of time, being able to track how much time has passed and how much is left until a known deadline with precision.{{book ref|tsm|34}}{{book ref|tsm|45}}

### Investiture Immunity
Sources of Investiture that would cause living beings harm do not seem to affect Auxiliary.{{book ref|tsm|5}} This immunity, which works similarly to the way Shardplate does,{{book ref|tsm|47}} allows him to act as a shield or insulation for others against harmful Investiture.{{book ref|tsm|5}}{{book ref|tsm|48}}{{book ref|tsm|epilogue}} The immunity extends not just to natural sources of Investiture like Canticle's sun, but also to refined and manufactured sources such as a sunheart-powered energy gun.{{book ref|tsm|48}}

## History
### With Szeth
{{sidequote|side=right|size=510px|What's life about, if not growth? I don't like the person I was back on Roshar either, before we knew each other. I like change, Nomad. My kind were too static for too long, particularly we highspren.|Auxiliary reflects on his past{{book ref|tsm|28}}}}

12124 bonded [[Szeth]] at some point before the [[Battle of Thaylen Field]]. During the battle, Szeth swore his Third Ideal and 12124 approved.{{book ref|sa3|121}} Since then, 12124 remained by his side, always judging his worthiness. While Szeth was guarding [[Gavinor]] and [[Dalinar]] on the [[Emul|Emuli]] front, 12124 chose to show himself to him in order to give him direction on his progression through the Ideals. 12124 told him he needed to complete his Fourth Ideal quest soon, and that he needed to rely more on his own judgement rather than that of Dalinar as part of his progress towards this Fifth Ideal.{{book ref|sa4|i|7}}

On the sixth day of the [[Cleansing of Shinovar]], the seventh of the final ten days before the [[contest of champions]], 12124 approached Kaladin. 12124 was filled with guilt and, after witnessing a therapy session with [[Szeth]], requested [[Kaladin]] conduct one with him as well. 12124 explicitly stated that he thinks that [[121]] is the worst. 12124 initially spoke in very formal manner. During therapy 12124 revealed to Kaladin, that highspren also take oaths with their knights. He spoke about the idea of joining dissenters but quickly came to regret mentioning it. He repeatedly said that therapy is easy and not much work, but still stated that he felt relieved after finishing it. He disappeared shortly after saying that.{{book ref|sa5|86}}

When later reflecting on this time of his life, Auxiliary disliked the person he was on Roshar before meeting Nomad.{{book ref|tsm|28}}

### Knight Radiant
Sigzil met the spren in Shadesmar at the [[Iriali]]'s convoy, just after the advent of the [[Night of Sorrows]] and soon after breaking his previous bond to [[Vienta]] and taking up the Dawnshard at the urging of [[Hoid|Wit]]. 12124 had rejected his old name, considering himself a failure, but not yet chosen a new one.{{book ref|tsm|15}}{{book ref|tsm|40}}{{book ref|sa4|10}}{{book ref|tsm|40}}{{book ref|tsm|20}}{{book ref|sa5|147}} When Sigzil started calling himself 'Nomad' is not precisely known. During their first meeting, Nomad told the spren that he wanted to lie low and take the easy road, describing himself as "the sensible one" who "didn't rush headlong into every fight that came your way." A sentiment which Nomad later pretended not to remember.{{book ref|tsm|14}}

The spren and Nomad decided to bond with one another, a decision that neither fully understood the ramifications of in relation to the held Dawnshard.{{book ref|tsm|20}}{{book ref|tsm|29}} The pair spent years bonded together including some amount of time on Roshar; at some point the spren took up the name Auxiliary.{{book ref|tsm|7}}{{book ref|tsm|20}} During this time, Auxiliary and Nomad grew to know each other better. Auxiliary was initially hesitant to show his true self, because of the events he was part of as 12124, but his expressiveness grew over decades spent together.{{book ref|tsm|6}} Early in their relationship, as he had when he was 12124, Auxiliary was prone to giving Nomad moral lectures but over time switched to asking him leading questions instead.{{book ref|tsm|28}} Nomad also experimented with the limits of their abilities together, once attempting to manifest Auxiliary as a clock but failing to do so until he had carefully studied the schematics for one.{{book ref|tsm|5}} At some point, Auxiliary also swore oaths to become a Knight Radiant in his own right.{{book ref|tsm|45}}

### Dawnshard and Death
{{sidequote|side=left|size=420px|I just want to say…I'm glad to be here. Seeing this all with you. Even with the cost, I'm glad to be here.|Auxiliary reflects on their journey{{book ref|tsm|28}}}}

Years after bonding, Nomad unconsciously reached out to the Dawnshard in a moment of need in an effort to find any energy source he could access.{{book ref|tsm|20}} The Dawnshard found Auxiliary, a being of pure Investiture, a convenient source of power to draw upon and consumed nearly all of Auxiliary's essence and soul.{{book ref|tsm|30}}{{book ref|tsm|40}} Auxiliary was able to selectively protect parts of his essence from immediate destruction and salvaged a small fragment of his mind and soul containing his personality.{{book ref|tsm|20}} Likely in response to Wit not informing Nomad of the potential dangers of the Dawnshard, Nomad later gave Auxiliary explicit orders not to contact him.{{book ref|tsm|11}}

### On the Run
Some time after Auxiliary's death, Nomad passed along the Dawnshard to someone else.{{book ref|tsm|21}} When the Night Brigade began hunting them in their pursuit of the Dawnshard, Auxiliary and Nomad began Skipping from planet to planet in an effort to outrun them.{{book ref|tsm|2}}{{book ref|tsm|6}} The pair visited "tens" of planets over the years including a dense jungle planet with complex mathematics and architecture but no concept of the wheel{{book ref|tsm|12}} and likely Threnody as well.{{book ref|tsm|7}}{{book ref|tsm|12}} However, they never went to Nalthis,{{book ref|tsm|13}} likely because of the planet's stricter customs enforcement of their perpendicularity,{{wob ref|14758}} and never returned to Roshar in order to protect people there whom they care about.{{book ref|tsm|6}}{{book ref|tsm|epilogue}} During their travels, people they met seemed to have a universal fascination with Auxiliary and attributed Nomad's powers as a sign of divinity or divine favor.{{book ref|tsm|11}}
Immediately prior to their time on Canticle, Auxiliary and Nomad were on a "cavern planet" and just barely got above the threshold for the Skip, leaving Nomad almost completely drained.{{book ref|tsm|1}}{{book ref|tsm|8}}

### Arrival on Canticle
{{image|SP4_wallpaper_vertical_front.jpg|side=right|width=300px|Nomad holding Auxiliary as a Shardblade on Canticle}}

Auxiliary and Nomad arrived on Canticle and Nomad immediately asked Auxiliary to Connect him to the planet for translation. The spren responded that he could not do so as Nomad was too low on Investiture. The nearby Charred attacked Nomad who called out to Auxiliary to become a weapon. However, Auxiliary reminded Nomad that it was he, not himself that must summon him as a weapon and that it was Nomad's Torment that was preventing him from doing so, a relatively recent development.{{book ref|tsm|1}}

As the dawn sunlight approached, catching the ground alight, Auxiliary observed that the light was potentially Invested but was unsure if they would be able to absorb it. His assessment was very shortly revised however, as the spren realized the overwhelming force of the sunlight and suggested Nomad not let it hit him. Nomad summoned Auxiliary as a crowbar and broke free of the restraints that had been placed on him and then as a hook and chain to latch onto the fleeing [[Hovercycle|hovercycles]]. Just as the deadly sunlight reached Nomad, Auxiliary's chain pulled taut and dragged Nomad behind the cycles.{{book ref|tsm|1}}

As Nomad was pulled across the ground behind the cycles, Auxiliary informed him that his healing was running out and that he should try not to get too beat up. He further added that he was not able to absorb any of the sunlight that had hit them, estimating that opening himself up to do so would have caused his destruction. Auxiliary observed that they were outpacing the sun and the planet must therefore have quite a slow rotation. Several people on the cycle attempted unsuccessfully to pry Auxiliary's chain loose, so they continued to drag Nomad along behind them until they reached the flying city of [[Union]].{{book ref|tsm|2}}

### Union
==== The Arena ====
Once docked at Union, Nomad was hauled upwards as someone pulled up Auxiliary's chain. To prevent revealing that he had access to a shape-changing tool, Nomad told Auxiliary to remain in his chain manifestation. Hearing the Cinder King and other Union officers speak, Auxiliary was able to partially recognize the language as one he was still faintly Connected to but was unsure of which one. However, he was able to translate some of their words as "One Who Escaped the Sun".{{book ref|tsm|2}}

Nomad was brought to Union's arena and was attacked by the Charred, [[Elegy Salvage]]. Nomad, pretending to find something in the mud, summoned Auxiliary to him as a crowbar which he used to defend himself. Knocking Elegy's machete away, Nomad scrambled onto a large metal box with the help of a captive Beaconite named [[Thomos]]. Auxiliary attempted to translate Thomos's words but was unable to make much sense of them. The pair observed the arena from the relative safety of the box while Elegy stalked them on the ground below. Nomad posited that the arena was likely a method for selecting who was to be left out for the sun. Auxiliary agreed, noting that those in the arena who had not been caught by the Charred looked both relieved and sad at once.

Elegy, jumping from one box to another, leaped at Nomad. However, while she was in the air, Nomad summoned Auxiliary as a heavy weight which the Charred crashed into causing all three to fall to the ground. Though Elegy was dazed, Nomad was able to rise and offered a challenge to the Cinder King. As he did so however, the Cinder King activated Nomad's bracers, rendering him unconscious.{{book ref|tsm|3}}

When Nomad awoke, Auxiliary filled him in on what had happened, saying that Nomad had been dragged to the side of the arena and chained to the wall and that Auxiliary himself had turned into a crowbar to avoid suspicion and had been tossed to the side by the Cinder King's underlings. The pair observed the Cinder King use a spear with a glowing ember at its tip to turn one of the captives into a new Charred. Nomad, recognizing the spear as Invested, asked Auxiliary if he thought the spear might be enough to power a Skip off of the planet but Auxiliary replied that he did not think so. The Cinder King next targeted Thomos to become a new Charred but Nomad, reacting almost instinctively and much to Auxiliary's surprise, summoned the spren as a Shardblade and threw him to embed in a post next to the Cinder King's head. Hoping to further distract the Cinder King away from Thomos and focus on himself, Nomad held out his hand and resummoned Auxiliary in a number of forms in quick succession. Taking the bait, the Cinder King walked over to Nomad who continued to shout and antagonize him, hoping that the Cinder King would stab him with the Invested spear which Auxiliary could then absorb. However, the Cinder King did not stab Nomad and instead demanded something of him which Auxiliary interpreted as wanting him to explain how Nomad had summoned the tools. Not receiving an answer, the Cinder King decided to stab Nomad with the spear but before he could do so one of the nearby ships exploded as Beacon began its raid on Union.{{book ref|tsm|4}}

==== Escape ====
As chaos erupted, Nomad summoned Auxiliary as an insulating layer between his skin and the bracers to avoid being frozen during the attack, a construction Auxiliary noted as being particularly unusual. The ship that Nomad was chained to began to take off but was hit by one of Beacon's bombs, throwing him to the ground and pinning him under a large chunk of metal. Auxiliary suggested that Nomad get out of the situation as the sunrise would be rapidly approaching and Auxiliary did not have the strength to protect Nomad from its power. Nomad summoned Auxiliary as a jack and used him to lift the metal enough to flip it to be underneath him. Still chained to the metal, Nomad looked up at the planet's rings and asked Auxiliary if they could absorb Investiture from their reflected sunlight. Auxiliary confirmed that he could, but only slowly.{{book ref|tsm|5}}

As Nomad lay there helpless, [[Rebeke Salvage]] descended to the arena floor with her brother [[Divinity Salvage|Divinity]] and loaded the unconscious Elegy onto their hovercycle. The Cinder King killed Divinity with an energy rifle shot and Nomad proffered Auxiliary in the form of a crowbar to Rebeke, begging for her help in exchange for his own. Realizing the futility of her situation, Rebeke took Auxiliary and attempted to free Nomad but was unable to do so before the Cinder King returned his attention to them and began firing again. Nomad formed Auxiliary into a shield and blocked the incoming energy blasts then called for Rebeke to give him her gun. Nomad used the gun to blast away his remaining restraint, much to Auxiliary's consternation, and ran to the hovercycle. He asked Auxiliary if he had seen how the cycle was operated, hoping to steal it but Auxiliary refused to answer, calling Nomad despicable.{{book ref|tsm|5}}

Rebeke returned to the cycle and, through Auxiliary's limited translation, offered to take Nomad with her if he could use Auxiliary as a shield to block the incoming fire. Nomad agreed and after a brief stop to rescue the nearby Thomos, the group took off away from Union, Nomad having expanded Auxiliary's shield to cover a larger portion of Rebeke's cycle.{{book ref|tsm|5}}
As the group fled Union on the hovercycle, Nomad altered Auxiliary's shield to become transparent, allowing Rebeke to see through it to the approaching Union ships from the driver's seat. The Charred aboard the enemy ships began targeting the cycle's sunheart-engine and Nomad moved to defend it with Auxiliary before leaping onto an enemy ship. Nomad tried and failed to summon Auxiliary as a sword before settling on an oversized wrench and engaging hand to hand, swapping between the wrench and the shield.{{book ref|tsm|6}}

While Nomad fought, Auxiliary noticed that a second enemy ship had nearly reached Rebeke's hovercycle and alerted Nomad. Nomad jumped to the second ship but was just barely able to grab its deck and summoned Auxiliary as a ladder to clamber aboard. Fending off the Charred, Nomad manifested Auxiliary as a crowbar and revealed the ship's sunheart in a hatch in the floor. Auxiliary confirmed to Nomad that this power source was more stable than the Cinder King's spear tips and that Nomad should be able to carry it without getting hurt. Before Nomad could absorb the sunheart's Investiture, the pilot of the enemy ship rolled the craft and Nomad was forced to summon Auxiliary as a magnetic boot to stay on board. Relieved to not have been thrown off, Nomad absorbed the Investiture and Auxiliary asked Nomad if he would like him to Connect Nomad to the planet. With Nomad's confirmation, Auxiliary began the process, informing him that it would take a few minutes to do so. While Auxiliary formed the Connection, Nomad returned to Rebeke's cycle and, with a false stumble to disguise his actions, affixed a rifle taken from the enemy pilot to the underside of the ship using Auxiliary as a specialized claw.{{book ref|tsm|6}} Unbeknownst to Nomad, Auxiliary reinforced Nomad's Connection with Wit while Connecting him to the planet.{{book ref|tsm|10}}

As the group continued their journey toward Beacon, Nomad's new Connection allowed him and Auxiliary to understand Rebeke's conversation with [[Zeal]] over the radio and Auxiliary noticed that, based on their names, these people were Threnodites.{{book ref|tsm|7}}

### Beacon
==== The Greater Good ====
After Nomad met with the [[Greater Good]] on Beacon, he returned to the hovercycle to retrieve the rifle and Auxiliary expressed his annoyance at being left out alone in the rain. Auxiliary inquired about what their next move was and Nomad explained that their best chance of reaching an Investiture source to get off the planet was to find the Scadrian research facility.{{book ref|tsm|9}}{{book ref|tsm|10}}

Nomad returned to the chambers of the Greater Good, now with Auxiliary in tow, and explained to them that he would help them find their mythical [[Refuge of Stone]]. Auxiliary, noting that Nomad did not intend to tell them that they would only find a Scadrian outpost, not a refuge, pointedly called Nomad callous but made no further remark, knowing as Nomad did that this was their best chance to get off the planet. [[Confidence]] asked Nomad about the spirit that accompanied him, referencing Rebeke's earlier observation of Nomad's ability to manifest objects at will. Nomad, unknowing about the [[Chorus]], was surprised that they figured out that Auxiliary was a spirit but rebuffed answering the leader's question, preferring to keep knowledge of the spren to himself.{{book ref|tsm|11}}

{{sidequote|side=left|size=440px|I remember…revelations in light. Transformation.|Auxiliary tries to explain why he Connected Nomad to Wit{{book ref|tsm|11}}}}

As Nomad waited for the leadership of Beacon to discuss the details of their mission to locate the door to the Refuge, he confronted Auxiliary about how the spren had directly disobeyed his orders and contacted Wit. Auxiliary, abashed, admitted that he'd hoped the conversation would lead to a sudden revelation and transformation like had happened in the past when Nomad had sworn the oaths of the Knights Radiant. Nomad replied that most change doesn't happen all at once but in a slow steady slide like how people age. Auxiliary retorted that Nomad no longer aged, implying that he was no longer capable of any kind of change, not just sudden revelation.{{book ref|tsm|11}}

==== The Chase ====
Auxiliary and Nomad joined Rebeke on her hovercycle acting as scouts for Beacon while they searched for the door to the Refuge. Auxiliary, noting the odd rock formations, asked Nomad how they might have formed. Nomad reiterated the question to Rebeke along with his own theory that they formed through volcanic activity followed by rapid cooling. Rebeke confirmed Nomad's hypothesis and Auxiliary was impressed at Nomad's ability to understand something with only a small piece of the information. Auxiliary later questioned how life could really have been so bad under the Cinder King that going off to live in perpetual darkness seemed the preferable option. This prompted Nomad to ask Rebeke about the Cinder King's taxation and Rebeke explained about the Cinder King's human lottery and that the sunhearts are made from living people left to die in the sun.{{book ref|tsm|12}}

The revelation appalled Auxiliary who reacted with sickened disgust. Now having the knowledge of the power sources' origins, Auxiliary postulated that they must become supercharged by the Investiture in the sun as normal people do not have enough inherent Investiture to account for the amount that they were able to earlier absorb. Rebeke told Nomad that the Cinder King was able to forcibly remove people's Investiture in the form of heat and had done so to thousands of people. Auxiliary warned Nomad that he should watch out for someone so highly Invested as they could be seriously dangerous. He was amused, however, at the idea that people on Canticle leach heat from one another during sex. While Nomad was lost in thought, Auxiliary pointed out a Union scout ship in the distance and the group gave chase.{{book ref|tsm|13}}

{{sidequote|side=right|size=480px|Please, no, the knight says. I'm no god. I work for a living.|Auxiliary reject's Nomad's explanation of his godhood{{book ref|tsm|14}}}}

Speeding after the Union scout, Auxiliary worriedly asked Nomad if he knew what he was doing getting into a high speed chase on a planet of expert pilots. They continued the pursuit through Canticle's unusual terrain, nearly getting hit by an erupting geyser of lava to Auxiliary's great shock. Rebeke finally shot the scout with her rifle, ending the chase. Nomad attempted to console Rebeke who was distraught after her first time killing another person by sharing with her the story of his own first experience taking a life. Auxiliary, confused, noted that Rebeke seemed to be comforted by Nomad's words even though what he actually said wasn't comforting at all. Nomad replied that knowing that you're not alone in your experience is comforting in its own right. Rebeke, noticing Nomad's reply to Auxiliary, asked him why he sometimes spoke to himself in a different language and Nomad, reaching for an explanation that he hoped might make sense to her, told Rebeke that he was offering prayers to one of the ancient gods of his realm. Auxiliary, rejecting the premise, retorted to Nomad that he is no god because he "works for a living."{{book ref|tsm|14}}

Rebeke decided that they should tow the downed scout's hovercycle back to Beacon as it would be easier for the Chorus to recycle than harvesting new iron from the [[Canticle#Geography and Ecology|iron fields]]. Auxiliary commented that Rebeke's description of the Chorus sounded interesting, hoping to bait Nomad into his old inquisitive self. Rebeke, despairing, told Nomad that she did not want to become a killer like he was and Auxiliary stepped in before Nomad could reply with a glib response, reminding him that Rebeke was hurting and he should not make it worse. Nomad, ashamed that a creature that was both dead and inhuman was being more empathetic than he was, softened his response. The group radioed to the leaders of Beacon that the scout had managed to get off a message to Union and that they should expect incoming forces shortly. Hunkering down at the direction of the Greater Good to watch the location of the downed Union scout, Auxiliary, Nomad and Rebeke were surprised to find the Cinder King's personal ship arriving unaccompanied.{{book ref|tsm|15}}

### The Cinder King
While Nomad parlayed with the Cinder King aboard his ship, Auxiliary sarcastically joked that the Cinder King seemed normal and well adjusted after hearing his theory about power. Auxiliary commented that he thought the Cinder King might have actually been smart, but revised that estimation when he brought in a captive Rebeke, thus breaking his oath to Nomad. The Cinder King fired his gun at Rebeke but Nomad, anticipating the move, manifested Auxiliary as a metal ball and threw him to intercept the blast.{{book ref|tsm|16}}

Nomad ran to the ship's control room with Rebeke and latched the door shut using Auxiliary as a door guard. After smashing through the windshield, Nomad used Auxiliary as a crowbar to release Rebeke's hovercycle and the group flew off, Rebeke piloting the cycle and Nomad using Auxiliary as a shield to deflect incoming shots. Auxiliary, seeing the Cinder King examine the false key to the Scadrian vessel, conjectured that he had figured out that the Beaconites had replaced his key and were hunting for the door. Reaching beyond the range of the Cinder King's guns, Rebeke and Nomad swapped places on the hovercycle so Nomad could use the radio. However, Rebeke refused to hold on to Nomad, embarrassed about her people's taboo against direct contact. Nomad, uncaring and wanting to keep moving, anchored Rebeke to the cycle using Auxiliary as a modified version of the door blocker they had used earlier.{{book ref|tsm|17}}

The group returned to Beacon to find it under assault by the Cinder King's forces. Auxiliary drew Nomad's attention to one of the Beaconite ships being dogged by a large enemy ship and noted that it was the one housing the Greater Good. After Auxiliary scolded Nomad for briefly considering taking up the Cinder King's offer, Nomad leapt onto the Greater Good's ship and used Auxiliary to break through its front window. The Charred inside turned away from the Greater Good and began to assault Nomad instead. Nomad led the Charred away from the old women but Auxiliary warned him seriously that he was barely keeping Nomad moving with all the hits he was taking.{{book ref|tsm|18}}

### The Southern Corridor
==== Running on Cold Souls ====
After fighting off the Union attackers, the Beaconite leadership met in the Greater Good's ship. They left Nomad lying on the ground which Auxiliary found hilarious, likening him to a sleeping dragon. The leadership discussed their current situation, having been pushed into a southern corridor with the Cinder King's forces patrolling to the north and impassable mountains to the south. Auxiliary was confused why they would not be able to fly over the mountains if they were only 1000 feet tall. Nomad determined that the only way forward was to modify Beacon's engines to fly up and over the mountains and retreated along with Auxiliary to Elegy's old chambers to run tests on the engines and sunhearts.{{book ref|tsm|19}}

During Nomad's tests, Auxiliary acted as a sounding board, asking probing questions for Nomad to explain or test. Auxiliary was particularly interested in Nomad's explanation of the laws of motion and challenged his assertion that all motion was defined by an imbalance of physical forces, reminding him that the mechanisms they used to use to fly together relied on different principals. ==Relationships==
After Auxiliary's death, his capacity for interacting with people other than Nomad becomes extremely limited. As such, most of his "relationships" are disproportionately one-sided and often involve either party thinking or saying things about the other without being able to interact directly.

### Nomad
{{sidequote|side=right|size=540px|Now, now, the knight says. You're not an idiot, Nomad. An idiot is someone without knowledge or ability. You're something else: a person with knowledge or ability who misuses it. That makes you a fool instead.|Auxiliary opines on the nature of Nomad's shortcomings{{book ref|tsm|33}}}}

Auxiliary and Nomad share a Nahel bond and were once both members of the Order of Skybreakers.{{book ref|tsm|40}} Though Nomad has since abandoned his oaths, Auxiliary never did,{{book ref|tsm|45}} leading him to refer to himself as "the knight" or "the hero" and to Nomad as his squire, valet, or servant; typically preceded by one of a number of colorful epithets.{{book ref|tsm|29}}

Auxiliary and Nomad are no longer able to read one another's minds after the spren's death, so Auxiliary has to ask Nomad directly if he is confused by Nomad's actions or plans and Nomad must speak his replies out loud to answer.{{book ref|tsm|30}} To maintain their privacy, Nomad typically speaks to Auxiliary in Alethi so the locals cannot understand even after Connecting to a planet which overrides his native Azish tongue with the local one.{{book ref|tsm|6}} Nomad generally withholds knowledge of Auxiliary from strangers so as to not draw undue attention to himself as well as to maintain a tactical advantage against any potential foes.{{book ref|tsm|2}}{{book ref|tsm|3}} Despite their inability to read one another's minds, Auxiliary is able to interpret Nomad's emotions and has become very good at predicting his responses to things over the years.{{book ref|tsm|15}}{{book ref|tsm|30}} Similarly, despite Auxiliary's monotone, Nomad has become quite adept at interpreting the spren's intentions even without vocal inflection.{{book ref|tsm|28}}{{book ref|tsm|33}}

{{sidequote|side=left|size=500px|The knight gives a long-suffering sigh, but is forced to admit the truth. Nomad is at the very least consistent in his stupidity.|Auxiliary relents to Nomad's questionable decision-making{{book ref|tsm|24}}}}

The pair frequently rib one another with biting jokes at each other's expense.{{book ref|tsm|12}}{{book ref|tsm|14}} Despite this, Nomad considers Auxiliary to be one of his dearest friends{{book ref|tsm|40}} and trusts him implicitly to pick up on details that he misses himself.{{book ref|tsm|34}} Nomad also relies on Auxiliary to act as a sounding board to test the plausibility of his ideas and theories.{{book ref|tsm|20}}{{book ref|tsm|28}}

Auxiliary and Nomad have a long and complex relationship. Upon first meeting one another in Shadesmar, Auxiliary was hesitant to show his true self but his expressiveness grew over the decades they spent together as a bonded pair until the spren's death.{{book ref|tsm|6}}{{book ref|tsm|11}} It is unclear how much responsibility Nomad bears for the incident and how much was the Dawnshard acting of its own volition.{{book ref|tsm|20}} This ambiguity underpins a good deal of the dynamic between the two. 

Nomad, though he recognizes the roles that Wit and the Dawnshard itself played in the situation, blames himself for Auxiliary's death.{{book ref|tsm|20}} Interestingly, Auxiliary seems to agree with this assessment, often needling Nomad about the subject in moments of pique.{{book ref|tsm|4}}{{book ref|tsm|12}}{{book ref|tsm|25}} Despite their apparent agreement about Nomad's culpability, the pair came to very different conclusions about what that means for their lives moving forward. Nomad is stuck in the past, constantly thinking about -or intentionally avoiding thinking about- how much he misses who Auxiliary used to be and his own responsibility for ending that.{{book ref|tsm|4}}{{book ref|tsm|6}}{{book ref|tsm|30}} Auxiliary, by contrast, is focused on the present and frequently encourages Nomad to stop regretting the past{{book ref|tsm|20}} and appreciate the moment.{{book ref|tsm|26}}{{book ref|tsm|33}}{{book ref|tsm|40}} To Nomad's surprise upon learning it, Auxiliary does not regret what he has become and is glad to be on the adventure with Nomad, even with the cost to himself.{{book ref|tsm|28}}

{{sidequote|side=right|size=540px|Sometimes the way you talk makes me think you believe, or can pretend, that you are an entirely different person now. But you aren't. You're still that man. The capacity for what you've become was always there.|Auxiliary tells Nomad he can't abandon his past{{book ref|tsm|28}}}}

Nomad has gone to great lengths to distance himself from his past in an effort to convince himself that he is no longer the same man who made the decisions he regrets so much.{{book ref|tsm|10}} However, Auxiliary abhors this strategy as he feels that the only way to make one's life better is to embrace one's past and thus recognize one's own capacity for change.{{book ref|tsm|28}} To that end, Auxiliary has made it his mission to encourage Nomad to engage in behaviors that remind him of his past self. Sometimes, Auxiliary does this by simply pointing out when something Nomad does on his own is more characteristic of his old ways than the new.{{book ref|tsm|5}}{{book ref|tsm|29}} Other times he openly encourages Nomad to let aspects of his old self shine through such as his scholarship or his [[Worldsingers|Worldsinging]].{{book ref|tsm|15}}{{book ref|tsm|26}} Auxiliary also asks Nomad probing questions about how and why things work like they do and asks Nomad to explain his thinking and reasoning as he knows that the old Nomad loved discovery and the pursuit of knowledge.{{book ref|tsm|12}}{{book ref|tsm|20}}

Auxiliary also considers himself to be Nomad's conscience,{{book ref|tsm|29}} encouraging him to make morally correct decisions and be considerate of people beyond himself.{{book ref|tsm|18}}{{book ref|tsm|24}} He is particularly attentive to how Nomad treats his would-be friends and allies; calling out when Nomad lies to them,{{book ref|tsm|5}}{{book ref|tsm|11}} and asking him to reconsider saying potentially hurtful things or telling them harsh truths without mollification.{{book ref|tsm|15}}{{book ref|tsm|23}} Though Nomad does sometimes feel ashamed that Auxiliary, a being who is neither human nor living, displays more empathy than he does,{{book ref|tsm|15}} he does on occasion disagree with Auxiliary's conception of what being good with people entails, for example that sometimes what people really need is frankness rather than assurances.{{book ref|tsm|23}}

{{sidequote|side=left|size=490px|You're a better man than you pretend to be, even still. Even broken as you are…Zellion. My friend. You're worth saving.|Auxiliary tells Nomad the words he cannot tell himself{{book ref|tsm|45}}}}

Auxiliary's ultimate goal with these efforts is to get Nomad to believe in his oaths again and rekindle them by saying the Immortal Words.{{book ref|tsm|45}} To achieve this he uses many of the same tactics as he does when trying to encourage Nomad to reconnect with his past self including asking leading questions to get him to consider others' plights,{{book ref|tsm|28}}{{book ref|tsm|33}} using humor to nudge him in a certain direction,{{book ref|tsm|11}} and direct confrontation when needed.{{book ref|tsm|11}} Though Nomad is not ultimately able to reignite his oaths, he does finally begin to learn the lessons Auxiliary has been trying to teach him and acts to save people without any prompting.{{book ref|tsm|42}}

Auxiliary cares deeply about Nomad and worries about his physical wellbeing. Though the spren is generally relaxed and jovial, he becomes much more serious when Nomad is low on Investiture and may be in more serious physical harm.{{book ref|tsm|2}}{{book ref|tsm|18}} He freely offers to protect Nomad from feeling physical pain when possible, even when the source of pain would be non-lethal given Nomad's healing abilities.{{book ref|tsm|4}} Auxiliary's concern about Nomad's physical pain is especially heightened when that pain is self-inflicted.{{book ref|tsm|5}} Upon arriving on Canticle, he is even concerned that Nomad might be suicidal,{{book ref|tsm|1}} and becomes extremely frustrated when his partner pursues risky and illogical behaviors.{{book ref|tsm|14}} Auxiliary sometimes acts as Nomad's voice of self-preservation, likely because he doesn't trust in Nomad's own.{{book ref|tsm|24}}

When Nomad is in mortal danger, Auxiliary is willing to sacrifice his remaining essence in order to save his life. Nomad considers this to be absolutely non-negotiable and is willing to push himself to physical extremes to prevent the spren from sacrificing himself.{{book ref|tsm|30}}

### Szeth
When Auxiliary chooses to show himself to Szeth, he kneels to him and moves away from Nightblood. He is reverential to him, indeed, the two have a relationship that resembles master-student much more than the close kinship of other Radiant bonds. Szeth describes the highspren as always judging his worthiness.{{book ref|sa4|i|7}}

### Wit
Auxiliary considers Wit to be an ally with regard to helping Nomad return to better mental composure and is willing to disobey Nomad's explicit orders not to contact Wit to achieve that end.{{book ref|tsm|10}}{{book ref|tsm|11}} At some point in the past, Wit spoke to Aux and taught him about the subtle difference between an idiot and a fool.{{book ref|tsm|33}}

### Elegy
Auxiliary is initially derisive of Elegy, sarcastically calling her "charming" when she invites Nomad to fight her.{{book ref|tsm|25}} Elegy is later able to correctly guess that Nomad is talking to someone in his head similarly to how she hears the Cinder King in her own.{{book ref|tsm|29}} When Elegy probes Nomad about how he can pick and choose his fights, Auxiliary becomes exasperated at Nomad's circumspection and asks why he doesn't just give her the answers to her questions.{{book ref|tsm|29}} As Elegy's questions become more difficult for Nomad to answer, Auxiliary admits that she is better at doing his "job" of moralizing Nomad than he is himself.{{book ref|tsm|29}}

Unlike anyone besides Nomad, Elegy seems to be able to faintly hear and even understand Auxiliary when in close proximity to Nomad.{{book ref|tsm|38}} Perhaps because of this unusual connection, Nomad later offers Elegy the use of Auxiliary as a Shardspear.{{book ref|tsm|41}} Auxiliary is a little concerned with how violently Elegy wields him and suggests to Nomad that he will blunt the tip if she goes too far.{{book ref|tsm|42}} When later wielding Auxiliary as a machete, Elegy considers the weapon to be magnificent in the way it allows her to never stop moving and fighting.{{book ref|tsm|43}}

### Beaconites
==== Zeal ====
Upon observing how Nomad interacts with Auxiliary, Zeal believes that Auxiliary must be a shade.{{book ref|tsm|25}} Auxiliary develops a fondness for Zeal, expressing jubilance upon Zeal's appearance at the Cinder King's vault.{{book ref|tsm|36}}

==== Rebeke ====
Auxiliary finds it is endearing that Rebeke believes Nomad cares about their social customs rather than just trying to understand their [[Invested Art|arcana]] for selfish reasons.{{book ref|tsm|21}}

==== Contemplation ====
Contemplation believes Auxiliary is a manifestation of [[Adonalsium|Adonalsium's]] plan and will in the cosmere.{{book ref|tsm|47}}

### Cinder King
The Cinder King first encounters Auxiliary when Nomad interrupts the Cinder King's creation of new Charred at the Union arena. He is initially confused by Auxiliary as he doesn't understand what he is seeing but becomes intrigued by the spren and sees him as a potential source of power to solidify his rule over the planet.{{book ref|tsm|4}}{{book ref|tsm|49}} Conversely, Auxiliary is sickened and disgusted by the idea that sunhearts are made from living people whose lives the Cinder King collects as a "tax".{{book ref|tsm|12}}{{book ref|tsm|13}} Auxiliary also considers the Cinder King to be extremely dangerous, warning Nomad that a person who has leached Investiture from thousands of people would be highly Invested.{{book ref|tsm|13}}

As with other human behavior, Auxiliary has a hard time understanding the motivations that would result in such a stark difference in the Cinder King's attitude between their first and second meetings. Upon hearing the Cinder King express his background and twisted philosophy of power, Auxiliary muses that he does not consider him to be normal, well adjusted, or reasonable. However, Auxiliary does initially consider the Cinder King to potentially be smart, a sentiment that is rapidly erased after the Cinder King breaks his oath to Nomad.{{book ref|tsm|16}}

## Trivia
* Auxiliary's dialogue is rendered in italics and without quotation marks, the same way that a POV character's internal monologue is denoted.
* It is unknown if Nomad will still be able to Connect to other planets in the future without Auxiliary's help.{{wob ref|16351}}
* Auxiliary was [[Bibliography/Audiobooks|William DeMeritt's]] favorite character to narrate in the audiobook.{{ref|name=Book Club YT}}
* The voice DeMeritt uses for Auxiliary in the audiobook was a collaboration between DeMeritt and the [[Dragonsteel Entertainment|Dragonsteel]] team. DeMeritt was initially concerned that using a true monotone as the character is described would quickly become grating in the audio medium and considered which accents in English lend themselves to the monotone feeling without being truly monotonous. Wanting to avoid the sci-fi/fantasy cliche of some kind of UK-based accent, DeMeritt asked the Dragonsteel team if he could use a modified version of the [[Wikipedia:Wakanda|Wakandan]] accent (initially developed by [https://www.imdb.com/name/nm0570159/ Beth McGuire] -with whom DeMeritt apprenticed in graduate school- based on the [[Wikipedia:Xhosa language|Xhosa]] accent) as it places equal stress on every syllable evoking a monotone.{{ref|name=Book Club YT}}
* The Highspren plushie sold by Dragonsteel as part of the [[Dragonsteel Entertainment#Words of Radiance 10th Anniversary Leatherbound BackerKit (2024)|Words of Radiance Leatherbound crowdfunding campaign]] is modeled after what Auxiliary would have looked like before his death.{{wob ref|16501}}
* Auxiliary's original name, 12124, can be read as A-U-X, as 'A' is the 1st letter of the Latin alphabet, 'U' the 21st, and 'X' the 24th.
