A **spikeway** is a path on [[Scadrial]] made up of metal anchoring points placed at regular intervals. [[Coinshot]]s and [[Allomancy|Mistborn]] can use these points to Push themselves through the air continuously and travel very rapidly from place to place.{{book ref|mb2|52}}

==Characteristics==
A spikeway typically consists of pairs of metal spikes or bars driven into the ground in a straight line between two locations. Additional metal anchoring points are placed to the sides of the line to allow the Allomancer to make small course corrections and maintain their bearing.{{book ref|mb1|13}} Traveling by spikeway uses an Allomancer's momentum and is far more efficient than [[pewter]]-dragging.{{book ref|mb2|54}} Spikeways are only useful to people that can utilize the Allomantic properties of [[steel]] including Mistborn, Coinshots, and some [[Hemalurgy|Hemalurgists]]; [[Lurcher]]s would be able to see a spikeway but unable to use it effectively.

Spikeways are uncommon, as they are difficult to set up. They are only useful in specialized situations where other metal anchoring points would be unavailable or a direct route through the air is needed; they are not often set up along established transportation routes.{{book ref|mb2|52}} They are not seen after the [[Catacendre]].

==History==
[[Kelsier]] used a spikeway made of small bronze bars to travel between the cities of [[Fellise]] and [[Luthadel]] during his time plotting to overthrow the [[Lord Ruler]].{{book ref|mb1|13}}{{book ref|mb1|19}} The spikeway, which was placed by an unknown party, reduced the travel time between the two cities from an hour by carriage to under ten minutes.{{book ref|mb2|52}} This was half the time it would have taken on horseback, the fastest conventional method of travel at the time.{{book ref|mb1|13}} [[Vin]] followed Kelsier one night and discovered the spikeway;{{book ref|mb1|13}} she was excited to learn about them, but was ultimately left frustrated as she was forced to continue traveling the route by carriage due to her disguise as Valette Renoux.{{book ref|mb1|20}}
===Vin's Horseshoe Trick===
Vin taught herself to use a combination of [[Iron]]pulls and Steelpushes to manipulate horseshoes, Pushing them forward and then Pulling them behind her to create a sort of moving spikeway.{{book ref|mb2|52}} She was the first Allomancer to ever figure out this trick,{{wob ref|8114}} and she was able to learn it very quickly because of her inherent skill and the advanced training that she had received from Kelsier.{{book ref|mb3|51}}{{wob ref|5435}} Once she mastered manipulating the horseshoes, she often used them to travel and looked like a "cyclone" of metal while doing so.{{book ref|mb3|3}}{{book ref|mb3|51}} [[Elend]] and [[Marsh]] were able to mimic Vin's impromptu spikeways using coins.{{book ref|mb3|51}}{{book ref|mb3|69}} Elend was rather frightened of the danger posed by the much heavier horseshoes, although he eventually learned to use them.{{book ref|mb3|51}}{{book ref|mb3|74}}

==Trivia==
*Brandon invented the spikeway as both as useful narrative tool (to get Kelsier back and forth between Fellise and Luthadel quickly) and an interesting application of Allomancy.{{wob ref|2334}} He would have liked to include another spikeway in *[[The Well of Ascension]]*, but did not have room to include one.{{wob ref|5435}}
