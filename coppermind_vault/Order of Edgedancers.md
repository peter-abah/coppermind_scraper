The **Order of Edgedancers** is an order of the [[Knights Radiant]] on Roshar.{{epigraph ref|sa2|46}}

The Edgedancers are [[Surgebinder]]s who use the [[Surge]]s of Abrasion and Progression,{{book ref|sa2|part=ars}} and they are associated with the diamond [[polestone]]. They bond [[cultivationspren]].

## Philosophy
{{sidequote
|I will remember.
|The core philosophy of the Edgedancers{{rpg ref|slhb|140}}
|side=right|size=250px
}}

Edgedancers generally subscribe to a philosophy of remembering the ordinary people of the world.{{wob ref|13775}}{{book ref|sa2|i|9}} The order considers it their solemn duty to remember that the people are the ones they truly serve.{{rpg ref|slhb|140}} Edgedancers tend to ignore high society.{{rpg ref|slhb|140}} They even aspire to remember and hear their enemies, as their stories could provide insight.{{book ref|sa4|36}} 

Edgedancers see it as their duty to care for the common folk and improve their daily lives and are similar to Good Samaritans.{{wob ref|684}}{{wob ref|16456}} They generally avoid battle to focus on healing, rescuing, and triaging.{{rpg ref|slhb|140}} They will also heal their enemies, as they are also people who others ignore. They tend to attract religious people and leaders and those who advocate for ordinary people.{{wob ref|13775}}

## Ideals of the Edgedancers
The Ideals of the Knights Radiant, also known as the [[Immortal Words]], are a set of rules by which the Radiants live. The First Ideal is shared by all of the orders, and is used as a motto for the Knights Radiant as a whole. Each of the orders then have an additional four Ideals that are unique to that order.{{book ref|sa1|59}} 

The Ideals of the Order of Edgedancers all focus on remembering and advocating for ordinary people.{{wob ref|13775}}{{rpg ref|slhb|140}}

### The First Ideal
{{quote
|Life before death, strength before weakness, journey before destination.
|The First Ideal of the Knights Radiant{{book ref|sa1|59}}
}}

The Edgedancers share the first ideal with all other orders of Radiants, which serves as the motto of the organization as a whole.{{book ref|sa1|59}} The words are broadly interpreted to state the value of life, the importance of strength and service and the importance of the methods by which one accomplishes a task.

### The Second Ideal
{{quote
|I will remember those who have been forgotten.
|The Second Ideal of the Edgedancers, as sworn by [[Lift]]{{book ref|sa2|i|9}}
}}

This oath is centered around remembering and bearing witness to people's lives.{{rpg ref|slhb|141}} This applies to both allies and enemies.

### The Third Ideal
{{quote
|I will listen to those who have been ignored.
|The Third Ideal of the Edgedancers, as sworn by [[Lift]]{{book ref|sa2.5|19}}
}}

This oath is focused on actively looking for people in distress and lending them an ear.{{rpg ref|slhb|141}} It must be reached before an Edgedancer can manifest their cultivationspren as a [[Shardblade]].{{book ref|sa2.5|19}}

### The Fourth Ideal
The specific words of this Ideal are individualized, but the overarching theme is advocacy, carrying normal people's concerns to those with power to address them.{{rpg ref|slhb|141}} Upon achieving this Ideal, an Edgedancer gains their [[Shardplate]], which is formed of [[lifespren]].{{rpg ref|slhb|144}}{{rpg ref|slhb|146}}

## Abilities
{{image|WOKLB - KS - Edgedancers by Steve Argyle.jpeg|width=250px|side=right}}
{{quote
|When [[Simol]] was informed of the arrival of the Edgedancers, a concealed consternation and terror, as is common in such cases, fell upon him; although they were not the most demanding of orders, their graceful, limber movements hid a deadliness that was, by this time, quite renowned; also, they were the most articulate and refined of the Radiants.
|[[Words of Radiance (in-world)|*Words of Radiance*]], chapter 20, page 12{{epigraph ref|sa2|46}}
}}

The Edgedancers use the Surges of **Abrasion** and **Progression**. [[Lift]]'s ability to metabolize food into [[Lights#Lifelight|Lifelight]] is a unique exception relating to the [[Old Magic]] and not a standard ability of Edgedancers.{{wob ref|11796}}

### Abrasion
[[Surgebinding#Abrasion|Abrasion]] grants them the ability to manipulate friction.{{book ref|sa2|part=ars}} They can apply this in different ways. In close range combat, this ability can make them hard to be stopped or grabbed. When used to decrease wind friction and friction on feet, they can travel long distances at considerable speeds, though keeping balance without friction requires a lot of practicing. They have been described, poetically, as being able "to ride the thinnest rope at speed, dance across rooftops, and move through a battlefield like a ribbon on the wind."{{book ref|sa2|i|9}} While the most frequently seen use of abrasion focuses on reducing friction to move with increased fluidity and speed, edgedancers can also increase the friction between parts of their body and the environment, allowing them to stick more strongly to surfaces and increase the force with which they push off of surfaces as they slide.{{book ref|sa5|i|13}} Increasing or more subtly manipulating friction may be a more advanced use of the Surge of Abrasion than simply canceling out friction entirely, requiring additional experience or higher level oaths.{{wob ref|1660}}

### Progression
[[Surgebinding#Progression|Progression]] allows them to make plants or other organic objects grow much faster than normal.{{book ref|sa2|part=ars}} They can also heal, and although the extent of this ability is unknown, it seems that the wielder is even able to heal others from wounds suffered from Shardblades.

### Shardplate Abilities
Edgedancers' [[Shardplate]] is formed of [[lifespren]].{{wob ref|16808}}{{rpg ref|slhb|146}}

### The Ancient Radiants
The Edgedancers considered it their solemn duty to remember that they were servants of the people, and saw it as their duty to care for the ordinary people of the world. Before the [[Day of Recreance]], the Edgedancers often had permanent assignments to live in towns across Roshar, providing healing with the Surge of Progression and otherwise assisting the community.{{wob ref|13775}}{{rpg ref|slhb|142}} When the Radiants decided to abandon [[Urithiru]], the Edgedancers were too busy relocating the city's servants and farmers to send anyone to record a message in the [[Urithiru gem archive|gem archive]] the other Radiants left behind.{{epigraph ref|sa3|72}} Despite their lack of interest in high society, they were often the most graceful and refined of the Radiants.{{epigraph ref|sa2|46}}{{wob ref|13775}}{{rpg ref|slhb|142}} Some felt that the Edgedancers ignored important things too much and considered them small-minded.{{book ref|sa2|i|9}}{{rpg ref|slhb|142}}

Though they became renowned for their deadliness,{{epigraph ref|sa2|46}} when forced into battle, the Edgedancers preferred to function as medics or rescue trapped allies.{{wob ref|13775}} When they fought, however, they were considered some of the most graceful fighters, and they occasionally functioned as scouts of joined a special forces team with Windrunners and Skybreakers.{{epigraph ref|sa2|46}}{{book ref|sa2|i|9}}{{wob ref|13775}}

The Edgedancers were generally the most religious of the Radiants.{{wob ref|13775}}

### The Refounded Edgedancers
The first known [[cultivationspren]] to initiate a [[Nahel bond]] since the Recreance was [[Wyndle]], who bonded [[Lift]].{{book ref|sa2|i|9}} Since then, many more Edgedancers have been created, and all known Edgedancers serve the Coalition of Monarchs.{{rpg ref|slhb|142}}
