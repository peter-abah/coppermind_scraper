## Known Death Rattles
### Common Interpretations

> I stand before him, above the world itself, and he speaks the truth. The Desolation is near … The Everstorm. The Night of Sorrows.
> — Observed circa . Subject was King [[Taravangian]]'s mother.
 
:::Refers to [[Honor]] telling [[Dalinar]] about the Everstorm and the True Desolation in the visions. Also may refer to [[Gavilar]] before a map of Roshar speaking of his experience with the Honor's visions to Taravangian. This would make the point of view that Taravangian's mother sees from Taravangian himself, and explains Taravangian's reaction to Gavilar's words. 

> The love of men is a frigid thing, a mountain stream only three steps from the ice. We are his. Oh Stormfather... we are his. It is but a thousand days, and the Everstorm comes.
> — Collected on , 31 seconds before death, by the Silent Gatherers. Subject was a darkeyed pregnant woman of middle years. The child did not survive.

:::This rattle came exactly 1227 days before the advent of the Everstorm. It is possible that these rattles are less than fully accurate, or that whoever gave this death rattle valued poetry over technically accurate detail or, given the specific phrasing, that this was perhaps a foreshadowing to a time 227 days in the future when someone else would prophesy that it would be 1000 days until the Everstorm.

> A man stood on a cliffside and watched his homeland fall into dust. The waters surged beneath, so far beneath. And he heard a child crying. They were his own tears.
> — Collected on , 30 seconds pre-death, by the Silent Gatherers. Subject was a cobbler of some renown.

:::Refers to [[Dalinar]] remembering his vision of the destruction of [[Kholinar]] with [[Nohadon]]. The child is [[Gavinor]] during the [[contest of champions]].
:::The title of Chapter 142 of [[Wind and Truth]].

> I'm dying, aren't I? Healer, why do you take my blood? Who is that beside you, with his head of lines? I can see a distant sun, dark and cold, shining in a black sky.
> — Collected on , 11 seconds pre-death, by the Silent Gatherers.

:::The description of the sun and sky, as well as the appearance of a [[Cryptic]], indicate the speaker here was viewing [[Shadesmar]]. The subject also apparently retained some consciousness while dying, evidenced by them asking the Silent Gatherers "Healer, why do you take my blood?" In light of those conditions, the speaker may not have actually been affected by Moelach.

> I have seen the end, and have heard it named. The Night of Sorrows, the True Desolation. The Everstorm.
> — Collected on , 15 seconds pre-death, by the Silent Gatherers. Subject was a darkeyed youth of unknown origin.

:::Likely refers to the true Everstorm, which covers all of Roshar except Urithiru and Azir, leaving it in eternal night.

> Victory! We stand atop the mount! We scatter them before us! Their homes become our dens, their lands are now our farms! And they shall burn, as we once did, in a place that is hollow and forlorn.
> — Collected on , 18 seconds pre-death, by the Silent Gatherers. Subject was a lighteyed spinster of the eighth dahn.

:::May refer to how the humans took Roshar from the singers during the First Desolation or a Fused victory over humans.
:::The title of Chapter 127 of [[Wind and Truth]].

> Ten people, with Shardblades alight, standing before a wall of black and white and red.
> — Collected on , 12 seconds pre-death, by the Silent Gatherers. Subject was an ardent member of the Silent Gatherers, overheard during his last moments.

:::Refers to the ten [[Unoathed]] who fought for the throne room of Azimir to protect it from the invading Singer army.
:::The title of Chapter 136 of [[Wind and Truth]].

> Three of sixteen ruled, but now the Broken One reigns.
> — Collected on , 84 seconds pre-death, by the Silent Gatherers. Subject was a cutpurse with the wasting sickness, of partial Iriali descent.

:::Most likely refers to [[Taravangian]] becoming [[Retribution]]. Honor, Cultivation, and Odium, the three [[Shards]] of Roshar, were replaced by only one when Honor and Odium merged and Cultivation left the system.

> He must pick it up, the fallen title! The tower, the crown, and the spear!
> — Collected on , 8 seconds pre-death, by the Silent Gatherers. Subject was a prostitute of unknown background.

:::Likely refers to [[Kaladin]] replacing [[Jezrien]] as Herald of Kings. The tower and the crown refers to the cape [[Dalinar Kholin]] gave to him, which displayed the Glyphpair of the Kholin house that includes both images. The spear refers to the Honorblade he was granted when he became a Herald, which took the shape of a spear. 
:::The title of Chapter 144 of [[Wind and Truth]].

> The burdens of nine become mine. Why must I carry the madness of them all? Oh, Almighty, release me.
> — Observed on , collected secondhand and later reported to the Silent Gatherers. Subject was a wealthy lighteyes.

:::This rattle appears to be from the perspective of [[Ishar]], who took the pain of the nine [[Heralds]], including himself, who abandoned [[Talenel]], the only Herald to return to [[Braize]] at the end of the [[Last Desolation]].
:::The title of Chapter 138 of [[Wind and Truth]].

> A woman sits and scratches out her own eyes. Daughter of kings and winds, the vandal.
> — Collected on , 73 seconds pre-death, by the Silent Gatherers. Subject was a beggar of some renown, known for his elegant songs.

:::This rattle likely refers to the Herald [[Shalash]], the daughter of the Herald [[Jezrien]], Herald of Kings and patron of the [[Order of Windrunners]]. Shalash has defaced depictions of herself by scratching her face out of paintings or by destroying statues depicting her.

> Light grows so distant. The storm never stops. I am broken, and all around me have died. I weep for the end of all things. He has won. Oh, he has beaten us.
> — Collected on , 16 seconds pre-death, by the Silent Gatherers. Subject was a Thaylen sailor.

:::Likely refers to the ascension of [[Retribution]] and the birth of the true Everstorm.
:::The title of Chapter 145 of [[Wind and Truth]].

> I hold the suckling child in my hands, a knife at his throat, and know that all who live wish me to let the blade slip. Spill its blood upon the ground, over my hands, and with it gain us further breath to draw.
> — Collected on , 23 seconds pre-death, by the Silent Gatherers. Subject was a darkeyed youth of sixteen years. Sample is of particular note.

:::Refers to Dalinar's choice of whether to kill or spare [[Gavinor]].
:::The title of chapter 137 of [[Wind and Truth]].

> Re-Shephir, the Midnight Mother, giving birth to abominations with her essence so dark, so terrible, so consuming. She is here! She watches me die!
> — Collected on , 8 seconds pre-death, by the Silent Gatherers. Subject was a darkeyed dock-worker in his forties, father of three.

:::This rattle refers to the Unmade [[Re-Shephir]] and her [[Midnight Essence]].

> Above the final void I hang, friends behind, friends before. The feast I must drink clings to their faces, and the words I must speak spark in my mind. The old oaths will be spoken anew.
> — Collected on , 45 seconds pre-death, by the Silent Gatherers. Subject was a lighteyed child of five years. Diction improved remarkably when giving sample.

:::This rattle appears to be from the perspective of Kaladin during the [[Battle of the Tower]], moments before he swears the [[Knights Radiant/Immortal Words#Ideals of the Windrunners|Second Ideal of the Windrunners]]. The "final void" is a chasm over which he is jumping. The "friends behind, friends before" are the members of Bridge Four behind him and the army of [[Dalinar Kholin]] trapped by Parshendi before him. The "feast I must drink" is Stormlight stored in the gemstones knotted into the malen Parshendi's beards. The "words I must speak" is the Second Ideal. The "old oaths" are the [[Immortal Words]].

> In the storm I awaken, falling, spinning, grieving.
> — Collected on , 13 seconds pre-death, by the Silent Gatherers. Subject was a city guardsman.

:::This rattle likely refer to Kaladin swearing the [[Knights Radiant/Immortal Words#Ideals of the Windrunners|Fourth Ideal of the Windrunners]] while falling in the [[highstorm]] towards the end of [[Raboniel]]'s occupation of [[Urithiru]]. The events of this Death Rattle had not occurred as of the [[Battle of Thaylen Field]].

> I wish to sleep. I know now why you do what you do, and I hate you for it. I will not speak of the truths I see.
> — Collected on , 142 seconds pre-death, by the Silent Gatherers. Subject was a Shin sailor, left behind by his crew, reportedly for bringing them ill luck. Sample largely useless.

:::It appears this subject is aware that the Silent Gatherers are killing people in order to obtain their death rattles and refuses to share his visions.

> They come from the pit, two dead men, a heart in their hands, and I know that I have seen true glory.
> — Collected on , 13 seconds pre-death, by the Silent Gatherers. Subject was a rickshaw puller.

:::This rattle appears to refer to Kaladin and [[Shallan Davar|Shallan]] returning to the warcamps at the [[Shattered Plains]] following their fall into the chasms. The "pit" is a reference to the chasms and the "two dead men" are Kaladin and Shallan, presumed to be dead after the fall. The "heart" is an emerald [[gemheart]] they harvested from a [[chasmfiend]] they encountered and killed on their return journey.
:::The title of chapter 75 of [[Words of Radiance]].

> I see them. They are the rocks. They are the vengeful spirits. Eyes of red.
> — Collected on , 8 seconds pre-death, by the Silent Gatherers. Subject was a darkeyed young woman of fifteen who was reportedly mentally unstable since childhood.

:::This rattle refers to the awakening of [[Thunderclast]]s.

> They named it the Final Desolation, but they lied. Our gods lied. Oh, how they lied. The Everstorm comes. I hear its whispers, see its stormwall, know its heart.
> — Collected on , 8 seconds pre-death, by the Silent Gatherers. Subject was an Azish itinerant worker. Sample of particular note.

:::When all Heralds, except Taln, broke the Oathpact after Aharietiam they lied and told humanity that the Voidbringers finally were defeated.

> All is withdrawn for me. I stand against the one who saved my life. I protect the one who killed my promises. I raise my hand. The storm responds.
> — Collected on , 18 seconds pre-death, by the Silent Gatherers. Subject was a darkeyed mother of four in her sixty-second year.

:::This rattle appears to refer to Kaladin saving [[Elhokar]] from [[Moash]], an event during which Kaladin swears the [[Knights Radiant/Immortal Words#Ideals of the Windrunners|Third Ideal of the Windrunners]]. "The one who killed my promises" likely refers to the fact that Elhokar, through a string of related events, was responsible for Kaladin being unable to keep his promise to protect Tien in battle. "I raise my hand. The storm responds." refers to the fact that Kaladin's words are accepted and Syl returns to him as a Shardblade.

> The day was ours, but they took it. Stormfather! You cannot have it. The day is ours. They come, rasping, and the lights fail. Oh, Stormfather!
> — Collected circa  by Taravangian. Subject was a young boy.

:::'Rasping' may refer to the rythms of the Fused. 'The lights fail' refers to the [[Night of Sorrows]] and the loss of [[Stormlight]].

> So the night will reign, for the choice of honor is life...
> — Observed circa  by Taravangian. Subject was King [[Valam]] of [[Jah Keved]].

:::May refer to [[Renarin Kholin]] and [[Rlain]] choosing to release [[Ba-Ado-Mishram]]. However, an alternate explanation may be that it refers to the choice of [[Dalinar]] (as Honor) to relinquish his power to [[Taravangian]], resulting in the [[Night of Sorrows]].
:::The title of Chapter 135 of [[Wind and Truth]].

> It comes! The Night of Sorrows! I stand on the precipice of dawn and watch it advance, consuming all light, all life, all hope! *IT COMES!*
> — Observed on  by [[Deti]], Sigzil's Windrunner squire.

:::Likely refers to the true Everstorm, which covers all of Roshar except Urithiru and Azir, leaving it in eternal night.

> I die! The Scholar with a Spear! I die by the hands of a friend! My spren screams in death, and I know that I have failed to lead! I am no captain! I am nothing! Vyre strikes me, and my eyes burn!
> — Observed on . Subject was [[Leyten]], a member of Bridge Four.

:::Refers to the death of [[Sigzil]] and [[Vienta]] at the hands of [[Moash]]. Prevented by Sigzil renouncing his oaths.

### Unknown Meanings

> I climb! I climb the wall of grief toward the light, locked away above! I climb, the weight of my darkened twin on my back, and seek the captive! The light I love! I ... Storms ... the light I *love*!
> — Observed circa . Subject was [[Tuko-son-Tuko]], the Windrunner [[Honorbearer]].

:::May refer to the freeing of [[Ba-Ado-Mishram]]. "I climb the wall of grief" may refer to [[Shallan]] coming to terms with her mother's death. "my darkened twin" could refer to Formless, who Shallan struggles with during the journey, mentally carrying her "on my back". "the captive" may refer to Ba-Ado-Mishram. "The light I love!" could refer to Shallan's husband [[Adolin]], whose name means "born unto light".

> You've killed me. Bastards, you've killed me! While the sun is still hot, I die!
> — Collected on , 10 seconds pre-death, by the Silent Gatherers. Subject was a darkeyed soldier thirty-one years of age. Sample is considered questionable.

:::May refer to the fact that the subject died before the advent of the [[Night of Sorrows]], which blotted out the sun across most of Roshar.

> Ten orders. We were loved, once. Why have you forsaken us, Almighty! Shard of my soul, where have you gone?
> — Collected on , 5 seconds pre-death, by the Silent Gatherers. Subject was a lighteyed woman in her third decade.

:::Likely refers to the ten orders of the [[Knights Radiant]]. May be from the perspective of [[Garith]], referring to the events surrounding the capture of [[Ba-Ado-Mishram]]. "Shard of my soul" could refer to [[Shmone]] (Garith's singer lover) having her [[Identity]] ripped away in front of his eyes, or to his spren companion, whom he unintentionally killed as part of the Recreance.

> I'm cold. Mother, I'm cold. Mother? Why can I still hear the rain? Will it stop?
> — Collected on , 32 seconds pre-death, by the Silent Gatherers. Subject was a lighteyed female child, approximately six years old.

:::May refer to the true Everstorm and the [[Night of Sorrows]].

> They are aflame. They burn. They bring the darkness when they come, and so all you can see is that their skin is aflame. Burn, burn, burn...
> — Collected on , 21 seconds pre-death, by the Silent Gatherers. Subject was a baker's apprentice.

:::May be a description of the humans when they first arrived on Roshar, covered in ash and burns from their experimentation with [[Surgebinding]] or of the singers, who, as the Voidbringers, are associated in legend with fire, ash, char, and terrible, burned skin.

> He watches! The black piper in the night. He holds us in his palm... playing a tune that no man can hear!
> — Observed circa . Subject was [[Cenn]], a member of [[Kaladin]]'s squad in Brightlord [[Meridas Amaram]]'s army.

:::This is not a reference to [[Hoid]]. May be a reference to Kaladin.

> I'm standing over the body of a brother. I'm weeping. Is that his blood or mine? What have we done?
> — Collected on , 107 seconds pre-death, by the Silent Gatherers. Subject was an out-of-work Veden sailor.

:::May refer to the assassination of king [[Gavilar Kholin]].

:::May refer to the potential clash between Honor and Odium during the Contest of Champions.
