The **Elendel Supremacy Bill** was a law enacted by the [[Elendel]] Senate in the year {{date|348|FA}} that made the Elendel governor the supreme authority over all other cities in the [[Elendel Basin]] and the [[Roughs]].{{book ref|mb7|2}}

## History
### Background
Supporters of the bill hoped that giving the Basin a single leader would help unite the region and strengthen their hand in dealing with the [[Southern Scadrian]]s. The outer cities were strongly opposed to the bill, as they could not vote in elections for the governor of Elendel.{{book ref|mb7|2}}

### Legislative Proceedings
The Senate debated the bill for several days prior to voting on it. The mayors of the outer cities, who have seats set aside for them to observe Senate proceedings, all boycotted the debate; Admiral [[Jonnes]], the [[Malwish]] ambassador, attended the debate and was given one of their seats.{{book ref|mb7|2}} Vice Governor [[Adawathwyn]] opened the debate and argued in support of the bill, claiming that the Southern Scadrians would soon go to war with the Basin and that the Basin would need to be united behind a strong leader when that happened. The Senate recessed sometime after the Vice Governor's speech and Admiral Jonnes did not return when the day's session resumed.{{file ref|The Two Seasons broadsheet.jpg|*The Two Seasons* broadsheet for Marewill 19, 348 PC}}

[[Waxillium Ladrian]], an opponent of the bill, negotiated to be the last senator to speak on the bill, hoping that speaking right before the vote would give him the best chance to persuade senators who had yet to make up their mind. In his speech, he warned that passing the bill would likely lead to a civil war in the Basin and presented letters from sixty politicians from the outer cities who hoped to find a compromise that would avoid such a conflict. Instead of elevating the governor of Elendel, Wax proposed to create a national assembly that would include representation from the outer cities and to have that assembly pick the Basin's supreme official.{{book ref|mb7|2}}

When the dockworkers' senator, [[Dupon Melstrom]], interrupted Wax to argue that the outer cities should be treated as subordinate to Elendel, Wax read out a letter proving that Houses [[House Hasting|Hasting]] and [[House Erikell|Erikell]] had paid Melstrom to support the bill. Wax then moved to hold impropriety hearings to investigate the matter and to postpone voting on the bill until the hearings were complete. Governor [[Varlance]] instead proposed holding the hearings after the vote, but requiring Melstrom, as well as the Hastings and Erikell senators, to sit out the vote, which the Senate agreed to. After a final plea to reject the bill from Wax, the Senate proceeded to vote and passed the bill by a vote of 122&ndash;118, with several senators who Wax had hoped to persuade voting for the bill.{{book ref|mb7|2}}

### After Passage
Shortly after the bill passed, Elendel received word that the Southern Scadrians had united into the [[Malwish Consortium]], which [[Steris Harms]] worried would make more senators see the bill as necessary.{{book ref|mb7|9}}

The law was presumably repealed as part of the creation of a new national assembly for all of the cities of the [[Elendel Basin]] some time around {{date|350|FA}}.{{book ref|mb7|epilogue|7}}
