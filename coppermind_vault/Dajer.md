**Dajer** is a Colonel in the [[Malwish Empire]].{{book ref|iote|36}} He leads an expedition of Scadrian military engineers to locate the [[perpendicularity]] in the [[Cognitive Realm#Drominad subastral|Drominad Subastral]] in order to secure it for Malwish use.{{book ref|iote|38}}

## Appearance and Personality
Dajer is a red-haired man, and he wears a traditional Malwish mask.{{book ref|iote|36}} Due to an injury he suffered in his past, his mask includes an oxygen tube that runs to his nose, providing him with the air he needs to function normally.

Dajer is ruthless and determined. He is an expert at manuevering conversations {{book ref|iote|38}} and exercises his authority with exactness. Despite his skill in manipulation, he underestimates those he believes to be less advanced or civilized than himself.{{book ref|iote|38}} He is inspired by feats of engineering he has seen on Scadrial and views them as reminders of the importance of hard work.{{book ref|iote|38}} He is knowledgeable and well prepared, and is up to date on goings-on in the cosmere.{{book ref|iote|36}}

## History 
Dajer was a member of the Malwish military as a ground soldier before being promoted to a Colonel.{{book ref|iote|38}} During his time as a front-line soldier, he participated in the [[Battle of Aheleha]]. The large amounts of smoke present at this battle caused permanent damage to Dajer's lungs, making him dependent on oxygen tubes to allow him to breathe normally.{{book ref|iote|38}} This injury necessitated his removal from front-line battle and he moved into the Malwish Military Engineer Corps. At some point during his military career, Dajer underwent a two-year cross-division training and earned an arcanist badge.{{book ref|iote|36}}

During the [[Ones Above]] negotiation's with the [[Eelakin]], Dajer went looking for the perpendicularity he knew existed in the Drominad subastral. He was convinced he would be able to find it, and expended a large amount of military resources on his search, against the better judgement of his superiors.{{book ref|iote|38}} During his search, he came across the island inhabited by the [[Dakwara]]. He believed the perpendicularity to Drominad was located there and began investigating the island. This investigation caused the death of six of his soldiers.{{book ref|iote|50}} 

After further exploration into the cavern, an attack from the Dakwara killed another two of his soldiers, necessitating a mandatory review from his superiors{{book ref|iote|54}}.
