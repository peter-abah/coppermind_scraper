**Molli** is an ewe owned by [[Neturo-son-Vallano]] and [[Zeenid-daughter-Beth]] on their homestead in [[Shinovar]].{{book ref|sa5|24}}

## Appearance and Personality
She is an old, mostly blind ewe loved by [[Szeth]], Neturo's son. Though blind, she has a good sense of smell, and does not like to be around other sheep.{{book ref|sa5|36}} She has scratchy wool. She is not terribly smart, and she regularly tries to eat dirt and stones. She prefers to stay close to the shepherds,{{book ref|sa5|24}} and often wanders to the homestead on her own rather than needing to be herded.{{book ref|sa5|36}} Szeth enjoys hugging Molli,{{book ref|sa5|24}} and sometimes she licks his face.{{book ref|sa5|36}} Szeth ascribes a degree of autonomy to Molli.{{book ref|sa5|36}}

## History
One day while Szeth was dancing for the family's sacred stone, Molli was grazing nearby. After finishing dancing, Szeth gave Molli a hug. He then found that Molli was trying to eat a new stone that had just emerged from the ground. He realized that he may have already stepped on the stone, and rushed to tell his father.{{book ref|sa5|24}} While Zeenid dug up the stone to relocate it, Szeth hugged Molli again.{{book ref|sa5|27}} 

When the horns warned that raiders were on the way, Neturo and [[Elid]] gathered up the sheep, including Molli, and brought them up the hill and inland to avoid the costal raiders. At some point, Molli wandered away from the flock and back to the homestead.{{book ref|sa5|27}}{{book ref|sa5|36}} When Elid did a count of the sheep, she noticed that three were missing, including Molli, and informed Szeth. Szeth could not find Molli among the herds of the other shepherds, and so left for the homestead to find her.{{book ref|sa5|36}}

When Szeth returned, he found that some of the soldiers had slaughtered and were eating Molli. This was first excused as a presumptuous thanks for their service in fighting off the raiders, but when pressed, a drunken [[Sunken Eyes|soldier]] with sunken eyes explained he did it to remind those who add of how Shin society relies on people like him. Enraged at Molli's death, Szeth attacked the soldier, who refrained from killing Szeth, as the punishment for doing so would be much more harsh than the punishment for killing Molli. Szeth killed the soldier, using the stone Molli found to repeatedly bludgeon him. Szeth then sat in place where he killed the soldier, holding Molli's pelt, until his community found him the next morning.{{book ref|sa5|38}} They took Molli's pelt away from him and buried it.{{book ref|sa5|48}}{{book ref|sa5|51}}

Zeenid saved some of Molli's wool and created a doll of Molli from it. She gave it to Szeth when the family joined the soldier camp at the [[Stoneward monastery]].{{book ref|sa5|51}} Szeth carried the doll around with him in a pouch on his belt. He rarely took the toy sheep out, only doing so when he was unobserved by his peers.{{book ref|sa5|57}} When Szeth became an acolyte of the [[Stone Shamanism|Shamans]], he was instructed to burn everything he owned.{{book ref|sa5|68}} He instead left the doll behind in a spot behind a block in the wall.{{book ref|sa5|66}}

Years later, when Szeth returned to Shinovar to cleanse it, he told [[Kaladin]] the story of the day Molli was killed as they traveled across Shinovar, visiting locations like his old homestead.{{book ref|sa5|22}}{{book ref|sa5|35}}{{book ref|sa5|40}} When they came to the Stoneward monastery, Szeth retrieved the doll made from Molli's wool. Szeth wept. This moment caused Kaladin to fully realize that Szeth was a child when he killed the soldier. Szeth returned the doll to the hole in the wall. After a conversation with Szeth about his desire to stop killing, [[Sylphrena]] took the doll back out of the wall and gave it to Kaladin, who put it in his pocket.{{book ref|sa5|66}} When Kaladin became a [[Herald]], Szeth looked through his pack and found the doll. He cried again.{{book ref|sa5|146}}
