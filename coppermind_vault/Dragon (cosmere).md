**Dragons** are a sapient species within the [[cosmere]]. Originally, they came from [[Yolen]], but can now be found on other worlds (typically in very small numbers).{{wob ref|4410}}{{wob ref|14441}}{{book ref|tsm|10}} They are one of Yolen's three sapient species, alongside humans and [[Sho Del]].{{wob ref|9676}} Like the Sho Del, Dragons are a form of [[fain]]life.{{wob ref|15686}}{{wob ref|16020}} Young dragons are called **Dragonets**.{{book ref|iote|prologue}}

## Physiology
{{image|Hoid and the dragon.jpg|side=right|width=300px|[[Hoid]] with [[Frost]]}}
{{quote
|She had another shape as well. Many of them, but one deeper and truer than the others.
|[[Taravangian]], on [[Koravellium Avast]]{{book ref|sa4|114}}
}}

Most dragons in the cosmere are [[fain]] creatures, and as such they are hexapedal, with four legs and two wings.{{wob ref|16020}} They age extremely slowly after reaching maturity, living tens of thousands of years, but they are not unkillable.{{book ref|iote|27}} Dragons take over thirty years to reach maturity.{{book ref|iote|prologue}} Dragons have stronger eyesight and hearing than humans.{{book ref|iote|26}}{{book ref|iote|49}}{{book ref|iote|56}}

In their true forms, they are not necessarily large; some are smaller than [[sandling]]s, while others can be as large as [[chasmfiend]]s.{{wob ref|2655}}{{book ref|sa4|80}} Their skin is covered with pearlescent scales, as well as silvery streaks of [[Dragonsteel (metal)|dragonsteel]] that run along the body, mostly the limbs, up towards their horns, which are made entirely of dragonsteel.{{book ref|Tress|51}} Even in this nonhuman form, they are still able to speak with a human-sounding voice.{{book ref|sa4|80}}{{book ref|tress|51}} Despite being reptilian, they are able to grow hair across the head, and under the neck and chin as a beard.{{book ref|tress|51}} At least some of them are carnivorous.{{book ref|yumi|28}}

### Shapeshifting
{{sidequote
|You are always a dragon, whether or not you have the shape of one.
|[[Frost]] to [[Starling]].{{book ref|iote|prologue}}
|side=right|size=300px
}}

Dragons have the inherent ability to shapeshift.{{book ref|tress|51}}{{wob ref|14996}}{{book ref|tsm|8}} The scope of their shapeshifting powers may vary by individual,{{wob ref|12076}} but shapeshifting is a core aspect of their life cycle. When in their humanoid form, dragons do not display any identifiable nonhuman characteristics.{{wob ref|7171}}{{book ref|tsm|8}}{{book ref|iote|27}} Their shapeshifting method is different to that of the [[kandra]], even if the result is similar.{{wob ref|148}}

Dragons can be locked into their human form with silver manacles made from dragons.{{book ref|iote|22}}

==== The Transformation ====
Dragonets are stuck in their human form until they become thirty years old. Then, on their exact birthday, they will absorb the first light of [[Yolen]]'s dawn and transform into their draconic form.{{book ref|iote|prologue}} It is not entirely clear if they only transform at the first light of Yolen's dawn, or if they would transform on any planet.

Before First Light appears, it is customary for the parent to speak of the ritual importance of inhabiting a human body for thirty years. The Dragons consider it to be [[Adonalsium]]'s wisdom, teaching them to understand other, much less grand, species.{{book ref|iote|prologue}}

==== Maturity ====
After the transformation, dragons live in their draconic form for decades to master their abilities.{{book ref|iote|27}} After that, they live their lives in both forms equally.{{wob ref|16187}}{{book ref|iote|27}} They assume their humanoid form when mating and bearing children.{{book ref|iote|27}} They can also reproduce with humans, and the child will be a dragon.{{wob ref|17045}}

## Abilities
{{image|IotE ch icon fig 100.png|side=right|width=200px|A dragon's head}}
Dragons can subsist directly on energy drawn from the [[Spiritual Realm]].{{book ref|iote|42}} Due to this, dragons do not need to eat, drink, or sleep. They also have special organs that fabricate nutrients and water by metabolizing [[Investiture]] from the Spiritual Realm. This [[Connection]] can be severed, but is extremely difficult to do. Dragons can choose to have their bodies sleep, and they will go to the [[Spirit Shores]] when doing so.{{book ref|iote|49}} If they cannot access the Spirit Shores, they instead see darkness.{{book ref|iote|42}} When their bodies are asleep, dragons are not aware of their surroundings.

Due to their highly Invested nature, dragons would struggle to use most [[Invested Art]]s or form bonds similar to the [[Nahel bond]].{{wob ref|16654}}{{book ref|iote|44}} In human form, dragons are able to manifest their soul, displaying a transparent and glowing image of their draconic form.{{book ref|iote|27}} They can release concentrated attacks of Investiture.{{book ref|iote|58}} Dragons are able to heal themselves with Investiture and are impervious to most modern firearms or poison.{{book ref|iote|43}} Even in human form, dragons can drop from any height and survive.{{book ref|iote|27}} One of the few ways a dragon can be killed is by a [[Type 1-6 Threnodite entity]].{{book ref|iote|25}}

When dragons die, their [[cognitive shadow]] stays in the Spirit Shores for their essence to fade away into the [[Beyond]], which can take millennia due to their highly invested nature.{{book ref|iote|32}}{{book ref|iote|51}} Similar to [[Shard]]s, contracts are more binding to dragons.{{book ref|iote|53}}

### Connection
Dragons are Connected to each other, as well as their followers with a [[tamukek]].{{book ref|iote|49}}{{book ref|iote|56}} Using a tamukek, one may speak to a dragon, and the dragon can, in turn, respond and influence the speaker's emotions and pain in some manner similar to a [[Brass|Soothing]] or [[Zinc|Rioting]].{{wob ref|16654}}{{wob ref|16187}}{{book ref|iote|26}}{{book ref|iote|58}} Dragons can temporarily take on prayers of another dragon.{{book ref|iote|prologue}}

Dragons can also feel and manipulate the emotions of those around them.{{book ref|iote|26}}{{book ref|iote|30}}

## Culture
{{quote
|The deal has been struck. I must pay you, however little I think the trade was worth.
|[[Xisisrefliel]] to [[Tress]].{{book ref|tress|52}}
}}

Most dragons are fond of bargains; in some cases, this can take the form of magical interactions that involve the dragon granting the petitioner a boon. They are likewise fond of collecting useful assets, which includes people, in form of slaves, followers or even corporate employees.{{book ref|tress|52}}{{wob ref|16187}}{{book ref|sa3|114}} They like to make [[#Cultural Influences|cultural influences]] throughout the cosmere.

At some point, dragons agreed to officially step down from direct political roles, believing they are causing stagnancy or strife.{{book ref|IOTE|40}} Aside from that, draconic personalities vary. [[Frost]] follows a strict non-interventionist policy; some take their self-appointed duties towards people extremely seriously; others devote themselves to scholarship; yet others simply do not care and do as they wish.{{wob ref|16187}} Many dragons do not keep to the ideal of refraining from direct political involvement. For example, [[Xisisrefliel]] follows it more loosely, leading a company with significant influence in the cosmere.{{book ref|tress|52}}{{epigraph ref|sa1|19}}{{book ref|IOTE|40}}

Most dragons have at least two names -- a full, long name often used for religious purposes and a shorter nickname for ease of interaction (for example, Xisisrefliel corresponds with some people using the name "Foil").{{wob ref|16182}}{{book ref|iote|32}}

It is considered dangerous to disturb a sleeping dragon.{{book ref|tsm|19}} There are also myths about dragons having gigantic hoards of gold and of eating men.{{book ref|iote|22}}

### Technology
The dragons have had highly advanced technology prior to the modernization of other planets. They were able to build magnificent cities such as [[Lar'Cal]] or the [[Silverlight]] palaces. They could also fabricate locks widely believed to be impenetrable.{{book ref|iote|40}}

However, Yolen has fallen behind technologically compared to many other planets in the cosmere. This is due to certain things that they can access and [[Shard]]ic influence.{{wob ref|16320}}

## Lesser Dragons
Aside from the so-called "greater dragons," the cosmere is also home to lesser dragons. Lesser dragons have some notable differences to greater dragons; they do not share the amphibian-like lifecycle of greater dragons, and are incapable of transformation into humanoid form. An unnamed planet is home to a variety of non-hexapedal lesser dragons known as **drakelings**.{{wob ref|16187}} Lesser dragons have a much easier time using Invested Arts.{{wob ref|16654}}

Nothing else is known about the biology or abilities of lesser dragons, and their relationship (if any) to greater dragons is unclear.

### Yolen
Even prior to the [[Shattering]], dragons were much more cosmere-aware than humans and [[Sho Del]], and a few may have gone off-world before anyone else did.{{wob ref|15063}} On [[Yolen]], dragons were worshipped as deities and considered themselves divine in turn, with believers praying to them through tamukeks.{{cite}}{{wob ref|16190}}{{wob ref|16142}} Dragons who rejected their position as deities, such as [[Koravellium Avast]], were seen as heretics.{{cite}} [[Adonalsium]], however, was not a dragon.{{wob ref|4556}}

Three dragons are known to have been involved in the Shattering of Adonalsium, with one being Koravellium Avast, who would go on to take up the [[Shard]] of [[Cultivation]], and Medelantorius, who took up the Shard of [[Valor]].{{file ref|Alethi Glyphs Page 2.jpg}}{{book ref|sa5|115}}{{wob ref|14564}} Another dragon, [[Frost]], would remain on Yolen after the Shattering, assuming a stance of non-intervention, although he would amass a significant knowledge of the wider cosmere through his relations with both the [[Seventeenth Shard]] and [[Hoid]].{{epigraph ref|sa1|26}}{{wob ref|2682}}{{wob ref|3679}}

Dragons possess a sort of "meditative realm" that contains the "wisdom of the ancient dead." This realm can be accessed via a tamukek.{{book ref|sa5|147}} Further details about this realm are currently unclear.

### Direct Influences
Dragons are said to live to inspire others, although many don’t live up to this ideal. The best of them do however, spending their entire existences sending comfort, confidence, and compassion to those who prayed to them.{{book ref|iote|27}}

The [[Cognitive Realm]]-located city of [[Silverlight]] was originally built as a collection of dragon palaces. Even in the present day, as the city grew to house worldhoppers of other species, dragons retain a substantial presence in the city, owning corporations, vast establishments and even entire skyscrapers.{{wob ref|16187}} However, even dragons who would consider Silverlight their home base occassionally depart for a long time. One such dragon is [[Xisisrefliel]], who for centuries has been living underneath the [[Crimson Sea]] on [[Lumar]] in order to research the breakdown of aethers and aether spores.{{book ref|tress|31}}{{book ref|tress|51}}

When dragons do settle, they will often grant boons to the locals. Xisis receives gifts of human slaves in exchange for various boons both material, such as advanced off-world technology, and more insubstantial, like information or medical treatment.{{book ref|tress|52}}{{book ref|tress|53}} Over on [[Roshar]], Koravellium Avast grants her own boons to various petitioners, though rather than taking anything in exchange, she grants a matching curse.{{book ref|sa3|114}} Whether this is her own choice or a compulsion is unclear.

### Cultural Influences
{{quote
|Like a grandmother tucking away that bit of string that wrapped her package, the dragons want to know they have a certain number of easily influenced cultures around, for the proverbial rainy day.
|[[Hoid]]{{book ref|tress|51}}
}}

Dragons frequently reveal themselves to developing human cultures, so as to plant the seeds for later use; the term "dragon" is known to nigh-on every cosmere culture.{{book ref|tress|51}} The effectiveness of this approach varies, however. On [[Roshar]], despite the world being home to a draconic Shard, dragons are virtually unknown in the present times, although ancient artwork depicting draconic creatures suggests this was not always the case.{{book ref|sa4|80}}{{file ref|Oathgate Locations.jpg|text=Oathgate Locations}} On [[Nalthis]], on the other hand, dragons are well-known mythological creatures, with the phrase "dragon's nest" referring to someplace highly dangerous.{{book ref|wb|17}}{{wob ref|1346}} Similarly, dragons are a familiar concept on [[Lumar]], though it's unclear how well-known they were before Xisis moved there.{{book ref|tress|51}}

The planet of [[Komashi]] appears to be particularly taken with dragons. They were popular in the times of the [[Torio]] kingdom, with sculptures depicting them frequently used as decorations.{{book ref|yumi|40}} Centuries later, in [[Nagadan]], dragons remained a popular motif, with plush dragons being sold as childrens' toys, and one of the years of the Nagadan calendar being named the year of the dragon.{{book ref|yumi|28}}{{book ref|yumi|16}} Despite this, Komashi myths seem to paint at least some dragons as antagonistic, as they have stories of dragon-slaying heroes.{{book ref|yumi|10}}

In many cultures, dragons are thought to hoard material wealth,{{book ref|iote|22}} which [[Hoid]] theorizes is due to the ridges of dragonsteel that cover their bodies.{{book ref|tress|51}}{{book ref|tress|52}} Many people also believe that dragons eat humans.{{book ref|iote|22}} It appears to be common for people to think that all dragons are royalty, although this isn’t true.{{book ref|iote|27}}

## Trivia
* Though still present in the cosmere in background roles, dragons are not slated to become major players until the [[Dragonsteel (series)|Dragonsteel series]].{{wob ref|12806}}
* Brandon likes dragons a lot;{{wob ref|2449}} they are also the only classical fantasy race to feature in the [[cosmere]].{{wob ref|13862}} However, the early books in the cosmere were published at the time where "dragon books", such as *[[wikipedia:Eragon|Eragon]]* and *[[wikipedia:How to Train Your Dragon (novel series)|How to Train Your Dragon]]* were extremely popular. As such, Brandon felt that the market was "dragon flooded" and postponed involving dragons in his stories until much later.{{wob ref|16187}}
* [[Hoid]] has dated a dragon at some point.{{wob ref|16259}}
