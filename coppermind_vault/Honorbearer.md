The **Honorbearers** are a group of warriors in [[Shinovar]] who wield the [[Honorblade]]s. The position was created after the people of Shinovar were entrusted with the Honorblades following the [[Heralds|Heralds’]] abandonment of [[Taln]]. The number of members varies over time, but it begins with nine, one for each Honorblade other than Taln’s. Each Honorbearer is the leader of their own Monastery, and has acolytes and [[Stone Shamans]] beneath them. They are considered highly holy in Shin culture. The role is presumably no more following [[Stormfall]] and the reforming of the [[Oathpact]].

## History
### Formation
Four thousand years before the events of the [[Stormlight Archive]], the [[Heralds]] abandoned the [[Oathpact]], shifting the weight of all of [[Roshar]] onto [[Taln]]’s shoulders. Ashamed of abandoning their oaths, the nine remaining Heralds left their [[Honorblade]]s in the ground of [[Shinovar]] and walked away from their roles as protectors.{{book ref|sa3|38}}{{book ref|sa5|77}}

The Heralds taught the Shin people that the [[Voidbringers]] would someday return, and that the Honorblades would be needed to fight them. With the Heralds’ guidance, the Shin took up the Honorblades as a sacred trust, naming them, and their role in keeping them, the Sacred Truth. They built ten monasteries, one for each Honorbearer. The monasteries became places for military training and defense, home to thousands of people.{{book ref|sa5|40}} Each monastery had soldiers, trainers, Stone Shamans, and one Honorbearer leading them all. 

This system continued with little variation for four millennia, other than Ishar and Nale claiming their own Honorblades at times, and Szeth being sent away with [[Jezrien]]’s in 1164.{{book ref|sa5|117}}{{book ref|sa4|111}}

### Ishar's Influence
Somewhere about a thousand years previous to the events of the Stormlight Archive, [[Ishar]] found [[Odium's Perpendicularity]], known as the Well of Control, and took a fraction of it for himself.{{book ref|sa5|119}} He used it to Connect himself to the land of Roshar, after the manner of [[Honor]], [[Cultivation]], and [[Odium]] themselves. This gave him immense power as a [[Sliver]], but further contributed to his madness and corruption. It also corrupted the land of Shinovar itself.{{book ref|sa5|128}} He eventually used his position to act as The Voice, speaking to the Honorbearers and corrupting the system over time to work for him. Thanks to his influence, the Honorbearers would not accept the Truth from Szeth, branding him Truthless and ignoring the signs of The End of All Things. 

The Honorbearers were an important piece of Ishar's plan to fight Odium. In addition to corrupting the Shin religion and attempting to creating a [[spren]] army, he planned to create human counterparts to the [[fused]]. The Honorbearers were his chosen subjects for this role, though [[Sivi]], [[Willshaper]] Honorbearer of the time, rejected him. Like fused, the corrupted Honorbearers would return after death, stealing the body of another person. Additionally, their connection to him meant that [[Ishar]] could control them directly, though his control was not perfect.{{book ref|sa5|128}}

### Szeth's Quest
For his fourth of five [[Ideals]], [[Szeth-son-Neturo]] swore to cleanse Shinovar of the blight overtaking it. On the first of the final ten days, he embarked on a pilgrimage, at [[Ishar]]’s command,{{book ref|sa5|33}} going from monastery to monastery, defeating each and every Honorbearer. He defeated [[Rit]] and gained [[Taln]]’s blade,{{book ref|sa5|41}} then took [[Kalak]]’s blade from [[Sivi-daughter-Sivi|Sivi]]’s rotted corpse.{{book ref|sa5|45}} Next, he fought [[Pozen-son-Nash|Pozen]] and the unnamed [[Order of Edgedancers|Edgedancer]] in [[Shadesmar]]. Szeth killed the Edgedancer with [[Nightblood]], and Pozen killed himself with his own Honorblade.{{book ref|sa5|53}} He out-thought [[Moss]]’s riddle, obtaining the [[Order of Lightweavers|Lightweaver]] blade,{{book ref|sa5|71}} and [[Kaladin]], with a warning from [[Syl]], saved him from the [[Truthwatcher]] Honorbearer, hiding among the Lightweaver acolytes.{{book ref|sa5|72}} He connected with [[Elid]] in the [[Dustbringer]] monastery, but Ishar summoned her, dissolving her body, before they could communicate much.{{book ref|sa5|90}} Finally, at the [[Skybreaker]] monastery, [[Nale]] demanded that Szeth swear obedience to him. Szeth refused, and refused to fight Nale, at which point Nale attacked. Kaladin defended Szeth, and Nale agreed that Kaladin could fight as his champion. Kaladin used the tone of Roshar and his Sylflute to finally get through to Nale, winning the Skybreaker Honorblade and leaving only one monastery remaining - Ishar's own.{{book ref|sa5|108}}

On the day of [[Stormfall]], Szeth faced [[Ishar]], who commanded that he fight the six corrupted Honorbearers who had reformed from their deaths. Initially, he refused to fight them, as his sister and father were both among them. However, as he was nearly dead and out of [[Stormlight]], he noticed that he had counted wrong, double-counting the [[Bondsmiths]], (his father and Ishar), and not noticing that the [[Order of Edgedancers|Edgedancer]] he'd killed with [[Nightblood]] had not returned. This led him to realize that Nightblood could kill the human fused permanently, and after swearing his fifth ideal, he used Nightblood to kill all six.{{book ref|sa5|135}}{{book ref|sa5|141}}

After this, Kaladin, Connected to Ishar, swore his fifth ideal and forcibly ejected Odium’s power’s corruption from Ishar’s soul. Finally able to agree, the two of them, together with Nale and the other [[Heralds]], reformed the [[Oathpact]], reclaiming their blades and removing the need for Honorbearers.{{book ref|sa5|144}}

## Structure
### Induction 
To become an Honorbearer, one must be a highly skilled acolyte or Stone Shaman in an Honorbearer monastery. After being noticed and selected by the current Honorbearers, they must go on a two-part pilgrimage. For the first part of the pilgrimage, the inductee must train at each of the monasteries, learning each of the surges to mastery. This journey is successful once they can defeat the current bearer they hope to replace in a duel without powers. This fight is typically fought until a party yields, although it can be to the death if a party refuses to. To celebrate their success and formalize their place, they begin their second pilgrimage. Here they must prove to each of the other Honorbearers’ satisfaction that they are worthy. If the other Honorbearers deem the person unworthy, the blade is returned to its previous owner, or a suitable replacement if the previous owner has died. If they are deemed worthy, however, they are a full Honorbearer. It is a process which takes years.{{book ref|sa5|73}} 

The only exception to this rule is the [[Bondsmith]] Honorbearer, who is elected by a vote.{{book ref|sa5|75}} 
### Hierarchy
Honorbearers are among the highest authorities in Shinovar, and are considered extremely holy. Between the nine of them, however, power fluctuates. Throughout most of the group’s existence, the Bondsmith had the highest authority and is known as the leader of their people,{{book ref|sa5|111}} but in Szeth’s time, [[Pozen-son-Nash|Pozen]] the [[Elsecaller]] is basically the most powerful of them. He seems to be essentially responsible for who gets chosen as future Honorbearers, and most of the other Honorbearers do as he commands. When one, such as Tuko-son-Tuko or Szeth, resists him, they are usually killed. Pozen and the Honorbearers who follow him obey The Voice - Ishar speaking into their minds - completely.{{book ref|sa5|68}}
