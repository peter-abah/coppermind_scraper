> Since he wouldn't have his wife to scribe for him on this battlefield, Adolin had asked Highprince Aladar if he could spare some of his staff. Aladar had taken that request to heart, and had sent his best: his daughter, May.
> — [[Adolin Kholin]]

**May Aladar** is a [[lighteyed]] member of the [[Alethi]] nobility on [[Roshar]]. She is the only known child of [[Highprince]] [[Aladar]]. She commands a group of archers during the [[Battle of Azimir]] and becomes one of the [[Unoathed]] while making a last-minute play to conquer [[Azir]] for the [[coalition of monarchs]] prior to the [[contest of champions]]. 

## Appearance and Personality

> I was never formally with May, but even I know she's never attracted a foolishness spren.
> — [[Adolin]] to [[Aladar]]

May Aladar is an [[Alethi]] [[lighteyes]], meaning she has tan skin and epicanthic folds. She's a short, pretty woman roughly in her twenties, with curly jet-black hair that she wears short. Her eyes are a pale yellow or a light tan, and she has a round face.

May is a competent, no-nonsense woman who tends to act and communicate with coolness and precision. She is comfortable with pushing the boundaries of what is considered socially acceptable for an Alethi woman to do. Inspired by [[Jasnah Kholin]], she is eager to be personally involved on the battlefield.

## Attributes and Abilities

> Skill in politics **is** a skill.
> — May to [[Adolin]]

As the daughter of a [[Highprince]], May has been trained in politics and is experienced in running her father's [[Aladar princedom|princedom]]. She is an expert archer who has won the women's division competitions three times, and she speaks [[Azish]] fluently. She commands a group of archers during battle, is knowledgeable about the nature of [[spren]] and their bonds, and plays [[towers]] competently. May has either been trained as a military officer or has picked up on the principles on her own, as she effectively deals with emotional situations in battle with the calm of an officer's training.

## History
After moving to [[Urithiru]], Highprince [[Aladar]] included May in many of his politically important events and meetings. She was invited to [[Dalinar]]'s and [[Navani Kholin]]'s wedding. She was also part of Dalinar's diplomatic team during his early attempts to form the [[coalition of monarchs]], alongside her father, Navani, [[Kalami]], and [[Teshav Khal]]. During the negotiations, she was mostly an observer. She also assisted Navani in collecting information that came in via [[spanreed]].

May was present when [[Torol Sadeas]]'s corpse was found. Following the death of [[Vedekar Perel]], May was instructed by her father&mdash;by then, named the [[Highprince of Information]]&mdash;to have Perel's family and associates questioned. In light of the murders, she suggested implementing laws to limit movement around the tower. She also participated in scientific discussions with scholars and [[ardent]]s regarding the functioning of Urithiru.

She was present when the news of [[Kholinar]]'s fall arrived, and reacted to it with extreme nausea.

> This is going to be a rough defense. The Fused will rip through this fortification like it's last year's cloak left out in the sun.
> — May

### Battle of Azimir
When Adolin asked Highprince Aladar if he could spare some scribes for the [[Battle of Azimir]], Aladar sent his daughter May. This caused a degree of tension at first, as Adolin had offended her at the Shattered Plains when she thought they were dating and Adolin thought they were not. May went to work checking on their supplies before they lost access to the [[Oathgate]], leaving a ward behind to handle reading and writing for Adolin. While preparing for the first defense, May approached Adolin while he was armoring up and gave him a report on the status of their infirmary accommodations. She informed him that one of her wards, [[Rahel]], was a budding [[Order of Truthwatchers|Truthwatcher]] who could provide some healing and explained that the reason she suggested the hospital should be located where she did was because of a saferoom located underneath. She then remarked on how Adolin's brotherly treatment of his soldiers should not work, according the usual models of military discipline. Following this, much to May's surprise, Adolin abruptly gave her command of the army's twenty best archers, with [[Beamlin Dorset]] and [[Talig]] as her seconds. She excitedly ran off to gear up for battle herself, leaving her ledger with [[Kaminah]], and returned in uniform before the fight began. During the battle, May and her archers watched Adolin through a spyglass and supported him by targeting [[Heavenly Ones]] as they attempted to close in on him. Realizing what was going on, the Fused turned their attack to May's division. After the first attack was turned away, the coalition forces celebrated; Adolin passed [[Maya]] around so others could hold her, and she happened to be in May's hands when he gave Maya his approval to leave and journey through [[Shadesmar]] to find help.

Several of May's wards, such as [[Challa]] and [[Makana]], acted as scribes for Adolin instead of May after she became involved on the battlefield. However, May still oversaw the messenger division as well. Adolin sent [[Zabra]] to her to become a messenger after Zabra tried to become a soldier, but he gave his permission for May to give Zabra archery training if she did well at running messages. May also gave regular updates to [[Jasnah]] about the state of Azimir.

> An invisible, flying spren to lead our messengers would be an advantage. And maybe he'll eventually realize what he should **truly** be doing.
> — May, about [[Notum]]

When [[Notum]] appeared, May asked several questions showing good insight into the nature of unbonded [[spren]]. She also made some pointed comments to tell the spren that she thought it was his duty to form a new [[Knights Radiant|Radiant]] bond, something Adolin disagreed with. She agreed to hand the messenger corps over to Notum, as she had her hands full with Zabra's training, reports, and being on duty for battle. Adolin and May's subsequent conversation about Notum's strangely quick adjustment to being in the [[Physical Realm]] led into a discussion of their relationship. May told Adolin that she was glad they were not a couple and that although she thought it was strange at first that Shallan and Adolin had gotten together, they made a good pairing because of their shared whimsy. May continued to train Zabra in archery, and Adolin found a chance to apologize to her for sending Zabra to her without asking first. May appreciated that Zabra was easy to motivate, however, and turned the conversation into telling Adolin that he needed to get more sleep.

When a [[thunderclast]] crushed Adolin's leg during another battle, May was on the scene with Rahel as he woke up. She dealt with the situation with the calm of training, explaining things to a dazed Adolin. She protested when Adolin wanted out of his Plate so he could go help command the battle, but [[Hmask]] did as he asked. At a later time, May served as Adolin's bodyguard when all of his regular guards had been called away to fight. He and [[Yanagawn]] were playing [[towers]], and after Yanagawn won a game, Adolin invited May to join them for a three-way game. As they played, May gave Adolin the news that the Shattered Plains had been lost and that they were trying to get some [[Order of Windrunners|Windrunners]] over to Azimir to help, but they may not make it in time. May and Adolin teamed up against Yanagawn, who was the first to lose the game of towers, and then May lost to Adolin. May expanded on the principle Adolin was teaching the emperor, explaining that her father had spoken to her a lot about the idea of weaker forces putting aside differences to team up back in the days of the Alethi highprinces' squabbling. When the horns sounded to call all able-bodied men to the battle, May helped Adolin across to the reserve tent. They were both given pikes but directed to different points on the line. After a long, exhausting fight that the coalition forces were losing, Yanagawn came looking for Adolin; May had seen where he was in the line, so she, her scribes, and Colot went to rescue him. May obtained a bow and arrows somewhere, so she was able to take out a [[singer]] who had been standing over Adolin about to kill him.

### Unoathed
The group retreated to the saferoom, where May drew towers pieces on scraps of paper to build a deck so the emperor could pass the time. She informed them that the group of Windrunners would be there soon, having already landed outside the city and met up with Notum. An hour later, [[Skar]] and [[Drehy]] arrived with a handful of squires, only to hear that Yanagawn and Adolin refused to evacuate with them. May supported the idea of attempting to sneak into the throne room, and she was one of the volunteers to accompany Adolin and the emperor. May led the group through the palace with Yanagawn, but they realized they had walked into a trap as soon as they got to the throne room. Facing impossible odds, the small group of coalition forces was unexpectedly aided by the group of [[deadeye]]s that Maya had brought back with her. May became one of the [[Unoathed]] as the deadeyes agreed to help by manifesting in the form of Plate and Blade. They successfully took and defended the throne room, claiming Azir in the last moments before the [[contest of champions]] took place and settled the borders. May and the other Unoathed gathered a month and a half later, where Adolin broke the news of [[Dalinar]]'s death and they discussed the new state of [[Roshar]] and its various implications.

## Relationships
### Highprince Aladar

> I asked her once if she could find a way to use a bow with only one hand...
> — [[Aladar]], about May

[[Aladar]] finds it embarrassing at first that his daughter participates in archery, as it makes her seem unladylike. He later opens up to the idea and even allows her to practice with Plate and Blade, although he warns Adolin that because of [[Jasnah]]'s influence May "might do something foolish" at the [[Battle of Azimir]]. As his relationship with May changes in that regard over the course of the events leading up to the [[True Desolation]], he also recognizes that one of his former goals was to marry May into the Kholin family in order to make a play for the throne; he sees that using his daughter like that was a small-minded thought. However, Aladar has also seen fit to explain politics to May and has contributed greatly to the competence she shows in [[Azimir]].

### Adolin

> I never thought we were dating. She...um...understood differently.
> — [[Adolin]] to [[Colot]]

During their time on the [[Shattered Plains]], [[Adolin]] pursues May, and at one point she believes they are dating while he does not. Adolin's reason for considering her is her skill as an expert sport archer, believing that they have something in common. This former misunderstanding between them brings a level of awkwardness to their first interactions in [[Azimir]]. However, during the Battle of Azimir, Adolin gives May command of a group of archers as a sign of respect. She ends up being glad that she and Adolin did not end up together and that he had found [[Shallan]], citing that they share a sense of whimsy. She also sees herself as the closest thing Shallan has for an advocate for Adolin present at the battle and pushes him to get some sleep more than one of his usual scribes would have.
