A currently untitled film set in the [[Final Empire]] on [[Scadrial]] based upon [[Mistborn: The Final Empire]].{{bws ref|mistborn-movie-news|Mistborn Movie News|date=2010-01-11}} Paloppa Pictures, LLC, optioned the original Mistborn trilogy in January 2010, and were shopping a script to studios in April 2012.{{bws ref|mistborn-movie-update-and-hugo-nomination|Mistborn Movie Update and Hugo Nomination|date=2012-04-09}} The option expired in January 2014.{{bws ref|4787-2|State of the Sanderson 2013|date=2013-07-24}}{{bws ref|mistborn-film-rights|Mistborn Film Rights|date=2014-02-21}} DMG Entertainment optioned most of the Cosmere in October 2016, though the Mistborn film was not worked on much while they had the option.{{ref|name=variety-oct2016}} In 2019, Brandon stated that he was planning to work on a screenplay himself,{{bws ref|state-of-the-sanderson-2019|State of the Sanderson 2019|date=2021-12-19}} and DMG's option on the Mistborn series expired in 2020. Brandon stated in his 2020 and 2021 updates that he was still working "in a hands on way" on a Mistborn film.{{bws ref|state-of-the-sanderson-2020|State of the Sanderson 2020|date=2021-12-18}}{{bws ref|state-of-the-sanderson-2021|State of the Sanderson 2021|date=2021-12-20}} Brandon then signed a deal with Apple TV to make a Mistborn film in January 2026,{{url ref|url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/movies/movie-news/brandon-sandersons-mistborn-stormlight-archive-movie-tv-1236487271/|text=Brandon Sanderson’s Literary Fantasy Universe ‘Cosmere’ Picked Up by Apple TV|site=The Hollywood Reporter|date=2026-02-28}} and began writing a screenplay for this soon after. {{url ref|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VBQmStfWDVE|text=Screen Rights and Apple TV + Weekly Update|site=Youtube|date=2026-02-03}}
