**Zeenid-daughter-Beth** is the mother of [[Szeth]] on [[Roshar]].

## Appearance and Personality
Zeenid has curly light brown hair. Before moving to the [[Stoneward monastery]] with her family, she wears a long green skirt as her splash, with a white apron over it. The skirt contains an unusually large amount of colored fabric for one of her station in [[Shin]] society.

Zeenid is skilled at managing people. She tends to avoid conflict, and is troubled by having to stay among the soldiers. She is convinced by Neturo to adopt a lax and unorthodox view of [[Stone Shamanism]]. It is unclear whether she returns more to orthodoxy once she leaves him. When Szeth is younger, she is fiercely protective of him, but as he ages, she begins to think that he should be capable of fending for himself.

## History
### Early Life
When Neturo met Zeenid, he shared his unorthodox view of Stone Shamanism, and she too began to question the orthodoxy. Zeenid married Neturo and moved to their homestead an hour's walk outside of [[Clearmount]]. They were forced to live in their homestead year-round, because nobody would rent a home in the town to them as they did for other shepherds. She and Neturo had two children, Szeth and [[Elid]]. While living in the homestead, Zeenid oversaw painting classes in Clearmount. She often told her children stories while sitting by the hearth or firepit at their homestead. As a family, they attended monthly devotions in town.

### The Fateful Stone
On the day in  Szeth discovered a new rock near the family's sacred stone, she was overseeing a painting class. Neturo sent a message to her using [[Tek]], one of the family's carrier parrots. Zeenid hurried back from town, carrying a shovel and gloves, and was relieved to see how small the new stone was. She and Neturo planned to move the rock a few hundred yards and wait until after some rain to tell others. They hoped this would avert the possibility of their homestead being declared holy ground, thus requiring them to move, possibly to inside the town of Clearmount proper. Szeth protested, and Neturo suggested they let him decide what to do with the stone. Zeenid suggested allowing Szeth to sleep on it before making a decision, but Szeth decided on the spot to move the stone according to his parents' plan. Zeenid dug up the stone, scraping it in the process. She reasoned she could bury it in such a way to hide the scrapes. Szeth asked further questions, and Zeenid tried to comfort him by explaining that moving the stone was sufficiently reverent for her. The deliberation was interrupted by the horns, warning of incoming raiders. Zeenid grabbed the stone with gloved hands, relieved to find the [[spren]] of the family's larger sacred stone didn't strike her down for the profanity of it, and moved it to the roots of a nearby tree and covered it with leaves. She intended to move it further away later, after the raid. She then went to return the shovel while the rest of her family gathered the sheep. They all headed towards the mountains to stay safely away from the coastal raiders.

Up on the side of the mountains, Zeenid helped direct the preperation of an evening meal for the gathered shepherds, to hopefully calm people down. She helped distribute biscuits and plates while her son Szeth cooked [[misir wat]] at her direction. When the [[Farmer]] came over, looking for Neturo, Zeenid volunteered to find him.

The next day, Szeth was found with the dead body of a [[Sunken Eyes|soldier]], killed with the rock they had found. He was also holding the pelt of [[Molli]], a sheep he loved. That evening, the Farmer came to the homestead to discuss how to handle Szeth's situation. Zeenid defended her son, blaming the Farmer for having such poor control over his soldiers that one would rob the family and attack Szeth. The Farmer agreed that Szeth had done nothing wrong, but insisted that he was officially one who subtracts. Zeenid objected to the juxtaposition. She attempted to shift blame away from Szeth again by claiming the entire family had found the stone (now a murder weapon) together, but the Farmer brushed it off. Zeenid once again protested at the injustice of splitting apart their family and turning an eleven-year-old boy into a killer. When Neturo declared that he would follow Szeth to the monastery, Zeenid declared that she and Elid would also follow.

Before burying Molli's remains, Zeenid saved a little wool to make a toy sheep out of. She gave the sheep to Szeth when the family entered the monastery, crying a little as she did so. She was the last to step onto stone as the family arrived at the soldier's camp.

### Life in the Soldier's Camp
Zeenid did not take well to life in the soldier's camp. She continued to yearn for the open fields and her previous pastoral life. She lost weight, but refused to get new clothes fitted, as her old clothes (minus the splashes, of course) were the only physical object she had from her old life. She went to bed early, deliberately avoiding Neturo whenever she could.

While living at the monastery, Zeenid noticed that her son Szeth was often jogging laps as punishment. Sometimes Elid would see Szeth jogging laps from the kitchen and tell Zeenid. She would then go offer him water and force him to take a break. She encouraged him to wash out of the soldier's training program and be sent to the mines, as she considered a solid (if undignified) job a much better fate than becoming a killer.

When Szeth was selected as an acolyte of the Shamans in , Neturo and Elid decided to come with him. Zeenid was frustrated with Neturo's over-protectiveness of Szeth, who was by now nineteen years old. She blamed him for forcing her to move to the soldier's camp, and declared her intent to repent and return to pastoral life. This opportunity was afforded to her as she had never killed anyone, unlike Szeth.

### Returning to the Pastures
Zeenid was able to repent and return to pastoral life. She lived with her sisters and became happy. Neturo estimates that she was happier without him than she ever had been when they were together.

Zeenid died of tumors sometime between  and . Elid was there to hold her hand as she died.

## Relationships
### Neturo
Early on, Zeenid and Neturo seem to get along well. Neturo converts her to an unorthodox view of Stone Shamanism and they are both dedicated to raising their children and improving their community. However, joining the soldier's camp at the Stoneward monastery takes a severe toll on Zeenid. She blames Neturo for forcing her to follow Szeth. Though she made the choice willingly, it seems that Neturo's forwardness in wanting to follow Szeth caused her to make a decision she wouldn't have otherwise. This causes their relationship to deteriorate. They stop sleeping together and Zeenid begins to avoid Neturo whenever she can. When Zeenid refuses to follow as Szeth becomes an acolyte, she finally says what she had been thinking all those years. While the relationship effectively ended then, it appears that they never officially nullified their marriage. Zeenid's sisters later write letters to Neturo, to reassure him that she is doing well.
