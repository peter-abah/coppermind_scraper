The **Ghostbloods** are a secret organization found on many planets in the [[cosmere]], formed for the purposes of protecting [[Scadrial]] from all threats.{{book ref|sa4|64}}{{book ref|mb7|39}}{{book ref|mb7|47}}

Members are required to get a tattoo of three overlapping diamonds somewhere on their bodies.{{book ref|sa2|64}}{{book ref|mb7|20}}{{file ref|Shadesmar Spren.jpg|Shallan's Sketchbook - Shadesmar Spren}} The symbol bears a resemblance to the three-petal shape of [[Marewill flowers]].{{book ref|mb7|28}}

## Tenets
The Ghostbloods have three general tenets:{{book ref|mb7|47}}
* Protecting Scadrial.
* Sharing what they know with each other, but not with outsiders.
* Trusting each other.

## Objectives
### Scadrian Ghostbloods
The Scadrian Ghostbloods are devoted to protecting Scadrial at any cost, and due to this came into conflict with the [[Set]] and [[Autonomy]].{{book ref|mb7|47}}{{book ref|mb7|epilogue|1}}

### Rosharan Ghostbloods

The [[Rosharan]] Ghostbloods seek power. They are searching for a way to get [[Stormlight]] ([[Investiture]]) offworld from Roshar through [[Shadesmar]]. They hunt [[Restares]] and the [[Sons of Honor]] as the Ghostbloods fear that the Sons of Honor are close to discovering the secret of making this possible. Stormlight is free, portable, easy-to-access power.{{book ref|sa4|13}} Selling it to worlds with very little or rare Investiture could make a huge profit. To attain this goal, they need control of the [[Oathgate]]s, which inspired their hunt for [[Urithiru]].{{book ref|sa4|9}} They seek [[perfect gemstone]]s that would allow the Stormlight to last long enough to get offworld, though there is a [[Connection]] problem.{{book ref|sa4|13}} They have found ways to circumnavigate this by blanking its [[Identity]].{{book ref|sa5|25}}

The Ghostbloods are obsessed with finding [[Ba-Ado-Mishram]], who has the ability to bind minds of an entire people, as leverage should [[Odium]] attempt to escape the [[Rosharan subastral]].{{book ref|sa4|24}} They are not interested in seeing Odium rule Roshar.{{book ref|sa4|13}} They seek to bond [[Sja-anat]], as her ancient knowledge would be invaluable.{{book ref|sa4|30}}

They also seek to imprison [[Herald]]s in gemstones in order to obtain information. [[Kelsier]] wants knowledge about an affliction that he shares with the Heralds, an inability to leave the [[subastral]] he is in.{{book ref|sa4|82}} They are also concerned about the maladies of the mind other Cognitive Shadows have shown, and are searching for a way to prevent them in Kelsier.{{rpg ref|slwg|141}}

Their desire to find a way to carry Stormlight off-world and to help Kelsier learn how to leave Scadrial likely ties into the Ghostbloods' goal of protecting Scadrial.{{book ref|mb7|47}} The Scadrian Ghostbloods, however, view them as running amok.{{book ref|mb7|epilogue|1}}

## Resources
The Ghostbloods own a tenement building in [[Highprince]] [[Sebarial]]'s [[Shattered Plains#warcamps|warcamp]] on the [[Shattered Plains]] that has a basement. In it was a display of trophies that [[Shallan]] saw, including several enormous [[gemheart]]s, a tusk that was probably from a [[whitespine]], an eye socket that looked like the structure of a [[santhid]]’s skull, a vial of pale [[Sand (Taldain)|sand]],{{wob ref|9288}} a couple of thick hairpins, a lock of golden hair, a [[Yolen|Yolish]] branch of a [[fain]] tree with writing on it,{{wob ref|5331}} a silver knife, an odd flower preserved in some kind of solution, and a chunk of pale [[Aether|pink crystal]] that looked like it might be some kind of gemstone.{{wob ref|12314}} There were also shells that Shallan vaguely recognized. There were no plaques describing any of these mementos.{{book ref|sa2|43}} The Ghostbloods also have acquired a number of Verdant [[Aether#Spores|Spores]].{{rpg ref|slwg|142}}

On Scadrial, they have a safehouse at house one eighty-seven, near Thirty-Third and Finete in the Knightbridge district of [[Bilming]].{{book ref|mb7|36}} It is protected with [[Identity]] locks and contains maps, a [[seon]], an armory, and three jars of purified [[Dor]] in a safe with an [[Awakening|Awakened]] lock.{{book ref|mb7|40}} They also own a large estate in [[Elendel]].{{book ref|mb7|epilogue|1}}

Though there is no evidence to this, they presumably have at least a couple more safe houses.

## Structure
The Ghostbloods operate across multiple planets within the cosmere.{{book ref|sa4|64}} They operate within a strict hierarchy, as a group of hunters without structure would turn on each other too easily. They do not strike against their own.{{book ref|mb7|47}} They do not negotiate upward.{{book ref|sa4|30}} It is common for a master to spy on their pupils.{{book ref|sa4|13}} [[Ialai Sadeas]] warns Shallan that the Ghostbloods will watch her to ensure she doesn't rise high enough to learn secrets that could hurt them.{{book ref|sa4|7}} 

Joining the Ghostbloods requires being brought in by a member of the organization and the swearing of oaths to the Ghostbloods. It is tradition for the one who offers membership to become the mentor of the one they invited.{{book ref|mb7|epilogue|1}} 

Upon joining the Ghostbloods, they share everything they know and the member can influence decisions. The Ghostbloods do not keep secrets from fellow members, but in most cases members are also not allowed to share Ghostblood secrets with non-members.{{book ref|mb7|47}} Mraize warned Shallan that if she were to break ties with the group, she would be hunted. He and his babsk would not hunt her, but all they could do was discourage others.{{book ref|sa4|13}} Members are not to share secret information with anyone outside of the organization; doing so would result in the assassination of the offender due to the danger it can pose to the organization.{{book ref|mb7|47}}{{book ref|mb7|epilogue|1}}

Kelsier is not on Roshar and only comes as what they consider an avatar;{{book ref|sa4|115}}{{wob ref|14641}} he does show directly to the Scadrian Ghostbloods.{{book ref|mb7|epilogue|1}} Kelsier does affect the Rosharan Ghostbloods' actions, but they are a little removed from him on Roshar.{{wob ref|15014}}

## History
### Formation
The Ghostbloods were formed on Scadrial at some point between the Scadrian year {{date|1|FA}} and the Rosharan year {{rosharan date|1168}}.

### Dealings with Gavilar
Kelsier traded information, including information about the heralds, with [[Gavilar]] in exchange for handing Kalak over to his agents.{{book ref|sa5|prologue}} 

### Attempted Murder of Jasnah Kholin
The Ghostbloods have made several attempts to murder [[Jasnah Kholin]], and claims that she has had a number of their members assassinated as well. When Jasnah spent time in [[Kharbranth]] to study the [[Voidbringers]], a Ghostblood agent named [[Kabsal]] infiltrated the local [[Ardentia]] in order to kill Jasnah. He used her young ward [[Shallan Davar]] to get close to Jasnah, which led to them forming a relationship. As Kabsal attempted to poison Jasnah with bread, he was inadvertently killed when he ate some of the poisoned bread and Jasnah [[Soulcast]] his antidote, mistakenly believing that to be the poison. Jasnah later found the Ghostblood tattoo on Kabsal's arm.

When Jasnah left Kharbranth, the Ghostbloods planted at least [[New kid|one operative]] aboard the ship *[[Wind's Pleasure]]* on the journey from Kharbranth to the [[Shattered Plains]]. That operative, along with other men Shallan did not recognize,{{book ref|sa2|7}} attempted to murder Jasnah. They thought they'd succeeded upon stabbing her, but in reality, Jasnah had escaped to [[Shadesmar]] using [[Surge#Transportation|Elsecalling]].{{book ref|sa2|34}}{{book ref|sa3|33}}

Jasnah assumes that she was targeted because of her [[Knights Radiant|Radiant]] [[Knights Radiant#Immortal Words|oaths]].{{book ref|sa3|33}}

### Recruiting Shallan Davar
A man identified with the Ghostbloods was financing [[Lin Davar]] in his bid to become [[Highprince]] of [[Vedenar]], [[Jah Keved]]. After an unknown Veden [[Shardbearer]] attacked [[Meridas Amaram|Amaram]], Amaram speculated that Kelsier and the Ghostbloods were behind the attack.{{book ref|sa1|51}} The unknown shardbearer turned out to be [[Helaran Davar]], Shallan's brother.{{wob ref|1257}}

{{image|Ghostblood_Agent_by_Soph_P.jpg|Mraize as an agent of the Ghostbloods|side=left|width=175px}}

The Ghostbloods were sought out by Shallan Davar, who, under the disguise of Veil, wanted to investigate them.{{book ref|sa2|42}} Shallan met with Mraize and Iyatil in a Ghostblood hideout located in Sebarial's warcamp, and managed to trick them into believing that she was sent there by [[Tyn]]. Mraize, unaware that Tyn was dead, ordered Shallan to tell Tyn to investigate the quarters of Meridas Amaram.{{book ref|sa2|43}} Shallan successfully performed this task, and brought her findings to Mraize, which led to her accidentally revealing her photographic memory. Mraize then deduced that Tyn was dead, killed by Shallan, but commended her for it. He then told her that she could get a place among the Ghostbloods if she completed tasks for them. However, he also allowed other Ghostbloods to attempt to murder her if they wanted to. Shallan managed to avoid the attempts on her life, and carried out her next task of investigating [[Taln]]'s presence in the warcamp of [[Dalinar Kholin]] in a satisfactory manner.{{book ref|sa2|52}}

After the [[Battle of Narak]], and the subsequent discovery of Urithiru, Mraize confronted Shallan, and revealed that he had figured out her real identity as Lin Davar's daughter, Jasnah Kholin's ward, and a Knight Radiant with the ability to Soulcast. Mraize reminded her of her family's debt to the Ghostbloods, and also revealed that Ghostblood agents had rescued her brothers from the civil war in Jah Keved and would bring them safely to Urithiru. The Ghostblood operatives succeeded, and the Davar brothers arrived in Urithiru after the [[Battle of Thaylen Field]].{{book ref|sa3|122}}

Shallan's identities debated their interest in joining the Ghostbloods. Veil was interested in joining, but refused to get the tattoo.{{book ref|sa4|20}} Shallan planned to break ties with the Ghostbloods.{{book ref|sa4|9}}{{book ref|sa4|13}} Formless, an imagined fourth identity for Shallan where she hid her self doubt and selfish ambitions, wanted to join the Ghostbloods.{{book ref|sa4|93}}

Mraize offered Shallan full membership in the Ghostbloods in return for assassinating [[Restares]], leader of the [[Sons of Honor]].{{book ref|sa4|82}} Shallan did not go through with it and broke ties with the group. She threatened Mraize to leave Urithiru and warned him that she would be hunting for Ba-Ado-Mishram. Mraize warned her that she'd moved against the group in the most offensive of ways.{{book ref|sa4|115}}

{{sidequote|This will not end well for you, Shallan. You make an enemy of the most powerful organization in all the cosmere.|Mraize to Shallan{{book ref|sa4|115}}|side=right|size=400px}}

### Cleansing Urithiru
Mraize asked Shallan to investigate the wrongness in Urithiru, which she was already sensing.{{book ref|sa3|22}} This led her to find, confront, and expel [[Re-Shephir]].{{book ref|sa3|30}}

### Pursuit of the Sons of Honor
The Ghostbloods wished to keep an eye on the activities of [[Sadeas princedom|House Sadeas]], and Mraize was personally spying on them, acting as a guard to Ialai Sadeas in Urithiru.{{book ref|sa4|13}} They murdered [[Thanadal]] and [[Vamah]]. They struck at Ialai several times and ultimately succeeded when [[Shallan]] assassinated her with [[Blackbane]], though Shallan claimed she did this for the good of [[Alethkar]].{{book ref|sa4|7}}{{book ref|sa4|93}} 

Shallan and the [[Unseen Court]] searched Ialai's bed chambers and found a notebook containing many Ghostblood secrets, including a map of [[Shadesmar]], a route through the [[Sea of Lost Lights]], and [[Thaidakar]]'s supposed offworld origins.{{book ref|sa4|13}}

Shallan hunted the [[Herald]] [[Kalak]] under the alias of Restares to [[Lasting Integrity]]. After an internal struggle, Shallan decided against killing him. She offered to share information about the Ghostbloods. He claimed to be close to getting offworld.{{book ref|sa4|93}}

### Recruiting  Sja-anat
During her wedding, Shallan is instructed in a letter from Mraize to recruit Sja-anat into the Ghostbloods.{{book ref|sa3|122}} [[Mraize]] requested a bond between a Ghostblood and one of her "enlightened" spren. Shallan only arranged a meeting.{{book ref|sa4|13}} Sja-anat sent [[Tumi]], who ended up bonding [[Rlain]].{{book ref|sa4|114}}

### Bringing other forms of Investiture to Roshar
The Ghostbloods have plans to commercialize Investiture on a much larger scale than what happened on [[Scadrial]]. In these efforts they bring [[Sand (Taldain)|sand]] from [[Taldain]], [[Seon]]s from [[Sel]], and [[Aviar]]s from [[First of the Sun]] to Roshar. They want to use the [[Oathgate]]s to facilitate the trade of these items on Roshar, as they would not need to rely on [[Perpendicularity|perpendicularities]]. Mraize also mentions that perfect gemstones would be a good tool for carrying Stormlight off Roshar;{{book ref|sa4|13}} [[Jasnah Kholin|Jasnah]] has noted that there are nowhere near as many perfect gemstones in circulation as there should be, which leads [[Adolin Kholin|Adolin]] to theorize that a group that is aware of their importance&mdash;like the Ghostbloods&mdash;has been secretly collecting them.{{book ref|sa4|29}} The [[honorspren]] of [[Lasting Integrity]] have collected several perfect gemstones over millennia,{{book ref|sa4|78}}{{book ref|sa4|87}} though it is unclear how many they have compared to how many Jasnah thinks there should be.

### Searching for Ba-Ado-Mishram
During the ten days before the [[contest of champions]], the Ghostbloods sought to enter the [[Spiritual Realm]] and find the prison of Ba-Ado-Mishram to obtain leverage against [[Odium]].{{book ref|sa5|65}} During a meeting at the Ghostbloods' hideout in [[Narak]], Mraize indicated that a contact had told him of Dalinar and Navani's planned trip to the Spiritual Realm and that he and Iyatil would follow them there. Before leaving, he placed [[Zora]] in charge of the Rosharan Ghostbloods cell.{{book ref|sa5|25}} By the time of the meeting, he had already obtained [[anti-Stormlight]] and he and Iyatil had bonded Enlightened spren. After a brief confrontation with Shallan, Iyatil transferred herself, Mraize, and [[Lieke]] to Shadesmar.{{book ref|sa5|28}}{{book ref|sa5|29}}

Once in Shadesmar, the three disguised themselves as guards traveling with some [[Windrunners]] from Narak to Urithiru. They accompanied Shallan, [[Renarin]], [[Rlain]], and the two Windrunners to the Cognitive counterpart of where Dalinar and Navani were preparing to cross over to the Spiritual Realm.{{book ref|sa5|31}} As the perpendicularity opened, Shallan realized the ruse, which caused the Ghostbloods to attack the Windrunners. After a short fight resulting in Lieke's death, Iyatil and Mraize lured the Windrunners away by telling them that the guards whose places they'd taken were being held as insurance. Mraize then stabbed the perpendicularity with an anti-Stormlight dagger, collapsing it. This sucked him and the others remaining in Shadesmar into the Spiritual Realm and cut off Navani and Dalinar's way out.{{book ref|sa5|32}}

In the Spiritual Realm, Mraize took the form of various characters within the visions to follow Dalinar and Shallan towards Mishram.{{book ref|sa5|49}} Iyatil pretended to be [[Formless]] to stay close to Shallan.{{book ref|sa5|65}} On the ninth day, Mraize talked to Shallan about his dreams of traveling the cosmere, and how they had been denied because he chose poorly in attempting to recruit her. She correctly guessed both that his punishment would be reduced if he could kill her, and that part of the point of the vision was to distract her while Iyatil searched for the prison. He ended the conversation by warning her that, one way or the other, their next confrontation would be the end.{{book ref|sa5|119}}

Mraize and Iyatil followed Shallan, Renarin, and Rlain to Mishram's prison when Shallan found the mindset that allowed the [[Connection]] to summon the vision.{{book ref|sa5|129}} When Mraize and Iyatil attempted to gain access to the prison, Shallan killed them both, but left their spren alive.{{book ref|sa5|134}}{{book ref|sa5|137}}

### Resistance against Trell
The Ghostbloods on Scadrial worked in order to resist the oncoming threat of [[Trell (Trellism)|Trell]].{{book ref|mb7|39}}

On {{date|348|FA}} the Ghostbloods attempted to recruit [[Marasi Colms]] and sent Moonlight to deliver a message to her telling that the Ghostbloods were watching her and were impressed.{{book ref|mb7|15}}{{book ref|mb7|8}}

Several days later the Ghostbloods contacted Kelsier via Seon in their hideout in Bilming informing him that the [[Set]] had developed a bomb to destroy Elendel.{{book ref|mb7|40}}
After Kelsier informed the Ghostbloods that he wouldn't get to Bilming in time, TwinSoul, Moonlight and Marasi discovered and infiltrated the Set's underground [[Wayfarer|caverns]].{{book ref|mb7|44}} Since Moonlight and Marasi were discovered and confronted by Set soldiers after Marasi's attempt to save a group of people from execution, Moonlight used a [[soulstamp]] to rewrite her history and become an [[Elantrian]] so Marasi could escape.{{book ref|mb7|50}}{{book ref|mb7|54}}. Ghostblood agents in Elendel were ordered to assist the local authorities with organizing the evacuation, as they too would not reach Bilming in time.{{book ref|mb7|40}}{{book ref|mb7|70}}

After [[Waxillium Ladrian|Wax]] and [[Wayne]] stopped the bomb that the Set sent to Elendel, Marasi met with Kelsier and the Ghostbloods in Elendel and told them that she wouldn't join with them because of their rule of keeping their information secret. After that, TwinSoul supposedly went on a mission to track down Moonlight.{{book ref|mb7|epilogue|1}}
