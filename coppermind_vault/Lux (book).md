***Lux: A Texas Reckoners Novel*** is a novel set in the universe of [[The Reckoners]]. It was developed under Brandon's [[Mainframe]] production company, which is focused on audio originals.{{url ref|url=https://www.awfulagent.com/catalogs/|site=awfulagent.com|text=JABberwocky Catalog & Rights Guide|date=2021-06-14}} It was released as an Audible original on July 22, 2021.{{tweet ref|1408139559217426434|Brandon's *Lux* cover reveal on Twitter|date=2021-06-24}} A prose version was planned,{{tweet ref|1408152396786659329|Peter Ahlstrom on Twitter|date=2021-06-24}} but it is unclear if it will be completed.{{wob ref|16353}}{{wob ref|16913}}

## Summary
{{for|/Summary|a complete chapter by chapter summary}}

[[Jax (Reckoners)|Jax]], a young Texas [[Reckoners|Reckoner]], engages in a confrontation against the [[Epic]] [[Wingflare]]. The story then goes back several years to Jax's backstory, in which the Epic [[Lovestruck]] kills his beloved older brother. Jax is then recruited by [[Prof]], who introduces him to the Reckoners and takes him to the [[Coop|Reckoners training facility]]. Jax is personally trained to be a Reckoner by [[Zeff]], the head trainer there. After an operation goes awry, leaving Zeff dead, Jax and the remaining Texas Reckoners decide to infiltrate the flying city of [[Lux (city)|Lux]], ruled by the High Epic [[Lifeforce]]. 

They climb up into the city, disguising their weapons and gear using a [[Motivator#Scorchnotes|motivator]]. Lux is kept in the air by an Epic called Wingflare, and the Reckoners sneak a motivator into the power grid so they can wrest control of the city from her. They try to assssinate Lifeforce, but they are betrayed by [[Briggen]], a member of their team. They neutralize the other Epics in Lux. They confront Lifeforce, who has given himself Wingflare's powers through scientific experimentation.  Jax kills Lifeforce after figuring out his weakness. As he dies, Lifeforce injects a serum into [[Paige]], one of the Reckoners. As Lifeforce dies, Lux slowly drifts into the sea. Paige awakes weeks later, with a mean attitude and dark thoughts. She hears a voice in her head, telling her that it is the demon that had lived inside Lifeforce. It calls itself [[Deathrise]].

## Development
In 2019, [[Steven Michael Bohls]] mentioned that he was working with Brandon on an expansion to the *Reckoners* universe, and had been given quite a bit of autonomy on the project. It was conceived as both a spinoff and a sequel of the original trilogy, following a different team of Reckoners. Steven anticipated writing three novellas for the audio original format that would then be consolidated as a sequel in print.{{wob ref|13423}} 

Brandon mentioned a planned *Reckoners* project for Mainframe in 2020 and again referred to the project as a trio of novellas.{{sots ref|2020}} In late 2020 and early 2021, Steven posted a few updates about the development process on social media; the working title was *Deathrise*.{{url ref|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/CI9H-yXA0RK/|date=2020-12-18|site=Instagram|text=Steven Bohls on Instagram}}{{tweet ref|1355331736805101568|Steven Bohls on Twitter|date=2021-01-29}} During the revision process, the team decided that the pacing of the three novellas worked better as a single novel.{{wob ref|14831}} The novel is intended to stand alone; it has some time overlap with the previous *Reckoners* books, but reading them is not a prerequisite.{{wob ref|14833}}

### Prose Version
In 2021, [[Peter Ahlstrom]] believed that a prose version of the novel would certainly be released,{{wob ref|14860}} and Brandon stated that there would be an eBook and eventually a print version.{{wob ref|15363}} However, Peter has since mentioned that there are several hurdles to overcome. The [[Dragonsteel_Entertainment|Dragonsteel]] team has been focused on Brandon's [[cosmere]] works, so finding the resources to work on a prose version of *Lux* has been difficult.{{wob ref|16353}}{{wob ref|16613}} Peter noted that it can be difficult to maintain good pacing while moving from audio to prose, and *Lux* also has some continuity issues with the other *Reckoners* works that need to be fixed.{{wob ref|16353}}{{wob ref|16913}} As of 2025, the prose version is in limbo.{{wob ref|16913}}

### Sequels
Two sequels to *Lux* were considered.{{wob ref|16913}} Although *Lux* was considered to be a success,{{wob ref|15363}} there are no firm plans to move forward with the sequels, presumably due to the prioritization of other projects. Brandon has referred to *The Reckoners* as "finished", with only a small chance that it could receive another sequel someday.{{wob ref|17016}} If one or more sequels are written, it would increase the chances of a prose version of *Lux*.{{wob ref|16913}}
