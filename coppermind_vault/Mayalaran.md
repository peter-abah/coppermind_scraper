**Mayalaran** is a [[deadeye]] [[cultivationspren]]{{book ref|sa3|102}} who was bonded to an [[Order_of_Edgedancers|Edgedancer]] before the [[Recreance]].{{wob ref|7095}} She is currently the [[Shardblade]] of [[Adolin Kholin]], who nicknames her **Maya**.{{book ref|sa3|120}} 

## Appearance and Personality
{{image|Maya.jpg|side=left|width=250px}}
### Appearance
{{for|/Gallery|more images}}

Maya's appearance in [[Shadesmar]] is that of a woman with scratches around her eyes like faded scars.{{book ref|sa5|147}} Previous to [[Ba-Ado-Mishram]]’s release, she had no eyes, and looked like a painting with the features scratched out rather than simply empty sockets.{{book ref|sa3|87}}{{book ref|sa3|89}} Her features seem to be made of ashen brown cords the thickness of hair,{{book ref|sa3|87}} giving her skin the appearance of rough cloth{{book ref|sa3|89}}, string{{book ref|sa3|101}} or brown vines.{{book ref|sa3|117}} She has crystalline hands with long nails.{{book ref|sa3|117}}{{book ref|sa3|102}}

In the [[Physical Realm]], Maya appears as a Shardblade. The blade itself is shaped like a larger version of a standard longsword, and bears some resemblance to the enormous two-handed broadswords traditionally wielded by the [[Horneater]]s. The cutting edge is smooth and sinuous, like an eel, while the back has ridges of crystal.{{book ref|sa2|14}} When summoned, the mist it forms from is in the shape of vines.{{wob ref|7095}}

### Personality
{{quote
|I am a soldier. I know what inspires soldiers. You look good. 
|Maya to Adolin{{book ref|sa5|34}}
}}

As a [[deadeye]], Maya was non-verbal and expressed almost no personality.{{book ref|sa3|89}}{{book ref|sa3|117}}{{book ref|sa3|120}} Her personality began to slowly reemerge after the trial at [[Lasting Integrity]],{{book ref|sa4|94}}{{book ref|sa4|115}} and then truly began to return in force after visiting the reawakened [[Urithiru]].{{book ref|sa5|34}}She has a solid grasp on tactics and strategic thinking, and thinking about it helps her focus. She is old-fashioned, and considers herself a soldier.{{book ref|sa5|34}}{{book ref|sa5|39}} 

Unlike [[spren]] like [[Syl]] or [[Pattern]], who are “innocent in the ways of romance and intimacy,” she has lived her life around soldiers, and so enjoys crass jokes, including teasing [[Adolin]] by calling him a slut and using tactical lingo as innuendo.{{book ref|sa5|37}} She supports Adolin very much, and encourages him to seek the help he needs. She challenges his self-defeating ideas and tries to push him to heal,{{book ref|sa5|128}} because she is grateful for the healing he helped her find.{{book ref|sa5|39}}{{book ref|sa5|42}} She is familiar with the [[Fused]] from her time with previous [[Knight Radiant|Radiants]], being able to identify [[Abidi|Abidi the Monarch]] when he appears at the [[Azir|Azish]] [[Oathgate]].{{book ref|sa5|41}}

Like many of the [[Spren#True Spren|true spren]], Maya considers the [[honorspren]] to be stuffy and boring.{{book ref|sa5|135}}

Adolin is relieved to hear her laugh in Azimir before the occupation.{{book ref|wat|37}}

## Attributes and Abilities
### Bond to Adolin
Maya and [[Adolin]] have a unique bond of an unknown nature. It does not grant Adolin [[surgebinding]] and he does not swear [[Ideals|oaths]], but it has revived Maya from her [[deadeye]] state, restoring her ability to communicate. As of [[Wind and Truth]], she can speak mentally with Adolin.{{book ref|sa5|34}} Their communication works even across great distances, though it becomes limited to feelings and individual words.{{book ref|sa5|67}}{{book ref|sa5|80}} Adolin can see through her eyes in [[Shadesmar]],{{book ref|sa5|135}} and he can summon her without the standard ten heartbeat wait.{{book ref|sa5|42}} She can change her shape, but not nearly to the extent of a [[Spren#True_Spren_2|radiant spren]].{{book ref|sa5|40}}

Like all the [[Unoathed]], she can be summoned as a blade. After [[Retribution]]’s creation, this is a unique trait among unbonded [[spren]] and [[deadeye]]s. According to Maya, this is a side-effect of the reforged Oathpact. She can also travel to [[Shadesmar]], even after [[Stormfall]], which is an ability unique to her.{{book ref|sa5|147}}She is able to swim through the beads to quickly travel Shadesmar, like all [[deadeye]]s.{{book ref|sa5|42}} She is able to communicate with other deadeyes, including recruiting them and asking them for help. During the [[Battle_of_Azimir|battle for the Azish Oathgate]] leading up to the [[Contest_of_champions|contest of champions]], she used these abilities to gather around fifty deadeyes, nine of whom worked with her to form the [[Unoathed]].{{book ref|sa5|135}}

## History
### The Recreance
Mayalaran was killed by her bonded Edgedancer when they abandoned their oaths, believing that the [[Nahel bond]] would bring about the destruction of [[Roshar]] the way it had for humanity's homeworld, [[Ashyn]].{{book ref|sa3|113}} During Adolin's trial, she reveals that she, along with all the other deadeyes, had chosen to sacrifice themselves through the Recreance: they were willing partners to the end of the Knights Radiant.{{book ref|sa4|94}}

At some point before the [[War of Reckoning]], [[Adolin]] dueled a [[lighteyes]] named [[Tinalar]]. Against everyone's expectations, he won the duel and secured Maya as his own. At the time, her name was unknown to anyone living. Believing it to be pretentious to give her one that wasn't hers, Adolin left her unnamed.{{book ref|sa2|14}}

### The War of Reckoning (1167-1174)
{{image|Adolin by Marie Seeberger.jpg|Adolin holding Maya in Shardblade form|side=right|width=200px}}

Adolin often spoke to Maya and imagined that she listened to him. He believed that something that seemed like an extension of the soul itself was impossible to wield without feeling as though it too was alive.{{book ref|sa2|14}} This in turn may account for some of the differences in the relationship between Adolin and Mayalaran in regards to other deadeye spren.

Maya was one of the shards placed on the line in Adolin and Dalinar's dueling campaign to disarm the [[Shardbearer|Shardbearers]] of the [[Highprince|Highprinces]].{{book ref|sa2|5}} The most important duel of these was the one against [[Relis]], [[Elit Ruthar|Elit]], [[Abrobadar]] and [[Jakamav]].{{book ref|sa2|56}} She was also present in many battles, including the [[Battle of the Tower]]{{book ref|sa1|66}} and the [[Battle of Narak]].{{book ref|sa2|81}}

### The True Desolation (1174-)
{{quote
|Hmmm. She is quite dead.
|[[Pattern]] upon seeing Maya{{book ref|sa3|89}}
}}

Adolin talked with Maya before he, [[Kaladin]], [[Shallan]], and [[Elhokar]] stormed [[Kholinar Palace]]. He acknowledged that she was a dead spren and that he was sorry there was nothing he could do to fix that. He thanked her for all the years they've fought together and promised to try and use her only to do good.{{book ref|sa3|83}}

Maya and Adolin met for the first time when he crossed over into [[Shadesmar]]{{book ref|sa3|87}} with [[Shallan]], [[Kaladin]], [[Vivenna]], [[Pattern]] and [[Sylphrena]] from the corrupted [[Oathgate]] in [[Kholinar]].{{book ref|sa3|85}} Pattern informed them all that Maya is quite dead. Usually, she trailed after Adolin, but when he didn't move, she remained motionless,{{book ref|sa3|89}}{{book ref|sa3|93}} and to go elsewhere she had to be led by the hand.{{book ref|sa3|102}}  

When traveling with the [[Reacher]]s, Maya was locked in a small room in the cargo hold by Captain [[Ico]], who introduced the term "deadeye" to the group. He told Adolin that deadeyes wouldn't watch where they're going, which is why he couldn't have her on deck. He wasn't happy with Adolin's attitude towards her, telling him that he should not pretend Maya is his friend, and that she was only a tool to him.{{book ref|sa3|101}} 

{{image|Mayalaran by Nozomi.jpg|Held by the [[honorspren]]|side=left|width=250px}}

At the [[Thaylen City]] Oathgate in Shadesmar, Maya protected Adolin from one of the [[Fused]] without prompting and as a result she was stabbed with a sword, but didn't react beyond screeching.{{book ref|sa3|117}} Later, once they reached Thaylen City proper, she and Adolin faced the [[thunderclast]]. Adolin believed she was made to fight them, being overly long for dueling, and that she wanted to fight because it reminded her of when she was alive. In response, she told him her name.{{book ref|sa3|120}} During their fight, Adolin was able to summon her in only seven heartbeats after he pled with her to appear before the thunderclast caught him.{{book ref|sa3|120}} After being too injured to continue, he loaned Maya to [[Hrdalm]], to which she responded with a sense of resigned agreement.{{book ref|sa3|120}}

### Resurrection
{{quote
|She was the only judge who ever mattered, and today was her chance to offer judgment. You passed.
|[[Blended]] to [[Adolin]]{{book ref|sa4|94}}
}}

Maya has been at least partially revived.{{book ref|sa4|94}} She regains consciousness during Adolin's trial by witness in [[Lasting Integrity]] while under physical restraint and emotional stress, in order to prevent the honorspren judge from using her as a witness to condemn Adolin for the [[Recreance]].{{book ref|sa4|94}} Maya explains that she willingly took that risk, knowing that it was possible that the spren would die when their Radiants forsook their oaths. She did not know that she would become a deadeye, but did accept the possibility of death.

Once partially revived, Maya did not return fully to her pre-deadeye form. Her eyes remain scratched out, but she is able to speak and converse. Her thoughts and speech seem slightly slow or off, although that may simply be due to being newly revived and overwhelmed. Being revived did not create a [[Nahel bond]] between Adolin and Maya, though Adolin notes that he can sense her emotions and awareness, which is not part of a Nahel bond.{{book ref|sa4|94}} 

Upon their return to [[Urithiru]], Maya began to change relatively quickly, having been reinvigorated by the light of the tower. This is when she and Adolin developed the ability to speak into each other’s minds and see through each other’s eyes.{{book ref|sa5|34}}

### Battle of Azimir
[[Adolin]] brought Maya with him to the [[Battle of Azimir]] on the third of the final ten days.{{book ref|sa5|34}} They talked about strategy together, and Maya explained doing so helped her focus. She, from [[Shadesmar]], scouted out the enemy forces and reported on them to him.{{book ref|sa5|39}} He fought the first waves of the siege using her. During the fight, Maya demonstrated the ability to change shape and be summoned instantaneously, like a living Radiant [[Shardblade]].{{book ref|sa5|41}} After the battle, Adolin asked if she wanted to give his soldiers a chance to feel what it was like to hold a Shardblade. She wanted to join the men's celebrations, so she dulled her edge so that they could safely hold her.{{book ref|sa5|42}} 

The night of the third day, Maya spoke with Adolin, worrying that their reinforcements might be blocked by the [[Fused]]. She suggested that she go through Shadesmar to recruit [[spren]], insisting that they could be helpful. Adolin agreed, and so she left.{{book ref|sa5|42}} As she traveled, she could communicate a limited amount with Adolin, but only feelings of reassurance{{book ref|sa5|67}}{{book ref|sa5|76}} and then singular words.{{book ref|sa5|80}} She continued to travel and find [[deadeye]]s for five days, and by the ninth day, she had gotten close enough to returning to be able to communicate more.{{book ref|sa5|119}} On the night of the ninth day, Adolin was fighting on the front lines of the siege, and began to lose hope. Maya spoke to his mind, encouraging him and reassuring him that she was almost back.{{book ref|sa5|125}}{{book ref|sa5|127}}

{{image|Founding of the Unoathed by Gar leyva.jpg|As a Blade with [[Adolin]] and the Shards of the first [[Unoathed]]|side=right|width=400px}}
On the morning of the tenth day, as Adolin was fighting [[Abidi|Abidi the Monarch]], she finally arrived back at Azir. She brought around fifty [[deadeye]]s with her, and nine of them, together with Maya herself, formed the [[Unoathed]].{{book ref|sa5|135}} The Unoathed were able to hold the throne room until the deadline arrived, ending the conflict officially, with [[Azimir]], and by extension the entire country of [[Azir]], safe.{{book ref|sa5|136}}

### Post-Stormfall
After [[Stormfall]], Maya traveled to [[Urithiru]] through [[Shadesmar]]. As [[Knights_Radiant|Radiants]] could no longer use their powers, and the [[Oathgates]] no longer functioned, this was the only way they had to reach Urithiru. She returned to report to Adolin and the other leaders at [[Azimir]] that [[Dalinar]] was dead and that Urithiru had been surrounded by “a strange glass bubble” which prevented people traveling to or from it in the [[Physical Realm]]. She also relayed the fact that Radiants at Urithiru could still use their powers, and that [[Ba-Ado-Mishram]] had been freed.{{book ref|sa5|147}}

Ba-Ado-Mishram’s liberation began healing all [[deadeye]]s across [[Roshar]]. For Maya, this included the return of her eyes, which until that point, had been scratched out.{{book ref|sa5|147}}
