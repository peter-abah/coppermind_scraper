> Our wight is quite friendly! Rarely curdles the milk, and once fixed my shoes with no offering. Hid a dead mouse in them though.
> — Leof to Sefawynn

**Leof** is a resident of the preserve outside of [[Wellbury]], and a follower of [[Yazad (Frugal Wizard)|Yazad]]. He acts as the door guard at night, and also tends to the horses.

## Appearance and Personality
Leof is elderly, and except for a few stray strands of hair on his head that stick straight up, he is mostly bald.  He also has a long beard with some brown streaks still in it. 

He worships [[Zoroaster|Ahura Mazda]], and he believes in [[wight]]s and [[aelv]]s. He cares about [[Yazad (Frugal Wizard)|Yazad]] and the people of the preserve, but he is wary of strangers.

## History
Some time ago, Leof and other residents of the preserve had warned [[Yazad (Frugal Wizard)|Yazad]] not to try preaching to the [[Hordaland|Hordamen]]. Despite the warning, Yazad left the preserve alone and was captured by the Hordamen, only to be rescued by [[Runian]], [[Sefawynn]] and [[Ealstan]]. That night, Leof was teary and happy to see Yazad when he returned along with the rescuers. Leof opened the door he had been guarding for the group, and then took the horses. He seemed to recognize Sefawynn as a [[skop]], but he informed her that her services wouldn't be required at the preserve as their [[wight]] was friendly.

After seeing that the horses were tended to, Leof returned inside the dwelling where the residents and guests were sleeping, and he woke a boy to watch the horses. He then put some more wood on the fire, and sat back down on his stool by the door to keep watch. He also kept a wary eye on Runian, who was still awake near the fire, as Leof believed he was an [[aelv]]. However, Runian smugly taunted Leof by pulling a stick from the fire and eating the charcoal from the burned end, and holding up pages he was reading from his book. The writing on the pages made a panicked Leof turn away.

> What manner of wight could do this...?
> — Leof in awe of the wight

Early the next morning, as the people of the preserve awoke, Leof dozed off in a corner of the room. A short time later, he woke up and gathered outside around the front of the preserve with many other gawking residents. There, small requests and offerings to their wight had been turned into a pile of around twenty woven mats, and an enormous mound of butter. Leof wondered aloud about the kind of wight that could achieve such a feat, and held one of the mats reverently. 

It is possible that Leof was among the faithful followers of Yazad who helped to smuggle Runian, Sefawynn, and Ealstan into [[Wellbury]] that evening.
