**Yolen** is a planet in the [[cosmere]] and [[Yolish system]] where humans first appeared, and where the [[Shattering of Adonalsium]] happened.{{wob ref|11602}}{{wob ref|3919}}{{rpg ref|slwg|85}} It's the homeworld of all the original [[Vessel]]s, along with [[Hoid]] and [[Frost]].{{wob ref|4736}}{{wob ref|8343}} The inhabitants of Yolen are called the Yolish.{{book ref|sa3|part=ars}}

## Geography and Ecology
Very little is known about Yolish geography, other than that it has some unusual features.{{wob ref|3377}} Its gravity and other measurements are considered the **cosmere standard** by [[cosmere]]-aware scholars like [[Khriss]].{{au ref|Scadrian}} Its ecology is divided into two distinct parts. Parts of it are similar to post-[[Catacendre]] [[Scadrial]], with similar flora and fauna. Other parts are covered in **fainlife**, an invasive ecosystem.{{au ref|Scadrian}}{{wob ref|14342}} Although fain has the shape of ordinary flora, the plants and land overtaken by it are bone-white.{{book ref|tsm|10}} There is something similar to a [[perpendicularity]] on Yolen, despite the lack of a Shard.{{wob ref|14885}}

Yolen is home to three sapient races: humans, [[Dragon (cosmere)|dragons]] and [[Sho Del]].{{wob ref|3768}}{{book ref|tsm|10}} By [[Roshar]]an year {{Rosharan date|1174}}, [[Frost]] is the only known inhabitant, and though there are more people living there, the Yolish population is rather small.{{wob ref|2565}}{{wob ref|6545}} While not all humans originated from Yolen, it was the first world on which they've appeared.{{wob ref|822}} Many languages currently in use across the [[cosmere]] are derived from Yolish ones.{{wob ref|3773}}{{wob ref|14900}}

Approximately half{{book ref|sa5|epilogue}} of the trees on Yolen, presumably mainly in fain areas, are bone-white.{{book ref|tsm|10}} Yolen’s sky is dominated by a feature known as the **greatstar of Yolen**, a glowing hole in the upper atmosphere. The dragons have an entire flying city up near the greatstar.{{book ref|iote|30}} In addition to this, the dragons have built **skyways**, likely some sort of road they use when flying. The Yolish skyways are described as ‘glittering’.{{book ref|iote|40}}

Yolen has a strange astronomy that would be impossible under ordinary laws of physics,{{wob ref|15595}} furthermore, there is a "weirdness" about the planet that makes discussion about location complicated.{{wob ref|11602}} 

Yolen is somehow hidden and inaccessible by the time of the [[Catacendre]]; how or for what reason, is currently unknown. Most information concerning Yolen known amongst arcanists and other researchers comes from stories, rather than on-site research.{{msh ref|3|2}}

## Culture
Before the Shards started getting involved with other planets, Yolen was one of the most technologically advanced planets in the cosmere, having reached the Bronze Age.{{wob ref|16320}} They were not aware of what the stars were, and many philosophers would debate at length on the subject.{{book ref|tsm|10}} What philosophical outlooks were present is unclear, but it is known that they possessed an understanding of philosophy and often had debates on the matter.{{book ref|tsm|10}}

At least some cultures on the planet had a form of economy and knew how to make alcohol.{{book ref|tsm|10}}{{book ref|sa2|epilogue}} However, the form the economy or currency took is currently unclear. Human society was divided into myriad city-states,{{book ref|sa5|104}} whilst dragon society developed what was deemed as a great civilization, and were far more technologically advanced than humans.{{book ref|sa5|100}}

Yolen has fallen behind technologically compared to many other planets in the cosmere. This is due to certain things that they can access and [[Shard]]ic influence.{{wob ref|16320}}

Within parts of Yolen, dragons were worshipped as gods. Dragons who did not wish to fulfill this role granted to them, such as [[Koravellium Avast]], were deemed heretical.{{cite}}{{wob ref|16142}} Many dragons had large palaces; places filled with their priests and servants. Each of these palaces had an upper palace and a lower palace, the upper palace was large enough to house a dragon in their draconic form.{{book ref|ilte|prologue}}

### Ethnicity and Language
Little is known about Yolish ethnic groups or cultures, except that it had a wide diversity of skin tones and features.{{book ref|sa3|56}}{{book ref|sa1|75}} Some groups on Yolen had a skin tone comparable to the Rosharan [[Makabakam|Makabaki]] people.{{book ref|sa5|104}} Various peoples from Yolen are seen to not possess epicanthic folds.{{book ref|sa3|56}}{{book ref|sa3|122}} 

Yolen is also known to have at least one major language, which is the ancestor to various languages across the cosmere.{{wob ref|3773}}{{wob ref|14900}}{{book ref|tsm|39}} Another language from Yolen that has been referenced is the "language of the gods," from which the word Microkinesis is derived. Further details concerning this language, or what individuals spoke it, are currently unknown.{{book ref|sa5|104}}

## History
Yolen is one of the oldest inhabited worlds in the cosmere, and the first on which humans have appeared.{{wob ref|822}} It's also the world of [[Adonalsium]], the entity believed to be the cosmere's creator deity.{{wob ref|11398}} Yolen had gods other than Adonalsium, the nature of these gods is unknown, however, at least some of them are known to have died.{{book ref|wb|32}}{{book ref|sa5|128}}{{wob ref|1921}} These gods predated the [[Dragon (cosmere)|dragons]], who themselves predated humanity.{{book ref|sa5|128}}

Over time, humanity developed various city-states across Yolen.{{book ref|sa5|104}} However, dragon society remained far more advanced than human society up to the eventual Shattering of Adonalsium, having developed what was considered to be, by [[Tanavast]], a great civilization.{{book ref|sa5|100}} Even millennia after the Shattering, dragons were considered to be the cosmere’s greatest creators, having built wonders such as the skyways and the city of [[Lar'Cal]]. At some point in Yolen’s history, shortly before the Shattering, a rivalry began between the fain and the unfain.{{book ref|iote|40}}

For some reason or another, various groups of humans decided to leave Yolen and travel across the cosmere, settling and forming societies across Yolen and on various planets.{{wob ref|16320}} Though these human exclaves, for some reason or another, developed far slower than those that remained on Yolen, being left stuck using stone tools while Yolen advanced to making use of bronze and other metals.{{wob ref|16320}}

At several points in Yolen's history, [[Microkinesis]] was misused to explosive effect and resulting in disastrous consequences that are not fully understood{{book ref|sa4|part=ars}}, but likely caused massive devastation of the Yolish population due to atomic explosions set off by Microkinesis. Dragons also went to war with eachother, using mortals as their soldiers.{{book ref|iote|40}}

People worldhopped to other planets on rare occasions over Yolen's history.{{wob ref|13219}}

At some point, a king within a city-state decided to have a test to prove his hypothesis that people of the lower class had equal capacity to learn and grow as those in high society. To prove this he took a child named [[Jerick]], the son of a lumberworker, and put him through various tests to prove his intellectual and social capacity. Hoid was involved in the third test, judging the creativity of a poem written by Jerick. However, Jerick went off to war and did not show up, thus losing the contest.{{book ref|sa5|127}}{{book ref|sa5|128}} The contest, in some way, led to a coup in which Jerick took part. During the coup he killed the barons of the city-state. The ultimate result of the coup is currently unknown.{{book ref|sa5|128}}

### The Shattering of Adonalsium
At some point, eighteen Yolish people,{{wob ref|7939}}{{wob ref|10773}}{{book ref|tress|56}}{{book ref|iote|47}} not all of whom were human,{{wob ref|4736}} came together to kill Adonalsium for various reasons.{{msh ref|3|2}} The [[Shattering of Adonalsium]], as it came to be known, dramatically altered the entire cosmere. All [[Investiture]] that once belonged to Adonalsium was divided between the sixteen [[Shard]]s created by its death, and the preexisting magic systems were altered in how they functioned.{{wob ref|8605}}{{wob ref|3314}}

Following the Shattering, everyone present except [[Hoid]] and [[Frost]] took up a Shard.{{wob ref|10773}}{{book ref|sa4|99}}{{book ref|iote|47}} Most Shards left Yolen to go their own separate ways.{{epigraph ref|sa3|39}} Many humans have also abandoned it, spreading across the cosmere.{{wob ref|3773}} Over the following millennia, it has become hidden in some way, shrouded from prying eyes, making reaching and researching it extremely difficult.{{msh ref|3|2}} By the time of the [[True Desolation]] on Roshar, its remaining population is small.{{wob ref|6545}}

Due to certain factors, technological advancement on Yolen after the Shattering progressed slowly, with other planets consistently surpassing it in technological advancement.{{wob ref|16320}} For millennia, the dragons native to Yolen had kept the secret that it was the source of nearly all life in the cosmere and the origin of the Shards. However, a few decades before the discovery of the Drominad perpendicularity, that news was revealed quite dramatically.{{book ref|iote|30}} Afterwards, Yolen became a popular, although tightly regulated, tourist destination, with some people having to wait decades before they are allowed to visit.{{book ref|iote|25}}

## Magic
Yolen appears to have several magic systems, though only two are known.{{wob ref|15144}}

### Lightweaving
{{for|Yolish Lightweaving}}

Lightweaving is the cosmere's original illusion magic. It's capable of creating auditory and visual manifestations, either independent or attached to a host.{{book ref|sa3|part=ars}} It can produce disguises, as well as alter the appearance of other substances, like smoke.{{wob ref|5775}}{{wob ref|8329}} It's most similar to the [[Surge]] of [[Illumination]] from [[Roshar]], albeit less restrictive, not requiring a [[spren]] bond.{{wob ref|8909}} Hoid learned this original form of Lightweaving on Yolen.{{book ref|warbreaker|32}}

The Shattering appears to have changed something in Lightweaving, as the original magic is only semi-functional.{{wob ref|11587}}

### Microkinesis
{{for|Microkinesis}}
Microkinesis is a form of magic related to the manipulation of atomic particles, known as **[[axi]]**,{{book ref|sa4|part=ars}}{{wob ref|11359}} The power allows its users to observe atoms and interact with them directly.{{wob ref|3812}} Axi can also be used to refer to the smallest theoretical division of matter.{{wob ref|11359}}

It can be used to set off atomic explosions, and this has happened several times in the past. Some Invested Arts, such as Stoneshaping, are based on Microkinesis, albiet with limitations placed upon them by the resident Shard.{{book ref|sa4|part=ars}}

## Trivia
* [[Brandon Sanderson]] named Yolen after [[wikipedia:Jane Yolen|Jane Yolen]], who wrote *Dragon's Blood*, one of the early fantasy books he read.{{wob ref|2656}}
* The tree branch with writing on it from [[Mraize]]'s collection comes from Yolen.{{wob ref|5331}}
* The [[Shattered Plains]], as well as the [[Bridge Four]] plot and the character of [[Lunamor]], were originally on Yolen. Brandon moved them to [[Roshar]] upon seeing concept art made by [[Ben McSweeney]] for *[[The Way of Kings]]*.{{wob ref|8601}}
* There is a saying on Yolen: "May two worlds become one to you." This is not referring to [[Realms]].{{wob ref|2741}}
* While gods existed and died on Yolen prior to the Shattering, these gods were not Shards.{{wob ref|1921}} Current information suggests these gods were dragons.{{wob ref|16142}}
* Yolen is the setting of the unpublished novels *[[The Liar of Partinel]]*, *[[The Lightweaver of Rens]]* and *[[Dragonsteel Prime]]*.{{wob ref|5984}} *[[Dragonsteel (series)|Dragonsteel]]* proper, the prequel to the cosmere as a whole, will be set there.{{wob ref|1024}}
* Yolen has Earth-like physical parameters, that is to say, its gravity and radius are comparable to Earth.{{au ref|scadrial}}{{wob ref|669}}
* In non-canon, trunked works the non-fain ecosystem was referred to as trunelife. This name has not yet been canonized.{{wob ref|13991}}{{wob ref|14409}}
