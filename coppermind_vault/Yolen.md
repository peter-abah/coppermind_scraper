**Yolen** is a planet in the [[cosmere]] and [[Yolish system]] where humans first appeared, and where the [[Shattering of Adonalsium]] happened. It's the homeworld of all the original [[Vessel]]s, along with [[Hoid]] and [[Frost]]. The inhabitants of Yolen are called the Yolish.

## Geography and Ecology
Very little is known about Yolish geography, other than that it has some unusual features. Its gravity and other measurements are considered the **cosmere standard** by [[cosmere]]-aware scholars like [[Khriss]]. Its ecology is divided into two distinct parts. Parts of it are similar to post-[[Catacendre]] [[Scadrial]], with similar flora and fauna. Other parts are covered in **fainlife**, an invasive ecosystem. Although fain has the shape of ordinary flora, the plants and land overtaken by it are bone-white. There is something similar to a [[perpendicularity]] on Yolen, despite the lack of a Shard.

Yolen is home to three sapient races: humans, [[Dragon (cosmere)|dragons]] and [[Sho Del]]. By [[Roshar]]an year , [[Frost]] is the only known inhabitant, and though there are more people living there, the Yolish population is rather small. While not all humans originated from Yolen, it was the first world on which they've appeared. Many languages currently in use across the [[cosmere]] are derived from Yolish ones.

Approximately half of the trees on Yolen, presumably mainly in fain areas, are bone-white. Yolen’s sky is dominated by a feature known as the **greatstar of Yolen**, a glowing hole in the upper atmosphere. The dragons have an entire flying city up near the greatstar. In addition to this, the dragons have built **skyways**, likely some sort of road they use when flying. The Yolish skyways are described as ‘glittering’.

Yolen has a strange astronomy that would be impossible under ordinary laws of physics, furthermore, there is a "weirdness" about the planet that makes discussion about location complicated. 

Yolen is somehow hidden and inaccessible by the time of the [[Catacendre]]; how or for what reason, is currently unknown. Most information concerning Yolen known amongst arcanists and other researchers comes from stories, rather than on-site research.

## Culture
Before the Shards started getting involved with other planets, Yolen was one of the most technologically advanced planets in the cosmere, having reached the Bronze Age. They were not aware of what the stars were, and many philosophers would debate at length on the subject. What philosophical outlooks were present is unclear, but it is known that they possessed an understanding of philosophy and often had debates on the matter.

At least some cultures on the planet had a form of economy and knew how to make alcohol. However, the form the economy or currency took is currently unclear. Human society was divided into myriad city-states, whilst dragon society developed what was deemed as a great civilization, and were far more technologically advanced than humans.

Yolen has fallen behind technologically compared to many other planets in the cosmere. This is due to certain things that they can access and [[Shard]]ic influence.

Within parts of Yolen, dragons were worshipped as gods. Dragons who did not wish to fulfill this role granted to them, such as [[Koravellium Avast]], were deemed heretical. Many dragons had large palaces; places filled with their priests and servants. Each of these palaces had an upper palace and a lower palace, the upper palace was large enough to house a dragon in their draconic form.

### Ethnicity and Language
Little is known about Yolish ethnic groups or cultures, except that it had a wide diversity of skin tones and features. Some groups on Yolen had a skin tone comparable to the Rosharan [[Makabakam|Makabaki]] people. Various peoples from Yolen are seen to not possess epicanthic folds. 

Yolen is also known to have at least one major language, which is the ancestor to various languages across the cosmere. Another language from Yolen that has been referenced is the "language of the gods," from which the word Microkinesis is derived. Further details concerning this language, or what individuals spoke it, are currently unknown.

## History
Yolen is one of the oldest inhabited worlds in the cosmere, and the first on which humans have appeared. It's also the world of [[Adonalsium]], the entity believed to be the cosmere's creator deity. Yolen had gods other than Adonalsium, the nature of these gods is unknown, however, at least some of them are known to have died. These gods predated the [[Dragon (cosmere)|dragons]], who themselves predated humanity.

Over time, humanity developed various city-states across Yolen. However, dragon society remained far more advanced than human society up to the eventual Shattering of Adonalsium, having developed what was considered to be, by [[Tanavast]], a great civilization. Even millennia after the Shattering, dragons were considered to be the cosmere’s greatest creators, having built wonders such as the skyways and the city of [[Lar'Cal]]. At some point in Yolen’s history, shortly before the Shattering, a rivalry began between the fain and the unfain.

For some reason or another, various groups of humans decided to leave Yolen and travel across the cosmere, settling and forming societies across Yolen and on various planets. Though these human exclaves, for some reason or another, developed far slower than those that remained on Yolen, being left stuck using stone tools while Yolen advanced to making use of bronze and other metals.

At several points in Yolen's history, [[Microkinesis]] was misused to explosive effect and resulting in disastrous consequences that are not fully understood, but likely caused massive devastation of the Yolish population due to atomic explosions set off by Microkinesis. Dragons also went to war with eachother, using mortals as their soldiers.

People worldhopped to other planets on rare occasions over Yolen's history.

At some point, a king within a city-state decided to have a test to prove his hypothesis that people of the lower class had equal capacity to learn and grow as those in high society. To prove this he took a child named [[Jerick]], the son of a lumberworker, and put him through various tests to prove his intellectual and social capacity. Hoid was involved in the third test, judging the creativity of a poem written by Jerick. However, Jerick went off to war and did not show up, thus losing the contest. The contest, in some way, led to a coup in which Jerick took part. During the coup he killed the barons of the city-state. The ultimate result of the coup is currently unknown.

> — [[Hoid]] referencing Yolen while explaining his style of storytelling to Siri.

### The Shattering of Adonalsium
At some point, eighteen Yolish people, not all of whom were human, came together to kill Adonalsium for various reasons. The [[Shattering of Adonalsium]], as it came to be known, dramatically altered the entire cosmere. All [[Investiture]] that once belonged to Adonalsium was divided between the sixteen [[Shard]]s created by its death, and the preexisting magic systems were altered in how they functioned.

Following the Shattering, everyone present except [[Hoid]] and [[Frost]] took up a Shard. Most Shards left Yolen to go their own separate ways. Many humans have also abandoned it, spreading across the cosmere. Over the following millennia, it has become hidden in some way, shrouded from prying eyes, making reaching and researching it extremely difficult. By the time of the [[True Desolation]] on Roshar, its remaining population is small.

Due to certain factors, technological advancement on Yolen after the Shattering progressed slowly, with other planets consistently surpassing it in technological advancement. For millennia, the dragons native to Yolen had kept the secret that it was the source of nearly all life in the cosmere and the origin of the Shards. However, a few decades before the discovery of the Drominad perpendicularity, that news was revealed quite dramatically. Afterwards, Yolen became a popular, although tightly regulated, tourist destination, with some people having to wait decades before they are allowed to visit.

## Magic
Yolen appears to have several magic systems, though only two are known.

### Lightweaving

Lightweaving is the cosmere's original illusion magic. It's capable of creating auditory and visual manifestations, either independent or attached to a host. It can produce disguises, as well as alter the appearance of other substances, like smoke. It's most similar to the [[Surge]] of [[Illumination]] from [[Roshar]], albeit less restrictive, not requiring a [[spren]] bond. Hoid learned this original form of Lightweaving on Yolen.

The Shattering appears to have changed something in Lightweaving, as the original magic is only semi-functional.

### Microkinesis

Microkinesis is a form of magic related to the manipulation of atomic particles, known as **[[axi]]**, The power allows its users to observe atoms and interact with them directly. Axi can also be used to refer to the smallest theoretical division of matter.

It can be used to set off atomic explosions, and this has happened several times in the past. Some Invested Arts, such as Stoneshaping, are based on Microkinesis, albiet with limitations placed upon them by the resident Shard.

## Trivia
* [[Brandon Sanderson]] named Yolen after [[wikipedia:Jane Yolen|Jane Yolen]], who wrote *Dragon's Blood*, one of the early fantasy books he read.
* The tree branch with writing on it from [[Mraize]]'s collection comes from Yolen.
* The [[Shattered Plains]], as well as the [[Bridge Four]] plot and the character of [[Lunamor]], were originally on Yolen. Brandon moved them to [[Roshar]] upon seeing concept art made by [[Ben McSweeney]] for *[[The Way of Kings]]*.
* There is a saying on Yolen: "May two worlds become one to you." This is not referring to [[Realms]].
* While gods existed and died on Yolen prior to the Shattering, these gods were not Shards. Current information suggests these gods were dragons.
* Yolen is the setting of the unpublished novels *[[The Liar of Partinel]]*, *[[The Lightweaver of Rens]]* and *[[Dragonsteel Prime]]*. *[[Dragonsteel (series)|Dragonsteel]]* proper, the prequel to the cosmere as a whole, will be set there.
* Yolen has Earth-like physical parameters, that is to say, its gravity and radius are comparable to Earth.
* In non-canon, trunked works the non-fain ecosystem was referred to as trunelife. This name has not yet been canonized.
