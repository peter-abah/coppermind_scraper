**Ba-Ado-Mishram,** sometimes called just **Mishram**,{{epigraph ref|sa4|97}} or the **Granter of Forms**,{{book ref|sa5|prologue}} is one of the [[Unmade]],{{epigraph ref|sa3|106}} an ancient and terrible [[spren]] of [[Odium]].{{book ref|sa3|62}} She is a sapient Unmade{{epigraph ref|sa3|106}} and is crafty and conniving.{{book ref|sa4|i|2}} She was a primary instigator of the [[False Desolation]], leading to her imprisonment in the [[Spiritual Realm]] by [[Honor]] and the [[Knights Radiant]],{{epigraph ref|sa3|80}}{{book ref|sa4|73}}{{book ref|sa5|87}} which put the vast majority of [[singer]]s into slaveform,{{book ref|sa3|17}}{{book ref|sa5|87}} and affected all spren on [[Roshar]],{{book ref|sa4|49}}{{epigraph ref|sa4|97}}{{book ref|sa5|121}} and even the planet itself.{{book ref|sa5|87}}{{book ref|sa5|121}}{{book ref|sa5|141}} She was eventually freed and the world restored on {{rosharan date|1175|5|2|5}}, on the onset of the [[True Desolation]].{{book ref|sa5|141}}

## Appearance and Personality

### Personality
Ba-Ado-Mishram's personality varies greatly from before and after her imprisonment, as she was affected by the betrayal of humanity and Honor,{{book ref|sa5|87}} and from her long period of isolation in the Spiritual Realm.

==== Pre-Imprisonment ====
Prior to her imprisonment, she viewed humans with more fondness and believed that peace between them and singers was a possibility, and she wished for unity and an end to the war and bloodshed.{{book ref|sa5|76}}{{book ref|sa5|87}} Despite this, she refused to heal humans when she found them injured in battles, but still willingly sang for them to try and make their transition more peaceful.{{book ref|sa5|76}} 

Despite this sympathy and wish for peace, she believed that humanity would only stop killing the singers if they were completely conquered and controlled. This came from her belief that humanity would never want peace with the singers. {{book ref|sa5|76}}{{book ref|sa5|121}}

She believed that the Singers deserved a far better god than Rayse,{{book ref|sa5|76}} this is likely what led her to try and supplant him.{{book ref|sa5|i|6}}{{book ref|sa5|86}} She believed that Odium would never allow the wars and suffering to end, for they serve him.{{book ref|sa5|76}}

When the Singers who would come to be known as the [[Listener]]s abandoned her and the war, she accepted their choice and let them go.{{book ref|sa5|121}} 

She is stated to be crafty and conniving by [[Sja-anat]].{{book ref|sa4|i|2}}

==== Post-Imprisonment ====
After her betrayal and imprisonment in the Spiritual Realm, she was left with nothing but a deep and violent hatred for humanity that only grew with time, as she sees all humans as inherently evil due to their betrayal of her.{{book ref|sa5|141}} This hatred leads her to being very aggressive towards humans, and to make violent threats towards them.{{book ref|sa5|76}} She also lost her belief in the possibility of peace between humans and singers, and couldn't understand why humans and singers would have any positive relationship.{{book ref|sa5|141}} 

Despite her hatred, she relied on a group of humans to find her and free her from her prison, thus she led them to her. Though she rationalized this to herself by saying she did it for the purposes of killing them.{{book ref|sa5|132}}

### Appearance
Ba-Ado-Mishram variably appears as either a large femalen singer, nine times average size for her species;{{book ref|sa5|121}} or at times somewhat shorter than average.{{book ref|sa5|87}} Regardless of the size she manifests at, her skin is coloured by alternating lines of white, red, and black.{{book ref|sa5|87}} She also, at times, appears as a black cloud with limbs forming out of it which carry it across the ground.{{book ref|sa5|76}}

## Mythology and Lore

In modern times, [[Hessi]] believed Ba-Ado-Mishram to be a highprincess of the [[Voidbringer]]s, and was their commander in some [[Desolation]]s.{{epigraph ref|sa3|106}} There was no record of Ba-Ado-Mishram in the present day, and so Hessi suspected that she returned to Damnation or was destroyed in [[Aharietiam]].{{epigraph ref|sa3|107}} Hessi's *Mythica* also states that she is an elusive figure, and that the true reason for her absence in the modern day is unknown.{{file ref|Page from Mythica - Ba-Ado-Mishram.jpg|Page from Mythica - Ba-Ado-Mishram}}

However, the reason why Ba-Ado-Mishram is absent in the modern era is because she was imprisoned during the [[False Desolation]], around the time of the [[Recreance]].{{epigraph ref|sa3|80}} It is also possible that the reason why there are legends of her being the commander of the enemy is because of her role in the False Desolation as well, rather than actually being a commander during actual Desolations with the [[Fused]].

## Attributes and Abilities
### Healer
Prior to absorbing the Well of Control and becoming the Granter of Forms, Ba-Ado-Mishram travelled the battlefield and healed injured Singers.{{book ref|sa5|76}}

### Granter of Forms
Ba-Ado-Mishram has some of the most powerful capabilities of individuals outside of [[Shard]]s. She was able to Connect with singers on a massive scale, Connecting with all of them (except the [[listener]]s) across the entirety of Roshar,{{epigraph ref|sa3|80}} which suggests that it was not a short-ranged, local process near Ba-Ado-Mishram--like [[Moelach]] or [[Nergaoul]]'s effects--but a continent-wide display of power.
{{sidequote
| Singer, this will not serve you. I stepped into God’s role. I took his pool, his perpendicularity, and am tied to every singer who holds a form. To every spren, to every fiber of Roshar.
| Ba-Ado-Mishram{{book ref|sa5|121}}
|side=right|size=200px
}} 

Following her absorption of the Well of Control, Ba-Ado-Mishram was able to supply the singers with [[Voidlight]] and forms of power, making singers into [[Regal]]s.{{epigraph ref|sa3|80}}{{book ref|sa3|i|7}}{{book ref|sa4|73}}{{book ref|sa5|121}} This shows she has access to a huge quantity of [[Investiture]] on a scale other Unmade lack. This ability was compared to [[Odium]] in previous Desolations, which likely means that Ba-Ado-Mishram could not supply infinite Voidlight to the Regals, as the Fused in Desolations did not have access to infinite Voidlight either.{{wob ref|11865}} It is unknown whether her Connection let Ba-Ado-Mishram speak to singers directly as Odium does, or command the conflict directly.

The Knights Radiant did not know Ba-Ado-Mishram could Connect with the singers, and did not know how she managed to do it.{{epigraph ref|sa3|80}} She had not done this prior to the False Desolation.{{book ref|sa4|73}} It was later discovered that she managed to Connect to Roshar and its people and spren the way she did due to her making use of the [[Well of Control]].{{book ref|sa5|87}}{{book ref|sa5|121}} She claimed to be tied to every fibre of Roshar, and Honor himself spoke of how the tones of the planet chose her, and the planet itself aligned with her, after she absorbed the power of the Well of Control.{{book ref|sa5|121}}{{book ref|sa5|124}} The power also protected her from Odium's touch.{{book ref|sa5|122}} The manner in which she fully Connected to the planet displays various similarities to the process of a Shard Investing a world. 

Her other abilities are unclear aside from her Connections and her healing abilities. If she was in fact a highprincess of the Voidbringers, or a commander of the enemy during actual Desolations,{{epigraph ref|sa3|106}} she would likely need to be powerful or fearsome for [[Fused]] to follow her. However, it is said that the Radiants feared [[Sja-anat]] most of all Unmade,{{epigraph ref|sa3|97}} not Ba-Ado-Mishram, so it is possible she was not the biggest threat to the Radiants.

She was imprisoned from the Recreance to the [[Night of Sorrows]] on {{rosharan date|1175|5|2|5}},{{book ref|sa5|87}}{{book ref|sa5|141}} and she would not fade in her gemstone like a [[Herald]] would.{{epigraph ref|sa4|96}} It is unclear how much time she experienced when trapped in the Spiritual Realm. 

### Realmatics
In the days leading up to the True Desolation, she began to be capable of manifesting her face and voice in the Cognitive Realm and controlling visions within the Spiritual Realm.{{book ref|sa5|54}}{{book ref|sa5|84}}

When not imprisoned in the heliodor, she is capable of pulling entities in the Spiritual Realm into the Cognitive Realm.{{book ref|sa5|141}} She finds herself more comfortable in the Cognitive Realm, compared to the Physical Realm.{{book ref|sa5|143}}

### Vessel Candidate
She is a possible candidate as the Vessel of the Shard of Odium, as the Shard itself sees her as a good Vessel, better than either Rayse or Taravangian.{{book ref|sa5|i|6}}{{book ref|sa5|86}} This, and her resonance with Roshar and its tones, are likely what led to her being considered one of the only rivals to Odium's Vessel.{{book ref|sa5|prologue}}{{book ref|sa5|86}}

==History==
===Early History===
Like all Unmade, Ba-Ado-Mishram's true origin is unknown, but she most likely existed in some form before becoming an Unmade, however what she was before this is unknown.{{book ref|sa4|i|2}}{{book ref|sa3|77}}{{book ref|sa3|84}}{{book ref|sa3|116}} She was made into one of the Unmade at some point after the creation of the [[Herald]]s as part of an arms race between Odium and Honor, but came to exist before the formation of the [[Knights Radiant]].{{book ref|sa5|120}}

Her role during the Desolations is unclear, but she was said to be a highprincess of the enemy, and a commander in some Desolations.{{epigraph ref|sa3|106}} Between Desolations, she was left behind on Roshar.{{book ref|sa5|95}}{{book ref|sa5|80}}

Four thousand five hundred and fifty years before the [[Night of Sorrows]], Ba-Ado-Mishram approached Honor and asked if he wanted peace, and if he would agree to make peace with her if Odium was unable to stop him from doing so. He answered her questions affirmatively, creating an oath with her, and she subsequently left.{{book ref|sa5|120}} Honor would ultimately turn on her, making an oath with Odium to help protect him from her. This betrayal would come to manifest multiple millennia later.{{cite}}

After the Last Desolation, Ba-Ado-Mishram continued to exist on Roshar. Little is known as to her influence on events during this period of the [[Era of Solitude]].

===The False Desolation===

{{sidequote
|Ba-Ado-Mishram has somehow Connected with the parsh people, as [[Odium]] once did. She provides [[Voidlight]] and facilitates forms of power. Our strike team is going to imprison her.
|Drawer 30-20, fourth emerald{{epigraph ref|sa3|80}}
|side=left|size=500px
}}

About two thousand five hundred years after Aharietiam,{{book ref|sa1|prologue}}{{book ref|sa3|56}} Ba-Ado-Mishram [[Connection|Connected]] with the vast majority of [[singer]]s on Roshar, using the [[Well of Control]], which she absorbed fully.{{book ref|sa5|121}} The only singers free from this Connection were the [[listeners]].{{cite}} Roshar, in response, aligned with her and the tones choose her. Her Connection to the planet and its tones filtered down to the Spren, connecting her to all spren as well.{{book ref|sa5|121}}{{book ref|sa5|124}} She granted the singers forms of powers--making them into [[Regals]]--and provided them with [[Voidlight]].{{epigraph ref|sa3|80}} 

Her actions initiated a new war, which was considered a different kind of Desolation, for it was one without the Fused. Scholars would later term it the [[False Desolation]].{{book ref|sa3|56}} Though many scholars and ardents reject that this event even occurred, as it contradicted their beliefs that the Heralds pushed all Voidbringers off Roshar during Aharietiam.{{cite}} <!---It's unclear what Ba-Ado-Mishram's motives were in starting this new war, nor is it obvious if she was acting at Odium's wishes.{{book ref|sa4|73}}--->

The [[Knights Radiant]] of the era felt that the singers fought with zeal,{{epigraph ref|sa3|77}} and did not know how Ba-Ado-Mishram could Connect with the singers.{{epigraph ref|sa3|80}} The fighting was particularly intense during this period.{{epigraph ref|sa2|41}} At some point, the singers pressed towards [[Feverstone Keep]], though the Radiants did not understand why the area interested the singers--or perhaps Ba-Ado-Mishram--so much. Some speculated that it had been part of a plan to capture the city of [[Rall Elorim]].{{epigraph ref|sa3|84}}

Her immunity to Odium's touch as a result of her power, and her plans to replace Odium, eventually led Odium to make a deal with Honor.{{book ref|sa5|122}}{{expand}}

The Radiants sought an edge in combat with the singers, and made a coalition of scholar Radiants with the goal of denying the singers their Voidlight.{{epigraph ref|sa3|78}}
They were told by Honor that an Unmade could be captured like regular spren, but it would require a special prison--likely a [[perfect gemstone]]--and [[Melishi]],{{epigraph ref|sa3|79}}{{book ref|sa5|87}} the [[Bondsmith]] of that generation.{{epigraph ref|sa2|58}}{{epigraph ref|sa3|67}} [[Naze-daughter-Kuzodo]] warned of potential side effects of sealing her away when she was Connected to the singers as she was, but it appears these warnings were ignored.{{epigraph ref|sa3|81}} So, the Radiants, assisted by [[Honor]],{{book ref|sa5|87}}{{cite}} formed a strike team to imprison Ba-Ado-Mishram. Melishi was confident in the plan, but other Radiants were uncertain on the effects this would have on the singers. Despite any objections, they hoped that their actions would lead to the end of the war with the singers.{{epigraph ref|sa3|82}} 

They attracted Ba-Ado-Mishram to them by talks of peace and unity, backed by Honor, who promised her he would make peace with her if she was leader fifty years before the Last Desolation.{{book ref|sa5|120}}{{book ref|sa5|87}}

The [[Herald]]s [[Nale]] and [[Kalak]], along with [[Honor]], [[Garith]], and [[Shmone]] were present at Ba-Ado-Mishram's binding.{{book ref|sa4|24}}{{epigraph ref|sa4|94}} Ultimately, the strike team was successful and Ba-Ado-Mishram was captured.{{book ref|sa4|49}}{{book ref|sa4|84}}{{epigraph ref|sa4|97}}

===Effects of the Binding===

{{quote
|Thousands of years ago, something happened that changed the singers. It hurt me too. [...] That terrible act touched the souls of all who belong to Roshar. Spren too.
|The [[Sibling]] to Navani{{book ref|sa4|49}}
}}

As Naze-daughter-Kuzodo predicted,{{epigraph ref|sa3|81}} Ba-Ado-Mishram's binding had massive effects on Roshar. It ripped out both [[Connection]] and [[Identity]] from the singers, which prevented them from being able to act independently.{{book ref|sa3|17}} This Spiritual damage persisted even through the singers' offspring. The few singers that were not part of the False Desolation--the [[listeners]]--termed these singers as slaveforms; singers with no form. Humans used these singers as slaves for two thousand years, until the [[Everstorm]] arrived and healed them.{{book ref|sa3|17}} The singers were furious at humanity for this act of enslavement, and this became a rallying cry for them to fight against humanity in the [[True Desolation]].

{{sidequote
|Trouble is, spren can get stuck in gemstones, and the humans figured this out. End result: Ba-Ado-Mishram got a really cramped prison, and everyone’s souls got seriously messed up.
|[[Ulim]] explaining to [[Venli]]{{book ref|sa4|73}}
|side=right|size=400px
}}
Fighting in the False Desolation occurred near Feverstone Keep,{{epigraph ref|sa3|84}} and since that is the site of many [[Windrunner]]s and [[Stoneward]]s abandoning their oaths,{{book ref|sa2|52}}{{book ref|sa3|56}}

Mishram's binding damaged more than the singers, and according to the [[Sibling]], touched the souls of "all who belong to Roshar," including the spren.{{book ref|sa4|49}} [[Deadeye]]s did not exist prior to the Recreance, so the Unmade's binding could be why the Radiants and spren in the Recreance caused the spren to unexpectedly transform into deadeyes. Furthermore, the very tones and rhythms of Roshar stopped for a period of three seconds in response to her being forcefully disconnected after what she did. It was described as something ripping within the world itself.{{book ref|sa5|87}} A discordant note would continue to vibrate the soul of Roshar in "the most terrible of ways," though it would only be noticed upon its cessation when she was later released.{{book ref|sa5|141}} This sealing away of Ba-Ado-Mishram, in general, left a deep and lasting wound on Roshar,{{epigraph ref|sa4|97}} and its inhabitants.{{book ref|sa4|49}}

The damage to the Sibling disabled [[Urithiru]]. The Sibling lost their ability to produce [[Towerlight]] en masse, and the tower city stopped working.{{book ref|sa4|49}} They could only create small quantities of Towerlight to power a few of the tower's basic [[fabrial]]s. The Sibling lost their power because they could no longer hear the two tones of Roshar, [[Honor]] and [[Cultivation]]'s tones. [[Navani Kholin]] thought this could be because [[Odium]]'s tone became a tone of Roshar as well.{{book ref|sa4|69}}

Ba-Ado-Mishram's binding made the Fused realize that spren could be trapped in gemstones, and a Fused named [[She Who Dreams]] came up with the idea that a Herald's soul could be trapped this way.{{book ref|sa4|84}} This led to the creation of [[Vyre's knife|knives]] with [[raysium]], one of which bound [[Jezrien]]'s soul, killing him.{{epigraph ref|sa4|93}}

==== Death of Tanavast and the Recreance ====
Less directly, Honor's betrayal of Ba-Ado-Mishram, and the damage it caused to Singers, the Spren, and Roshar, led the Shard to abandon him as Vessel. Tanavast was subsequently killed by Odium, though a portion of his soul and his memories remained, going to the [[Stormfather]].{{book ref|sa5|87}}{{book ref|sa5|124}}{{book ref|sa5|126}}

However, before Honor completely left Tanavast, he travelled to Urithiru and showed the Knights Radiant there the destruction they were capable of, and how they could destroy the world. This, along with the effects the binding had on the Singers, spren, and Roshar itself, precipitated the [[Recreance]], where the majority of Knights Radiant would abandon their oaths and break their Nahel bonds.{{book ref|sa5|124}}{{cite}} The Recreance itself led to the creation of a large amount of [[deadeye]]s, due to the intersection of the broken oaths and the effects of the binding of Ba-Ado-Mishram.{{cite}}

### Lead-up to the True Desolation
{{quote
|Why do you want the gemstone that holds Ba-Ado-Mishram, Mraize? What are you intending to do with it? What power do the Ghostbloods seek with a thing that can bind the minds of an entire people?
|Shallan confronting Mraize{{book ref|sa4|115}}
}}

In 1175, during the [[True Desolation]], the location of Ba-Ado-Mishram's gemstone prison remained unknown, but [[Thaidakar]] and the [[Ghostbloods]] sought it.{{book ref|sa5|prologue}} [[Ialai Sadeas]]'s journal which [[Shallan]] acquired at Ialai's death described the Ghostbloods being "obsessed" with Ba-Ado-Mishram. Shallan suspected [[Mraize]] thought that Ba-Ado-Mishram would be in [[Urithiru]], but Shallan realized this must have been wrong, because if that was true, [[Re-Shephir]] would have freed Mishram. Shallan wondered that since Mraize wished to move [[Stormlight]] off Roshar that perhaps Ba-Ado-Mishram could be used for this purpose.{{book ref|sa4|24}}

Kalak believed this was why the Ghostbloods hunted him. Shallan confronted Mraize using her [[seon]] [[Ala]], and demanded to know what the Ghostbloods' plan was with a spren which could bind minds of an entire people. Mraize didn't respond, and Shallan declared that she would find Ba-Ado-Mishram's gemstone before they would.{{book ref|sa4|115}}

In Kalak's journal, which he wrote as he hid in [[Lasting Integrity]], he urged Ba-Ado-Mishram to be found and freed.{{epigraph ref|sa4|97}}

==== Search for Ba-Ado-Mishram ====
On {{rosharan date|1175|5|1|2}}, [[Shallan Davar]], [[Renarin Kholin]], and [[Rlain]] traveled into the Spiritual Realm, followed by [[Mraize]] and [[Iyatil]], with the latter two entering with the purpose of finding Ba-Ado-Mishram.{{cite}}{{expand}}

While she hated humans, she ultimately tried to lead Shallan and Renarin to her, alongside Rlain, as she was desperate to escape from her prison.{{book ref|sa5|132}}{{expand}}

===True Desolation===
{{quote|[[Dalinar]] felt the moment Renarin and Rlain released Ba-Ado-Mishram. A long-lasting discordant note, vibrating the soul of Roshar in the most terrible of ways, was finally extinguished.|Dalinar Kholin on Ba-Ado-Mishram's release{{book ref|sa5|141}}}}

Eventually, Ba-Ado-Mishram was freed by [[Renarin]] and [[Rlain]] on {{rosharan date|1175|5|2|5}}. This ended a everlasting discordant note that had vibrated the soul of Roshar since she was imprisoned, and righted the wrongs done to the planet.{{book ref|sa5|141}} Spren began to heal from being deadeye following her release.{{book ref|sa5|147}}

## Relationships
### Ulim
{{quote
|Eventually the Unmade decided to start a war without us. That turned out to be exceedingly stupid. In the past, Odium granted forms of power, but Ba-Ado-Mishram thought she could do it. Ended up handing out forms of power as easily as Fused give each other titles, Connected herself to the entire singer species. Became a little god. Too little.
|[[Ulim]] to [[Venli]]{{book ref|sa4|73}}
}}

Ulim thinks that Ba-Ado-Mishram's actions in the False Desolation were extremely stupid. He belittles her actions, calling her a "little god" and that she was just an oversized spren which they relied on.{{book ref|sa4|73}}

### Kalak
{{quote
|As one who has suffered for so many centuries . . . as one whom it broke . . . please find Mishram and release her. Not just for her own good. For the good of all spren.

For I believe that in confining her, we have caused a greater wound to Roshar than any ever realized.
|[[Kalak]] on Ba-Ado-Mishram{{epigraph ref|sa4|97}}
}}

The Herald Kalak is the only known individual to refer to Ba-Ado-Mishram as simply "Mishram." This is the only instance of an Unmade's name being shortened to just one part of their hyphenated name.{{epigraph ref|sa4|97}} It's unclear why this is, or if Kalak had familiarity with Ba-Ado-Mishram in the past. In Kalak's journal, he appears to have some caring for Ba-Ado-Mishram's own well-being, and urges someone to find and free her, both for her sake and Roshar's.

### Rayse
[[Rayse]]'s relation with Ba-Ado-Mishram is complex, with her serving him, but believing that he is bad for mankind and holding a desire to overthrow him as the Vessel of Odium.{{cite}} This desire eventually led her to attempt to directly betray him.{{cite}} 

The Shard itself likes Ba-Ado-Mishram, and sees her as a more optimal Vessel than either Rayse or Taravangian.{{cite}}

### Others
Another Unmade, [[Sja-anat]], did not believe herself to be the most clever of the Unmade. She compares herself to Ba-Ado-Mishram, and refers to Ba-Ado-Mishram as crafty and conniving. This could indicate that Sja-anat believes Ba-Ado-Mishram is actually the most clever of all the Unmade.{{book ref|sa4|i|2}}

It is unclear what Ba-Ado-Mishram's relationship was with the [[Fused]]. Though Hessi thinks Ba-Ado-Mishram was a commander during the Desolations,{{epigraph ref|sa3|106}} it's unknown if the Unmade ordered Fused around directly.
