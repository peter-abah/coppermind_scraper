**Yezier** is a landlocked princedom in southwestern [[Roshar]] and one of the member states of the [[Azir|Azish Empire]]. It's ruled by a princess.

## Etymology
Yezier might be derived from *Yaezir*, the name used for [[Jezrien]] across [[Makabak]].

## Geography
Yezier lays in the western part of the geographical region known as [[Makabak]], in the south-west of [[Roshar]]. It's bordered by [[Desh]] to the North, [[Azir]] to the East, and [[Tashikk]] and [[Liafor]] to the South. Its western border sits on an unnamed river, which it shares with [[Alm]], while in north-east, it reaches the [[Misted Mountains]] separating it from [[Shinovar]]. It claims about 396 thousand square kilometers of land, making it the fifth largest state in the Azish Empire, and the seventeenth largest country on Roshar as a whole.

Like most Makabaki states, Yezier appears to be rather dry and sparsely-populated. Other than its border with Alm, there are no major rivers there, and no settlements worth noting.

## History
During the [[Silver Kingdoms]] era, Yezier was part of [[Makabakam]]. It's unknown when they split off. Eventually, they joined a Makabaki coalition under the leadership of the [[Prime Aqasix]], becoming a client state to the [[Azir|Azish Empire]].

Following the [[Everstorm]], [[Dalinar Kholin]] sought to persuade them to join his coalition, hoping that they would help convince other states in the region. While he managed to speak with their princess, she demured, awaiting the decision of the Prime Aqasix. After the Azish agreed to join, Yezier followed suit, with the princess herself coming to [[Urithiru]].

Shortly before the [[Battle of Thaylen Field]], the Azish withdrew from the coalition; however, Yezier remained, and the princess sought to tie herself and her country closer to [[Alethkar]]. This might've been partially motivated by [[Iri]] defecting to Odium, as Yezier was worried about being invaded by them.

## Politics and Culture
Yezier is a member of the [[Azir|Azish Empire]], and as such subject to the rule of the [[Prime Aqasix]]. However, the country has rulers of its own - currently, an unnamed princess. Recently, she began courting [[Halam Khal]], one of [[House Kholin]]'s highlords, although she cannot marry him without the Prime's permission.

Yezier is traditionally very close to the Azish throne, and the princess is noted to live in [[Azimir]] for half of every year. Despite this, the nation does not always choose to follow its lead as closely as other nations such as Tashikk. [[Alm]]’s  designated diplomat family lives in a quaint palace near the shared border with Yezier.

The country has no [[Oathgate]]s. It's unknown whether they possess any [[Shardwielding|Shards]], though ordinarily they, like any other member state, can borrow the Imperial [[Shardblade]]s of Azir. Whether that still holds true during the [[True Desolation]] is unknown.

They are known for their [[stormwarden]]s and timekeepers. The World-class highstorm scholars in Yezier use advanced mathematical theorems to track and predict the arrival of highstorms across Roshar. They then deliver their calculated forecasts to agents in Tashikk via spanreed, where the forecasts are sold and widely distributed across Roshar. Along with the neighboring country of [[Liafor]], they are one of the centers of fashion on Roshar, and have been for decades. Bulk designs are made near the Yezier and Liafor border for export, and top fashion designers from the two nations travel the world with their tailors to make and sell pieces to foreign nobility. They incorporate fine Makabaki fabrics with stylistic modifications to suit the customs and religious requirements of their host countries. They likely speak one or more of the Makabaki languages.

## Notable citizens
* The princess - the ruler of Yezier. Comes to [[Urithiru]] personally to plead for help against a possible [[Iri]] invasion.
* A [[Skybreaker]] - one of [[Nale]]'s trainees in [[Tashikk]] is from Yezier, and complains about the Tashikki coyness.
