A **tobok** is a traditional outer garment of two layers of thick cloth worn in the past on [[Komashi]]. The clothing typically has long sleeves and may come in a variety of colors.{{book ref|yumi|2}}{{book ref|yumi|23}} It is unclear whether the bell skirt accompanying a tobok is considered part of the tobok itself.

The [[yoki-hijo]] wears one of several bright-colored ceremonial toboks, depending on the day of the week.{{book ref|yumi|23}} She wears the tobok over a sash around the bust, a white wrap over the chest, and loose leggings, and is dressed by her attendants.{{book ref|yumi|2}}{{book ref|yumi|23}}

[[Yumi]] purchases an elegant dress cardigan at the [[Shinzua Shopping Center]] which reminds her of the upper portion of the tobok she usually wears, although it is made of stiffer fabric, has a shorter body and sleeves, and is more form-fitting.{{book ref|yumi|15}}

## Trivia

The term "tobok" is inspired by the Korean word "bok", meaning "clothing".{{wob ref|15564}}
