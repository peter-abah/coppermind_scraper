**Noril** is an [[Alethi]] refugee on [[Roshar]]. He arrives at [[Hearthstone]] on the day the city is evacuated to [[Urithiru]].{{book ref|sa4|1}}

## Appearance and Personality
Noril is a bearded Alethi darkeyed man with three moles on his upper left cheek and a cowlick in his greying hair.{{book ref|sa4|1}}{{book ref|sa4|i|10}} His entire left arm is missing, lost years prior to his arrival in Hearthstone.{{book ref|sa4|25}} He suffers from suicidal depression but is inspired by Kaladin to fight to keep his emotions from dominating his life.{{book ref|sa4|i|10}}

## History
{{sidequote
|You **need** someone to talk to, Noril, when the darkness is strong. Someone to remind you the world hasn't always been this way; that it won't always be this way.
|Kaladin{{book ref|sa4|25}}
|side=right|size=300px
}}
Noril tried to escape the horrors of the war in [[Alethkar]], in which he saw his family brutally killed while he was tied to a tree and unable to help, by emigrating to [[Hearthstone]].{{book ref|sa4|1}}{{book ref|sa4|25}} He arrived to the town manifesting symptoms of severe battle shock, so [[Lirin]] took extra care to make sure he was sent to a tent where he would be monitored.{{book ref|sa4|1}} Noril was put on the *[[Fourth Bridge]]* for evacuation with the rest of the people at Hearthstone. Upon their arrival at [[Urithiru]], Lirin sent Noril to the ardents for treatment. Those ardents, who only treated physical ailments, in turn sent him on to the [[Devotary of Mercy]] for treatment for mental symptoms. At the Devotary, Noril told them he had no living relatives and made one attempt to commit suicide. The ardents there kept him in a dark, bare room until [[Kaladin]] and [[Teft]] came looking for him. Kaladin managed to get Noril to agree to come outside to talk to him by displaying a profound understanding of how Noril felt. Outside on the balcony, Kaladin and Teft reminded Noril that his niece [[Cressa]] would be devastated if something happened to him; Noril felt better as they talked, even though he still couldn't bring himself to fully believe in and accept the outlook on life the other two tried to present him with.{{book ref|sa4|25}} His situation sparked Kaladin's idea to begin group therapy sessions with those who suffered from depression, and Kaladin insisted that the ardents release Noril and, later, several others into his care.{{book ref|sa4|25}}{{book ref|sa4|33}}

During the occupation of Urithiru, Noril ended up in the Radiant infirmary not long before the tower was restored, where he painted *shash* [[glyphs]] on people's foreheads. [[Hesina]], irked by Lirin's attitude towards Kaladin, initiated a conversation between Lirin and Noril in which Noril explained why he wore the glyph on his forehead: Kaladin's persistence in the face of his own depression was an inspiration to Noril to keep fighting.{{book ref|sa4|i|10}} After Lirin spoke to the others in the room who wore the glyph, he allowed Noril to paint it on his own forehead.{{book ref|sa4|i|10}}{{book ref|sa4|110}} Later, after Teft's death and Kaladin's final defeat of [[Lezian]], Noril and a handful of others led the humans in defending the Radiant infirmary from the Pursuer's troops.{{book ref|sa4|107}} After the ensuing battle in the atrium, [[Rlain]] spoke to the group of survivors, including the [[singers]] who had just fought alongside the humans; Noril took the lead in encouraging the humans to trust Rlain.{{book ref|sa4|109}}

After the tower was restored, Noril joined Kaladin, [[Dabbid]], and the other patients rescued from the ardents' sick rooms in helping each other to decompress.{{book ref|sa4|116}}

## Relationships
### Kaladin
{{quote
|He's got the emptiness, bad as I do. I can see it in him. We all can. But he gets up anyway.
|Noril, about [[Kaladin]]{{book ref|sa4|i|10}}
}}
Noril and [[Kaladin]], suffering from a similar malady, inspire each other. Noril is suicidal and has given up on life when Kaladin first finds him. Seeing Noril's plight, locked away in a dark room because no one knows what else to do with him, Kaladin realizes he might have ended up the same way had his life not gone how it had. This inspires Kaladin to begin group therapy sessions with Noril and the others being treated at the [[Devotary of Mercy]].{{book ref|sa4|25}}{{book ref|sa4|37}} In fact, he begins to view his [[Ideals]] in a different light{{book ref|sa4|37}} and makes peace with the fact that it really is better for him to pursue mental health treatment in place of fighting on the front lines of battle with the rest of the [[Order of Windrunners|Windrunners]].{{book ref|sa4|116}}

Noril is likewise inspired by Kaladin to get up and face each day. Although he struggles at first with accepting the perspective shift that Kaladin tries to give him, he slowly becomes much more functional and ends up one of Kaladin's staunchest supporters.{{book ref|sa4|25}}{{book ref|sa4|i|10}}

### Lirin
{{quote
|Lirin remembered, with some embarrassment, what he'd finally let that one-armed fool Noril do to him. A painted **shash** glyph on Lirin's forehead.
|[[Lirin]]{{book ref|sa4|110}}
}}
[[Lirin]] meets Noril as yet another sufferer of battle shock and does what he can for him by having him monitored and sending him to the ardents on suicide watch.{{book ref|sa4|1}}{{book ref|sa4|i|10}} Later, Lirin advises Kaladin in his work to help people like Noril.{{book ref|sa4|33}} When Lirin and Noril come face to face again in the [[Urithiru]] Radiant infirmary toward the end of the tower's occupation, Noril is painting *shash* [[glyph]]s on people's foreheads, and Lirin immediately dismisses him as foolish. At [[Hesina]]'s prompting, Lirin begins a discussion with the man by asking him why he wears the glyph. Noril explains why Kaladin is such a source of inspiration to him, and Lirin begins to see his point of view.{{book ref|sa4|i|10}} Eventually, he lets Noril paint a *shash* glyph on his forehead, too, in support of his son.{{book ref|sa4|110}}
