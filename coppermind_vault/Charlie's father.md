**Charlie's father** is the **duke**  of [[Diggen's Point]].{{book ref|tress|2}} He cares little for his son and particularly disapproves of his relationship with [[Tress]].{{book ref|tress|3}} He is one of the few people who is allowed to leave Diggen's Point, and does so periodically to report to the [[Verdant King|king]].{{book ref|tress|4}} 

## Appearance and Personality
The duke is a tall man, who stands up straight and dresses in military decor covered in military medals that appear to stick his clothes to him.{{book ref|tress|3}}

The duke is a very grumpy person, regularly found grumbling in his mansion atop of Diggen's Point. He is described as taking his job of yelling a lot very seriously.{{book ref|tress|2}} The one time Tress remembers seeing the duke smile, he was punishing old [[Lotari]] for trying to escape the island.{{book ref|tress|3}}

## History
The duke believed [[Charlie]] was not fit to be his heir because he was not physically strong, nor was he a war hero. His father also seemed to disapprove of his son’s intelligence. After witnessing the close bond between Tress and Charlie, and as a last attempt to make Charlie into a proper heir, the duke took him off the island to be married to a princess.{{book ref|tress|4}} Charlie loved Tress and did not wish to marry someone else, so he deliberately acted boring when he was around the princesses so that they would not want to marry him. Eventually the duke was so exasperated by Charlie that he sent his son to be married to [[the Sorceress]], assuming it would result in Charlie's death. He then promptly replaced Charlie with his nephew, [[Dirk]]. The duke was happy with this swap, as Dirk was strong, a war hero, and didn't talk very much.{{book ref|tress|5}} When the duke returned to Diggen's Point, he announced that there had been an unfortunate accident, and he was adopting Dirk as his new heir. He then threw a party at the feast hall to celebrate.{{book ref|tress|5}} 

When Tress and Charlie eventually returned from the Sorceress’s island, the duke had been left by his wife because she didn’t approve of his getting rid of her son. He was outraged that people were now allowed to leave Diggen's Point, but was powerless to stop the King's writ from taking effect.{{book ref|tress|epilogue}}
