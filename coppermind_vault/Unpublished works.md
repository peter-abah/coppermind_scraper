This page lists all of [[Brandon Sanderson]]'s works, written or unwritten, that have not been published. Some of these are written novels that have been "trunked", some of which have been re-written or cannibalized from. Some are ideas Brandon has talked about briefly but it is unknown if they will ever actually be written. The remainder are forthcoming works that, while unwritten, will come out eventually.

A list of those works that have been published can be found in the [[Bibliography]] article.

## Forthcoming Works

### Cosmere Works
; [[Hoid's Storybook Collection]]
: A series of four picture books illustrated by [[Howard Lyon]], based on cosmere folk tales. It will include reworked versions of "The Dog and the Dragon", "[[The Girl Who Looked Up]]", and the story of [[Derethil]] and the *[[Wandersail]]*, plus a story featuring the [[ChasmFriends]]. It will be sold through a crowdfunding campaign in March 2026 and released before the end of the year.{{wob ref|17015}}
; *[[The Fires of December]]*
: Also known as Secret Project 6, it was announced at Dragonsteel Nexus 2025, where Brandon did [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RXyWtp8tuYY: a reading of the first two chapters.] Part of the new *[[Hoid's Travails]]* series, it is set on a new world. A premium illustrated version will be included in the "Hoid's Storybook Collection" crowdfunding campaign in March of 2026 with a release expected at Nexus 2026.{{wob ref|17015}}
; *[[The Stormlight Archive]]*
: **Books 6-10**
:: Book 6 will feature flashbacks from [[Lift]], Book 7 from [[Renarin]], Book 8 from [[Shalash|Ash]], Book 9 from [[Taln]], and Book 10 from [[Jasnah]].{{wob ref|12214}} They will not be written until after the *[[Mistborn: Ghostbloods]]* trilogy.{{wob ref|11548}} Brandon may do some early work on outlining before *Ghostbloods* is completed.{{wob ref|17019}}
: ***Horneater***
:: The tentative title of a novella about [[Lunamor]], the [[Unkalaki]] also known as Rock.{{wob ref|13546}} It is set post-*Oathbringer*, involving him returning to his homeland.{{wob ref|12765}} Brandon previously believed it would be available as part of the *Words of Radiance* leatherbound Kickstarter in 2024, but due to his writing time being booked up, it was delayed.{{wob ref|16172}}{{wob ref|16202}}{{wob ref|16435}} He plans to return to the project within the next few years.{{wob ref|17019}}
; *[[Mistborn (series)|Mistborn]]*:
:; *Ghostbloods* (*Mistborn* Era 3): A trilogy that takes place in an urban setting with a 1980s technology level, with elements of a Cold War-era spy thriller. The first book is Brandon's main focus post-*Wind and Truth*, and is mostly done with an expected release in 2028.{{wob ref|17015}} The other two books in the series are planned for 2029 and 2030.{{wob ref|17019}} Formerly known as the *Mistborn* Era 3 trilogy.{{sots ref|2023}}
:; *Mistborn* Space Opera trilogy: A space opera, with interplanetary travel. Sanderson has confirmed Hoid to be a main character in this era.{{wob ref|10012}} The 1980s trilogy and the space trilogy are "cornerstones" of the *Mistborn* story and will have wide-ranging consequences in the cosmere.{{wob ref|14382}}{{wob ref|5142}} He anticipates the novels in this era to go Stormlight length.{{wob ref|13553}}
:; *[[Book of Nails]]*
:: [[Isaac Stewart]] is working on a story featuring [[Nicki Savage]];{{wob ref|16217}} he previously wrote her stories in the [[Elendel]] broadsheets. He describes it as "an adventurous woman with erratic magical powers travels with an expedition to an island to find a legendary book with the power to raise an undead army."{{wob ref|15256}} Brandon helped with the outline, but it would be the first full-length cosmere work primarily written by another author.{{wob ref|15391}} It was intended to be a novella,{{wob ref|15159}} but grew into a novel,{{wob ref|16054}} and the first draft was completed in 2022.{{wob ref|16049}} It was originally referred to as *Boatload of Mummies*.{{wob ref|15159}}
; [[Elantris trilogy|*Elantris* trilogy]] 2-3
: Originally conceived as taking place ten years later and focusing on different characters.{{wob ref|9851}}{{wob ref|12863}} Needs to be written before the third, 1980s-esque, *Mistborn* trilogy, and likely to be written between *Stormlight* 5 and 6.{{wob ref|11523}}{{wob ref|9154}}{{wob ref|1053}} Post-*Wind and Truth*, Brandon believes he finally has time in his writing schedule to write a sequel soon.{{wob ref|17016}} *Elantris* is one of the top priorities along with *Ghostbloods*, and the sequels could be released in the 2029-2030 time frame.{{wob ref|16783}}{{wob ref|16788}}
:; [[Elantris sequel|*Elantris* sequel]]: Could follow [[Kaise]], [[Daorn]], and [[Adien]] as ambassadors to [[Fjorden]].{{wob ref|9851}}{{wob ref|12863}} Called *Dakhor* in Brandon's notes, it will also feature the return of [[Jaddeth]].{{wob ref|15951}}{{wob ref|15991}}
:; [[Elantris finale|*Elantris* finale]]: A trilogy is planned,{{wob ref|15991}}{{wob ref|16788}} although the story would not continue directly through each book.{{wob ref|12863}}
; *[[Nightblood (book)|Nightblood]]*: Sequel to *[[Warbreaker]]*.{{wob ref|9174}} Although Brandon does not necessarily like to write prequels,{{wob ref|4480}} he has mentioned that the backstory of several Nalthian characters could be explored in the book;{{wob ref|11904}}{{wob ref|12665}} he considers this an exception due to the circumstances around the writing and release of *Warbreaker*,{{wob ref|14208}} and may also introduce new characters.{{wob ref|8535}}{{wob ref|5853}} Will be written sometime after *Stormlight 5*, possibly after *Stormlight 6*; *Mistborn* Era 3 and *Elantris* sequels are likely to come first.{{wob ref|12486}}{{wob ref|12276}}{{wob ref|9174}}{{wob ref|17016}}
; [[Dragonsteel (series)|Dragonsteel series]]
: A series of books that will only be written after the Stormlight Archive is completed.{{wob ref|12313}} The number of installments has changed over time but it was most recently stated to be three books.{{wob ref|10741}}
:; *[[The Liar of Partinel]]/[[The Lightweaver of Rens]]*: [[Hoid]]'s origin story.{{wob ref|2820}} They are likely to be folded into the *Dragonsteel* sequence, as the series needs to be almost entirely reworked.{{wob ref|4970}}{{wob ref|2820}}
; *[[Aether of Night]]*
: A rewrite of Brandon's tenth novel, as various elements of the original were cannibalized for other works (such as Decay becoming Ruin). No longer is trunked.{{sots ref|2015}} Brandon has said this will be drastically different from the unpublished novel and will only retain the location in the cosmere and the magic system.{{sots ref|2017}} Brandon is still unsure when he will fit this story into the overall cosmere timeline, but it is planned to be three novels.{{sots ref|2019}}
; *[[The Night Brigade]]*
: A novel set on [[Threnody]] has been part of Brandon's plan for the cosmere since before he was published. Originally planned to be the story of the people who fled to the [[Forests of Hell]] after the [[Evil]] destroyed their [[Homeland]]. He has recently considered changing the story to that of an expedition being sent back to the Homeland.{{sots ref|2016}} A tentative title was thought to be *The Dust Brigade*, but in reality it was *The Dusk Brigade*. Brandon later changed the title to *The Night Brigade* because a character named [[Dusk]] already exists in the cosmere.{{wob ref|15573}} It would be darker than other cosmere works{{wob ref|11224}} and have some relation to Silverlight.{{wob ref|12766}} It is not considered a core cosmere book and could be cut if Brandon does not have time to write it.{{wob ref|13784}}{{wob ref|11904}} It will feature a cosmere group that Brandon wants mentioned before the cosmere sequence ends.{{wob ref|12635}}
; *[[The Silence Divine]]*: A novel set on [[Ashyn]] in the [[Rosharan system]], where diseases grant magical abilities.{{wob ref|8869}} At one point, it was likely to be renamed, as Brandon had recently written [[Shadows for Silence in the Forests of Hell|another novella with "Silence" in the title]], though it has been several years since then.{{wob ref|3369}} Unlikely to be written anytime soon, as Brandon needs to research more about immunology and has limited time.{{wob ref|12797}} While being mentioned as a novella for the longest time, this has most recently been stated to be planned as a novel.{{sots ref|2022}} It would not be expanded into a series.{{wob ref|17029}}
; *White Sand* prose rewrite
: There will be a prose rewrite of *[[White Sand (prose)|White Sand]]* that would better match the events of the first three graphic novels.{{sots ref|2020}}{{sots ref|2023}} Brandon worked on it in 2025, but it was set aside for work on *[[The Fires of December]]*, and is still in progress.{{wob ref|17016}}

; *[[The Apocalypse Guard]]*
: A follow-up of [[The Reckoners]] for Random House. It was originally set in the Reckoners universe{{wob ref|7619}} and even had the potential to cross over into the [[Cytoverse]],{{wob ref|13608}} but the current setting is unknown. Features a team that goes to alternate universes to prevent world-ending events.{{ref|name=ag}} Planned as a trilogy, initial attempts at writing the first installment "did not work".{{sots ref|2017}} Brandon attempted a somewhat unusual collaboration with author [[Dan Wells]] to try and fix the project.{{wob ref|9622}}{{wob ref|10930}} The collaboration progressed with additional revisions and "[got] weirder".{{sots ref|2018}} After the revisions, neither Brandon nor Dan was totally satisfied and the project was shelved.{{wob ref|13257}} It has not been abandoned, and Brandon plans to work on it after *Skyward 4*.{{sots ref|2020}}
; *[[Songs of the Dead]]*
: A novel originally featuring a necromancer pizza delivery man.{{sots ref|2013}} Was on hiatus for years.{{sots ref|2017}} Progress was made, and the main character's job was changed to a heavy metal singer.{{wob ref|11948}} Brandon decided to collaborate with [[Peter Orullian]] due to his knowledge of heavy metal culture.{{sots ref|2018}} A draft was completed in 2018,{{sots ref|2018}} and it was further revised in 2019-2021.{{sots ref|2020}} Brandon eventually handed the project off to Peter, citing a lack of time to work on it. As of 2023, the book was complete.{{sots ref|2023}} It will be published in summer 2026 as the first book in a trilogy called *[[The Strata Wars]]*. Peter will write the two other books in the series.{{url ref|url=https://people.com/brandon-sanderson-peter-orullian-strata-wars-trilogy-exclusive-11815598|text=Brandon Sanderson and Peter Orullian Team Up for Strata Wars Fantasy Trilogy|site=People.com|date=2025-09-24}}
;*[[The Rithmatist]]* sequels
: Two sequels were originally planned, tentatively titled *[[The Aztlanian]]*{{sots ref|2013}}{{sots ref|2014}} and *[[The Nebraskian]]*.{{wob ref|3174}} Writing a sequel to *The Rithmatist* has proved to be difficult.{{wob ref|11523}} Brandon has completely changed the concept and considered bringing in a coauthor;{{wob ref|15112}}{{wob ref|14972}} the biggest issue is that it is hard for him to pick up the thread of a work he wrote nearly twenty years ago.{{wob ref|16833}} Brandon still intends to wrap the series up, but has various other projects that will be worked on first.{{wob ref|17016}} It is possible that only one sequel will be written, and the title of the potential third book is up in the air.{{wob ref|17029}}

## "Trunked" Works
These are works that are unlikely to ever be published either because elements have been co-opted for use in other books (as with *Mythwalker* and *Aether of Night*) or because the book has been re-written from scratch (as with *White Sand* Prime and *The Way of Kings* Prime).

; [[White Sand Prime|*White Sand* Prime]]
: Brandon's first novel, later re-written as [[White Sand (prose)|*White Sand* (prose)]].{{wob ref|6096}}
; *[[Star's End]]*
: Brandon's second novel, a short alien-relations/sci-fi story.{{wob ref|6096}}
; *[[Lord Mastrell]]*
: Brandon's third novel, a sequel to *White Sand* Prime.{{wob ref|6096}}
; *[[Knight Life]]*
: Brandon's fourth novel, a fantasy comedy.{{wob ref|6096}}
; *[[The Sixth Incarnation of Pandora]]*
: Brandon's fifth novel, a far future sci-fi involving immortal warriors{{wob ref|1985}}
; [[Dragonsteel Prime|*Dragonsteel* Prime]]
: Brandon's seventh novel, served as his undergraduate honors thesis. Will be rewritten for the main cosmere sequence. Made available as both ebook and hardcover as part of the *Words of Radiance* leatherbound crowdfunding campaign.
; [[White Sand (prose)|*White Sand* (prose)]]
: Brandon's eighth novel, a complete rewrite of the first. This later served as the basis for his graphic novels.
; *[[Mythwalker]]*
: Brandon's ninth novel, a book that included the characters [[Vivenna]] and [[Siri]]{{wob ref|6023}}
; [[Mistborn Prime|*Mistborn* Prime]]
: Brandon's eleventh novel, elements were later combined with *Final Empire* Prime for *[[Mistborn: The Final Empire]]*.
; [[Final Empire Prime|*Final Empire* Prime]]
: Brandon's twelfth novel, elements were later combined with *Mistborn* Prime for *[[Mistborn: The Final Empire]]*.
; [[The Way of Kings Prime|*The Way of Kings* Prime]]
: Brandon's thirteenth novel, later re-written as *[[The Way of Kings]]*. This is being published as part of the Way of Kings 10th edition Kickstarter in hardcover form and is available as an ebook for free on [https://www.brandonsanderson.com/the-way-of-kings-prime/ Brandon's website].
; *The King's Necromancer*
: An early draft was partially completed in 2010.{{wob ref|10132}} Elements of the magic system were later incorporated into the project that became *[[Songs of the Dead]]*. The text of the original draft has been completely discarded, and there has not been any indication that it is still in the works.{{wob ref|12199}}
; *Climb the Sky*
: An early cosmere book that was the origin of the character that eventually became [[Syl]].{{wob ref|8338}} It would have possibly involved aethers and flying magic.{{wob ref|7811}}
; *Soulburner* aka *Starburner*
: A space opera/fantasy work first mentioned in 2015 that never progressed past the worldbuilding stage.{{wob ref|9167}}{{wob ref|12489}} It was eventually pitched as a video game concept, and became the setting of *[[Moonbreaker]]* after Brandon significantly expanded the worldbuilding guide.{{wob ref|15793}}{{wob ref|16867}}
; *[[Dark One: Prophetic Histories]]*
: A second audio original in the *Dark One* franchise that would have been based on Brandon's original outline.{{wob ref|13823}} Elements of the same outline were used for *[[Dark One Book 1]]* and the unpublished novel being written by [[Dan Wells]].{{wob ref|14036}}{{wob ref|16848}} The project no longer exists in any form.{{wob ref|17029}}
