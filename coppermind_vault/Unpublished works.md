This page lists all of [[Brandon Sanderson]]'s works, written or unwritten, that have not been published. Some of these are written novels that have been "trunked", some of which have been re-written or cannibalized from. Some are ideas Brandon has talked about briefly but it is unknown if they will ever actually be written. The remainder are forthcoming works that, while unwritten, will come out eventually.

A list of those works that have been published can be found in the [[Bibliography]] article.

## Forthcoming Works

### Cosmere Works
; [[Hoid's Storybook Collection]]
: A series of four picture books illustrated by [[Howard Lyon]], based on cosmere folk tales. It will include reworked versions of "The Dog and the Dragon", "[[The Girl Who Looked Up]]", and the story of [[Derethil]] and the *[[Wandersail]]*, plus a story featuring the [[ChasmFriends]]. It will be sold through a crowdfunding campaign in March 2026 and released before the end of the year.
; *[[The Fires of December]]*
: Also known as Secret Project 6, it was announced at Dragonsteel Nexus 2025, where Brandon did [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RXyWtp8tuYY: a reading of the first two chapters.] Part of the new *[[Hoid's Travails]]* series, it is set on a new world. A premium illustrated version will be included in the "Hoid's Storybook Collection" crowdfunding campaign in March of 2026 with a release expected at Nexus 2026.
; *[[The Stormlight Archive]]*
: **Books 6-10**
:: Book 6 will feature flashbacks from [[Lift]], Book 7 from [[Renarin]], Book 8 from [[Shalash|Ash]], Book 9 from [[Taln]], and Book 10 from [[Jasnah]]. They will not be written until after the *[[Mistborn: Ghostbloods]]* trilogy. Brandon may do some early work on outlining before *Ghostbloods* is completed.
: ***Horneater***
:: The tentative title of a novella about [[Lunamor]], the [[Unkalaki]] also known as Rock. It is set post-*Oathbringer*, involving him returning to his homeland. Brandon previously believed it would be available as part of the *Words of Radiance* leatherbound Kickstarter in 2024, but due to his writing time being booked up, it was delayed. He plans to return to the project within the next few years.
; *[[Mistborn (series)|Mistborn]]*:
:; *Ghostbloods* (*Mistborn* Era 3): A trilogy that takes place in an urban setting with a 1980s technology level, with elements of a Cold War-era spy thriller. The first book is Brandon's main focus post-*Wind and Truth*, and is mostly done with an expected release in 2028. The other two books in the series are planned for 2029 and 2030. Formerly known as the *Mistborn* Era 3 trilogy.
:; *Mistborn* Space Opera trilogy: A space opera, with interplanetary travel. Sanderson has confirmed Hoid to be a main character in this era. The 1980s trilogy and the space trilogy are "cornerstones" of the *Mistborn* story and will have wide-ranging consequences in the cosmere. He anticipates the novels in this era to go Stormlight length.
:; *[[Book of Nails]]*
:: [[Isaac Stewart]] is working on a story featuring [[Nicki Savage]]; he previously wrote her stories in the [[Elendel]] broadsheets. He describes it as "an adventurous woman with erratic magical powers travels with an expedition to an island to find a legendary book with the power to raise an undead army." Brandon helped with the outline, but it would be the first full-length cosmere work primarily written by another author. It was intended to be a novella, but grew into a novel, and the first draft was completed in 2022. It was originally referred to as *Boatload of Mummies*.
; [[Elantris trilogy|*Elantris* trilogy]] 2-3
: Originally conceived as taking place ten years later and focusing on different characters. Needs to be written before the third, 1980s-esque, *Mistborn* trilogy, and likely to be written between *Stormlight* 5 and 6. Post-*Wind and Truth*, Brandon believes he finally has time in his writing schedule to write a sequel soon. *Elantris* is one of the top priorities along with *Ghostbloods*, and the sequels could be released in the 2029-2030 time frame.
:; [[Elantris sequel|*Elantris* sequel]]: Could follow [[Kaise]], [[Daorn]], and [[Adien]] as ambassadors to [[Fjorden]]. Called *Dakhor* in Brandon's notes, it will also feature the return of [[Jaddeth]].
:; [[Elantris finale|*Elantris* finale]]: A trilogy is planned, although the story would not continue directly through each book.
; *[[Nightblood (book)|Nightblood]]*: Sequel to *[[Warbreaker]]*. Although Brandon does not necessarily like to write prequels, he has mentioned that the backstory of several Nalthian characters could be explored in the book; he considers this an exception due to the circumstances around the writing and release of *Warbreaker*, and may also introduce new characters. Will be written sometime after *Stormlight 5*, possibly after *Stormlight 6*; *Mistborn* Era 3 and *Elantris* sequels are likely to come first.
; [[Dragonsteel (series)|Dragonsteel series]]
: A series of books that will only be written after the Stormlight Archive is completed. The number of installments has changed over time but it was most recently stated to be three books.
:; *[[The Liar of Partinel]]/[[The Lightweaver of Rens]]*: [[Hoid]]'s origin story. They are likely to be folded into the *Dragonsteel* sequence, as the series needs to be almost entirely reworked.
; *[[Aether of Night]]*
: A rewrite of Brandon's tenth novel, as various elements of the original were cannibalized for other works (such as Decay becoming Ruin). No longer is trunked. Brandon has said this will be drastically different from the unpublished novel and will only retain the location in the cosmere and the magic system. Brandon is still unsure when he will fit this story into the overall cosmere timeline, but it is planned to be three novels.
; *[[The Night Brigade]]*
: A novel set on [[Threnody]] has been part of Brandon's plan for the cosmere since before he was published. Originally planned to be the story of the people who fled to the [[Forests of Hell]] after the [[Evil]] destroyed their [[Homeland]]. He has recently considered changing the story to that of an expedition being sent back to the Homeland. A tentative title was thought to be *The Dust Brigade*, but in reality it was *The Dusk Brigade*. Brandon later changed the title to *The Night Brigade* because a character named [[Dusk]] already exists in the cosmere. It would be darker than other cosmere works and have some relation to Silverlight. It is not considered a core cosmere book and could be cut if Brandon does not have time to write it. It will feature a cosmere group that Brandon wants mentioned before the cosmere sequence ends.
; *[[The Silence Divine]]*: A novel set on [[Ashyn]] in the [[Rosharan system]], where diseases grant magical abilities. At one point, it was likely to be renamed, as Brandon had recently written [[Shadows for Silence in the Forests of Hell|another novella with "Silence" in the title]], though it has been several years since then. Unlikely to be written anytime soon, as Brandon needs to research more about immunology and has limited time. While being mentioned as a novella for the longest time, this has most recently been stated to be planned as a novel. It would not be expanded into a series.
; *White Sand* prose rewrite
: There will be a prose rewrite of *[[White Sand (prose)|White Sand]]* that would better match the events of the first three graphic novels. Brandon worked on it in 2025, but it was set aside for work on *[[The Fires of December]]*, and is still in progress.

### Non-Cosmere Works
; [[Cytoverse]] works
:;*[[Riftwake]]*
::The sequel trilogy to [[Skyward (series)|Skyward]], written by [[Janci Patterson]]. The first book, *[[Blightfall]]*, is completed with an expected release in September 2026. The second book (tentatively titled *Songrise*) is mostly completed with an expected release in 2027, and the third book has been outlined.
;*[[Super Awesome Danger]]*
: A middle grade story written in secret with the help of his sons, Brandon envisions it as a graphic novel. As of late 2025, artwork proofs have been completed, but the overall process is taking a while.
; *[[The Apocalypse Guard]]*
: A follow-up of [[The Reckoners]] for Random House. It was originally set in the Reckoners universe and even had the potential to cross over into the [[Cytoverse]], but the current setting is unknown. Features a team that goes to alternate universes to prevent world-ending events. Planned as a trilogy, initial attempts at writing the first installment "did not work". Brandon attempted a somewhat unusual collaboration with author [[Dan Wells]] to try and fix the project. The collaboration progressed with additional revisions and "[got] weirder". After the revisions, neither Brandon nor Dan was totally satisfied and the project was shelved. It has not been abandoned, and Brandon plans to work on it after *Skyward 4*.
; *[[Songs of the Dead]]*
: A novel originally featuring a necromancer pizza delivery man. Was on hiatus for years. Progress was made, and the main character's job was changed to a heavy metal singer. Brandon decided to collaborate with [[Peter Orullian]] due to his knowledge of heavy metal culture. A draft was completed in 2018, and it was further revised in 2019-2021. Brandon eventually handed the project off to Peter, citing a lack of time to work on it. As of 2023, the book was complete. It will be published in summer 2026 as the first book in a trilogy called *[[The Strata Wars]]*. Peter will write the two other books in the series.
;*[[The Rithmatist]]* sequels
: Two sequels were originally planned, tentatively titled *[[The Aztlanian]]* and *[[The Nebraskian]]*. Writing a sequel to *The Rithmatist* has proved to be difficult. Brandon has completely changed the concept and considered bringing in a coauthor; the biggest issue is that it is hard for him to pick up the thread of a work he wrote nearly twenty years ago. Brandon still intends to wrap the series up, but has various other projects that will be worked on first. It is possible that only one sequel will be written, and the title of the potential third book is up in the air.

## Potential Works
These are projects that Brandon has mentioned as possibilities but there are no firm plans in place.
===Cosmere Works===
;*Arcanum Unbounded* 2: Will contain more short stories and the [[Nalthis]] essay. However, many of the short stories that could have been published in an *Arcanum Unbounded* 2 became full-length Secret Project novels.
; *[[Mistborn (series)|Mistborn]]*
:; 1940's *Mistborn*
:: Potentially more short adventures set before Era 3. Could be a novella.
:; Near-future Cyberpunk *Mistborn*
:: Potentially more adventures set between *Ghostbloods* and the space opera era. It is not a cornerstone of the overall *Mistborn* story, and could be done as a novella or not at all. Brandon likes the idea and feels like he has internally committed to writing it, but has consistently declined to "promise" that it will happen due to limits on his time.
:; *Mistborn: Secret History 2*
:: Brandon has mentioned a potential sequel to *[[Secret History]]* on several occasions, potentially including flashbacks and additional backstory related to both Era 1 and Era 2. However, he has explicitly stated that it is a low priority compared to other cosmere works and may never be written, especially since the first *Secret History* took nearly a decade to write and Brandon considers this type of side project "self-indulgent". [[Kelsier]] is planned to be involved in *Mistborn* Era 3, which increases the likelihood of another *Secret History* when Brandon gets to that point.
:; Parallel *Mistborn* novel from Hoid's perspective
:: Like *Back to the Future Part II*. Last mentioned as of 2018.
; *[[White Sand]]*
:; Second Set of *White Sand* graphic novels: These would be set on the Darkside of Taldain, and would also be a trilogy. They would not, however, be prose.. Might be the same works as *The Arcanist* by Isaac Stewart (see below).
; *Kingmaker*: a young adult novel set on [[First of the Sun]]. It would not feature Dusk. Brandon [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mpcdGJgVDeA read from the first draft] at the *Cytonic* release event in November, 2021.
; Untitled *[[The Emperor's Soul]]* sequel: a story set in the [[Rose Empire]] on [[Sel]]. Initial plans were for a story not centered around [[Wan ShaiLu]] except perhaps a cameo role. However, Brandon later came up with a story idea for Shai that is more of a direct sequel.
; YA book set in a minor cosmere planet: Set in the future of the cosmere (between *Mistborn* era 3 and 4), it would follow one of Hoid's apprentices, stranded on a minor planet and trying to figure out their kite-based magic system. Has not been written, and could include [[Starling]]. Brandon needs some concept art because it is hard to describe kite-based magic with text.
; [[Mythos]] series: In recent State of the Sandersons it was mentioned that Brandon was thinking about writing a series set on [[Mythos]], a planet briefly mentioned in *[[The Lost Metal]]*. No other information has been offered.
; *The Grand Apparatus*: A book called *The Grand Apparatus* was first mentioned in 2023 and remains on the potential works list. It would take place on a new planet that was constructed for a specific purpose, somewhat akin to [[Canticle]].
; Additional Cosmere stories by [[Isaac Stewart]]
: Brandon was originally resistant to collaborating on cosmere stories, but has since noted that Isaac is the only person he would trust to work in the shared universe and do it justice. Isaac now has a "blank slate" to develop cosmere works. The first of these potential projects is *[[Book of Nails]]*, but other ideas have been mentioned, including a story titled *Son of Bones* and set on [[Scadrial]] (possibly on the southern continent), *The Arcanist* featuring [[Khriss]], and a story set on [[Threnody]]. The first draft of *Book of Nails* was finished as of 2022.
; Additional Cosmere stories by [[Dan Wells]]
: It was announced in 2022 that Dan Wells had been appointed as VP of Narrative at Dragonsteel, part of this involves writing new stories set in the Cosmere. Brandon has stated that he is working closely on these with Dan. Dan himself indicated that work has begun on his first novel which is focused upon something that has not been covered by the Cosmere, or the fantasy genre as a whole.
; Untitled [[Silverlight]] story
: A novella set in [[Silverlight]]. Would need to follow the Threnody novel. It is uncertain if it will be written.
;Free Fall Seven Layer Burrito World
:In [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=v0SBhLTzens episode 32] of the *[[Intentionally Blank]]* podcast with Dan Wells from Dragonsteel Mini-con, Brandon introduced a "bad story idea" for of a city freefalling through an unending, perpetual hole. This would introduce interesting problems for the residents, who would have to deal with external factors like air resistance complicating everyday life. Dan mentioned the city falling through different "layers" that each have different resources like water or food. This turned into jokes about the world being like a seven-layer dip or burrito. Although Brandon laughingly called the discussion "stupid", he liked the concept and started mentioning it as a potential Cosmere work in the annual State of the Sanderson posts as a "someday" project.
;Caveman Heist
:First mentioned in 2025. Nothing is known about it, but it is listed as a Cosmere work.
;Untitled Ashyn novel
:Brandon has an idea for another story on [[Ashyn]], separate from *[[The Silence Divine]]*. It appears to be low on the priority list.
;*The Princess*
:Told in Hoid's voice, the story is about a spoiled princess who decides to slay a dragon in order to be eligible to inherit the kingdom. Brandon [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2GpGwRQlY8A read the first three chapters] at FanX 2025. Currently he feels the story is too silly to remain in the cosmere and might pull it out of the setting to finish it. Set on the same planet as *[[I Hate Dragons]]*.
;[[Lift]] Food Heist novella
:[[Dan Wells]] has an affinity for news stories about food heists that has come up numerous times on the [[Intentionally Blank]] podcast. It was mentioned that Lift would be a natural fit for such a story, and Brandon later noted that he would actually consider a food heist novella.

===Non-Cosmere Works===
; Additional [[Cytoverse]] works
:; *The Eyes*
:: A short story set in the [[Cytoverse]]; it is a space opera inspired by [[wikipedia:Fermi's paradox|Fermi's paradox]]. A draft was made available in an early Patreon campaign. Brandon quickly decided that the lore was not working well and shelved it. He repurposed an idea from the story about "living" power sources into the [[taynix]]. A tweaked version of the story could still take place in Cytoverse continuity and Brandon has not ruled out rewriting it.
; Additional *[[The Reckoners]]* works
:; *[[Lux (book)|Lux]]* (prose)
:: The audio original was released in 2021, and a prose version was planned some point in the future. However, the prose version is on indefinite hold due to pacing and continuity issues and other priorities.
:; *Lux* sequels
:: Two sequels to *Lux* were considered, with the first sequel featuring [[Mizzy]]. Brandon had previously mentioned some ideas for a standalone Mizzy book, so these would have likely been integrated into the sequel, with [[Steven Michael Bohls]] continuing as co-writer. However, Brandon has referred to *The Reckoners* as "finished", with only a small chance of any future sequels.
; *[[Dark One (franchise)|Dark One]]* novel
: *Dark One* was planned as a multimedia franchise, with three graphic novels, two audio originals, and a possible TV show and podcast. A graphic novel, [[Dark One Book 1|*Dark One* Book One]], was released in 2020, and a prequel audio original, *[[Dark One: Forgotten]]*, was released in 2023. As of 2025, most of these projects have stalled, and the second audio original titled *[[Dark One: Prophetic Histories]]* has been cancelled. However, [[Dan Wells]] has continued to work on a novelization of the original Dark One outline that could be published in the next few years. The novel may include some of the characters from *Dark One: Forgotten*.
; *[[Adamant]]*
:Formerly titled *The Lurker*. Planned as a sequence of six novellas, two of which will be written by other authors. Brandon wants to wait until all four of his novellas are written before finding co-authors. Only one episode has been written, but he has had a breakthrough in some visuals and worldbuilding. The second novella could be written at any time. There was some consideration of a shared universe with *Skyward*, but this is not likely. It remains in limbo due to other projects taking precedence.
; *[[I Hate Dragons]]* novel
: After Brandon made a short dialogue-based writing exercise named *[[I Hate Dragons]]*available for free on [https://www.brandonsanderson.com/i-hate-dragons/ his website], as well as 4 chapters of a more [https://www.brandonsanderson.com/i-hate-dragons-extended-version/ fleshed-out version], he created an outline for a full book, which hasn't been further realised. The story was included in *[https://a.co/d/g1txveB Dragon Writers: An Anthology]* and the *[[Tailored Realities]]* collection. Although he still has the full book outline, Brandon has not mentioned revisiting the project in many years.
; *Zeek Harbinger, Destroyer of Worlds*: Middle grade sci-fi humor, potentially a follow-up to the Alcatraz series. About a young man who whenever he visits a planet, the planet blows up shortly after for various reasons (such as a nuclear catastrophe).  Each book would be set on a different world and tell the story of how it blows up. Has not been mentioned since 2012.
; *Mulholland Homebrew's Sinister Shop of Secret Pets*: Middle grade/YA humor, about a girl who accidentally apprentices herself to a guy who runs a fantasy pet shop. First mentioned in 2012, Brandon is still interested in the story as of 2022, but it's more likely to be adapted in a different format, if at all.

## "Trunked" Works
These are works that are unlikely to ever be published either because elements have been co-opted for use in other books (as with *Mythwalker* and *Aether of Night*) or because the book has been re-written from scratch (as with *White Sand* Prime and *The Way of Kings* Prime).

; [[White Sand Prime|*White Sand* Prime]]
: Brandon's first novel, later re-written as [[White Sand (prose)|*White Sand* (prose)]].
; *[[Star's End]]*
: Brandon's second novel, a short alien-relations/sci-fi story.
; *[[Lord Mastrell]]*
: Brandon's third novel, a sequel to *White Sand* Prime.
; *[[Knight Life]]*
: Brandon's fourth novel, a fantasy comedy.
; *[[The Sixth Incarnation of Pandora]]*
: Brandon's fifth novel, a far future sci-fi involving immortal warriors
; [[Dragonsteel Prime|*Dragonsteel* Prime]]
: Brandon's seventh novel, served as his undergraduate honors thesis. Will be rewritten for the main cosmere sequence. Made available as both ebook and hardcover as part of the *Words of Radiance* leatherbound crowdfunding campaign.
; [[White Sand (prose)|*White Sand* (prose)]]
: Brandon's eighth novel, a complete rewrite of the first. This later served as the basis for his graphic novels.
; *[[Mythwalker]]*
: Brandon's ninth novel, a book that included the characters [[Vivenna]] and [[Siri]]
; [[Mistborn Prime|*Mistborn* Prime]]
: Brandon's eleventh novel, elements were later combined with *Final Empire* Prime for *[[Mistborn: The Final Empire]]*.
; [[Final Empire Prime|*Final Empire* Prime]]
: Brandon's twelfth novel, elements were later combined with *Mistborn* Prime for *[[Mistborn: The Final Empire]]*.
; [[The Way of Kings Prime|*The Way of Kings* Prime]]
: Brandon's thirteenth novel, later re-written as *[[The Way of Kings]]*. This is being published as part of the Way of Kings 10th edition Kickstarter in hardcover form and is available as an ebook for free on [https://www.brandonsanderson.com/the-way-of-kings-prime/ Brandon's website].
; *The King's Necromancer*
: An early draft was partially completed in 2010. Elements of the magic system were later incorporated into the project that became *[[Songs of the Dead]]*. The text of the original draft has been completely discarded, and there has not been any indication that it is still in the works.
; *Climb the Sky*
: An early cosmere book that was the origin of the character that eventually became [[Syl]]. It would have possibly involved aethers and flying magic.
; *Soulburner* aka *Starburner*
: A space opera/fantasy work first mentioned in 2015 that never progressed past the worldbuilding stage. It was eventually pitched as a video game concept, and became the setting of *[[Moonbreaker]]* after Brandon significantly expanded the worldbuilding guide.
; *[[Dark One: Prophetic Histories]]*
: A second audio original in the *Dark One* franchise that would have been based on Brandon's original outline. Elements of the same outline were used for *[[Dark One Book 1]]* and the unpublished novel being written by [[Dan Wells]]. The project no longer exists in any form.
