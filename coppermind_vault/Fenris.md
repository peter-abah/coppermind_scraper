> Unfortunately, the Black Bear sought out the great wolf, Fenris, once bound by Tiw. Forced to heel to the Bear's dictates, Fenris brought with him the implements of metodgodas, the end of gods.
> — [[Sefawynn]]

**Fenris**, the great wolf, is an enemy to many of the gods in the [[Earth (Frugal Wizard)|alternate medieval dimension]]. It is said that he will consume the world.

## History

> With her dying breath, she bound the wolf to the hill of the Black Bear, to slumber until the final death of the gods.
> — [[Sefawynn]] tells [[John West]] of [[Friag]]'s sacrifice

At some point in the past, Fenris took the warrior god [[Tiw]]'s hand and tasted his blood. Tiw then bound Fenris to the land. 
Later, during the [[battle of Badon]], the [[Black Bear]] turned to the darkness, freed Fenris from his binding, and forced the wolf to obey him. Fenris was in possession of the implements of [[metodgodas]], which would bring about the end of gods. This caused the gods to retreat from the battle, as they did not want to risk the end of the world. However, [[Friag]] returned to the battle when she heard the men cry out in pain, death, and desperation. After pushing back the Black Bear with the finest boasts, and binding him to the land as if he were a [[wight]], Friag was weakened. The wolf still remained unbound, and under the Black Bear's orders, Fenris took the opportunity to consume the weakened god, and he killed her. Though with her dying breath, Friag had bound Fenris to the hill of the Black Bear, where it is said the wolf will remain until the final death of the gods.

By sacrificing herself, Friag usurped fate, as according to [[Sefawynn]], it should have been Tiw who was killed by Fenris.

## Trivia
*Fenris is likely the alternate medieval dimension's version of Fenrir, a monstrous wolf from real-world Norse mythology.
* In real-world Norse mythology, Fenrir is the son of Loki. [[Logna]] seems to be the alternate medieval dimension's equivalent of Loki, and she is called "mother of monsters", so it is possible that Fenris could be Logna's son.
*Also in real-world Norse mythology, the monstrous wolf Fenrir bit off Týr's (in Old English, "Tiw") right hand after realising that the gods had bound him.
