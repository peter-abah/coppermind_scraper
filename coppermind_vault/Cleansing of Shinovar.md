The **cleansing of Shinovar** is a mission undertaken by [[Kaladin]] and [[Szeth]], accompanied by their spren and [[Nightblood]].{{book ref|sa5|6}} Their initial objectives were to to help [[Shinovar]] escape the influence of an [[Unmade]] who was believed to be there and to heal the [[Herald]] [[Ishar]] and seek his help with the upcoming [[contest of champions]].{{book ref|sa5|6}} However, they later discovered that Ishar himself was in fact responsible for the dark influence on Shinovar. On the day of the contest, they succeeded in healing Ishar and removing his influence from Shinovar. This, in turn, allowed Ishar to reforge the [[Oathpact]] and induct Kaladin as a new [[Herald]] of [[Honor]] in [[Jezrien]]'s place when [[Taravangian]] Ascended to [[Retribution]], which enabled them to protect the [[Wind]] and the [[spren]] from Retribution.

## History
### The Pilgrimage
Kaladin and Szeth would travel to Shinovar by flying along with the [[highstorm]].{{book ref|sa5|13}} Their goal was to cleanse it of an [[Unmade]]'s influence, and heal [[Ishar]], who was mentally unstable due to taking up part of the power of Odium.{{book ref|sa5|6}} 

At the start of their quest, they encountered the [[Herald]] Ishar who challenged Szeth to complete a pilgrimage, a Shin religious ritual where a person would travel to each of the ten monasteries that were dedicated to each Herald and housed many of the [[Honorblade]]s.{{book ref|sa5|33}}{{book ref|sa5|24}}{{book ref|sa5|42}} 

Kaladin and Szeth first visited the [[Stoneward monastery]]. Inside, Szeth found and fought the [[Honorbearer]] [[Rit-daughter-Clutio]]. During the fight, Szeth restrained himself from using the Surge of Division at the order of his [[12124|spren]]. Eventually, Szeth allowed Rit to entomb him in stone, and after finally being granted permission to use both of his surges, he utilized division to free himself and killed Rit. With Rit dead, Szeth was able  to claim [[Talenel]]’s Honorblade and continue on.{{book ref|sa5|40}}{{book ref|sa5|41}}{{book ref|sa5|42}} 

They then went to the [[Willshaper monastery]], where they found the Honorbearer, [[Sivi-daughter-Sivi]] dead having resisted the Unmade. They claimed her Blade and left.{{book ref|sa5|42}}{{book ref|sa5|45}} Throughout this time, Kaladin had been trying to help Szeth heal mentally, which Szeth's spren, [[12124]], did not like.{{book ref|sa5|6}}{{book ref|sa5|52}} The night after they visited the Willshaper monastery, Szeth would be brought to [[Shadesmar]] by the [[Order of Elsecallers|Elsecaller]] Honorbearer [[Pozen-son-Nash]] to fight him and the [[Order of Edgedancers|Edgedancer]] Honorbearer.{{book ref|sa5|53}} As Szeth’s spren did not like Kaladin’s ideas, he fetched the Herald [[Nale]], who tried to cleanse Kaladin's influence of Szeth.{{book ref|sa5|66}}{{book ref|sa5|69}} 

After Nale’s arrival, they traveled towards the [[Lightweaver monastery]], where Szeth killed the [[Order of Lightweavers|Lightweaver]] Honorbearer, [[Moss-son-Farrier]], and Kaladin killed the [[Order of Truthwatchers|Truthwatcher]] Honorbearer who was trying to kill Szeth from behind.{{book ref|sa5|69}}{{book ref|sa5|70}}{{book ref|sa5|71}}{{book ref|sa5|72}} They then visited the [[Truthwatcher monastery]] as a formality and then planned to visit the remaining monasteries, excluding the [[Windrunner monastery]], as the Honorblade there had been corrupted by [[Odium]].{{book ref|sa5|72}}

On the eighth day, the group arrived at the [[Dustbringer monastery]], where Szeth was tasked with fighting his sister [[Elid-daughter-Zeenid]], the new Honorbearer. Instead of complying, Szeth chose to allow Elid the chance to kill him, which Elid did not take. Elid then revealed that the pilgrimage's real purpose was for Szeth to become a Herald, before dissolving into black smoke. Nale then intervened, telling Szeth that Ishar planned to use him to refound the [[Oathpact]]{{book ref|sa5|90}}, and admitting that he was worried Elid would prove to be too weak.

The next day, Szeth and Kaladin arrived at the [[Skybreaker monastery]] where Szeth encountered Nale, instead of an Honorbearer.{{book ref|sa5|103}}{{book ref|sa5|108}} Nale said that he would let Szeth go if he swore to become a Herald, which Szeth did not do. Nale then tried to kill Szeth which Kaladin stopped. Kaladin and Nale then fought.{{book ref|sa5|108}} Kaladin did not do well in the fight until the [[Wind]] played the song that Kaladin had been playing the whole journey, the same one that had led the Heralds from [[Ashyn]] to [[Roshar]]. Kaladin then told Nale the story of the [[Derethil]] and the [[Wandersail]], which made him end the fight and understand that the law was not perfect. Before they left the Skybreaker monastery, Nale told them that they were not after an [[Unmade]], but Ishar himself.{{book ref|sa5|109}}{{book ref|sa5|110}}{{book ref|sa5|119}}

### The Contest
On the tenth and final day, Kaladin, Szeth and Nale found Ishar, in the [[Bondsmith monastery]].{{book ref|sa5|129}} They challenged him, but Ishar revealed his forces: the Honorbearers, brought back as human [[Fused]], including [[Neturo]].{{book ref|sa5|130}} They attacked Szeth, beating him up, while Kaladin and Nale, as well as the [[Wind]] tried to reason with Ishar, to no avail.{{book ref|sa5|131}}{{book ref|sa5|132}} Throughout the fight Ishar had been telling Szeth to give in and obey. This caused Szeth, tired of obeying orders, to swore the Fifth Ideal becoming the law and dismissed 12124, breaking the bond.{{book ref|sa5|133}}{{book ref|sa5|135}} Szeth would then try to use Nightblood to destroy the Honorbearers.{{book ref|sa5|135}}

Intimidated, the Honorbearers backed away. Ishar then Connected himself to Szeth and Kaladin, allowing them to feel the Heralds' pain, crippling them.{{book ref|sa5|135}} But, Kaladin stood, and swore the Fifth Ideal, while still Connected to Ishar, which healed him.{{book ref|sa5|138}}{{book ref|sa5|139}}{{book ref|sa5|140}} Kaladin Connected to [[Nightblood]] allowing it to feed on his [[Stormlight]] while Szeth wielded it, which allowed Szeth to use the Surges and he attacked the Honorbearers, killing them permanently.{{book ref|sa5|140}}{{book ref|sa5|141}} 

However, after burning through all of Kaladin’s stormlight Nightblood threatened to consume them all, until Kaladin reasoned with it enough to stop destroying evil.{{book ref|sa5|142}}{{book ref|sa5|143}} Szeth was knocked unconscious after losing an arm to Nightblood.{{book ref|sa5|143}}

==== The Oathpact Reforged ====
Ishar realized that the Oathpact must be reforged, in order to protect the [[spren]] from [[Retribution]], and had discovered a way to do so, linking the Heralds to a vision in the [[Spiritual Realm]] in between [[Desolation]]s. Kaladin agreed to become a Herald in the place of [[Jezrien]], and was made immortal.{{book ref|sa5|144}} The other remaining Heralds, [[Taln]], [[Shalash]], [[Vedel]], [[Battar]], [[Chanarach]], [[Kalak]], [[Pralla]], came there, healed and reforged the Oathpact, returning to the Spiritual Realm with Syl.{{book ref|sa5|145}}{{book ref|sa5|postlude}}

### The Aftermath
Szeth eventually awoke and found Kaladin's mortal body dead, his soul in the [[Spiritual Realm]], and buried it with the help of [[Masha-daughter-Shaliv]], his future wife.{{book ref|sa5|146}}{{epigraph ref|sa5|147}} The day came to be known as Stormfall, years later, specifically in the book [[Knights of Wind and Truth]].{{epigraph ref|sa5|147}}
