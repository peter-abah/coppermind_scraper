The **Honorblades** are the magical swords carried by the [[Herald]]s to fight the [[Voidbringers]] during the [[Desolation]]s. They were pieces of [[Honor]]'s [[Investiture]] that gave their wielders access to his essence.{{book ref|sa5|65}}{{wob ref|1572}}

## Mechanics
{{image|Weep As You Kill by Ari Ibarra.jpg|side=left|width=250px|[[Szeth]] using [[Jezrien]]'s Honorblade}}
{{quote
|With this sword, someone can do what you can, but without the . . . checks a spren requires.
|[[Syl]] {{book ref|sa2|87}}
}}

There are ten Honorblades total, one for each Herald.{{book ref|sa5|130}} Every Honorblade is unique, covered in various glyphs and patterns.{{book ref|sa1|part=prelude}} Honorblades do not need to be shaped as a sword, with Kaladin's Honorblade taking the form of a spear.{{book ref|sa5|144}} However, the Honorblades of the original ten Heralds took the shape of a sword.{{file ref|Navani's Notebook - Honorblades.jpg|Navani's Notebook - Honorblades}} If a new Herald is made, an Honorblade is created at the same time and appears before them.{{book ref|sa5|130}}{{book ref|sa5|144}}

They are technically a type of [[Shardblade]]; they were prototypes of the [[Knight Radiant]] Shardblades, and share many qualities with them.{{wob ref|8499}}{{book ref|sa5|53}} Among others, they can be bonded, summoned and dismissed much like a [[Shardblade]] can.{{book ref|sa1|prologue}} Like Radiant Shardblades, they can be used to operate [[Oathgate]]s,{{book ref|sa3|16}} and can dull their edges.{{book ref|sa5|45}} Unlike Radiant Shardblades, they cannot change shape.{{book ref|sa4|i|4}} It's unclear if they require ten heartbeats to be summoned, like dead Blades, or if they can appear instantly; this is likely tied to their wielders' perception.{{book ref|sa4|i|4}}{{wob ref|12612}}{{book ref|sa4|111}} They are near-indestructible, with the only thing known to be capable of damaging them being [[Nightblood]].{{book ref|sa4|111}} Honorblades are made out of the [[God Metal]] of Honor, and they are a physical and solid manifestation to his essence.{{book ref|sa5|65}}

Unlike Shardblades, Honorblades do not manifest as [[spren]] in the [[Cognitive Realm]], and will transition normally when brought into the Realm.{{book ref|sa5|53}} They are made of [[Honor]]'s own [[Investiture]], which he deliberately splintered off himself and crystallized into metallic form.{{book ref|sa3|16}}{{wob ref|13996}} Honorblades are self-aware, but it is currently unknown to what extent.{{wob ref|15901}}{{book ref|sa5|53}} They are able to have conversations with Nightblood.{{book ref|sa5|45}}{{book ref|sa5|53}} In this sense, Honorblades are comparable to the blade wielded by [[Vivenna|Azure]], which is also known to posses an unknown level of sapience.{{wob ref|8970}}{{book ref|sa3|108}} 

One does not need to be a Herald in order to use an Honorblade.{{wob ref|4459}}{{book ref|sa1|prologue}} Likewise, a Herald dying does not cause the Honorblade to be destroyed or vanish.{{book ref|sa3|122}} Honorblades can be corrupted if the Herald dies permanently.{{book ref|sa5|72}} The Honorblades do go with the Heralds when they travel to [[Braize]], and they appear to have a crucial role in the [[Oathpact]], as nine of the Heralds leave them behind in the [[Aharietiam]].{{book ref|sa1|part=prelude}} They are considered an anchor for the Oathpact.{{book ref|sa5|89}} A Herald seems to have a special [[Connection]] to their Blade, as [[Taln]] is still able to feel and locate his Blade despite someone else having bonded it.{{rpg ref|slsw|54}}

### Surgebinding
Holding an Honorblade inherently grants the ability to [[Surgebind]] without a [[Nahel bond]].{{wob ref|4685}}{{book ref|sa2|i|1}} This ability is similar to that of [[Feruchemy#Unsealed_Metalminds|unsealed metalmind]]s, though they rely on different magic systems.{{wob ref|9408}} 

Honorblade Surgebinding is in many ways inferior to Radiant Surgebinding; among others, the Surgebinding healing bestowed by Honorblades is less efficient than Radiant healing.{{book ref|sa1|prologue}}{{book ref|sa5|53}} Additionally, [[Surge]]s require more [[Stormlight]] as fuel to power them, up to dangerous levels.{{book ref|sa3|98}}{{wob ref|4788}} While the latter still applies to the Heralds, they were once powered directly by [[Honor]], which made the issue of Stormlight consumption irrelevant.{{wob ref|1572}}

If a Surgebinder uses an Honorblade that matches the power they already have, their powers will be slightly enhanced.{{wob ref|5618}}

## History
{{image|Aharietiam by Micah Epstein.jpg|side=right|width=300px|The [[Herald]]s leaving their Blades behind at Aharietiam}}
### Heralds and Shinovar
{{quote
|Our Blades must be left. The Oathpact ends now.
|[[Jezrien]]{{book ref|sa1|part=prelude}}
}}
The Heralds received the Honorblades from Honor during the creation of the [[Oathpact]].{{book ref|sa5|65}} They used them throughout the [[Desolation]]s until the [[Aharietiam]], whereupon nine of them left them at the site of the final battle before walking away from the Oathpact, feeling that as they had broken it, they no longer deserved the Honorblades.{{wob ref|16298}} [[Talenel]]'s Honorblade disappeared back to Braize along with him.{{book ref|sa1|part=prelude}} It is possible that these blades were left behind as more than just a symbol.{{file ref|Navani's Notebook - Honorblades.jpg|Navani's Notebook - Honorblades}}

After the Heralds departed, one of the [[Shin]] soldiers who participated in the battle stumbled upon them. Likely because of this, the nine Honorblades ended up in [[Shinovar]].{{book ref|sa3|38}}{{book ref|sa3|106}} Over the millennia, they retained the Blades, and even occasionally used them, most notably during the Shin invasions.{{wob ref|12700}} Even after the invasions failed, they would still train some of their people in the use of the Blades and the associated Surges.{{book ref|sa3|98}} Those selected to bear the Blades were known as [[Honorbearers]], and held one of the highest stations within Shin society.{{book ref|sa5|57}}

Sometime between {{Rosharan date|1159}} and {{Rosharan date|1166}},{{book ref|sa5|75}}{{book ref|sa5|83}} the Herald [[Nale]] reclaimed his own Blade from the Shin, leaving them with eight.{{book ref|sa3|106}}

In {{Rosharan date|1159}}, [[Szeth]] was chosen by [[Pozen-son-Nash]], the current bearer of [[Battar]]'s Blade, to replace [[Tuko-son-Tuko]] as the Windrunner Honorbearer.{{book ref|sa5|73}}{{book ref|sa5|75}} After completing his pilgrimage, he was given [[Jezrien]]'s Honorblade.{{book ref|sa5|83}} Around the same time, his father [[Neturo-son-Vallano]] was training to become the bearer of [[Ishar]]'s;{{book ref|sa4|i|7}}{{book ref|sa5|111}} and in time Szeth's sister [[Elid-daughter-Zeenid]] became the bearer of [[Chanarach]]'s.{{book ref|sa5|90}}

### War of Reckoning
{{image|Navani's Notebook - Honorblades.jpg|side=right|width=350px|The ten original Honorblades}}
When Szeth was declared [[Truthless]] by the Honorbearers in {{Rosharan date|1166}}, the voice of Ishar directed them to let Szeth keep Jezrien's Blade, an unusual arrangement for a Truthless.{{book ref|sa1|prologue}}{{wob ref|5089}}{{book ref|sa4|i|7}}{{book ref|sa5|117}} The Blade would go on to cut a swath through the [[Roshar]]an leadership as Szeth became the infamous Assassin in White. Among others, [[Gavilar Kholin]], [[Hanavanar]] of [[Jah Keved]] and two [[Prime Aqasix]]es of [[Azir]] fell to Jezrien's Honorblade between {{Rosharan date|1167}} and {{Rosharan date|1173}}.{{book ref|sa1|prologue}}{{book ref|sa1|i|9}}{{book ref|sa2|i|9}}

At roughly the same time, the Herald Talenel returned from [[Braize]], carrying his own Honorblade. He dropped it upon entering [[Kholinar]], but for unknown reasons, it didn't vanish.{{book ref|sa1|epilogue}} Talenel was later collected by one of [[Dalinar Kholin]]'s soldiers, [[Bordin]], to be brought to the [[Shattered Plains]]. The Blade was taken along with them; however, at some point during the transit, it was swapped out for a regular [[Shardblade]].{{book ref|sa2|76}} It's possible that the Stone Shamans were the ones who performed the swap, but in any case, they were eventually able to recover Taln's Blade and bring it to the [[Stoneward monastery]] in Shinovar.{{book ref|sa5|40}}

During the [[Battle of Narak]], Szeth used Jezrien's Honorblade to battle [[Kaladin]] above the Plains. At the end of the fight, Szeth unbonded it and it ended up in Kaladin's hands.{{book ref|sa2|87}}

### The True Desolation
Kaladin later passed the Honorblade to Dalinar, who hid it in one of the vents in [[Urithiru]].{{book ref|sa3|16}} After a while, he gave it to [[Bridge Four]], to use for training and Oathgate operation in absence of their leader.{{book ref|sa3|64}} The bridgemen would swap the Blade among themselves every once in a while in order to hide who currently had it and remain unpredictable.{{book ref|sa3|96}}

Despite that, agents of the [[Diagram (group)|Diagram]] managed to figure out the time when [[Eth]] held the Honorblade and attacked him. They snuck up to him with [[Teft]]'s sold-off uniform coat, then killed him with a [[Shardblade]] and stole the Honorblade before running off.{{book ref|sa3|i|14}} [[Taravangian]] later gave the Blade to [[Odium]] at the latter's demands. The Blade was later given to [[Leshwi]], who passed it onto [[Moash]] to allow him to join the [[Fused]] in the skies.{{book ref|sa3|122}}

At some point very recently, Ishar killed Neturo-son-Vallano and took back his Blade.{{book ref|sa4|i|9}}{{book ref|sa4|111}} Several of the Honorblades were (slightly) damaged by [[Nightblood]] during Szeth’s fight with Ishar’s Fused.{{book ref|sa5|141}}
