**Peakspren** are a subset of [[Spren#True Spren|spren]] from [[Roshar]]. They are capable of forming a [[Nahel bond]], granting people the abilities of the [[Order of Stonewards]].{{book ref|sa4|22}}

## Appearance
{{quote
|You're a mountain on this side, Ua’pam!
|[[Zu (Roshar)|Zu]]{{book ref|sa4|22}}
}}
### Physical Realm
In the [[Physical Realm]], peakspren take forms that appear to be made of stone. They can hide inside real rocks if they wish; when they want to reappear, they emerge as if breaking their form out of the rock.{{file ref|Shallan's Sketchbook - Peakspren.jpg|Shallan's Sketchbook - Peakspren}}

### Cognitive Realm
In the [[Cognitive Realm]], peakspren inhabit humanoid bodies formed of stone. The population of peakspren is quite varied as to the type of stone, which seems to include every variety found on Roshar.{{file ref|Shallan's Sketchbook - Peakspren.jpg|Shallan's Sketchbook - Peakspren}} When they touch or rub parts of their body together, it sounds like stone grating together; they also move heavily, and their voices sound rather like grinding rock.{{book ref|sa4|22}}{{book ref|sa4|24}} The skin of peakspren is etched with cracks that emanate molten light and sometimes seem to form shapes akin to a [[glyph]] or symbol. Despite the molten quality to the fissures' appearance, their skin is only warm to the touch.{{book ref|sa4|22}} Peakspren wear human clothing, which is sometimes tailored to their specific form,{{book ref|iote|32}} and tend to be bald, with their heads sporting more cracks than the rest of their bodies.{{book ref|sa4|22}}{{file ref|Shallan's Sketchbook - Peakspren.jpg|Shallan's Sketchbook - Peakspren}}

## Abilities
Peakspren themselves seem to have no special abilities beyond those inherent to all spren living in [[Shadesmar]]; Unativi in particular defers to Shallan's [[Knights Radiant|Radiant]] powers as more suitable than his own abilities to deal with any unexpected danger.{{book ref|sa4|29}}

### Nahel bond
When a peakspren [[Nahel bond|bonds]] with a human, the human becomes a [[Stoneward]] and gains the ability to manipulate the [[Surges]] of [[Cohesion]] and [[Tension]].{{book ref|sa4|part=ars}}

## Culture
{{quote
|Kasiden peakspren, from the east? They are fools! Forget them.
|[[Ua'pam]]{{book ref|sa4|22}}
}}
Peakspren share some culture, speech patterns, and mannerisms with the [[Unkalaki]].{{file ref|Shallan's Sketchbook - Peakspren.jpg|Shallan's Sketchbook - Peakspren}} They tend to use a gendered version of "it", instead using "he" or "she", particularly for emphasis.{{book ref|iote|32}} Their naming conventions are at least sometimes similar to the Unkalaki.{{rpg ref|slhb|175}}

They have different tribes or nations in Shadesmar, such as the Kasiden peakspren that originate in the east. There is a rivalry between these groups just as tends to arise between humans.{{book ref|sa4|22}} The city of [[Haka'alaku]], near [[Cultivation's Perpendicularity]] on the Shadesmar side, is ruled by Peakspren.{{book ref|sa5|147}}

Peakspren also differ in personal opinions and attitudes towards issues, rather than being one homogeneous group. For instance, [[Ua'pam]] argues in favor of throwing their lot in with the humans to defeat [[Odium]], while [[Unativi]] believes that they can ride out the conflict without getting involved.{{book ref|sa4|26}}

## History
The first time members of the [[coalition of monarchs]] took note of peakspren during the era of the [[True Desolation]], a group including [[Adolin]], [[Shallan]], [[Kaladin]], their spren, and [[Azure]] noticed some while traversing the city of [[Celebrant]] in [[Shadesmar]] in {{Rosharan date|1173}}. As they were seeking passage to either an [[Oathgate]] or a [[perpendicularity]], they asked a peakspren who reported that he was headed east towards [[Freelight]] if he would consider taking them aboard his ship. The friendly spren seemed ready enough to do so, but told them to return in a half hour after the ship's inspection had taken place; before they had opportunity, however, [[Syl]] arranged for them to be taken on board the *[[Honor's Path]]* to avoid a group of pursuing [[Fused]].{{book ref|sa3|102}}

In the year following this encounter, several [[Stoneward]]s, including [[Badali]] and [[Zu (Roshar)|Zu]], moved to [[Urithiru]] with their peakspren. Zu and [[Ua'pam]] were chosen to go on a diplomatic mission to [[Lasting Integrity]],{{book ref|sa4|21}} while the other Stonewards and their squires stayed behind and endured the occupation of the tower by [[Raboniel]] and her forces.{{book ref|sa4|39}}{{book ref|sa4|43}}

As the diplomatic detail to the [[honorspren]] required a ship to transport them, Ua'pam recruited his cousin [[Unativi]] to take them on his barge. When Adolin saw that a group of other peakspren comprised the crew, he mentioned the peakspren leaving them "stranded" in Celebrant. Ua'pam dismissively told him to forget the Kasiden peakspren. Even Unativi, who had agreed to sail the group of humans, refused to go to Lasting Integrity itself, compromising by taking them straight south to [[Nameless]].{{book ref|sa4|22}}

The peakspren were entranced by how well Adolin had "trained" [[Maya]]{{book ref|sa4|22}} and enjoyed visiting with Zu.{{book ref|sa4|24}} They welcomed [[Vathah]]'s help with odd jobs around the barge during the trip and spent some time using their payment of [[Stormlight]] to manifest goods from the ocean of beads; this gave rise to an argument between Ua'pam and Unativi when the latter wanted to capitalize on Ua'pam's talent for manifestations and couldn't understand why he felt like he needed to fight in the war.{{book ref|sa4|26}} When the party finally landed at Nameless, the crew of peakspren were delighted to have an opportunity to peruse the town when Shallan offered to stay behind on the barge with their possessions.{{book ref|sa4|29}}
