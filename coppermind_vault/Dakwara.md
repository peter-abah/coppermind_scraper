The **Dakwara** is a [[Evil|Type 1-6 Threnodite entity]] that lives in the [[Cognitive Realm#Drominad subastral|Drominad subastral]].

==Mythology==
In [[Eelakin]] mythology, the Dakwara is a beast that [[Cakoban]] defeats in order to safely bring his people to [[First of the Sun]]. The Dakwara is referred to as "the monster child of a distant god." Cakoban blinds the Dakwara with torchlight and tricks her into tying herself into knots. Following her defeat, the Dakwara agrees to serve Cakoban and protect his family. The Eelakin then find [[Patji's Eye]] and First of the Sun. 

It is believed that you must never run from the Dakwara, as impressing her with bravery is the sole way to defeat her.

According to ancestral Eelakin, the Dakwara belong to a class of entities called the daccwaga, though whether this term originated with this specific entity or if this entity gained its appearance and attributes based on existing stories of the daccwaga is unknown.

==Description==

The Dakwara appears as a gigantic snakelike creature. Over a hundred feet tall, it consists of nearly pure [[Investiture#Anti-Investiture|anti-Investiture]]. It is very difficult to harm with conventional or Invested weapons. It differs from other Type 1-6 Threnodite entities because of the influence of the thoughts and beliefs of the Eelakin people. 

Before being bested by [[Sixth of the Dusk]], the entity resides in a moat at the center of a flat doughnut-shaped [[Dakwara's island|island]] in the Drominad subastral, guarding a cave in the very center. It swims in circles in the unsea, surrounded by hundreds of [[skullsnake]]s. After being bested by Sixth, the Dakwara is then commanded to surround and guard Drominad's [[perpendicularity]] island in the subastral. Unlike other entities of its kind, the Dakwara moves smoothly, without any afterimages or strobe effect.

## History
The Dakwara inhabited the [[Cognitive Realm#Drominad subastral|Drominad subastral]] for an unknown amount of time. It is unknown if the Type 1-6 Threnodite entity inspired the legend of the Dakwara in [[Eelakin]] mythology, or if the thoughts and beliefs of the Eelakin people influenced it to become the Dakwara; [[Sixth of the Dusk|Dusk]] believed that [[Cakoban]] may have influenced the entity to become what his people knew as the Dakwara. At some point in the past, Cakoban grappled with the Dakwara and allowed the Eelakin to discover [[First of the Sun]]. Since this confrontation, the Dakwara inhabited an [[dakwara's island|island]] in the Drominad subastral, guarding Cakoban's bones.

During the space-age of the [[cosmere]], the [[Malwish]] military set up a base on the Dakwara's island, as they believed a [[perpendicularity]] was housed in the cavern the Dakwara guarded. This was due both to Dusk's lies and to the Malwish belief that the Dakwara must be guarding something. After some time on the Scadrian outpost, Dusk defeated the Dakwara by safely making it to the inner island that it was guarding. According to Eelakin legend, the Dakwara must serve the person by whom was defeated, and because the Dakwara had been influenced by the Eelakin people, Dusk was able to give it commands for at least one hundred years. This period could be longer if no one else bested the Dakwara.

After being used by Dusk to save [[Chrysalis]] and the crew of the *[[Dynamic]]* from the Malwish military, Dusk commanded the Dakwara to guard the Drominad perpendicularity, preventing outside players like the Malwish military from using it. This allowed the people of Drominad to gain a foothold in the cosmere without outside pressure or influence. Shortly before Dusk left to accompany the crew of the *Dynamic*, he commanded the Dakwara to serve [[Vathi]] and anyone who was later elected to her position.
