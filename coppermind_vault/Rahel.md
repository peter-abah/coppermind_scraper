**Rahel** is a [[Order of Truthwatchers|Truthwatcher]] and a [[ward]] of [[May Aladar]] on [[Roshar]].{{book ref|sa5|39}}

## Appearance and Personality

### Appearance
Rahel is a young, presumably [[Alethkar|Alethi]] woman, around seventeen years old. She has long ombre hair that goes from dark brown to light brown.{{book ref|sa5|50}}

### Personality
She is uncomfortable with her status as a Truthwatcher and prefers that the public know that she is instead an [[Order of Edgedancers|Edgedancer]].{{book ref|sa5|39}} While working in the hospital, she is ashamed that she cannot heal [[Talenel|Taln]]'s mind -- the same way she feels about her inability to heal lost limbs. She is also repeatedly reminded of the horrors of war after having likely never seen war in her life.{{book ref|sa5|50}} She is also remarkably brave as she volunteers to enter the Azish imperial palace in an attempt to reclaim the throne, though she had foreknowledge of enemy resistance.{{book ref|sa5|127}}

## Attributes and Abilities
### Surgebinding
Rahel has a [[Nahel bond]] with an unnamed [[mistspren]]. As such she has access to the Surges of [[Progression]] and [[Illumination]].{{book ref|sa5|39}} Like all Surgebinders, she has superhuman speed, strength, and healing when holding [[Stormlight]].

==== Progression ====
Rahel is most practiced with this power as she uses it throughout the [[Battle of Azimir]], being the only Radiant that could heal (and the only Radiant in general, for most of the fight).{{book ref|sa5|39}} With this power she can heal others, though she cannot yet heal lost limbs or those with damaged minds.{{book ref|sa5|50}} She should likely be able to make plants grow with her powers.{{book ref|sa2|part=ars}}

==== Illumination ====
Though she is less practiced with it, Rahel has access to the Surge of Illumination. This allows her to make illusions like a [[Order of Lightweavers|Lightweaver]].{{book ref|sa2|part=ars}}

### Wardship
Rahel is the ward of [[May Aladar]] and was a part of [[Adolin Kholin]]'s scribing team. As a ward she would have all the abilities of one.{{book ref|sa5|39}}

## History
Shortly before the Battle of Azimir, Rahel bonded an unknown [[mistspren]], making her a [[Order of Truthwatchers|Truthwatcher]] and was trained by Radiant [[Precilia]] in healing. She later went to the [[Azir|Azish]] capital of [[Azimir]] to act as a scribe for Adolin with her teacher May Aladar the day the [[Battle of Azimir]] began. After May told Adolin about her, Rahel was assigned to be a healer for the battle, where she stabilized the most direly wounded.{{book ref|sa5|39}} Throughout the battle, while she was not needed she would read novels.{{book ref|sa5|50}}

On the second day of the battle, she was visited by Adolin, who was doing a round of the hospital. She told him that she had tried to heal Taln's mind and felt guilty that she could not heal him. Adolin reassured her by telling her that it was her job to just keep the most direly wounded alive until other medics could get to them.{{book ref|sa5|50}}

A few days later, she was brought to heal Adolin who had lost a leg after the dead [[thunderclast]], [[Terushal]], had fallen on him.{{book ref|sa5|85}}

Two days later, as Azimir fell, she was brought to the emperor's saferoom, where she healed Adolin's wounds from fighting the [[singer]]s.{{book ref|sa5|125}}

On the day of the [[contest of champions]], Rahel joined the group that would enter the palace to try and reclaim the throne; just before they left, she healed Adolin's stump one last time.{{book ref|sa5|127}}{{book ref|sa5|128}} While Adolin fought the [[Fused]] [[Abidi]] in the throne room, she waited outside the doors waiting for the singers to attack. When Adolin emerged victorious over Abidi, she, alongside [[Noura]] -- who had reclaimed the Azish [[Shardblade]] from Abidi -- and [[Jaskkeem]], would hold the throne against until the contest of champions began.{{book ref|sa5|135}}

She likely survives the battle, as the only enemy to enter the throne room was killed before it could do any damage.{{book ref|sa5|136}}
