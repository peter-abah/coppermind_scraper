The **Malwish Empire** is a nation on [[Scadrial]]. They are a dominant force on their home planet, and one of the most powerful ones in the entire cosmere. They are also considered to be a very aggressive and fascist regime.

## Culture
### People
The Malwish Empire is home to a wide variety of peoples and species. At their head, however, stands the Malwish people themselves, from the planet of Scadrial. It is unclear whether or not the other Southern Scadrian ethnic groups are a part of the empire. The empire also works with some rather exotic species that need to be extremely hot to survive comfortably. Many of the Malwish themselves have either pale or tan skin, but some are of a browner skin tone, similar to that of the [[Eelakin]]. The Malwish, and Scadrians in general, are said to love their secrets. They see the [[Sleepless]] as terrible abominations. The people of the empire speak Malwish, the original language of the Malwish people. They assume everyone else speaks Malwish too, which has the side effect of forcing many people across the cosmere to learn the language. 

The Malwish Scadrians are said to be incredibly proud of their technological wonders. The people of the empire also have their own way of measuring time. Malwish Scadrians are said to have a fetish for metal; they have a tendency to eschew other materials such as plastic, and make things out of metal instead. They enjoy eating [[kalofruit]], and have been known to drink [[ravamak]] and [[kaftea]]. 

### Fashion
#### Clothing
The Malwish Scadrians wear a wide variety of different clothing styles, but some features are distinctly theirs. They often have large shoulder pads, and they wear slacks. Many of the people from colonized planets inside the empire dress similarly to the Malwish Scadrians, trying to imitate their styles. In the early days of the Space Age, some Malwish wear black jeans, and their space force loves a specific type of fancy shirt.

#### Masks
Like most of the other Southern Scadrian peoples, the Malwish, those from Scadrial at least, also wear masks over their faces. The type and make of their mask can vary wildly, but they are generally made of metal, and they cover at least some of their features. Some Scadrians wear full face masks, some wear helmets instead, and some just wear adornments. At least some Malwish make certain changes to their masks which indicate things such as what political movement or party they belong to. Those with red enamel on their masks belong to the [[TimeTellers]].

Some masks appear as a pair of two-inch-long metal grafts at the sides of the face, extending along the cheekbones and beneath the hair under the ears. These masks are attached directly to the skin. Others, the ones that look like simple adornments, appear as smaller, masklike metallic bits at the edges of one's face, as if stuck there by glue. Full Malwish arcanists often wear ceremonial masks. These masks are made of wood, carved after the old style of mask, painted black, and "weighty with import." Lastly, some soldiers have been known to wear war masks when they are going to fight. A war mask is a large mask that extends from nodes at the side of one's face, plating one's forehead, cheeks, and the ridge of the nose in a way that outlines the eyes with sharp angles. It appears that war masks also have a psychological function, as some people find them to be intimidating. Malwish Scadrians often take their masks off, but they tend to put them on when concentrating or when something is getting serious.

### Technology and Resources
#### Spacecraft
The Malwish have developed great quantities of spacecraft, used to both civilian and militaristic ends. They are known to have large capital ships, which they use as a home base while making contact with yet uncontacted peoples or planets. In addition to this, they have sleek vehicles to ferry themselves from the ship to the planet and in reverse. These machines take the appearance of a silvery triangle pointed toward the sky and seem to work via Steelpushing. The Malwish are capable of creating spaceships completely by themselves, a necessity, considering that they are at war. They often claim, and truly believe, that they're the most technologically advanced planet in the cosmere, but many dispute this. 

Their military spacecraft have protective shells around the most important equipment, preventing a Sleepless's nodes from entering and destroying the vehicle. In addition to this, the Scadrians are known for their composite metal hulls, which are bought and used by many of the cosmere's powers. Scadrian ships work best when set up inside a Scadrian [[steelfield]]. In places where deploying such a field is impossible, such as in Shadesmar, the Scadrians can't help using the tools of their enemies, like Rosharan anti-grav technology. In the early days of the Space Age, the Malwish create many research centers which are sent to faraway planets, such as [[Canticle]]. Each of these centers has an authorization key, a modern device to let the center be located and give it authorization to return to one of the Scadrians' exploratory starships.

#### Other Technologies and Resources
[[Nomad]] believes that the Malwish would be very interested in the Canticlite [[Chorus]], Threnodite shades who are somewhat self-aware and who can rearrange the structure of metal to create devices by directly following blueprints. They have also developed a technology called Awakened Steelminds, often used inside starships. The Malwish know how to make [[Feruchemy#Special Metalminds|metalminds]] that can force people to store or tap an attribute. They use this technology to create metalminds that adjust one's weight automatically, allowing Scadrians to more easily colonize other planets. There are [[aetherbound]] serving the Malwish.

## Politics
### Government
#### On Scadrial
The government of the Malwish Empire is located on the planet of [[Scadrial]]. They are considered to be, at least by some people, a fascist regime. Their government seems to made up of various ministers and is ruled by a prime minister, who appears to be the head of party. It seems that many of the government's officials are former members of the military. Their courts are considered to be biased and to contain corrupt judges. Despite being a fascist regime, it seems that the Malwish have many different political movements.

Many of the Malwish elites dislike [[Hoid]]. The government requires ships that land on Malwish soil to have a registration number. There are aetherbound serving the Malwish, making them far less vulnerable to the Dhatrian planetary network. The Malwish are generally not very tolerant of politically-minded aspirants who fall from grace.

#### Colonial Dominions in the Cosmere
Besides their own planet, the Malwish rule (parts of) a number of other planets. It is unclear how official this is, as at least some of the planets are considered to be independent still, although the Malwish rule in all but name. Each occupied planet is governed by a colonial governor, who is at least sometimes one of the natives. The Malwish are known for their habit of claiming land, sending patrols, and coming up with their own rules and regulations. They patrol half the lanes between planets in Shadesmar, including the [[Vapor-Firmament corridor]], but not including the territories near [[Silverlight]]. They have the power to place things under their "exploratory jurisdiction." They are generally cautious regarding the [[Night Brigade]], collecting shades to use as leverage.

### Foreign Policy
#### Roshar
The Malwish are at war with at least some Rosharan peoples. This war isn't an ordinary kind of war, however, because none of those involved dares risk full-on invasion. They all jostle for position, trying to gain an advantage, occasionally coming to a battle or skirmish but mostly trying to threaten and dominate everyone who isn't involved. The two powers are constantly competing for territories. Both of these powers, for example, are involved in the attempt to claim First of the Sun.

#### Eelakin
To the [[Eelakin]], the native inhabitants of [[First of the Sun]], the Malwish are known as the *Ones Above*. The Malwish contact them sometime during the Space Age; to the Eelakin, they seem to be gods with incredible technologies. Artistic depictions of them from the broadsheets at the time tend to depict them mysteriously, as beings with dark holes for faces. The Malwish want [[Aviar]], Invested birds native to the planet. However, they cannot just invade, as that might anger one of the Shards. Instead, they have to follow various treaties and laws which state that invasion can only be ratified if the Eelakin pose a threat.

The Malwish try various ploys to claim the planet, giving the Eelakin gifts of technology and trying diplomacy to win the people over. Both of these ploys fail, allowing the Eelakin to hold out. Eventually, the Eelakin find a way to ensure that the Malwish, along with other cosmere powers, leave the planet alone.

### Economics
#### Dominions
The Malwish Empire has a large and well-faring economy. Its wealthiest planet is Scadrial, where the Malwish homeland is. Besides Scadrial, the Malwish have a great deal of other dominions. These planets and countries are also economically dominated by the Scadrians. They own all the best real estate, and many resources make their way back to the home planet.

#### Space Travel and Fuel
The Malwish spacecraft, like nearly all others in the cosmere, rely on aethers as fuel. Most of these aethers are mined on a small planet called [[Lumar]]. Due to Lumar's distance from the other systems, ships have to use FTL travel to reach it, which is incredibly expensive. The Scadrians earn a great deal of coin by producing and selling parts needed to make spacecraft, such as hulls.

## Military
The Malwish are known for their military prowess, along with their aggressiveness. Malwish troops are extremely disciplined, capable of carrying out very precise orders while under attack. Losing seven people during a non-combat operation leads to a mandatory external review. The military also pays spousal pensions to the spouses of those who die. The Empire has many military installations across the cosmere and maintains the ability to seize assets and imprison folk for trespassing on such installations.

### Organisation and Hierarchy
#### Branches
The Malwish military is divided into several main branches. The largest and most important branch in the military is the [[Malwish Planetary Guard]]. This branch is responsible for safeguarding Malwish interests offworld. Another branch is the military police. This branch exists to make sure that members of the military don't break laws and to punish them if they do. The military police is known to have their own prisons, where deserters, among others, are sent to. The prisoners in these facilities are made to do hard labor. In addition to this, the military has its own arcanists, who work to uncover the cosmere's secrets and design new weapons, such as the [[Intensifier]]. At least some of these scientists work secretly, pretending to be neutral in the conflict. The Planetary Guard, if not other branches as well, has an entire corps made up of engineers. Many people also have a subspecialty, gained by serving in another branch.

#### Ranks
The Malwish military has a number of different ranks. A colonel, for instance, is put in charge of a whole group of soldiers and often leads their own missions. In addition to this, the military has ensigns and even special ops. What branches someone has served in or what subspecialties they have can easily be recognized from their badges.

### Equipment
Due to the Malwish's state of war, and their current regime, the Malwish military is extremely well funded, probably one of the richest militaries in the cosmere. It possesses large capital ships with cannons that can reduce large cities, like those of the Eelakin, to rubble with a casual shot or two. In addition to this, it possesses a number of smaller capital ships and many fighters, which are said to be among the cosmere's best. Their ships function best when inside of a Scadrian [[steelfield]]. Their soldiers don't usually carry [[Lodestar (technology)|lodestar]]s, although they do receive them. This is possibly due to the Scadrians having produced all of their own equipment, rather then relying on outside producers. It seems that only people of a certain rank can wear white gloves.

## History
### Early History
It is unclear when and how the Malwish Empire formed. However, it can be assumed that it formed before the Space Age truly started, as the Malwish were already a major power during the early days of space travel.

### War with Roshar
#### Early War Effort
It is unclear how the war with Roshar started, but in the century since FTL travel was invented, the war had already developed into a full-blown arms race. A group of researchers known as the TimeTellers, who were ostensibly neutral in the conflict but were in reality a part of the Malwish military, set to work discovering the secrets of the cosmere. They travelled to [[Canticle]], facilitating the Cinder King's conquest of the planet, and learned about its local Investiture. At some point, the Malwish and Rosharans fought at [[battle of Aheleha|Aheleha]].

#### Expansion towards Drominad
At some point during the war, people realized that there was a planet in the [[Drominad system]], appropriately called Drominad by the Scadrians, that held a lot of Investiture. Curiously, reports from before the Space Age mentioned the Invested birds that lived there, and so people realized that there had to be a perpendicularity there. The birds, capable of facilitating a [[Nahel bond]], were valuable to the Malwish, but a perpendicularity would be even more valuable. Not only would it open up an entirely new section of space for cheap travel, saving billions in FTL expenditures, but it would boost the Malwish's military might, as the planet was situated somewhat in between Roshar and Scadrial.

The Malwish traveled to the planet and made contact with the natives. They needed to make the planet into one of their dominions; however, the presence of such a large amount of Investiture meant that they couldn't just invade, lest they accidentally anger one of the [[Shard]]s. Instead, they needeed to take over the planet by negotiating and signing treaties. The [[Eelakin]], who were just about entering the industrial age and completely unaware of the wider cosmere, began negotiations with the Scadrians. The Malwish gave them new technologies and focused on trying to buy the birds. The Eelakin resisted, however, refusing to sell their [[Aviar]]. Meanwhile, the Malwish began searching for the perpendicularity, at one point even plainly asking the Eelakin about it.

The years dragged on, and the Malwish became increasingly irritated. They hadn't found the perpendicularity, and the locals kept refusing any deal regarding the Aviar. The Scadrians began trying other ploys. They gave new technologies to the locals, trying to advance them so that they would have a legitimate reason, according to cosmere-wide treaties and laws, to conquer the planet. In addition to this, they made the Eelakin reliant on their aid and goods and even purposely waited with sending their goods so that life in the [[Eelakin Islands]] came to a halt. After eight years, the Scadrians managed to back the Eelakin into a corner, and they signed a provisional treaty regarding the trading of the Aviar. Meanwhile, they managed to find a small, round island, which contained a moat and an inner island inside of it, in the Cognitive Realm that was guarded by a [[Dakwara|giant Invested snake]]. The island also contained a deadly tunnel leading to the inner island. The Malwish in the area, under Colonel [[Dajer]], believed that they had found the perpendicularity, which was sure to be through the tunnel on the inner island. However, everyone sent through the tunnel died there. Even the machines they sent in were destroyed, and the snake prevented things from flying to the island by air.

During those eight years, starfighers became significantly more expensive. The Malwish also developed a new device known as the [[Intensifier]]. They developed it in secret but let a few external arcanists witness its power as a way of showing off Malwish military might. Following its construction, they used it to break open the lock of one of dragonkind's ancestral caverns, the one that had held a [[Dawnshard]], and set to work tracing the weapon via Spiritual [[Connection]]. Six years after their first contact with the Eelakin, the Malwish prime minister, [[Yadramak]], was assassinated by partisans. They attacked him while he was speaking with a Sleepless spy, and during the attack some of the Sleepless's nodes were killed as well, causing the Malwish to think that a Sleepless had assassinated him.

Colonel Dajer spent three weeks trying to figure out how to get through the tunnel. Eventually, his men spotted a [[Dynamic Storyteller's Incredible Conveyance|trading vessel]] that had heard of the perpendicularity. They captured it, along with one of the [[Sixth of the Dusk|natives]] who had appeared near the island in a small boat. The native man told Dajer that he could get through the tunnel, and eventually he managed to do so, discovering that the tunnel led to nothing. The man, while everyone was distracted, managed to cross the moat onto the inner island, defeating the snake [[Evil|entity]] and creating a bond between them. While the crew of the trading vessel tried to escape, the native ordered the Dakwara to help them, and the snake killed Dajer. After this, the Eelakin man led the snake to the real location of the perpendicularity, which he had used to cross into Shadesmar. The Dakwara began guarding the perpendicularity, ensuring that no Malwish could come close without permission. In addition to this, the Scadrian Malwish were forced to retreat from the planet in the Physical Realm, and they had to abandon every hope of ruling the planet.
