**Rockbuds** are plants with rock-like shells native to [[Roshar]]. The term refers to all plants that have shells as well as to the "true" or "common" rockbud. The shell protects the more sensitive organism within from [[highstorm]]s. After storms, tendrils reach out of the bud to lap up the water on the ground. Rockbuds have also been seen to ingest [[Chasmfiend]] blood. Rockbuds form their shells by drawing minerals from their environments, most often from cremwater.

## Domestic Cultivation
The vines of domestic rockbuds have slower reactions than that of wild buds. The [[listener]]s are known to accelerate growth of rockbuds using [[Stormlight]] from [[gemstone]]s, though this method eventually breaks the gemstone. Colder climates will stunt the growth of rockbuds. In warmer areas buds can grow nearly to the size of a barrel with vines as thick as a human wrist; however, in the [[Frostlands]] they grow no bigger than a fist and move slowly, as if numbed by the cold.

## Uses
Rockbuds provide a variety of uses. They contain watergourds which can be used for medicinal purposes, provide food for both [[chull]] and human consumption, and dried rockbud pulp is used in paper making. Though they do not burn as well as wood, the shells may be used as fuel for fire as the exterior creates a barrier between the interior and moisture. The inside of certain rockbud shells can also be shredded to create textiles. Some dishes are also steamed inside rockbud husks. Many archers on Roshar, both human and [[Listener|Parshendi]] use certain types of rockbud leaves to fletch their arrows. The flowers from certain species of rockbud are traded and used as decorations.

## Rockbud Family
Besides referring to the True Rockbud, also known as the Common Rockbud, the term "rockbud" can signify a variety of other plants on Roshar. The following plants are categorized as rockbuds: 
* [[Lavis|Lavis Polyp]]
* [[Prickletac]]
* [[Vinebud]]
* [[Curnip]]

## Trivia
* When found upon stone buildings rockbuds must be scraped away as they can eat away the stone in the walls, creating pockmarks and cracks.
