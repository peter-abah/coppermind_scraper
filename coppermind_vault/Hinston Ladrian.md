**Hinston Ladrian** is the son of [[Edwarn Ladrian]] and the heir to [[House Ladrian]] on [[Scadrial]].{{book ref|aol|10}} He is a cousin to [[Waxillium]] and [[Telsin Ladrian]].

## History
Prior to Hinston's birth, Edwarn had started training Wax and Telsin to be the heirs of House Ladrian, essentially raising them. Hinston was born to Edwarn and his wife as their first and only child in {{date|317|FA}}, when Wax was eighteen.{{book ref|mb5|5}} Once Hinston was born, Edwarn started preparing him to take over the house instead.{{book ref|mb4|epilogue}}

By around 340-{{date|341|FA}}, Edwarn felt that Hinston was ready to assume the title of High Lord of House Ladrian. Edwarn enacted plans to funnel House Ladrian's money to the [[Set]], intending to fake his own death and disappear. Once these plans were already in motion, Hinston succumbed to an unknown disease and died prematurely at the approximate age of 24. Edwarn could not abandon his plans, so he faked his death anyway and named Wax his successor instead, hoping that he could be manipulated.{{book ref|mb4|epilogue}}

## Legacy
A few weeks after Hinston's death, Edwarn, his wife, and Telsin went for a carriage ride in the [[Outer Estates]], supposedly to take their minds off of their grief. They took a carriage to a viewpoint because Hinston's mother was said to have been too weak for a hike. During the trip, their horses bolted and the carriage went over a cliffside.{{book ref|mb4|10}} In fact, the accident had been contrived so that both Edwarn and Telsin could fake their own deaths.{{book ref|mb4|epilogue}}{{book ref|mb6|3}} The fate of Hinston's mother is unknown, but it is likely that she died in the accident.

Edwarn planned for Hinston to take over House Ladrian even though he knew it would be destitute; Edwarn had also offended most of the other nobility in [[Elendel]].{{book ref|mb4|epilogue}}{{book ref|mb4|1}} Hinston had therefore been set up to fail, and was likely unaware of his father's behind-the-scenes dealings with the Set. Edwarn does not express much grief about Hinston's disease and untimely death, calling it "unfortunate".{{book ref|mb4|epilogue}}
