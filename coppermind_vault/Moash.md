**Moash**, also known as **Vyre**, is an [[Alethi]] man on [[Roshar]]. Originally a [[bridgeman]] in [[Bridge Four]],{{book ref|sa1|14}} he becomes a soldier and bodyguard under [[Kaladin]]; eventually, he leaves Bridge Four and switches sides to the [[Diagram (group)|Diagram]], then the [[Fused]], serving them as a slave and warrior. He's the current holder of [[Jezrien]]'s [[Honorblade]].

### Appearance

Moash is lean, though strong, with a narrow hawkish face and brown hair speckled with black.{{book ref|sa1|30}}{{book ref|sa1|14}}{{book ref|sa1|64}} He is tall, has a prominent nose, and has a scar on his chin.{{book ref|sa2|2}}{{book ref|sa4|102}} Born [[darkeyed]], Moash has brown eyes which turn tan after he receives a full set of Shards.{{book ref|sa2|77}} When using [[Jezrien]]'s [[Honorblade]], Moash's eyes glow blue.{{book ref|sa1|i|3}} Moash is a young man, potentially close to Kaladin's age.{{book ref|sa1|14}} He is Alethi, and thus has tan skin and an [[Wikipedia:Epicanthic fold|epicanthic fold]].{{wob ref|4079}}

Unlike many other members of Bridge Four, Moash does not have slave brands on his forehead. Due to this, he gets his Bridge Four tattoo on his left upper arm.{{book ref|sa2|2}}{{book ref|sa3|45}}

Following being named Vyre, Moash wears a custom uniform in the same style as the Bridge Four uniform, but dyed black and with no shoulder patch.{{book ref|sa4|6}} He also appears to have lost weight, with his cheeks appearing sunken in.{{book ref|sa4|102}}

After the Occupation of Urithiru, Moash gets impaled with crystal eye spikes through his empty eye sockets after his eyes get burned away.{{book ref|sa5|i|7}} The crystal eyes glow violet, possibly with Voidlight, and jut from the back of his skull and the sides of his head, resembling a crown of crystals. Moash still has eyelids around his eye spikes, and can blink.{{book ref|sa5|67}}{{book ref|sa5|81}}

### Personality

Moash comes off as standoffish and grim, holding people at arm's length when he first meets them. Though once he does warm up to someone, he gets attached extremely quickly.{{book ref|sa1|14}}{{book ref|sa1|30}} Once he joins a group, he's friendly and amicable, and often the driving force behind more relaxed, off-duty activities.{{book ref|sa2|46}} He values his friendships, and tries to salvage them when he can.{{book ref|sa2|83}}{{book ref|sa2|56}} He's eager to gain power and he admires those who do have it.{{book ref|sa1|73}} At the same time, he's highly suspicious of those above him in the social order.{{book ref|sa2|2}} He has a strong sense of justice,{{book ref|sa3|48}} originating with the injustice visited on his grandparents earlier in his life.

Moash tends to view people's actions by the external factors that motivate said actions, external factors such as one's position as a lighteyes or darkeyes in the Vorin caste system.{{book ref|sa3|48}} As he becomes more involved in the Fused army and pushes deeper into Odium's emotional voiding, his old priorities slip away.{{book ref|sa2|87}}{{book ref|sa3|51}} 

His need to see [[Elhokar]] and then all [[lighteyes]] face retribution consumes him and pushes him further and further away from friends and into the waiting arms of the [[Voidbringers]].{{book ref|sa3|84}} Eventually, as he gives in to Odium's power completely, he seems to lose any drive altogether, telling himself he feels no emotions even as he accomplishes his vengeance. In this period, he follows the commandments to seek out passion and follow the orders of the Fused.{{book ref|sa3|121}}{{book ref|sa3|122}} Although he continues to care for Kaladin, he thinks that suicide is the only way that Kaladin can be saved from Odium's torment, and in his own despair, urges Kaladin to kill himself rather than risk Kaladin joining Odium.{{wob ref|14165}}

## Attributes and Abilities
Moash is a skilled spearman, enough so to kill an attacking [[Fused]], and was one of the best fighters while he was in Bridge Four.{{book ref|sa2|2}}{{book ref|sa3|43}} A fast learner, Moash tends to pick up new skills quickly and works diligently at them.{{book ref|sa2|25}}{{book ref|sa1|63}} He's also a decent spear teacher and commander, able to train a group of [[singer]]s before the attack on [[Kholinar]].{{book ref|sa3|51}}

For a time, Moash possessed a set of [[Shardblade]] and [[Shardplate]]. While he was able to move naturally in his Plate after less than a week, he is less skilled with the Shardblade, finding it too clunky, and still defaulted to the spear when his life was on the line.{{book ref|sa2|68}}{{book ref|sa3|43}} Eventually, he's given [[Jezrien]]'s [[Honorblade]], granting him control over the [[Surge]]s of [[Gravitation]] and [[Adhesion]]. He appears to be natural at using it, as it takes him no time at all to drink in [[Stormlight]] and use Lashings.{{book ref|sa3|122}}

Following the Occupation of Urithiru, he was left blind until Odium and [[Battah]] impaled [[crystal spike]]s into his eyes, allowing Moash to see [[Investiture]] as outlines of light - seeing living things, infused gemstones, and [[spren]], but not color. Moash is able to distinguish different "rhythms" of light. This procedure appears to have granted Moash sight similar to [[Steel Inquisitor]] Steel Sight, although focused on Rosharan Investiture. It is currently not known if this is due to the material used, nature of Investiture on Roshar, or the intent present when performing the Hemalurgy like procedure.{{book ref|sa4|111}}{{book ref|sa5|i|7}}

## History
### Early Life
Both of Moash's parents died when he was a child. He was raised by his aging grandparents, [[Ana]] and [[Da]], who both worked as silversmiths in [[Kholinar]].{{book ref|sa2|44}} It was his grandfather who named him. Later, Moash would discover that his name was [[singer]] in origin.{{book ref|sa3|54}} Moash was generally met with hostility and distrust by other people, and likely had a troubled childhood because of it. This was one of the reasons Moash joined the caravans for work, which his grandparents encouraged.{{book ref|sa3|45}} He did not want to enter the family business, preferring jobs that were more physically active.{{book ref|sa2|44}} Moash worked the caravans with his uncle when he was young.{{book ref|sa3|48}} Later during his time as a caravaneer, Moash would also work with [[Rill]], a mercenary, and [[Guff]], a wheelwright.{{book ref|sa2|46}}{{book ref|sa3|45}}

While he was working on a caravan for a few months, his grandparents became victims in the [[Roshone#Kholinar_and_the_Roshone_Affair|Roshone Affair]] which led to them dying while incarcerated in the palace dungeons. Moash ultimately blamed [[Elhokar Kholin]], who was then a royal prince but would later become the king of Alethkar, for their deaths. Ana and Da had been dead for two months by the time Moash returned and was told of their passing by a neighbor after returning to an empty home. The pain of losing them helped shape Moash's personality.{{book ref|sa2|44}}

### Bridge Four
==== Sent to the Bridgecrews ====
Moash joined the war voluntarily with the hope of winning a Shardblade, and traveled to the Shattered Plains in [[Jam (caravaneer)|Jam's]] caravan.{{book ref|sa3|45}} Assuming he would be made a spearman, Moash instead got sent to the bridge crews along with most of the other people who joined with him, ending up in [[Bridge Four]]. Moash initially tried to run away from the bridge crews, but he was caught and beaten for it, with the threat of receiving a slave brand if he tried to run again.{{book ref|sa1|46}}

When [[Kaladin]] first began his attempts to reform Bridge Four, Moash was singled out to be made an example due to his strong appearance and since he was one of the men who ignored Kaladin's command to get up, instead rolling back over to sleep more. Kaladin pulled Moash out of bed and punched him in the stomach, picking him up over his shoulder and taking him outside the barracks while he was stunned. Irritated at this treatment, Moash called out to [[Gaz]], asking if they needed to follow Kaladin's orders. Receiving a negative from Gaz, Moash told Kaladin to storm off unless he planned on beating all of them up, and he and the other bridgemen dispersed.{{book ref|sa1|14}}

Moash continued to distrust Kaladin's intentions and openly disliked him. While many of the other bridgemen also disliked Kaladin, he was one of the most vocal.{{book ref|sa1|27}} When Kaladin chose to stand instead of resting on a bridge run, Moash swore at him.{{book ref|sa1|17}} He was the first one to laugh at the idea of pooling their money and food to help the wounded bridgemen Kaladin brought back, claiming that if it were him, he'd rather die quickly on a bridge run than slowly in the barracks.{{book ref|sa1|21}} Moash, along with [[Sigzil]], refused to look at Kaladin when he tried to pull the bridgemen into conversation on chasm duty.{{book ref|sa1|27}} Moash did let Kaladin use his flint and steel to cauterize wounds, however, which he likely had due to it being standard equipment that caravaneers carried with them.{{book ref|sa1|17}}{{book ref|sa3|45}}
 
When Kaladin picked up and stared at a spear during chasm duty, Moash taunted him, calling Kaladin "lordling," which he picked up from talking with Gaz. Moash shared with [[Narm]] what he learned from Gaz, claiming that Kaladin was lying about being a previous squadleader. This triggered the other bridgemen to start complaining about Kaladin with each other, until they were stunned into silence by Kaladin's kata that he performed. Moash himself dropped the spheres he was holding in shock. That night, Moash accepted [[Rock]]'s stew, though Moash still refused to rise the next morning to train when Kaladin called for it.{{book ref|sa1|27}}

Later, Kaladin named Moash a subsquad commander for Bridge Four, along with Rock, [[Teft]], and [[Skar]]. Kaladin told the four to go gather their men, but Moash lingered behind to ask Kaladin why he chose him to be one of the subsquad leaders. He explained that it was because Moash was so vocally resistant to Kaladin's efforts for so long, and that in addition to being capable and intelligent, it showed Moash was strong-willed, which Kaladin could use. Moash accepted this, but claimed that he was not like the others who revered Kaladin, and he still didn't trust him. When asked why Moash was choosing to obey him then, he claimed he was curious before moving off to follow Kaladin's previous orders.{{book ref|sa1|30}}

Despite his efforts to distrust Kaladin, Moash joined Teft and Rock in telling Kaladin they won't forget him and will carry on his legacy after Kaladin is beaten and strung up for attempting the side carry maneuver on a bridge run. Moash commented that this way they would only lose one member thanks to what Kaladin did, and that he did it for them.{{book ref|sa1|34}} Moash was awed by the side carry maneuver at the time, not realizing the harm it had done to the greater battle.{{book ref|sa1|32}}

Moash shared with Kaladin his desire to win a Shardblade and how he was sent to the bridge crews after Sigzil told them about how the kingdom [[Babatharnam]] decides who gets placed in power. Unlike Kaladin, Moash said that he'd join the lighteyes if given the opportunity and that he still planned to win a Shardblade to change the world so abuses wouldn't happen to anyone. Even though Moash said he would make lighteyes work the difficult and dangerous jobs instead of darkeyes, he denied Sigzil's warning that the world would stay the same.{{book ref|sa1|46}}

==== Learning to Fight and Trust ====
{{sidequote
|You’re a fool and an instigator. But you’re an honest one. If you get us killed, it won’t be on purpose. Can’t say that for some I’ve served under.
|Moash to [[Kaladin]].{{book ref|sa1|53}}

Moash was eager to begin training with spears in the chasms.{{book ref|sa1|43}} When Bridge Four started their training, Moash was one of the first who changed into a fighting man and only needed two hours to start exercises Kaladin usually wouldn't start until the second or third day of training. Only Skar and Drehy were nearly as talented as Moash was.{{book ref|sa1|49}} Later, on the bridgerun when [[Dunny]] died, Moash used one of the moves Kaladin taught him to knock Kaladin to the ground and prevent him from running onto the bridge in an attempt to try and reach Dunny's body, and probably saved Kaladin's life. After Kaladin tended to the wounded bridgemen, Kaladin told Moash that he was right to stop him, and Moash then told Kaladin that he was wrong about him and they shook hands.{{book ref|sa1|53}}

Once Moash fully accepted putting his trust in Kaladin, he later defended him when Skar questioned how Kaladin would get the spears out of the chasms once it was time to run, much to Kaladin's surprise. During that same chasm duty shift Kaladin found an emerald broam, which Moash became infatuated with and insisted he could swallow so they could keep it to spend on medical supplies. Moash was crestfallen when Kaladin repeatedly told him it would be too suspicious for a bridgeman to spend a broam, despite Bridge Four being able to keep an equal amount of other spheres in smaller denominations.{{book ref|sa1|55}}

Bridge Four continued to get more disciplined and loyal to Kaladin. Moash and the other bridgemen always chose to stand beside Kaladin at parade rest while the army crossed their bridge, despite passing soldiers jeering and throwing overripe [[Pilevine|pile-vine]] fruits at them, one of which hit Moash in the face. After they returned from that bridge run, Moash led Bridge Four in doing their first salute to Kaladin, which Teft had taught them.{{book ref|sa1|57}}

The situation for Bridge Four became more dire after [[Hashal]] informed them they would be required to go on every bridge run while continuing to perform chasm duty every night.{{book ref|sa1|59}} Exhausted after barely getting any sleep, Moash and the other bridgemen felt discouraged on their chances of survival. Moash suggested charging the soldiers and [[Torol Sadeas|Sadeas]] as he passed, and said that if they were to die anyway they could take out a few lighteyes before they were killed. Some of the bridgemen liked this idea, but Kaladin dismissed it and said he had another plan, which satisfied Moash. Kaladin's plan, which was to wear armor adorned in singer bones and carapace to draw arrow fire to himself instead of the bridgemen, worked, much to the joy of Bridge Four. Moash was excited by the idea and immediately began thinking about expanding on it. Later, Moash commented that their luck that day was almost enough to make Moash buy his own prayer armband despite his criticism of Kaladin's purchase of one before the bridge run.{{book ref|sa1|62}}

Following Kaladin's success, Bridge Four's scavenging quota in the chasms was decreased, giving them more time to train secretly. Moash was "surprisingly good" with the spear, and often kept training while the other men took breaks. Kaladin recognized the dedication in Moash and related to how he felt after [[Tien]] died, and advised Moash not to overwork himself. Moash told Kaladin when asked that he worked so hard because of vengeance, but declined telling Kaladin who he wanted to kill. Moash then told Kaladin that it was because of him that Moash's plans were still possible, and swore to guard Kaladin with his life. Kaladin felt more comfortable with Moash because he didn't seem to revere him in the same way the others did.{{book ref|sa1|63}}

====Battle of the Tower====
On Tanatashev 1173, Bridge Four participated in the [[Battle of the Tower]], where the combined armies of Highprinces [[Torol Sadeas|Sadeas]] and [[Dalinar]] assaulted the Tower. Moash was one of the four decoys wearing carapace armor, along with Kaladin, used to attract Parshendi arrows. Teft was injured in the approach with an arrow to the shoulder, and Moash carried Teft urgently over to Kaladin to be tended to once their bridge was set.{{book ref|sa1|63}}{{book ref|sa1|65}}

Over an hour later, Kaladin called Moash to stay by him when he noticed Sadeas retreating from the battle, and followed Kaladin through the crowd to watch Sadeas, unharmed, cross back over bridge ten.{{book ref|sa1|66}} Bridge Four left with the rest of Sadeas' army, falling slowly behind due to carrying their two wounded members. Despite the opportunity to escape once they got far enough behind, Moash and the rest of Bridge Four agreed with Kaladin to go back and help Dalinar's army.{{book ref|sa1|67}}

After Kaladin exhausted himself from using too much Stormlight in order to protect Bridge Four on the approach, Moash commanded Bridge Four and directed them to lift the bridge back up to run it into place.{{book ref|sa1|67}} Once Kaladin said the Second Ideal and recovered to fight on the other side of the bridge, Moash led a group to defend the bridge from Parshendi that had jumped the chasm to attack. Though all three dead were on Moash's side of the battle, he felt energized by the hard fight instead of weakened. Moash stayed with Kaladin when he went to find someone in command once the Alethi soldiers broke through to the bridge. As Kaladin fought through Parshendi to get to [[Adolin Kholin]] to tell him to retreat, Moash followed close. He reluctantly went back with Adolin when told to do so by Kaladin.{{book ref|sa1|68}}

When the battle was over, Dalinar traded his Shardblade, [[Oathbringer (Shardblade)|Oathbringer]], for the bridgemen.{{book ref|sa1|69}} Moash and the rest of Bridge Four celebrated with stew in the Kholin warcamp that night, and Moash expressed eagerness to try and learn how to use Radiant powers like Kaladin.{{book ref|sa1|73}}

Moash decided to stay to become a bodyguard for [[Dalinar Kholin]] underneath [[Kaladin]], who was now appointed as a captain. Kaladin led Moash, [[Skar]], and [[Teft]] through the camp the next morning and explained the plan to train the rest of the bridgemen. Moash was skeptical that the hundreds of worn down bridgemen could be turned into fighting men, and was also reluctant to put his trust in Dalinar. However, he was excited to be further trained by Kaladin and hoped to train with swords despite being darkeyed. Kaladin named Moash a lieutenant as second in command behind Kaladin, along with Skar, Teft, [[Rock]], and [[Sigzil]]. Skar and Moash were specifically chosen to focus on guarding Dalinar, as they were the most proficient and dangerous of Bridge Four.{{book ref|sa2|2}}

Later that day, Bridge Four received tattoos over their slave brands, marking them as free men. Moash felt conflicted on getting the tattoo, since he did not have a slave brand, and decided to get the tattoo on his upper arm to Kaladin's approval. He was the only member - even of those without a slave brand - to get the tattoo placed on his arm instead of his forehead. Kaladin then lead Bridge Four to the quartermaster, [[Rind]], to receive their uniforms. Moash received his uniform first, and was skeptical of how it would look on him. However, after he saw himself in the mirror and was complimented by the quartermaster, Moash blushed and accepted that the uniforms looked good, which cued the rest of Bridge Four to put on theirs.{{book ref|sa2|2}}

Shortly after becoming a bodyguard, Moash was approached by [[Rill]], a mercenary who Moash worked with when he was a caravaneer. Rill introduced Moash to [[Graves]], who brought Moash into his plans for assassinating King [[Elhokar Kholin]].{{book ref|sa2|46}}

A bit under two Rosharan weeks later, Kaladin took Moash, [[Drehy]], [[Mart]], and [[Eth]] to guard Dalinar during a meeting with Elhokar at the palace. Moash tried to threaten the low ranking lighteyed guard at the palace gates after the guard doubted Kaladin, but Kaladin cut Moash off and chastised him, much to Moash's annoyance. After having a moment to think and cool down, Moash apologized to Kaladin and agreed that Bridge Four is above provoking fights and that he'll do better. Once at the meeting, Drehy and Moash took watch on the balcony. Dalinar, realizing that their plans would put more than just him at risk, expanded Kaladin's team to guarding [[Navani]], [[Adolin]], and [[Renarin]] as well as having them slowly replace the King's Guard.{{book ref|sa2|5}}

The first time Moash guarded Adolin and Renarin while they trained, he was underwhelmed by how ordinary the lighteyed training grounds looked. After Kaladin spoke disrespectfully to an [[ardent]] about the King's Blades, Moash reminded Kaladin that he had previously told him that they should avoid provoking lighteyes. Before moving off to his guard position, Moash questioned Kaladin about who [[Amaram]] was, having noticed Kaladin's distress since he arrived at the warcamps. Kaladin told Moash that Amaram was the one that had given Kaladin his slave brands, and Moash confirmed with Kaladin that they would somehow get back at the lighteyes who had wronged them.{{book ref|sa2|16}}

====Shardbearer====
When [[Adolin]] tried to give [[Kaladin]] the gift of [[Shardblade]] and [[Shardplate]], Kaladin instead gave them to Moash, thereby making him a lighteyes in rank.{{book ref|sa2|66}} The Shardblade had a twisting vine pattern along the center of the blade, and the Shardplate, previously belonging to [[Abrobadar]], was painted orange.{{book ref|sa2|66}} Moash later had the Plate repainted to be blue with red accents.{{book ref|sa2|68}} His eyes started to lighten a week after receiving the Shards,{{book ref|sa2|77}} though he still did not consider himself lighteyes.

Moash became involved in a plot with [[Graves]] to assassinate King Elhokar Kholin, which was ultimately thwarted by Kaladin. Moash took his Plate and Blade and fled the warcamps with Graves after their failed attempt.{{book ref|sa2|87}}

They head out into the wilderness with a caravan of stolen goods. Graves intended to introduce Moash to the [[Diagram (group)|Diagram]], hoping that bringing back a full Shardbearer could compensate for his plan's failure. A short while into the trip, they were attacked by a group of [[Fused]]. Graves was killed, and Moash attempted to fight with his Shardblade but deemed it too unwieldy. He got hold of a spear and was able to kill [[Leshwi]], one of the Fused.

Upon seeing this, the other Fused gave Moash a choice, he could give up his weapons and come with them, or die at their hands.{{book ref|sa3|43}} They did this because they thought that Leshwi would want to have revenge once she was reborn. Leshwi did not want revenge however, considering Moash to have great passion.{{book ref|sa3|54}}

### Servant of the Fused
{{image|Moash - IX. Hermit.jpg|side=left|width=250px}}
As a slave, he pulled supply sledges across the country. On one of his routes, he saw a group of singers pulling sledges like the other slaves. He stood up for the group, gaining more respect among the Fused and the singers.{{book ref|sa3|48}} Eventually, he joined the assault on Kholinar as a warrior for the Fused's army.

In Kholinar, he and his singer companions fought with Kholinar's Wall Guard. At the climax of the fight, Moash found his way to King [[Elhokar]], and stabbed him through the chest. He then stabbed the king again in the eye, just before Elhokar could finish swearing the [[Immortal Words#The_First_Ideal|First Ideal]]. His vengeance was fulfilled, and he looked up to see Kaladin, who was in Kholinar with the king on a mission to save the city. Moash saluted his former captain, who had frozen in shock at what happened, and left.{{book ref|sa3|84}}

Following the siege, he was put to work as a laborer clearing out the debris. There he was approached by a Fused, [[Hnanan]], who gave him a [[Vyre's knife|bright golden knife]] with a sapphire attached to it. The Fused then sent him to assassinate the [[Herald]] [[Jezrien]], whom he killed with the knife.{{book ref|sa3|121}} Some time after the successful assassination, the Fused, [[Leshwi]], offered Moash Jezrien's [[Honorblade]] as well as a new identity. He accepted, took the singer name **Vyre**, which meant *He Who Quiets*, and used the Honorblade to join the Fused in the air.{{book ref|sa3|122}}

{{image|Renarin confronting Moash by rspixart.jpeg|side=right|height=300px|Being confronted by [[Renarin]]}}
A year later, Moash took part in the Fused assault on Hearthstone, where he murdered Roshone in front of Kaladin, who went into a state of battle shock. Moash, now no longer capable of reasoning in a conventional way,{{wob ref|14165}} then attempted to persuade Kaladin to commit suicide. He was stopped by Renarin, who showed Moash a vision of the man he could have been had he not betrayed Bridge Four, a Windrunner of the Third Ideal, causing Moash to flee.{{book ref|sa4|8}}

In Kholinar, he sat in on a session of the Nine, where he warns them that Kaladin will foil their plan to occupy Urithiru.{{book ref|sa4|14}} Later, he was approached by Odium, who intended to break Kaladin and questioned Moash on the best ways of tormenting his former captain.{{book ref|sa4|i|4}}

### The Occupation of Urithiru
{{dialogue

|3=Odium|4={{small caps|And yet, you think so often of Kaladin.}}
|5=Moash|6=I am … mostly unchained.

During the occupation of Urithiru, Vyre used his Honorblade to operate the [[Oathgate]] between Kholinar and the tower for the [[Fused]].{{book ref|sa4|41}} When the uprising to take Urithiru back began, he set a trap for Kaladin in his family's clinic, but the trap was instead sprung by [[Teft]] and [[Lift]]. Moash injured Lift, then used a [[raysium]] dagger containing [[anti-Stormlight]] to permanently kill Teft's spren, [[Phendorana]], before killing Teft.{{book ref|sa4|103}}{{book ref|sa4|104}} He handed [[Lirin]] to the Fused as insurance against Kaladin, ordering them to kill Lirin in front of his son should Kaladin fight back.{{book ref|sa4|105}}

Moash then descended to the basement of Urithiru to kill Queen [[Navani]]. Nearly succeeding, he was ambushed by an injured [[Raboniel]], which gave Navani time to bond the [[Sibling]]. After restoring the tower, she used its Light to incapacitate Moash and forcibly return his pain to him.{{book ref|sa4|110}} Moash fled, hating himself for what he had done to Teft and realizing that he did not truly regret the murder, only the guilt. He eventually broke his spine in a fall and nearly died before being rescued by a few Heavenly Ones and given Stormlight. When he recovered, he found that he was blind.{{book ref|sa4|111}}

### Transformation
{{image|Spiked Moash by ghostfellow.jpg|side=right|width=250px}}
After being brought to [[Kholinar]], Moash found that Odium was no longer feeding on his emotions, and began to dwell on his actions in Urithiru. Moash was certain he had seen Teft's spirit in the glow behind Navani, the last thing he saw before he was blinded. Laying in bed, Moash clawed at his face and begged to have his pain taken away again, the protection from his guilt being the reason Moash followed Odium in the first place. Odium appeared and told Moash that unlike [[Rayse]], he didn't think taking emotions was right and refused Moash's pleas. He insisted Moash was not a monster, but rather a hero for his actions, and explained that he needed to be able to handle the price of what he had done if he wanted to be worthy of his title and [[Honorblade]].

Odium then placed a vision into Moash's mind of "every wrong being righted" - the Fused armies bringing unity and peace to every world, lead by the Blackthorn, with working class people getting retribution against kings. He told Moash that if he joined him, he would have a part in that, and his pain would be worth something. Moash asked how, and Odium stepped back to allow [[Battah]] to strap Moash down and impale [[crystal spike]]s into Moash's empty eye sockets with a mallet. While Moash screamed, in his thoughts he claimed to reject his guilt and condemned Kaladin for serving lighteyes despite claiming to protect.

When the procedure was finished, Moash found that he could see [[Investiture]] as outlines of light - seeing living things, gemstones, and spren, but not color. Odium appeared to him as a blinding source of light, while Battah appeared as a different "rhythm" of light. When asked if he was ready to serve, Moash confirmed that he will "do what must be done".{{book ref|sa5|i|7}}

### Siege of the Shattered Plains
{{quote
|This will hurt me. It is the pain of building a new empire.
|Moash to Sigzil.{{book ref|sa5|118}}
}}
Five days before the [[Contest of champions]], Moash arrived on the Shattered Plains with [[El]] and the other reinforcements through the Elsegate created by [[Dai-Gonarthis]].{{book ref|sa5|67}} Moash did not join the battle until two days later when [[Leyten]] and his squires found Moash. Leyten sent a code over spanreed to [[Sigzil]] that they had found him, and intended to wait for Sigzil to arrive to engage but was caught by the fabrial Moash held that negated Radiant abilities. When Sigzil rushed over to help, he was also caught by the fabrial and he and his squires fell to the ground. Since Moash was using an Honorblade, his abilities still worked.

As Sigzil recovered, Moash killed one of Leyten's squires with a Shardblade strike to the head and kicked Leyten away. Sigzil sent his squires to retreat and Leyten used himself to distract Moash while his squires ran, Sigzil finally arriving to in turn distract Moash from Leyten. Moash didn't flinch after being stabbed in the back by Sigzil, and Sigzil then noticed Moash's crystal eyes for the first time, unnerved.

Knowing he was outmatched without his Radiant abilities, Sigzil attempted to get Moash talking and asked why he's fighting them. Moash responded that Sigzil chose the lighteyes over him despite everything they'd done to them, and that he welcomes his emotions now. Able to see Radiant spren, Moash suddenly Lashed upward when Sigzil trailed off and used his [[anti-Stormlight]] dagger to kill [[Ethenia]], Leyten's spren. Afterwards, Moash stabbed Leyten in the chest with the same knife before giving a Bridge Four salute and retreating. Leyten died in Sigzil's arms after shouting a [[Death Rattle]] that prophesied Moash killing Sigzil and [[Vienta]].{{book ref|sa5|81}}

Two days later, the final day before the Contest of Champions, Moash appeared again on the battlefield to prevent Sigzil from retreating with the rest of the army. Moash activated his fabrial, causing Sigzil to drop from the sky from a height that broke his legs and made him unable to move them. Landing next to him, Moash explained to Sigzil that he demanded the fabrials be given to him, despite their rarity and the Fused fearing the power of them. Sigzil tried to get out his knife to fight back, but Moash stepped on Sigzil's wrist and caused him to drop it, and said Sigzil was in the way of what he was going to build with the new Odium.

Moash then attacked Vienta and nicked her arm, destroying it, but did not land a direct enough hit to kill her. Before Moash could land a killing blow, Sigzil renounced his oaths as a Windrunner, which caused Vienta to disappear before Moash's attack hit. Once Vienta appeared on the ground as a [[deadeye]] Shardblade, Sigzil used it to fend Moash off. Distracted, Moash did not notice [[Lopen]], who tackled him to the ground and destroyed the fabrial, restoring Radiant powers in the area. The rest of Bridge Four then appeared to defend Sigzil and Lopen, and deciding he was outmatched, Moash retreated.{{book ref|sa5|118}}

==Relationships==
===Ana and Da===
{{quote
|He murdered them, Kaladin. That sorry excuse for a king killed the only family I ever had.
|Moash{{book ref|sa2|83}}
}}
The death of Moash's grandparents, [[Ana]] and [[Da]], influenced him greatly. Ana and Da raised Moash after the death of Moash's parents when he was a child.{{book ref|sa2|44}} They were kind, and the ones that encouraged Moash to join the caravans for work.{{book ref|sa3|45}} Moash considered them the only family he had ever had, despite also having an uncle that he worked on the caravans with.{{book ref|sa2|83}}{{book ref|sa3|48}}

===Kaladin===
{{quote
|Moash … had been his friend. They’d spent hours by the fire, talking about their lives. Kaladin had opened his heart to this man, in ways he hadn’t to most of the others. [...] Kaladin had failed Moash. As soundly as he’d failed Dunny, Mart, and Jaks. And of them all, losing Moash hurt the most. Because in those callous eyes, Kaladin saw himself.
|Kaladin's thoughts about Moash.{{book ref|sa4|8}}
}}
Moash and [[Kaladin]] have a complex relationship. During their first moments in Bridge Four, Moash was Kaladin's biggest detractor, and only later trusted him after Kaladin proved he was genuine in his efforts.{{book ref|sa1|53}} From then on, Moash supported Kaladin and they became close friends and confidants.{{book ref|sa1|55}}{{book ref|sa2|5}}{{book ref|sa4|8}} Moash and Kaladin both saw themselves in each other, and they have similar histories with lighteyes, including both having their lives significantly changed by the [[Roshone#Kholinar_and_the_Roshone_Affair|Roshone Affair]].{{book ref|sa1|63}}{{book ref|sa2|41}}{{book ref|sa2|62}}

However, Moash's desire for vengeance against King [[Elhokar Kholin]] split them apart. Moash simultaneously resented Kaladin for having defended the person who killed his grandparents, and felt guilty for having tried to kill Kaladin to get to Elhokar.{{book ref|sa3|54}}{{book ref|sa3|43}} After he finally succeeded in killing Elhokar during the siege of Kholinar, it was partially Moash's guilt in seeing how Kaladin reacted that caused him to agree to have his pain taken away by [[Odium]].{{book ref|sa3|121}}

Once Moash had his pain taken away and became Vyre, Kaladin was the only thing that regularly caused his painful emotions to return briefly. Moash became fixated on getting Kaladin to admit Moash was right so that he could become truly painless, and believed convincing Kaladin to kill himself was a mercy compared to Odium potentially taking Kaladin as Odium had taken Moash. Moash refused to physically kill Kaladin himself, however, and believed it to be impossible.{{book ref|sa4|i|4}} Though Kaladin had initially felt guilty and hurt over what had happened between him and Moash, after these actions by Moash - including killing [[Teft]] - Kaladin no longer missed him.{{book ref|sa4|8}}{{book ref|sa5|12}}
