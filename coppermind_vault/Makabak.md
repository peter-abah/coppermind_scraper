**Makabak** is a region in southwestern [[Roshar]] occupied by numerous nations that share a similar ethnicity and culture.{{book ref|sa3|12}} It is named for the former [[Silver Kingdoms|Silver Kingdom]] of [[Makabakam]] and includes most of that kingdom's territory.{{map ref|Silver Kingdoms}}{{map ref|Roshar}}

## Etymology
The name of Makabak comes from the Silver Kingdom of Makabakam.{{map ref|Silver Kingdoms}} Makabakam itself is derived from the name of [[Makibak]], one of the major leaders of the people in the early days of humanity on Roshar.{{book ref|sa5|58}}

## Geography
The region covers the southern center of the Rosharan continent. It is framed by mountain ranges to the north, east, and west, and has a long coastline on the [[Southern Depths]].{{map ref|Roshar}} The region includes the nations of [[Alm]], [[Azir]], [[Desh]], [[Emul]], [[Greater Hexi]], [[Liafor]], [[Marat]], [[Steen]], [[Tashikk]], [[Tu Fallia]], [[Tukar]], [[Yezier]], and about half of [[Triax]].{{map ref|Roshar}} The Azish Empire includes many of these nations, although it does not encompass the entirety of Makabak.{{book ref|sa3|65}} Makabak has a single [[Oathgate]], in the Azish capital of [[Azimir]].{{book ref|sa3|65}}

## Culture
There are approximately thirty Makabaki languages, most of which branched off from Azish.{{wob ref|3977}} Azish is still the most common and widely understood within the region. Deaf citizens of the empire favor Azish Sign Language, which is also practiced by ambassadors and translators.{{rpg ref|slwg|36}} Many of the nations are rich in culture{{book ref|sa3|106}} and have thriving trade.{{book ref|sa1|54}} The area has higher population density than the nations to the east{{wob ref|3825}} and the city of Azimir is larger than [[Kholinar]].{{wob ref|3824}}

Residents of several Makabaki nations are thought to avoid offending others as much as possible, and would rather dodge a request than deny it, especially if it comes from a foreigner.{{book ref|sa3|12}} Azish people are considered verbose and sometimes intentionally vague in order to conceal their true intentions.{{book ref|sa3|12}} The Azish Empire has many skilled clerks and scribes, and is thought of as highly lawful, bureaucratic, and organized.{{book ref|sa3|96}}

While the Makabaki nations have some similarities, there is variation in the specific cultures of different nations and peoples, including their social and governmental structures. Citizens who travel to other countries within the Azish Empire can often be identified by the distinct clothing of their homeland.{{book ref|sa2.5|4}}

Most cultures within Makabaki nations share a love for hospitality. Most Makabaki religions center around one of the Heralds as a singular god, and the others as demigods under their guidance; the specific Herald worshipped varies by region.{{rpg ref|slwg|28}}

Makabaki people generally have dark brown skin, black hair, and dark eyes.{{book ref|sa1|14}}{{book ref|sa2|20}}{{book ref|sa2.5|2}} The region borders [[Shinovar]], but the Makabaki people typically possess epicanthic folds, unlike the Shin.{{book ref|sa1|i|7}} They appear to be descended from [[Makibak]] and his people.{{book ref|sa5|58}}{{book ref|sa5|104}}

They appear to be split into several different peoples, including the [[Askarki]] people, and can sometimes be further divided into tribes.{{book ref|sa1|55}} The nature of any genetic and cultural links between these peoples is currently unknown.

## Politics
Azir is the largest nation in the region, and its [[Prime Aqasix]] traditionally claims authority as emperor of Makabak; in practice, the Prime only rules Azir.{{book ref|sa3|12}} Other member states of the Azish Empire recognize the monarchy and still follow certain traditions in regards to the Prime Aqasix,{{book ref|sa3|24}} but they are actually governed with a high degree of autonomy.{{book ref|sa3|65}} Even though Azir does not actually rule the entire region, it is still highly influential.{{book ref|sa3|12}}{{book ref|sa3|24}} Feuds between nations are thought to be common, although the true nature of the feuds is disputed.{{book ref|sa1|54}} Emul and Tukar are currently at war.{{book ref|sa3|8}}{{book ref|sa3|65}} The leaders of each nation are sometimes addressed by the name of their nation rather than their proper name.{{book ref|sa3|96}}

The region is thought of as politically unimportant by residents of other nations;{{book ref|sa3|106}} the lack of centralized political power causes many Makabak heads of state to be indecisive.{{book ref|sa3|24}} The large [[Vorin]] kingdoms see the Makabaki as pagans and sometimes refer to the nations as "tribes" and to the people as "barbarians".{{book ref|sa1|55}}{{book ref|sa3|24}} Conversely, the Azish harbor ill will towards the [[Alethi]] due to a genocide perpetrated by their former king, [[Sadees]] the Sunmaker.{{book ref|sa3|42}}

During [[Dalinar Kholin]]'s attempts to unite Roshar, [[Noura]] unexpectedly tells him that Azir and several other Makabaki nations have agreed to meet with him in [[Urithiru]].{{book ref|sa3|65}} It is likely that some of the Makabaki leaders were more receptive to Dalinar's offer because the [[Voidbringers]] invaded the area during the [[True Desolation]], conquering portions of Emul and Marat as they positioned their armies to attack [[Thaylen City]].{{book ref|sa3|107}} Makabaki leaders are seen strategizing with Alethi leadership, which is notable because there have not been any formal alliances between the two regions in centuries.{{book ref|sa3|111}} Dalinar thinks of the Azish generals as scribes and is surprised to learn that they have skill in military strategy.{{book ref|sa3|107}}
