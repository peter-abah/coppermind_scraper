**Aviar** are birds that have gained magical powers, or "talents," on the island of [[Patji (island)|Patji]] on [[First of the Sun]].{{wob ref|8900}}{{book ref|iote|6}} They are capable of sharing their powers with humans. Aviar were long thought to be exclusively native to the [[Pantheon]] archipelago, but other birds can become Aviar if they visit Patji and ingest the fruit of [[Cakoban's finger]]s.{{book ref|iote|18}}{{17s ref|post|1962363|*Isle of the Emberdark* typos|date=2025-07-08}}

## Appearance and Anatomy
{{image|IotE ch icon fig 13.png|side=left|width=200px|Mating plume}}
Most Aviar are relatively large birds, approximately three fists tall.{{book ref|iote|6}} Some species may be smaller or larger.{{book ref|iote|12}}{{book ref|sa4|i|5}} Their appearance varies between species, with colors including red, green, black, blue, and white, sometimes in combination.{{book ref|iote|6}}{{book ref|sa4|i|5}} Some Aviar have sharp raptor-like talons and beaks.{{book ref|sa4|i|5}} Juvenile Aviar are barely able to fly,{{book ref|iote|18}} but healthy adults are typically good fliers unless their wings are clipped.{{book ref|iote|4}}

### Behavior
{{sidequote
|The Aviar were like people—they understood things—but they relied more on emotions than definitions.
|Dusk{{book ref|iote|24}}
|side=right|size=300px
}}

Aviar are more intelligent than ordinary animals.{{book ref|iote|6}}{{wob ref|13847}} Wild Aviar exhibit behaviors similar to other birds. They live in flocks, roost in trees, and build nests where they lay eggs and raise their young.{{book ref|iote|8}} Their mating behavior somehow involves long single feathers known as "mating plumes," which are usually found near nests.{{book ref|iote|16}} Although their powers give them some protection from predators, they will quickly flee if startled.{{book ref|iote|18}} They are known to eat fresh fruit, and will also eat seeds, dried fruit, and fish.{{book ref|iote|2}}{{book ref|iote|4}}

They display distinct personalities; for example, [[Kokerlii]] is oblivious and happy, while [[Sak]] is far more reserved.{{book ref|iote|4}} They seem to understand some human speech, as they can respond to commands or other sounds from their master.{{book ref|iote|46}} Some can mimic sounds, and some trappers can understand their moods and intentions.{{book ref|sa5|i|13}}{{book ref|iote|24}} They can be trained to perform specific behaviors, such as returning to their master if they whistle,{{book ref|iote|12}} or even to attack other animals.{{book ref|sa4|i|5}}

## Abilities
The power obtained by Aviar is different for each species of bird.{{book ref|iote|18}}{{book ref|iote|12}} Many Aviar have abilities related to the [[Cognitive Realm]], but [[Spiritual Realm|Spiritual]] and even [[Physical Realm|Physical]] powers also exist.{{wob ref|10820}}{{wob ref|8449}} Some abilities work passively, while others must be intentionally activated by the Aviar, sometimes at the request of a bonded person.{{wob ref|11793}}{{book ref|iote|12}} Aviar abilities are usually very reliable, although they were disrupted by an unusual device that was given to the [[Northern Interests Trading Company]] by the [[Ones Above]].{{book ref|iote|10}}

### Nature of Abilities
{{quote
|The Aviar are special. Everyone knows the separate breeds and what they do. Why assume that a fish would learn to breathe air, if raised on land? Why assume a non-Aviar would become one if raised on Patji...
|Vathi, after learning of Sak's talent{{book ref|iote|12}}
}}

Juvenile birds can gain magical powers, or "talents", by consuming special worms that live on the island of [[Patji (island)|Patji]]. Aviar from the various islands of the [[Pantheon]] migrate to Patji to gain their talent. The worms infest fruit that falls from Cakoban's fingers that grow next to [[Patji's Eye]]; the birds then consume the worms when they eat the fruit.{{book ref|iote|18}} Birds that are not native to the Pantheon usually do not develop these unique powers. However, [[Sixth of the Dusk (character)|Sixth of the Dusk]] raised a non-native bird on the isles, [[Sak]], and found that she also developed a unique talent.{{book ref|iote|12}}{{17s ref|post|1962363|*Isle of the Emberdark* typos|date=2025-07-08}}

The abilities of the Aviar stem from a symbiosis with the worms that they eat. The worms are [[Investiture|Invested]]; once eaten, the worms grant the birds access to certain magical powers depending on their species.{{book ref|iote|18}} The Aviar can then serve as an intermediary to allow humans to use or benefit from their powers.{{wob ref|12621}} If an Aviar's [[Connection]] or [[Identity]] are manipulated, the abilities they would grant could change.{{wob ref|16767}}

Aviar do not seem to have to continue eating worms to allow their powers to function, as they are often exported from the Pantheon to the distant mainland. Trappers have established that the birds must be juveniles to gain talents;{{book ref|iote|18}}{{book ref|iote|29}} it is not clear what would happen if an adult bird ate the worms. Aviar can be transported to other planets in the [[cosmere]], although this is difficult and very rare.{{wob ref|10820}}{{wob ref|11689}} It is unclear if taking Aviar to other worlds has an effect on their abilities. However, individuals that are not native to [[First of the Sun]] are able to bond Aviar, even individuals that have access to other manifestations of Investiture such as [[Feruchemy]].{{book ref|sa4|77}}

A human cannot eat the worms or an Aviar in order to gain powers, as the symbiosis is specific to the worms and the birds.{{wob ref|8768}}{{wob ref|10841}} The only known exception to this are [[Navigator]]s.{{book ref|iote|52}}{{book ref|iote|epilogue}} It would be theoretically possible for other organisms to evolve a similar symbiosis, but it would take many generations.{{wob ref|10841}} A [[kandra]] in the form of an Aviar would not be able to gain their powers, even if they ate the worms.{{wob ref|12602}} It is possible to steal an Aviar talent through [[Hemalurgy]], but it would be very difficult to do so.{{wob ref|9527}}{{wob ref|3347}}

### Bonds
Aviar are able to share their powers with humans through a Spiritual [[Connection]] similar to the [[Nahel bond]]. This bond is not as powerful as the Nahel bond, but because of that it can be shifted between people at will.{{wob ref|11628}}{{book ref|iote|12}} The bond includes a mental connection; Dusk refers to Sak "sending" him her visions.{{book ref|iote|20}} Aviar are also able to sense the location of the person they have bonded with.{{book ref|sa4|i|5}}{{book ref|sa4|77}} The nature of the bond allows some Aviar to share their talents with people other than their usual master.{{book ref|iote|2}} A bond does not need to be fully formed in order to share talents.{{book ref|iote|24}} If a person has an ability, such as a Navigator, they may be able to share their ability with their Aviar.{{book ref|iote|33}}

Most people bond a single Aviar, but some [[trapper]]s have bonded at least three Aviar at the same time.{{book ref|iote|12}}

### Known Talents
Aviar talents have some parallels to other manifestations of [[Investiture]] in the cosmere.{{wob ref|1556}} Aviar exist in the same universe as other manifestations of Investiture, so the powers that they provide share some characteristics with other magics such as [[Allomancy]] and [[Surgebinding]]. Although known Aviar powers are similar to Allomantic [[copper]] and [[electrum]], not every Aviar talent has an Allomantic counterpart, and vice versa.{{wob ref|10820}}

==== Cognitive Concealment ====
{{quote
|It was fortunate [Kokerlii's] breed was so mind-invisible, even [[deathant]]s would consider him no more edible than a piece of bark.
|Dusk{{book ref|sotd}}
}}
One Aviar talent is the Cognitive ability to conceal their mental activity and those of others around them.{{book ref|iote|2}} The ability can be turned off, and the Aviar can allow certain entities in while keeping the rest out.{{book ref|iote|6}}{{book ref|iote|24}} It doesn't appear taxing to the Aviar to keep it on for long periods of time.{{book ref|iote|2}} It gets less effective the farther away the Aviar is.{{book ref|iote|4}} 

Some version of this ability appears to be relatively common, as flocks of wild Aviar are able to conceal themselves from mind-sensing predators such as [[nightmaw]]s. Herds of herbivores are known to sleep near Aviar roosts in order to take advantage of the mental shield that they provide.{{book ref|iote|12}}

Additionally, Aviar Cognitive concealment impedes the ability of others to sense the use of Investiture, much like an Allomantic [[copper]]cloud.{{wob ref|1556}}{{wob ref|9437}} They would be able to block an [[Awakener]]'s [[Heightening|life sense]]{{wob ref|7436}} and interfere with other methods of detecting Investiture, such as Allomantic [[bronze]] and [[voidspren#secretspren|secretspren]].{{wob ref|8897}}{{wob ref|9437}}

Five known breeds of Aviar are able to conceal mental activity, including streamers.{{book ref|iote|15}}{{book ref|iote|6}}

==== Future Sight ====
Another Aviar talent is the ability to see into the future, using the property of [[Fortune]] to glimpse the [[Spiritual Realm]].{{wob ref|792}}{{wob ref|10298}}{{wob ref|10820}} Aviar are able to see potential deaths of a person, and manifest these potential deaths as visions of corpses wherever they could be killed.{{book ref|iote|4}} Dozens of corpses can appear at once.{{book ref|iote|10}} Deaths can be from a few minutes into the future to years later.{{book ref|iote|7}}{{book ref|iote|12}}

Corpses can show an immediate, physical danger, which often shows the corpse wounded from the threat.{{book ref|iote|10}} Corpses can also be used to represent a more nebulous, abstract danger.{{book ref|iote|7}}{{book ref|iote|20}} Corpses can be given different shades, which the Aviar can use to denote different degrees of danger.{{book ref|iote|12}} If a threat disappears, the corpses will slowly fade away.{{book ref|iote|18}}

The corpses are only visible to those with whom the Aviar chooses to share their talent. This ability can be turned on or off at will, and prolonged use can tire the Aviar.{{book ref|iote|9}}{{book ref|iote|24}} Aviar are able to show others the visions, and can show the corpses of people they are not bonded with.{{book ref|iote|7}}{{book ref|iote|12}} Other Aviar may also be able to see the visions.{{book ref|iote|9}}

[[Sak]], a raven not native to the Pantheon, is the only known Aviar that has ever possessed this ability.{{book ref|iote|12}}{{book ref|iote|15}} Other ravens brought to the Pantheon have gained a different ability.

==== Other Talents ====
* Color sight: An unknown variety of Aviar allows those who bond with them to see colors they cannot normally see.{{book ref|iote|7}} They are bright red.
* Navigation: At least one unknown Aviar ability could assist with "navigation".{{wob ref|7447}}

## Ecology
{{image|Pantheon by Heatherly.jpeg|side=right|width=300px|Aviar flying over the [[Pantheon]]}}
Many species of birds in the Pantheon can become Aviar;{{wob ref|11631}} neither trappers nor scholars are immediately familiar with every species. Each species is typically found on a specific island. They can be sent to live in the [[homeisles]] or the [[mainland]] without issue, but wild Aviar are only found in the Pantheon.{{book ref|iote|18}} During the eighth year after the arrival of the [[Malwish Empire|Malwish]], there were thought to be enough Aviar for every inhabitant of First of the Sun to bond one, with more to spare.{{book ref|iote|32}} Some Aviar are referred to as "lesser" Aviar,{{book ref|iote|6}} although it is unclear if the term refers to their physical size, the nature of their abilities, or both.

## Domestication
{{quote
|An Aviar raised around humans never quite fit back in among their own kind. The same could be said of a man raised around Aviar.
|Dusk{{book ref|iote|6}}
}}
When Aviar are raised by someone from birth, they form a bond with them and prefer to stay nearby, treating them as part of their flock.{{book ref|iote|8}} Due to the incredibly dangerous conditions in the Pantheon, highly trained outdoorsmen known as trappers are the only people that can survive in the islands and raise the birds. Aviar that are raised by the same trapper usually get along. They often enjoy being scratched affectionately, and may show displeasure by lightly biting.{{book ref|iote|8}} They do well living in nesting boxes in [[safecamp]]s; trappers can allow them to come and go freely with no concern that they will not return.{{book ref|iote|8}} Trappers use Aviar as their personal companions, and also breed and sell them as their trade. Each trapper chooses a specific island to live on and becomes most familiar with the species that live on their chosen island.{{book ref|iote|2}} Most Aviar find the soft clicking of a tongue a soothing sound.{{book ref|iote|38}}

## Known Varieties
* Streamers: A breed of which the members have Cognitive concealment abilities.{{book ref|iote|6}} They have green and white colors.
* Ravens: Ravens have an unspecified talent.{{book ref|iote|15}} Sak is unique among ravens, as she grants future sight.

Various other species with different types of plumage have been observed, but the names of their species are unknown.

## History
Aviar is presumably an [[Eelakin]] term, and throughout First of the Sun's history they were found only in the Pantheon. The magical abilities and the various species of birds had been well documented by the people of First of the Sun, and centuries passed without the discovery of a new type of Aviar. The people who were most familiar with Aviar were the trappers, who underwent extensive training in order to survive on the islands and had no qualms about killing each other when they got there. They were strongly bound by tradition, and had a reverence for the islands, particularly Patji. Trappers were notoriously reticent and stubborn, and detailed information about the Aviar and their powers remained their secret.{{book ref|iote|18}} Aviars feature in folkloric stories, such as that of [[Linji]], who attempted to sail around the world without one.{{book ref|tress|24}}

At some point, several Aviar were taken to other planets. During the [[True Desolation]] on [[Roshar]], Aviar were seen in the possession of [[Mraize]] and [[Gereh]], a [[Worldhopper]] that was not from First of the Sun.{{wob ref|14529}}{{wob ref|8807}} It is not clear how the Aviar were taken off-world, as this was very difficult at the time.{{wob ref|10298}} Gereh's Aviar was later cared for by [[Lift]].{{book ref|sa4|116}} Like other birds, Aviar are referred to as "chickens" by most Rosharans.{{book ref|sa4|i|5}}

On First of the Sun, Aviar had always been exported from the Pantheon exclusively by trappers.{{book ref|iote|8}} Mercantile companies long desired to find a way to set up operations on the islands so they could raise Aviar and sell them. However, trappers refused to help them, and commercial expeditions to the islands failed due to [[deepwalker]]s and other dangerous animals. Aviar therefore remained relatively rare in the homeisles and the mainland, since trappers could only provide a limited supply. After the arrival of the mysterious [[Ones Above]], companies quickly developed new technologies such as warships with marine propulsion. This emboldened the Northern Interests Trading Company to undertake the [[Patji expedition]].{{book ref|iote|10}}

{{sidequote
|We can’t fight them. They’ll find an excuse, they’ll seize the Aviar. It makes perfect sense. The Aviar use the worms. We use the Aviar. The Ones Above use us. It’s inevitable, isn’t it?
|[[Vathi]], on the [[Ones Above]]{{book ref|iote|20}}
|side=right|size=300px
}}

During the expedition, a scientist and explorer employed by the company named [[Vathi]] befriended a trapper named [[Sixth of the Dusk|Sixth of the Dusk]] and learned several facts about the islands and Aviar that were previously unknown to the general public. Notably, Dusk told her about the fruit of Cakoban's fingers that gave Aviar their powers; Vathi was able to deduce that [[Sak]] became a new type of Aviar despite her origins in the mainland.{{17s ref|post|1962363|*Isle of the Emberdark* typos|date=2025-07-08}} She was shocked by these revelations, which contradicted centuries of assumptions by the Eelakin people.{{book ref|iote|12}}

The Company had several devices given to them by the Ones Above, including one that could detect Aviar on the islands. When some of the company opened this device on Patji, all of the Aviar became agitated, and their powers were disrupted. Dusk and Vathi eventually realized that the Ones Above desired the Aviar but were somehow prohibited from taking them from a less civilized culture. They were trying to furtively accelerate the industrial development of First of the Sun in order to skirt these rules. Dusk and Vathi vowed to try and stop them.{{book ref|iote|20}}
