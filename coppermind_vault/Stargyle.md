**Stargyle** is a [[Order of Lightweavers|Lightweaver]] of the [[Unseen Court]] on [[Roshar]].{{book ref|sa4|20}}

## Appearance and Personality
Stargyle is tall, friendly, and handsome with a ready smile.{{book ref|sa4|20}}{{book ref|sa4|98}} He has the build of a soldier, although he refuses to wear a soldier's uniform anymore. The [[lighteyed]] women of the [[Alethi]] court have a favorable opinion of him.{{book ref|sa4|98}}

{{image|Stargyle by Dragontrill.jpg|side=left|width=250px|Stargyle Lightweaving}}
## Attributes and Abilities
Stargyle is a [[Order of Lightweavers|Lightweaver]], bonded to an unknown [[cryptic]]. This grants him access to the [[surges]] of [[Surgebinding#Illumination|Illumination]] and [[Surgebinding#Transformation|Transformation]]. He is particularly skilled at seeing into [[Shadesmar]].{{book ref|sa4|20}} When he creates illusions, his method of focus is to act as if he were painting.{{book ref|sa4|98}}

## History
Stargyle was born to a pigmenter, so he grew up viewing the world through its colors. He likely was born with a different name and took the name of Stargyle later.{{book ref|sa4|98}} At some point he served as a soldier, married, and found his way to [[Urithiru]].{{book ref|sa4|21}}{{book ref|sa4|98}}

[[Shallan]] recruited Stargyle into her group of Lightweavers, which she began calling the [[Unseen Court]]. As he was one of the newest recruits, and therefore least known to her, she asked him to accompany the diplomatic mission to [[Lasting Integrity]].{{book ref|sa4|20}} At the last minute, however, his wife fell ill and he had to back out of the trip. [[Beryl]] asked to accompany the envoy to the [[honorspren]] instead.{{book ref|sa4|21}}

Stargyle ended up accompanying the [[coalition of monarchs|coalition's]] force that recaptured [[Emul]]. When [[Dalinar]] determined that the best way for him to learn to use his [[Bondsmith]] abilities was to speak to [[Ishar]], [[Sigzil]] took Stargyle and a couple of [[Windrunner]]s to scout out the situation in [[Tukar]]. On their return, Stargyle was able to create an illusion for Dalinar that played out a depiction of what the group saw as they flew over Ishar's army.{{book ref|sa4|98}}
