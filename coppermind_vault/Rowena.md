> New times? Those come whether we want them to or not. And we shall bear them as we bear the changing of seasons - which bring friends and foes, men and spirits.
> — Rowena stands up to [[Ulric]]

**Rowena** is [[Ealstan]]'s wife, and lady of [[Stenford]]. 

==Appearance and Personality==
Rowena is a tall woman. She remains calm when antagonised, and also in the face of death.

==History==
On the night that [[John West]] arrived in their dimension, Rowena and [[Ealstan]] spoke with [[Ulric Stromfin]] and [[Quinn Jericho]] inside the [[Stenford]] meeting hall. Shortly after, the four of them, along with three servants and [[Wyrm]], walked out into the entry hall. [[Oswald]], a guard and nephew to Rowena and Ealstan, held open the door for them. A further brief, and antagonistic conversation with Ulric ensued, where Rowena asserted that the people of Stenford could handle whatever came their way. Ulric took that as his cue to demonstrate how devastatingly powerful his weapons were, and blasted Oswald with his [[Torrington 11940|gun]]. After a single shot, only Oswald's feet remained in the doorway where he'd just been standing. Ealstan stepped protectively in front of Rowena, though everyone else just stood there, stunned. After some further taunting from Ulric, Quinn then pushed Rowena and Ealstan aside, and grabbed Wyrm and held him [[bearn-gisel|hostage]]. Ulric had mistaken him to be the lord and lady's son. Ulric and Quinn then left Stenford, taking Wyrm with them.

> I will prepare an urn for his burial and place him in our barrow. He died in our defense.
> — Rowena after the murder of [[Oswald]]

Rowena was the first to act after Ulric and Quinn had left, kneeling beside Oswald's remains and shaking her head. She ordered a guard to fetch [[Hairud]]. Rowena wanted to speak with both her and Oswald's mother, to tell them how he died heroically in their defence. She also intended to bury him in their barrow. As John and [[Sefawynn]] revealed themselves from their hiding spot near the entry hall, Rowena, Ealstan, and a guard went down on one knee and bowed their heads to John. They believed that he was an [[aelv]], and that their previous imprisonment of him had caused Ulric to kill Oswald. Ealstan begged for John's mercy. Sefawynn angrily denounced John as anything supernatural, because if he was, she believed he would've been able to help against Ulric. She asked Ealstan for his fastest horse so that she could give chase to Ulric and Quinn, and rescue her brother. Ealstan agreed, and Rowena asked that Sefawynn allow guards to be sent with her, as well as time to prepare some supplies. 

Sefawynn retreated to the [[wēoh]] to meditate, and Rowena continued to tend to Oswald's remains. She placed a shield over his torso which had been separated from the rest of him, and she gathered a group of female servants to assist her in caring for the body. Shortly after, as Sefawynn, Ealstan, and John were preparing to leave on the rescue mission, Rowena stepped out of the lord's manor, carrying Ealstan's shield and axe. She handed them to Ealstan, and he kissed her goodbye.

While on the rescue mission, John, Ealstan, and Sefawynn devised a plan to inform Rowena if their encounter with some [[Hordaland|Hordamen]] raiders was to go poorly. Ealstan suggested sending a packhorse home with his seal and a bloodied axe mark on a strap. He believed that Rowena would understand that to be a warning, and that she would prepare Stenford for invasion. Luckily, their encounter with the Hordamen was a success, and there was no need to inform Rowena of an impending invasion.

It is unknown if Rowena joined Ealstan in [[Maelport]] after he became the new earl.
