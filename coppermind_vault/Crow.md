**Crow** is a [[spore eater]] native to [[Lumar]]. There she was captain of the pirate ship *[[Crow's Song]]*{{book ref|tress|12}} until she became a servant of the dragon [[Xisisrefliel]] at the bottom of the [[Crimson Sea]].{{book ref|tress|52}} Later she relocated to [[Silverlight]], still under the employ of Xisis.{{book ref|iote|32}}

## Appearance and Personality
{{image|Crow by Marie Seeberger.jpg|side=left|width=250px}}

Crow is a stout, distinctive figure with a black feathered hat and a severe face.{{book ref|tress|12}} She has light tan skin with a ruddy cast to it, and is of normal height.{{book ref|iote|25}} Her heart is described as a twisted lump of smoldering coal.{{book ref|tress|25}} To the midnight essence, she smells more like a plant than a person.{{book ref|tress|31}} The only time the crew knew her to be mirthful is when a sailor speared himself in the crotch with his own boarding hook.{{book ref|tress|33}} Crow considers a nice cup to be loot.{{book ref|tress|35}}

There are limits to the jerkiness. She works alongside the crew in the rainstorm and gives the crew a barrel of booze after Pakson's death. In some cases it's calculated self preservation, as when she acceded to Salay on robbing the merchants. Salay thinks Crow's stubbornness has allowed her to survive the spore infestation for as long as she has.{{book ref|tress|45}} Crow is not the type to help out of the goodness of her heart. She can also be very paranoid.{{book ref|iote|23}} During the Space Age, she wears modern clothing, though some aspects of it still have a certain old-school nautical feel to it, as does her lodestar. Crow doesn't speak of her past. She keeps a silver flagon, always filled with water, on her person at all times.{{book ref|iote|25}} Xisis considers her to be very sharp; one of his best acquisitions.{{book ref|iote|32}}

## Attributes and Abilities
Crow is a [[spore eater]], which is a symbiosis granting extensive defensive abilities at the cost of constantly draining water from her body.{{book ref|tress|28}} She does not have true control over the Verdant vines, with them reacting on their own to protect anything that could bring harm to her. She has survived about one year longer than would be expected.

She has a sixth sense about others such as a clergyman might have,{{book ref|tress|20}} but she uses it to dominate others.{{book ref|tress|48}} She is also able to best several people in an attempted mutiny without relying on verdant aether powers.{{book ref|tress|50}} She is also a crack shot.{{book ref|tress|26}}

She uses [[Midnight Essence]] to spy on her crew members, using Midnight [[Aether spores]].{{book ref|tress|49}}

## History
### On Lumar
Crow was infected by the Verdant Aether at an unknown point in time before the events of her journey to the [[Crimson Sea]]. Later, she became captain of the *Crow's Song*, a fine merchant vessel, and convinced the crew to turn to piracy as an alternative to participating in the Verdant King's war against [[the Sorceress]].{{book ref|tress|17}} In collusion with [[Laggart]], she plans to cross the line from piracy to deadrunning, which will make the entire crew into outlaws among outlaws. Crow sought this end so her crew would be more willing to sail into the Crimson Sea. Here, Crow planned to find [[Xisisrefliel]] the dragon, who, as she learned from [[Ulaam]] the [[kandra]], might be able to cure her of her infection. The ship's prior sprouter, [[Weev]], tried to blackmail her, so she had him executed with the rationale that he wouldn't join the crew in turning to piracy. The first ship that the crew sunk was the *Oot's Dream*.{{book ref|tress|17}} They sewed their first pirate flag after Tress boarded the ship.{{book ref|tress|13}}
 
However, Crow's plot was discovered and thwarted by [[Tress]], along with other members of the *Crow's Song*. Crow attacked and defeated Tress and her fellow conspirators, but kept Tress alive, planning to sell her to Xisis in order to cure her infection.{{book ref|tress|51}} However, Tress outwitted Crow and sold the pirate captain to the dragon in exchange for various boons. Xisis accepted this bargain. Due to this, Crow was kept at the bottom of the Crimson Sea and forced to serve Xisisrefliel for the rest of her days.{{book ref|tress|52}}

### Working for Xisis
After many long years, Xisis sent Crow to captain the *[[Dynamic]]* following his inheriting of the ship.{{book ref|iote|25}} She acted as captain for six months, until her crew mutinied to save an Iriali family from a [[Evil|Type 1-6 Threnodite entity]]. Crow, having been told of a faint distress signal near the edge of the route and figuring that they could provide Xisis with extra loot, ordered the ship closer. Upon inspection, the signal came from a small Iriali ship that, after a fire, was left to float on the Unsea. They also spotted an Eidolith close to the ship. Crow told the crew to wait, figuring that they could loot the ship after the entity had killed the people aboard. However, the crew mutinied and locked the doors of the bridge, effectively blocking Crow out while they were saving the family instead.{{book ref|iote|25}} After the mutiny, remembering the lessons that her first one had taught her, she left for her rooms, and upon reaching [[Silverlight]], she left the ship, going straight to Xisis to tell him about it.{{book ref|iote|32}}
