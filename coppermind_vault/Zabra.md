**Zabra** is an [[Azish]] member of the [[Unoathed]] on [[Roshar]].{{book ref|sa5|147}}

==Appearance and Personality==
Zabra is light{{book ref|sa5|60}} and willowy{{book ref|sa5|80}}, although not much shorter than the typical Azish soldier.{{book ref|sa5|80}} She is obviously young.{{book ref|sa5|128}}

After joining the Unoathed, she wears [[Azir#Fashion|hokra]] signifying her rank as a [[Shardbearer]] and keeps her hair in braids.{{book ref|sa5|147}}

==Attributes and Abilities==
Before enlisting, it is possible Zabra had some previous training in footwork and stances.{{book ref|sa5|60}}

Zabra has some preliminary training in the bow from [[May Aladar]].

Zabra is an Unoathed and Shardbearer, able to wield her shards without [[stormlight]].

==History==
Zabra attempted to enlist to fight in the [[Battle of Azimir]], dressing as a boy, but was rejected for being too light. She argued over this decision with Adolin Kholin, citing the Azish folk tale of [[Aqqil]], but after a practical demonstration she agreed he was right not to allow her in a shield wall. Adolin offers her his knife, for self-defense should the dome fail, the chance to serve as a messenger under May Aladar, with the possibility of being trained in the bow and arrow.{{book ref|sa5|60}}

Some two days later, Adolin finds Zabra with May on the dome's balcony, struggling to draw a bow. She complains to him that the draw weight is too heavy, and he explains that it's the only way to pierce carapace-armored singers. When Zabra compares her skill and opportunity to May and later emperor [[Yanagawn]], he explains that the former has years of experience and the latter has shardplate. She jokes about killing a shardbearer, possibly him, for their plate, before she is sent off with a message. {{book ref|sa5|80}}

After the dome in Azimir falls to the enemy, Adolin asks any survivor in Yanagawn's bolt-hole that can stand to take up arms and join them in taking the throne room, including Zabra.{{book ref|sa5|127}} The group makes its way, hastily cloaked, across Azimir and through a secret entrance into the palace.{{book ref|sa5|28}} Zabra and the others are trapped outside the throne room while Adolin duels with [[Abidi|Abidi the Monarch]].{{book ref|sa5|29}} When Adolin bests Abidi and allies with the forgotten deadeyes, Zabra takes up shards as one of the Unoathed. She holds the throne room with her fellows, liberating Azir according to the [[contest of champions]]. {{book ref|sa5|134}}

A month and a half later, Zabra has settled into her position as an Unoathed and Shardbearer. Much to the distress of vizier [[Noura]], Zabra is familiar with emperor Yanagawn, as he considers the Unoathed worthy of friendship. She alerts a conference in Azimir that the rain from [[Retribution]]'s storm has let up.{{book ref|sa5|147}}
