The **Malwish Consortium**, or just **Malwish**,{{book ref|mb7|epilogue|7}} is a nation state on Scadrial located upon the [[Southern Scadrian|Southern Continent]]. It is a confederation of five South Scadrian nations under the Malwish banner.{{book ref|mb7|9}} The unification of these states was mostly achieved by a single faction. This faction is opposed to the northern Scadrians, and hates that they have caused them so much trouble over the centuries, warning that the new trellium bombs are only the beginning of new hardships. They see the north as chaotic and unpredictable. Despite their unity, the various nations still keep their own leaders, making it unclear to what extent they have really united.{{book ref|mb7|epilogue|7}}

## History
Originally, the Malwish Consortium was five separate nations; they later banded together upon the discovery of the Elendel Basin and their taking of the [[Bands of Mourning]]. As part of this merging, the other four nations adopted the banner of the Malwish nation.{{book ref|mb7|9}}

The tensions between the Malwish and [[Elendel Basin]] grew stronger during the six years of the unification of the South because Wax gave the Bands of Mourning to the [[kandra]], and the Malwish believed that they were the rightful owners of the Bands.{{book ref|mb7|9}}

Wax worried that news of the Consortium forming would make Elendel politicians believe that the [[Elendel Supremacy Bill]] was needed more than ever.{{book ref|mb7|9}}

The Malwish sent Admiral [[Daal]] as an ambassador to [[Elendel]].{{book ref|mb7|9}} When the select council received [[Waxillium Ladrian|Wax's]] message about the bomb the [[Set]] sent to Elendel, they decided to use the Bands of Mourning to push it away. Admiral Daal said that the Bands would have to be surrendered, since this would break their treaty.{{book ref|mb7|43}} When [[Adawathwyn]] tried to use the Bands and discovered that they were drained, Daal said that this still met his terms for exchange and took them. After that, he fled Elendel.

Six months after the detonation of the Set's bomb, the tensions between the Malwish and Elendel were growing even stronger because the Basin felt that the Bands were taken unjustly and the Malwish claimed that the Basin had shown aggression by even considering using them. There was talk of closing borders to Northerners.{{book ref|mb7|epilogue|5}}{{book ref|mb7|epilogue|7}}

Two years after the detonation, the Basin sent [[Marasi Colms]] as an ambassador to the Malwish Consortium.{{book ref|mb7|epilogue|7}} At an unspecified time after this, the Malwish Consortium transformed into the [[Malwish Empire]].{{ref|group=fn|text=It is not clear whether the empire came directly after the consortium, or if there were other states inbetween them.{{book ref|iote|35}}}}

## Economy
At first, the Malwish consortium and the Elendel Basin establish tenuous trade relations.{{book ref|mb6|31}} While the Malwish consortium does appear to have trade-focused airships,{{book ref|mb7|7}} most trade with the North still goes by regular ships.{{book ref|mb7|20}} However, following the Bands of Mourning being drained, the consortium bans most forms of trade with the North, especially that of [[harmonium]], as well as all tourism.{{book ref|mb7|epilogue|7}}

## Airship Design and technology
The Malwish Consortium makes use of a lot of airships. Malwish airships are headed by a single captain and multiple ‘subcaptains’.{{book ref|mb6|25}} They have portholes, two large pontoons and are very heavy, despite weight reduction of the people onboard.{{book ref|mb6|29}} It is customary to give back borrowed medallions to a ship’s captain when you leave.{{book ref|mb6|31}}
