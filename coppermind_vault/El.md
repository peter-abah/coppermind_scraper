**El** is a [[Fused]] on [[Roshar]]. El has no title and cannot hear the [[Rhythm]]s.

## Appearance and Personality

### Appearance

> Tall, with magnificent metal horns—and with his carapace somehow transformed to shimmering steel. It looked violent, but with an artistic touch, all smooth lines until the bits came to points here and there.
> — [[Venli]]'s description of El

El has horns. At each rebirth, he removes his carapace and replaces it with metal. The metal heals and integrates into his body through a combination of [[Voidlight]] healing and his own, currently unknown, abilities.

### Personality

> Humans are a poem. A song.<br />For ones so soft, they are somehow strong.<br />For ones so varied, they are somehow intense.<br />For ones so lost, they are somehow determined.<br />For ones so confused, they are somehow brilliant.<br />For ones so tarnished, they are somehow bright.<br />Radiant.
> — El's thoughts on humans

El expresses an admiration for humans. He considers them strong, intense, determined, brilliant, and bright. He studies human art forms, including writing his own [[ketek]]. He looks forward to ruling humans, and sees them as effective weapons that channel their Passion. He at one point believed that humans should not be killed, but rather repurposed to further the goals of the Fused.

Other [[Fused]], even one as powerful and dangerous as [[Lezian]] the Pursuer, are wary of crossing or complaining to him. El is clever, esteemed by the Heralds as one of the best of the singers, and is said, by Leshwi, to rarely lie. He is willing to kill his own kind simply as a way to experiment with the effectiveness of [[Lights#Anti-Voidlight|anti-Voidlight]]. He finds joy in being feared, primarily by humans.

## History
===Heraldic Epochs===
El was one of the most powerful and talented singers before the [[Oathpact]] was founded. He was lethally stabbed by [[Jezrien]], before becoming a [[Fused]], which served as a warning to the future [[Herald]]s about the resurrection powers of the Fused. At some point in the past, El suggested that humans should not be killed. He instead wanted to unite all of [[Roshar]] under [[Fused]] rule for a future war. As punishment, his [[Rhythm]]s and title were stripped from him. Prior to this point, he had the title Vyre, which translates to He Who Quiets; [[Lezian]] considered it disrespectful that this title was later given to a human, [[Moash]], though El himself did not appear to be bothered.

===The True Desolation===

> My kind wear thin, like shoes walked upon for too many miles. My honor was stripped in part because I warned of the signs. We cannot rule much longer.
> — El on the state of the Fused

When El returned during the [[True Desolation]], he began once again studying human art from the time he was on [[Braize]]. He particularly notes the way humans depict Fused as [[Voidbringer]]s in the centuries before the True Desolation. He wrote a ketek to celebrate the return of [[the Sibling]]. El appears to have increased in favor when [[Taravangian]] killed [[Rayse]] and took up [[Odium]]. He expresses admiration that this Odium was recently human. After Lezian's death in [[Urithiru]], Odium accelerated his return to give El a suitable subject for testing [[anti-Voidlight]]; El permanently killed Lezian, confirming its reported properties.

He later finally killed Jezrien, destroying the gemstone, out of respect of their friendship. He then spoke with Odium, identifying him as Taravangian. He was chosen to lead a second force to the [[Shattered Plains]] and use [[Dai-Gonarthis]] to create an Elsegate there, and, if succeeding, to rule the humans.

Before seizing the Shattered Plains, El's army agreed to a truce with [[Venli]] and the Listeners. The next day, Venli revealed that due to the deadline of the Contest, the Listener force held the Shattered Plains. Another Fused was enraged, but slain when El summoned and drove his [[Shardblade]] through their head. He agreed to the truce and left the Plains.

## Musings

> I look forward to ruling the humans. I had my title and my rhythms stripped from me for daring insist they should not be killed, but should instead be reconditioned. Repurposed. Humans are weapons. We singers revere Passion, do we not? How can we throw away such an excellent channeling of it? I love their art. The way they depict us is divine, all red shades and black lines. We appear demonic and fearsome; they project all fear and terror upon us. To humans, our very visages become symbols. You find echoes of it even in the art from centuries before this Return. Watch them struggle. Witness their writhing, their refusal to surrender. Humans cling to the rocks with the vigor of any Rosharan vine. Humans are a poem. A song. For ones so soft, they are somehow strong. For ones so varied, they are somehow intense. For ones so lost, they are somehow determined. For ones so confused, they are somehow brilliant. For ones so tarnished, they are somehow bright. Radiant. And so I am not at all dissatisfied with recent events. Roshar will be united in its service of the greater war. And I will march proudly at the head of a human legion. They should not be discarded, but helped to their potential. Their final Passions. Yes, I look forward to ruling the humans. Nearly as much as I look forward to serving you, newest Odium. Who was so recently one of them. You understand. And you are the one I’ve been waiting to worship.
> — Musings of El, on the first of the Final Ten Days

## Trivia

* Although their names are similar, El and [[Elodi]] are not the same person.
