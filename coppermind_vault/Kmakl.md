**Kmakl** is Queen [[Fen]]'s husband and the prince consort of [[Thaylenah]] on [[Roshar]].{{book ref|sa3|58}}

## Appearance and Personality
{{quote
|By Kelek, storms, and Passions alike, what is that?
|Kmakl, seeing the red mist of [[Nergaoul]]{{book ref|sa3|118}}
}}
He is aging, and has drooping mustaches that match his long Thaylen eyebrows.{{book ref|sa3|58}} He wears a vest and a cap.{{book ref|sa3|58}} He is described as "a navy man",{{book ref|sa4|3}} and holds the rank of Admiral in the Thaylen navy.{{book ref|sa4|3}} He is not a [[Shardbearer]] and does not carry a sword; Dalinar notes that this would be unusual for someone of Kmakl's rank in [[Alethkar]].{{book ref|sa3|59}}

He is among the most skilled naval tacticians in the [[coalition of monarchs]].{{book ref|sa4|50}} He is intelligent, optimistic, and courageous, and seems to be physically fit given his age.{{book ref|sa3|115}}{{book ref|sa3|118}} He honors tradition and established policies, but he is willing to embrace innovation.{{book ref|sa4|3}}{{book ref|sa4|19}} He is an amiable man, although he jokingly refers to himself as a "grouchy old sailor".{{book ref|sa4|3}}

Like many Thaylens, Kmakl seems to have an affinity for both [[Vorin]]ism and the [[Passions]].{{book ref|sa3|118}}

## History
Kmakl was present when [[Dalinar Kholin]] arrived in [[Thaylen City]] to assess the damage from the [[Everstorm]].{{book ref|sa3|59}} He was attended by several scribes and instructed them to write down important bits of Fen's conversation with Dalinar for the official record.{{book ref|sa3|59}}

He and Fen attended a meeting of Dalinar's coalition of monarchs in [[Urithiru]], and Kmakl seemed satisfied with [[Navani]]'s suggestion that the Thaylens oversee trade and supplies.{{book ref|sa3|96}}

### Battle of Thaylen Field, 1174
As the [[Battle of Thaylen Field]] unfolded, Kmakl, Fen, and Navani Kholin surveyed the battlefield from the city walls.{{book ref|sa3|115}} Kmakl believed that the coalition might successfully defend the city until a [[thunderclast]] appeared nearby, shaking the ground and severely damaging the wall.{{book ref|sa3|115}} Kmakl suffered a cut to his scalp, but he and Fen were able to escape down a staircase to a guard chamber.{{book ref|sa3|116}} However, they were seized by soldiers from [[Sadeas princedom|House Sadeas]] under the influence of the [[Thrill]].{{book ref|sa3|117}} They were bound and gagged{{book ref|sa3|117}} and the soldiers moved to kill Kmakl, since [[Amaram]] would only care about the queens.{{book ref|sa3|118}} Kmakl tried to face his death with dignity, but the soldiers continued to manhandle him until Navani was able to incapacitate them with her [[painrial]].{{book ref|sa3|118}}

Kmakl and Fen subsequently witnessed [[Nergaoul]]'s appearance on the battlefield,{{book ref|sa3|118}} Dalinar summoning [[Honor's Perpendicularity]],{{book ref|sa3|119}} Jasnah [[Soulcast]]ing a new section of one of the city's walls,{{book ref|sa3|119}} and their son [[Kdralk]] attempting to recover a [[Shardblade]].{{book ref|sa3|119}} Kmakl thought the new wall would hold off [[Odium]]'s forces until he saw the sheer number of [[singer]]s that were prepared to attack, driven by the Thrill.{{book ref|sa3|119}}

### True Desolation
Although [[Odium]]'s forces stole a number of ships from Thaylenah, the country retained a fleet large enough to win a decisive naval victory in late {{Rosharan date|1174}}, giving them control of most of Roshar's southern seas.{{book ref|sa4|17}}
{{sidequote|side=right|size=370px
|I can't decide if I’m glad to be old enough to wish my world a fond farewell, or if I envy the young lads who get to explore this new world.
|Kmakl reflecting on [[fabrial]] technology potentially making ships obsolete{{book ref|sa4|3}}
}}
In early {{Rosharan date|1175}}, Navani invited Kmakl aboard the *[[Fourth Bridge]]* for its mission to evacuate [[Hearthstone]].{{book ref|sa4|3}} He was fascinated by the airship and considered it to be fast given its size.{{book ref|sa4|19}} However, he felt sadness regarding its existence because he believed that it was a sign that naval forces would become obsolete.{{book ref|sa4|3}} By this time, Kmakl was part of Dalinar's military leadership of the coalition of monarchs, and he believed a large fleet of the airships would be an incredibly powerful weapon for both direct assaults and troop support if they could be protected from enemy [[Surgebinder]]s.{{book ref|sa4|3}}{{book ref|sa4|6}}{{book ref|sa4|19}}

Kmakl joined the coalition's forces in [[Emul]] during the planning for their military offensive. Two days after King [[Taravangian]] betrayed the coalition, Kmakl informed them during a military meeting that the Thaylen navy had locked the Veden ships into their ports. He believed the blockade would be effective unless the enemy committed a large number of [[Fused]] or [[Skybreakers]] to try and break through.{{book ref|sa4|50}}

## Relationships
### Queen Fen
Fen remarks that Kmakl was particularly smitten during their first days courting.{{book ref|sa4|19}} He is often by the queen's side and sometimes acts as an informal advisor,{{book ref|sa3|96}} but he does not interfere with her duties as monarch, ensuring that she has final say on all decisions.{{book ref|sa3|59}}{{book ref|sa4|50}}

### Dalinar and Navani Kholin
Both Navani and Dalinar are fond of Kmakl.{{book ref|sa4|3}}{{book ref|sa4|19}} Dalinar sometimes gets annoyed by the fact that bureaucracy slows Kmakl and Fen's decision-making.{{book ref|sa4|50}}
