**Chrysalis** is a [[Sleepless|Dysian Aimian]] and member of the crew of the [[Dynamic]]. She works as the ship's doctor.{{book ref|iote|23}}

## Appearance & Personality
Chrysalis is one of the Sleepless, also called Clemaxin's Hordes. This means that she is composed entirely of [[Sleepless#Hordelings|hordelings]], in the general shape of a human body. Some of these hordelings have the appearance of human skin or facial features. When she is in a human shape, she has dark tan skin with tattoos, which partially cover the seams between the various hordelings. She looks like a thick-bodied woman with a lean face and blonde hair. Though her hordelings can be of either sex, she presents female.{{book ref|iote|23}} She is around six thousand years old.{{book ref|iote|46}}

She can be abrasive and unfriendly, and is typically cold and stiff in conversation. She is very aware that the other members of the crew are wary of the Sleepless, and resents this. Self-preservation is her top priority, and she doesn't trust easily. Because of this, she resists [[Starling]]'s efforts to integrate her with the rest of the crew. She avoids making friends because of her difficult past, but slowly begins to find a home with the crew of the *Dynamic*. She longs for acceptance, but the Sleepless are generally unpopular.{{book ref|iote|60}}{{book ref|iote|epilogue}}

Chrysalis has sworn the Oath of the First, meaning that she will never do harm to another living being.{{book ref|iote|35}}

## History
### Scadrial
Two years before the discovery of the [[perpendicularity]] on [[First of the Sun]], Chrysalis was on [[Scadrial]], working for the [[Malwish]] prime minister. He had hired her as a spy, and when he was assassinated by partisans, some of Chrysalis' hordelings were also killed. Because of the hordelings left behind at the scene, the Scadrians assumed a Sleepless must have killed the prime minister, forcing Chrysalis to go on the run.

### The Dynamic
Hoid found Chrysalis and gave her a position as the ship's doctor on the *Dynamic*. The rest of the crew were highly apprehensive of her, with the exception of Starling. Chrysalis was very knowledgeable about human biology, and so was a good fit in the role.{{book ref|iote|35}} 

Chrysalis was not involved when the crew mutinied against Captain [[Crow]], and expressed resentment that her opinion was not considered. When the crew celebrated their newfound freedom, Chrysalis refused to join in and left the room.{{book ref|iote|28}} Weeks later, upon arriving at the [[Dakwara's island]] and being captured by the Malwish, Chrysalis was forced to hide in an [[aluminum]] box. Starling confronted Chrysalis, asking if she was the horde that assassinated the Malwish prime minister. Chrysalis did not answer.{{book ref|iote|35}} 

When the crew were trying to plan an escape, Chrysalis sent one of her hordelings to impersonate their voices and prevent their captors from hearing the true discussion. The crew were disturbed by this, but accepted her help.{{book ref|iote|42}} Chrysalis sent another hordeling to [[Dusk]] to seek his help in locating the perpendicularity. She let him see through her eyes and experience what it is to be a horde, and explained the nature of the Sleepless. She offered to help the [[Eelakin]] in their negotiations with the Scadrians, and Dusk sent her away so that he could consider.{{book ref|iote|46}}

The next time they spoke, Dusk told Chrysalis his worries about the Cave of Death. Chrysalis figured out that Dusk was able to hide some of his thoughts from her, and was impressed.{{book ref|iote|50}} Shortly afterwards, the Malwish discovered some of Chrysalis' hordelings and raised the alarm.{{book ref|iote|54}} She spoke to Dusk and let him watch as the Malwish set up an interference cloud to prevent her hordelings from communicating with one another.{{book ref|iote|55}} The Malwish then knocked out most of her hordelings with a gas, and shovelled her into a container.{{book ref|iote|56}}

When Dusk challenged the [[Dakwara]] and the crew of the *Dynamic* escaped, the Malwish planned to execute Chrysalis by superheating her hordelings. With the interference cloud gone, Chrysalis was able to contact Dusk in a desperate final bid. Before Dusk was able to reach her, Starling arrived. Starling destroyed the control panel and freed Chrysalis, who was surprised that Starling would even care to do so. Many of her hordelings were destroyed, but enough remained for her to survive. She thanked the crew for saving her, growing emotional.{{book ref|iote|60}} 

Over the course of the next six months, Chrysalis slowly regrew her horde, and began spending more time with the crew. She suggested to Starling that the other Sleepless could help free her from her manacles.{{book ref|iote|epilogue}}

## Relationships
### Starling
{{quote
|Why...care?
|Chrysalis to Starling after Starling saves her{{book ref|iote|60}}
}}
Starling made a concerted effort to befriend Chrysalis, but Chrysalis resisted this at first. She was rarely outright hostile towards Starling, but often tried to avoid her.{{book ref|iote|23}} In an emergency, Chrysalis did help out Starling and the crew, but prioritized preserving herself. Chrysalis believed that Starling would abandon her if necessary, and was shocked when Starling chose to save her life.{{book ref|iote|60}} She then began to trust Starling and the others a little more, though she is still somewhat reserved.{{book ref|iote|epilogue}}

### ZeetZi
[[ZeetZi]] and Chrysalis have a very tense relationship. ZeetZi's people, the [[lawnark]]s of the [[Grand Apparatus]], were enslaved by the Sleepless. Because of this, ZeetZi detested Chrysalis and advocated to Starling that she be removed from the ship. Chrysalis used her hordelings to spy on ZeetZi and the others, expecting them to try to kill her.{{book ref|iote|22}}{{book ref|iote|23}} When Chrysalis was about to be killed by the Malwish, ZeetZi insisted on going to save her.{{book ref|iote|60}} Six months after this, he is still wary of her, but accepts her as a member of the crew.{{book ref|iote|epilogue}}

### Dusk
Chrysalis appears to trust Dusk more than the crew of the *Dynamic*, because he isn't aware of the stigma surrounding her species. Dusk views her as a god, but does not worship her.{{book ref|iote|46}} Since Dusk, as a [[trapper]], is accustomed to insects, Chrysalis assumed that he would be more accepting of her physical appearance. However, Dusk found the hordelings nauseating. This upset Chrysalis, as she just wants to be viewed and accepted as a person. Dusk was enraged that the Malwish would try to destroy Chrysalis, and cried when he thought he was too late to save her.{{book ref|iote|60}}
