**Moss-son-Farrier** is the [[Order_of_Lightweavers|Lightweaver]] [[Honorbearer]] in [[Shinovar]]. He dies in 1175, when [[Szeth]] kills him and the other Honorbearers with [[Nightblood]].{{book ref|sa5|141}}

## Appearance and Personality
Moss's physical appearance is not specifically described, but he is a younger [[Shin]] man, like Szeth.{{book ref|sa5|83}}

He is Szeth's closest friend among the [[Honorbearer]]s, treating him like a colleague from the beginning. Though he is prone to boasting and showing off, he is still friendly and caring.{{book ref|sa5|83}} After his corruption by [[Ishar]], he is more brutal and mocking.{{book ref|sa5|70}}{{book ref|sa5|71}}

## Attributes and Abilities
As the [[Lightweaver]] [[Honorbearer]], he has access to the [[Surgebinding|Surges]] of [[Illumination]] and [[Transformation]]. This allows him to create illusions and transmute objects. Like all Honorbearers, he is trained in the use of all ten Surges, though Illumination and Transformation are his specialty. It is possible, as an advanced Lightweaver, that he has learned [[substantiation]].{{cite}}

[[Ishar]], in his corruption of the [[Honorbearer]]s, made him and his colleagues into human [[Fused]], which allows him to be reborn after being killed through most means. Similar to the Fused, this requires the body of a living human, though, unlike the Fused, the process is very painful and takes several days.{{book ref|sa5|128}} Moss and the other Honorbearers are abnormally strong with their Surges,{{book ref|sa5|41}}{{book ref|sa5|131}} and can access them without using any Stormlight.{{book ref|sa5|42}} This may be a power granted to them by their [[Connection]] to Ishar, similar to how the [[Herald]]s received power directly from [[Honor]].
Moss is clever, something Szeth considers a staple of Lightweavers. He devises a multi-layered riddle to challenge Szeth in his monastery, one designed such that almost no matter what Szeth does, Moss will win. Only with [[Syl]]'s help did Szeth overcome the riddle.{{book ref|sa5|71}}

## History
Moss went on his pilgrimages sometime during the 1150’s and became an [[Honorbearer]] about four years before [[Szeth]] did, in approximately 1161. Like most of the other Honorbearers of the time, he was groomed for his role by [[Pozen-son-Nash|Pozen]], and by an extension [[Ishar]].{{book ref|sa5|83}} He and Szeth became close friends, with the year that Szeth spent at his monastery being one that Szeth treasured.{{cite}}

Over time, he was corrupted fully by Ishar. He ruled over his monastery and the surrounding region, enforcing Ishar’s control, until Palahanah 1175, when [[Kaladin]] and Szeth came to [[Shinovar]] to complete Szeth’s [[Knights_Radiant#Immortal_Words|fourth ideal]]. On the sixth of the final ten days, after visiting [[Rit]] in the [[Stoneward monastery]] and [[Sivi-daughter-Sivi|Sivi]] in the [[Willshaper monastery]], they came to the [[Lightweaver monastery]] to confront Moss. He presented them with a riddle: thirty copies of Shalash, standing still in the center of the monastery. Each of them was imperfect in some way, he said, but he was hidden behind the illusion that was perfect. Twenty-nine of those standing were safe, while one was dangerous.{{book ref|sa5|70}}

Szeth searched all of the illusions thoroughly, unable to decide which one would be Moss. Only once [[Syl]] came up to him and helped him think through it did he realize that Moss would have intentionally built the puzzle to be essentially unwinnable. After a moment of thought, he realized that Moss was hiding underneath the floor, which had a large depiction of Shalash’s face. Acting on impulse, he stabbed through the floor with [[12124]], killing Moss.{{book ref|sa5|71}} Moss’s final trick was standing among the illusions; the [[Truthwatcher]] [[Honorbearer]] was one of the people disguised as Shalash and attempted to attack Szeth after Moss's death. Kaladin, with a warning from Syl, was able to save Szeth’s life.{{book ref|sa5|72}}

Over the next few days, as Szeth and Kaladin continued Szeth's quest, he was reborn, taking an unknown Shin person’s body. By the tenth day, he was reborn and stood ready with Ishar and the other Honorbearers at the [[Bondsmith monastery]]. At Ishar’s command, he and the others attacked Szeth, nearly killing him. As Szeth was lying on the ground, injured, out of [[Stormlight]], he finally chose to accept agency, swearing his [[Szeth#The_Fifth_Ideal|fifth ideal]].{{book ref|sa5|133}} He promptly broke his oaths with [[12124]], before using [[Nightblood]] to kill the Honorbearers.{{book ref|sa5|136}} Because Nightblood consumes all aspect of things he attacks, when he killed the Honorbearers, they died permanently.{{book ref|sa5|132}} Moss died second, after [[Pozen-son-Nash|Pozen]].{{book ref|sa5|141}}
