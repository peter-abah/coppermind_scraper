The ***Dynamic Storyteller's Incredible Conveyance*** is a cargo ship currently owned and captained by the [[Yolish]] [[dragon (cosmere)|dragon]] [[Illistandrista]]. Previously owned by [[Hoid]] and after his disappearance inherited by the dragon [[Xisis]] based out of [[Silverlight]], Starling was given ownership of the craft after confirming the existence and coordinates of [[Drominad]]'s secret [[perpendicularity]], locally known as [[Patji's Eye]].

## Layout and Construction
The *Dynamic Storyteller's Incredible Conveyance* makes use of many different technological strains; it has a [[Scadrial|Scadrian]] composite metal hull, [[Roshar]]an anti-grav technology and [[Dhatri]]an [[Aether]]s to provide thrust. The ship has three small decks. The lowest one contains the cargo hold, the crew's living space; each crewmember has their own small room, and a combined kitchen and dining area, which is the ship's largest gathering space. The crew often play cards here. The middle deck contains the engine room and the ship's medical bay. The engine room
of the *Dynamic* is very cramped, even more so than the ship's hallways. The round room has a high ceiling, and is clogged with machinery. Large bundles of tubes run up and down the walls, tied off every few feet or so to keep them together. These tubes look like cords, only a little thicker, and sometimes pulsing with water or aether. They are a staple of the ship, and of its class. To the back of the room, a hammock hangs from a rivet on the wall and a stack of large barrels. The *Dynamic's* machinery needs constant attention; it uses older spores, which tend to be drowsy, requiring someone with the proper training to wake them up. The medical bay is the place where the ship's doctor works and keeps all of their resources. There is a large metal tube, with a ladder inside, near the stern. This ladder is used to climb to the highest deck, the bridge.

The ship's interior is generally metallic, with flickering fluorescent lights, pipes for decor and barely enough space to walk upright; it takes a lot of energy to keep a ship like the *Dynamic* flying, and designers learned to be economical. The ship also has several portholes. Like the rest of the ship, the bridge of the *Dynamic* is cramped. The front wall is divided: the left half is a thick viewing window while the right half is a bulkhead with a Scadrian electronic control system. The bulkhead has one small viewscreen. The *Dynamic's* bridge has five seats, a row of two at the front for using the equipment, then a cramped row of three seats behind it. Those rows take up almost all of the floor space. The ceiling is covered in a network of tubes, some of them colorful, fanning out from the controls and running to other systems in the ship. The pilot’s seat is in front of the window and the technician's seat is by the viewscreen and the many sensor controls. Generally, two of the seats in the back row are reserved for the captain and first officer. The ceiling slants forward above the piloting and operations stations, and the entrance to the bridge has a small blast door.

The bridge has a built-in Scadrian control panel. This panel is notable for displaying little lines of electricity, like tiny lightning bolts, connecting the user's fingers to the panel. The controls are written in the [[Steel alphabet]]. While the *Dynamic* doesn't have weapons, it does have excellent sensors. The ship's engines are not built for combat maneuvers, and the crew might have to engage the emergency antigrav stabilizers, which work without engines, to stabilize the ship while going through difficult maneuvers. The *Dynamic* is also in possession of [[Investiture|Invested]] signal flares. Upon docking at a settlement, the crew usually draws lots for leave. The ship also has a gangway somewhere in the middle. Since Illistandrista became captain, the crew all became partners. It also contains an [[aluminum]] smuggler’s box, which is undetectable to even modern scans. It is hidden deep within the ship’s machinery. As a cargo ship, the *Dynamic* sometimes travels with a large amount of unkeyed Investiture, which is housed in aluminum crates. When on a less legal trip however, the ship contains a lot of mail, usually the same four pieces over and over again. Besides the mail, they also have a second, smaller fake cargo. Usually hidden, so that they can pretend to be smugglers. This fake cargo can't be too valuable, so that the fine if they're caught is low.

## History
Little is known about the *Dynamic's* early flights, but Hoid is known to have gathered the crew himself, picking [[Leonore]] for her ability to fly a ship and [[ZeetZi]] for his mastery of technology. Furthermore, he took Starling, who was his apprentice, with him and installed her as lieutenant. Hoid also took on [[Chrysalis]], telling the crew that he trusted her. They flew together for a while, but at some point Hoid left, and [[Xisis]] inherited the ship along with its debts. Xisis immediately installed [[Crow]] as the captain and ordered her to make him a profit.

Sixth months after Hoid's dissapearance, Crow had the ship follow a distress signal into the [[Cognitive Realm#Emberdark|Emberdark]]. The signal led to an [[Iriali]] family, who were being hunted by a [[Evil|type 1-6 Threnodite entity]]. Upon discovering that Crow meant to let the family die and loot their ship, the crew mutinied and took control of the *Dynamic*, and, due to the skilled piloting of [[Leonore]], the Iriali were rescued. After returning to [[Silverlight]], captain Crow immediately reported Starling's mutiny to Xisis. Starling later met with him to negotiate the future of her crew. Xisis wanted to split the crew up and send Starling away, she managed to negotiate a deal with him however: they would fly to the location of where they believed a perpendicularity to be and confirm its existence to him.

The crew followed their map to an [[Dakwara's island|island]] in the [[Drominad]] subastral. A group of [[Malwish]] [[Scadrial|Scadrians]] had beaten them there, and sent an inspection team to the *Dynamic*. The leader of the Scadrians, [[Dajer]], took them prisoner, but the crew managed to escape. Leonore powered on the *Dynamic*, while Nazh scared off Dajer and sabotaged the Malwish' fighters. They took to the air, but Dajer aimed a large cannon at them to destroy the Dynamic. Dajer was killed by the [[Dakwara]] however. Before leaving [[First of the Sun]], a man named [[Sixth of the Dusk]] joined the crew, functioning as [[Navigator]].
