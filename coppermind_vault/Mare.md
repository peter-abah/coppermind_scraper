**Mare** is a [[tin]] [[Misting]] (aka a [[Tineye]]){{book ref|mb1|5}} on [[Scadrial]]. She is [[Kelsier]]'s wife and a member of his crew. She is fascinated by pre-Ascension times, following the pre-Ascension religion of [[Larstaism]]{{book ref|mb3|10}} and collecting artifacts from that era, including a picture of a flower. She is also active in the skaa rebellion. She and Kelsier are captured by the [[Lord Ruler]] when they try to infiltrate [[Kredik Shaw]], and sent to the [[Pits of Hathsin]]. She is later killed there. After her death, Kelsier dedicates his life toward making a world where flowers grow again, the world she had always dreamed of.

## Appearance and Personality
{{image|Mare by Shuravf.jpg|side=left|width=250px}}
{{for|/Gallery|more images}}
Mare has dark hair and a stubborn personality.{{book ref|mb1|17}} Kelsier describes her as having had 'a hidden streak of unconventionality'.{{book ref|mb1|29}}

## Attributes and Abilities
### Allomancy
Mare is a [[Tineye]], meaning she can burn tin to enhance her senses.

### Thieving
Mare is a competent thief, working with Kelsier and Dockson as well as other thieves.{{book ref|mb1|5}} She is also very skilled at impersonating a noblewoman.{{book ref|mb1|29}}

## History
Mare was drawn to the Skaa underground because of both her status as a skaa [[Misting]] and her interest in pre-Ascension times. This was how she met Kelsier.{{book ref|mb1|17}} They got married, although [[Marsh]] had also been in love with her.{{book ref|mb1|20}} Mare was also friends with [[Sazed]], and introduced him to her husband.{{book ref|mb1|17}}

{{image|Mare by Egilde Art.jpg|side=right|width=250px|With her flower}}

She wanted children, particularly a daughter whom she could teach to ‘walk the line between noblewoman and thief’.{{book ref|mb1|29}} However, Kelsier thought they would be a hindrance and so they had none — something he later regretted.{{book ref|mb1|17}}

She expressed disappointment when Kelsier's vigilante actions against nobles would throw their life into upheaval.{{msh ref|4|2}}

When Kelsier heard that the Lord Ruler was hiding something valuable in his secret room,{{book ref|mb1|24}} Mare and a [[Smoker]] named [[Redd]] helped him in his attempt to break into the room.{{book ref|mb1|31}} However, they arrived to find [[Steel Inquisitors]] outside the secret chamber, waiting for them.{{book ref|mb1|14}} They killed Redd immediately.{{book ref|mb1|31}} The Lord Ruler then thanked Mare for betraying Kelsier to him, before the two were separated.{{book ref|mb1|17}}

She arrived in the Pits of Hathsin a few weeks after Kelsier. Though he still loved her, Kelsier questioned her loyalty due to the Lord Ruler's words.{{book ref|mb1|17}} Despite Mare's claims of innocence, Kelsier believed the lie that Mare had betrayed him, although he was never completely certain about what had happened.{{book ref|mb1|17}} She died eighteen months later, having sacrificed herself for Kelsier. He had been unable to find an [[atium]] geode, without which he would have been killed by the taskmasters. Mare convinced him to take hers, lying that she had found two that week. When it was revealed that she had none, the taskmasters beat her to death. This event is what caused Kelsier to [[Snap]].{{book ref|mb1|21}}

[[Preservation]] informed Kelsier that Mare went to the [[Beyond]] after her death.{{msh ref|1|1}} Kelsier later sought to confirm this, but received no answer from the [[Shard]].{{msh ref|4|2}} However, Mare was not a [[kandra]],{{wob ref|12573}} nor does she exist as a [[Cognitive Shadow]].{{wob ref|3956}}

## Legacy
{{image|Mare Eyeronis.png|side=left|width=170px}}
Years later, [[Vin]] realized that the Lord Ruler and his Inquisitors were able to pierce [[copper]]clouds. She concluded that the Inquisitors had sensed Mare's use of Allomancy on the night of the break-in, allowing them to apprehend Kelsier's small team.{{book ref|mb1|31}} This meant that it was possible that Mare hadn't betrayed Kelsier after all, although Kelsier struggled to process this new information.{{book ref|mb1|32}}

[[Marewill flowers]] that grow in post-[[Catacendre]] Scadrial were named after Mare by [[Spook]].{{wob ref|697}}
