**Izumakamo**, better known to her friends as **Izzy**, is an eccentric [[nightmare]] [[painter]] in [[Kilahito]] on [[Komashi]]. She has a unique fascination in [[hion viewer]] programs, having developed the idea of [[dramascope]]s, and is interested in becoming a viewer critic.{{book ref|yumi|16}}{{book ref|yumi|33}}

==Appearance and Personality==

Approximately twenty years old, Izzy is a woman with long limbs and bleached yellow hair.{{book ref|yumi|33}} She is seen wearing trousers and a sweatshirt,{{book ref|yumi|21}} and occasionally wears white in contrast with [[Masaka]]'s black.{{book ref|yumi|26}}

Izzy has a whimsical personality that often leaves her friends surprised and vexed, unsure of how serious she is being.{{book ref|yumi|16}} She sometimes changes the topic of conversation abruptly and forcefully.{{book ref|yumi|16}}{{book ref|yumi|21}}{{book ref|yumi|26}} Izzy is bisexual, and claims to be comfortable with the idea of dating an alien.{{book ref|yumi|21}} She pays very close attention to current events.{{book ref|yumi|21}}

==History==

At some point in time prior to her professional career, Izzy developed the idea of dramascopes--a form of divinatory practice which involves hion viewer programs alongside more conventional methods. She defended the practice adamantly despite her friends' doubts, hoping that it would one day make her rich and famous.{{book ref|yumi|16}}

In upper school, studying to become a nightmare painter, Izzy became friends with [[Akane]], [[Tojin]], Masaka, and [[Nikaro]]. She, with the rest of these friends, came to believe that Nikaro would join the [[Dreamwatch]] and that she would be one of his companions. Izzy became deeply distrustful of Nikaro after they discovered he was lying. After graduating, she became a painter and primarily worked as partners with Masaka.{{book ref|yumi|26}}

Akane introduced [[Yumi]] to Izzy at the [[Noodle Pupil]] during the yoki-hijo's first days in Kilahito, and after shaking her hand Izzy shared Yumi's dramascope.{{book ref|yumi|16}}

Later, when Akane reported Yumi had been spending too much time studying, Izzy joined her friends in encouraging Yumi to take a break and join them for dinner. At dinner, she noticed Yumi staring at a shirtless Tojin and teased him to put his clothes back on for Yumi's sake. When the topic of Painter came up, Izzy had little interest in talking about him and changed the subject to their spaceship, which was soon scheduled to launch.{{book ref|yumi|21}}

Izzy was quick to take off from work when Yumi was caught hunting a stable nightmare, and more than anyone else was intent on the yoki-hijo understanding Nikaro's history of lying. When Yumi supposed he might be telling the truth, Izzy was the first to point out that he can't have seen a stable nightmare because it would have attacked the city already.{{book ref|yumi|26}}{{book ref|yumi|27}}

Before Yumi returned to [[Torio]] for a final time, Izzy informed her about the surprise ending of *[[Seasons of Regret]]*. The plot twist in this episode had a profound effect on Izzy, and inspired within her a desire to become a viewer critic and to write a book about the benefits of viewer programs for mental health.{{book ref|yumi|33}}

When Nikaro eventually returned and asked his friends for help in the coming attack, Izzy was incredulous when Tojin decided to assist Nikaro.{{book ref|yumi|39}} At one point during the [[Battle of Kilahito]] Izzy froze up during an attack, and Nikaro pushed her aside and saved her with a painting of a bird.{{book ref|yumi|40}}

==Relationships==

===Nikaro===

Izzy is particularly critical of Nikaro for the way he lied to them about joining the Dreamwatch. Prior to the [[Battle of Kilahito]], Izzy was often very outspoken with her negative opinions. While others in the group tend to discuss Nikaro with solemnity, Izzy frequently offers derisive laughter and dismisses everything he says as untrustworthy.{{book ref|yumi|21}} When Yumi is later caught hunting the stable nightmare, her friends try to handle the topic delicately while Izzy is adamant that Yumi understand her brother has a history of lying.{{book ref|yumi|26}}{{book ref|yumi|33}}
