The '''Expulsion''' is an ancient event in pre-[[Roshar]]an history. 

==Vorin Doctrine==

In [[Vorin]] mythology, it marks the defeat of the population of the [[Tranquiline Halls]] and their exile to [[Roshar]] by the [[Voidbringer]]s.{{file ref|The History of Man.jpg|The History of Man}} Following the Expulsion, the Voidbringers followed mankind to Roshar and the [[Desolation]]s began. It is unclear why the humans lost the war in the Tranquiline Halls or how they traveled from there to Roshar.

==Historicity==
{{quote | They came from another world, using powers that we have been forbidden to touch. Dangerous powers, of spren and Surges. They destroyed their lands and have come to us begging. |From the [[Eila Stele]]{{epigraph ref|sa3|115}}}}
{{image|Arribal by Kevin Sidharta.png|side=right|width=350px|Arrival in Roshar}}
The humans who first settled on Roshar arrived from [[Ashyn]]--another inner planet in the [[Rosharan system]].{{wob ref|9464}} While the exact reason for their migration is unknown, [[Honor]] implied that they needed a new home after destroying their previous planet with the [[Dawnshard]]s.{{book ref|sa3|113}} Firestorms had raged across the surface of Ashyn and the very stones melted in pools of lava, due to altercation between an empire made by Odium's favoured people with access to Surges and an opposing Kingdom set up by people who had been granted the power to resist the imperialist nation. According to Honor, the sky burned. The [[Eila Stele]], an ancient [[singer]] text, claimed that the humans were welcomed to Roshar by their gods despite access to "dangerous powers, of [[spren]] and [[Surge]]s." The stele further claimed that the humans had betrayed them, as subjects of their own god--[[Odium]], and ultimately implied that the humans themselves were the [[Voidbringers]], come to conquer Roshar.{{book ref|sa3|111}} The myth of the Expulsion seems to be based on these events.

Ishar, who had some access to Elsecaller-like abilities opened a portal to Roshar following a tone.

Rats could make the transition, but dogs, due to their sparse numbers, died out after a few generations.
