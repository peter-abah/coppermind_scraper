'''Alethela''' is one of the [[Silver Kingdoms]] on [[Roshar]].{{map ref|Silver Kingdoms}} As one of the Silver Kingdoms, its name is perfectly symmetrical, due to the [[Vorin]] belief in holiness of symmetry.{{book ref|sa1|33}} It is generally considered to be the predecessor of [[Alethkar]]. Alethela's borders included parts of the present-day countries of [[Herdaz]], [[Jah Keved]], [[Alethkar]] and the [[Unclaimed Hills]].

== Geography ==
=== Territories and Features ===
==== Territories ====
Alethela lies in eastern [[Roshar]], stretching across the entire continent, from the [[Steamwater Ocean]] in the North to the [[Tarat Sea]] in the South-West. To the East Alethela touches on the [[Ocean of Origins]] while to the South and South-east, it shares a border with the Silver kingdom of [[Natanatan]]. Additionally, Alethela’s south sees a short border with [[Thalath]]. To the West, Alethela has a long border with [[Valhav]] and to the North-west, Alethela borders [[Rishir]].{{map ref|Silver Kingdoms}}

Besides the mainland, the Alethelan people govern a number of territories off the coast. Almost all of these are located on the [[Steamwater Ocean]], where a small island chain is under Alethelan control. As Alethela controls the northern half of [[Mevan Bay]], they might also control several of the islands in the aforementioned bay.{{map ref|Silver Kingdoms}}

==== Geographic Features ====
Alethela has numerous rivers crossing it, the largest one being the [[Windrunner River|Windrunner]]. The Windrunner River runs East to West, to the Bay of Elibath, which is located in Rishir, and has numerous tributaries across the northern half of the country. Additionally, Alethela also has another large river, distinct from the Windrunner, ending in Rishir and one that goes south to the Tarat Sea. The West of Alethela contains a long, cross-shaped masiff later called the [[Sunmaker Mountains]]. The Eastern part of the country also sees a large and hilly area of land. Alethela also controls the northern half of a large lake called the [[Sea of Spears]].{{map ref|Roshar}}{{map ref|Silver Kingdoms}}

=== Climate ===
Due to how far east it is, Alethela is buffeted by particularly heavy highstorms. This, like in its successor state Alethkar, forces settlements to primarily be built on the leeward sides of laits.{{book ref|sa1|16}} These leeward sides are also far more lush with plant life, due to the protection the laits provide from powerful highstorm winds.{{file ref|Lait.jpeg|Lait study}} 

Like the rest of Roshar, Alethela does not experience true seasons, as they are unrelated to irradiance.{{wob ref|11448}} The east of the country experiences a moister climate than the west.{{book ref|sa1|23}} Clouds are also less frequent in this region.{{book ref|sa3|58}}

=== Ecology ===
==== Flora ====
Like with many countries, the flora one finds depends heavily on the region of Alethela. In the northwest, common flora includes thickets of [[snarlbrush]], stout [[markel]] trees and [[stumpweight]] trees.{{book ref|sa1|31}} Some forests are made up of purely [[vivim]] trees, which can bind their branches together to ensure that the forest is almost impenetrable for a man.{{book ref|sa3|10}} Central Alethela is filled with rockbuds, and here the roots of trees sprout from the ground.{{book ref|sa3|48}} Southwest Alethela is also home to sturdy trees as well as many varieties of vines, although there are less vines than in Valhav.{{book ref|sa3|52}} In some areas of Alethela, rockbuds can grow nearly to the size of a barrel, with vines as thick as a human wrist;{{book ref|sa3|5}}

==== Fauna ====
Alethela also has a large variety of fauna. Large wild Fauna includes [[Whitespine]]s,{{book ref|sa1|44}} [[Chull]]s,{{book ref|sa1|45}} [[axehound]]s{{book ref|sa1|45}} as well as many more. Another common species found across Alethela is the [[lurg]], a small amphibian.{{book ref|sa1|16}} Alethela isn’t home to any [[sarpenthyn]], but [[skyeel]]s are common near at least some of it’s coasts.{{book ref|sa2|55}}{{book ref|sa2|40}}

== History ==
=== Creation of Roshar ===
The land that would become known as Alethela was created at an unknown point before the [[Shattering]] by Adonalsium, along with the rest of Roshar and the Rosharan supercontinent over ten-thousand years prior to Vorin year {{Rosharan date|1174}}.{{wob ref|3953}}{{wob ref|3952}}{{wob ref|1355}}{{book ref|tsm|11}} It is unclear how much the geography of Alethela has changed compared to the land at creation.

=== Pre-Human Arrival ===
Before humanity arrived on Roshar from Ashyn, the land that would come to be known as Alethela was inhabited by [[Singers]].{{book ref|sa3|113}} 

Circa -5825 in the Vorin calendar,{{book ref|row|111}}{{book ref|row|14}} humanity arrived on Roshar after fleeing Ashyn and were granted the land that would become [[Shin Kak Nish]]. After less than a generation,{{wob ref|11945}} they headed an invasion, slaughtering the Singers and expelling them from their land and claiming it as their own, this included the land that would come to be known as Alethela.{{book ref|sa3|113}} This history was lost to man until the discovery of the [[Eila Stele]].{{book ref|sa3|113}}

=== The Establishment of Alethela ===
{{image|Silver Kingdoms.jpg|side=right|width=450px|Map of the Silver Kingdoms}}
[[Alethela]] was founded in the [[Silver Kingdoms]] Epoch. They were an extremely militaristic nation which remembered and studied the arts of war so that they would be able to teach it to new generations whenever a Desolation came.{{book ref|sa1|19}} Due to this, the Knight's Radiant primarily lived in Alethela, even if their operations were based in [[Urithiru]].{{book ref|sa1|19}} This militarism laid the foundation for later Alethi militarism and military strength.

Due to the Desolations, they were constantly returned back to early technological levels, often to the point to making use of stone tools. Though they advanced each time with the help of the Heralds whenever they returned, often receiving this assistance from [[Kalak]] and his [[Willshapers]].{{book ref|sa2|i|7}}{{book ref|sa1|part=prelude}}{{wob ref|1430}}{{wob ref|13779}} Old manuscripts and texts dating back to these times mention quick-spreading diseases that killed tens of thousands.{{book ref|sa4|1}}

Very early in the nation's history, the Dawncity of [[Kholinar]] was created and made into its capital.{{book ref|sa1|60}} Kholinar was eventually made into the home of the Alethelan Oathgate.{{book ref|sa3|87}} It was the place that many people wanted Urithiru to be built, likely due to Alethkar being the home to the Knight's Radiant. Despite this, for a reason obvious to many others at the time, it could not be, instead being built in the mountains near [[Makabakam]].{{epigraph ref|sa1|35}}

==== The Rule of Nohadon ====
At some point early in its history, prior to year 31 of the Sixth Epoch,{{wob ref|16437}}{{book ref|sa1|18}}{{book ref|sa1|59}} it was ruled over by King [[Nohadon]].{{book ref|sa1|60}}{{book ref|sa1|24}} During one Desolation in his life, they fought for over eleven years and nine-in-ten Alethelan citizens were killed.{{book ref|sa1|60}} 

Nohadon wanted to abdicate due to the tragic events of this Desolation, however, he kept his throne and tried to rebuild. Over the decades, he successfully managed to rebuild Alethela into a bustling and colorful place.{{book ref|sa3|103}} Eventually, he went to war with [[Makabakam]] over trade routes, the full effects this had on both kingdoms are unknown.{{book ref|sa3|103}}

==== Further Desolations ====
The Sixth Epoch lasted at least thirty-one years, and likely significantly longer, before its Desolation arrived.{{wob ref|16437}} 

One of the many Desolations that struck the nation, along with the rest of Roshar, arrived in the year {{rosharan date|337}} of the Eighth Epoch. This Desolation was heralded by the release of [[Midnight Essence]] in a small settlement in [[Natanatan]].{{book ref|sa1|19}} The length of this Desolation is unknown.

During some of the Desolations, the Fused managed to take the capital of Alethela, though were ultimately pushed out.{{book ref|sa4|65}}

Another Desolation occurred later and ended circa -3333, during an unknown Epoch. This left society wounded and weak, forcing them into a period of recovery.{{book ref|sa1|part=prelude}}

==== The Last Desolation (c.-3332) ====
The Last Desolation was a large scale Desolation which occurred circa -3332, around 4500 years before the [[Assassination of Gavilar]] Kholin. This Desolation was particularly devasting due to the fact that it happened only a year after the previous Desolation, and thus society was weakened and still recovering. Tired of the Desolations and their torture on Braize, the nine Heralds still on Roshar refused to return to Braize, leaving Taln as the sole Herald on the planet for millennia.{{book ref|sa1|prelude}}{{book ref|sa1|prologue}}

After the Last Desolation, Desolations ceased to ravage the nation. Due to this, society was able to advance much further than ever before, giving them the strength to potentially resist any potential Desolation without complete societal collapse.{{book ref|sa3|119}} The era heralded by the Last Desolation came to be known as the Era of Solitude.{{book ref|sa1|45}}

==== After Aharietiam ====
At some unknown point in history, after Aharietiam, Alethela collapsed. It is unknown if this occured before or after the [[Oldblood]] Dynasty's rule, and they might have ruled over Alethela.

==== People ====
The Alethelan people are ethnically similar to their successor people, the [[Alethi]]. They are generally tan with dark, typically black hair.{{book ref|sa1|3}} They are extremely tall, standing on average at least a foot taller than people living further West.{{book ref|sa2.5|10}} Like most Rosharan people, they also possess Epicanthic folds.{{book ref|sa2|38}} The Alethelan people in the northwest of the country have curly dark hair.{{book ref|sa2|40}} Many Alethelan people enjoy haggling.{{book ref|sa3|103}}

Although the orders of [[Knights Radiant]] were centered in Urithiru, many spent their lives in Alethela, and Radiants lived in cities throughout the nation, crossing borders to protect others.{{book ref|sa1|19}} Of these radiants, members of the [[Order of Windrunners]] were particularly prominent in Alethela.{{book ref|sa3|61}} Alethela was also the most militaristic of the Silver Kingdoms, and it’s people had the duty, during at least some of the desolations, to protect all the other nations. People could also go to Alethela if they could fight or wanted to be trained. Those who had fought the [[Ten Deaths]] could go to Alethela and be trained so that the changes wrought in them by fighting the Ten Deaths would not destroy them.{{book ref|sa1|19}}

==== Fashion ====
Alethelan men wore long skirts, tied at the waists with wide girdles, some of which came all the way over their stomachs. Above that, they wore either a simple overshirt, or went barechested. Some high ranking men also wear robes, sometimes with voluminous sleeves that completely engulf the wearer’s hands. Some men also pull their hair back into a braid, and beards are kept short, with a point end.{{book ref|sa1|60}}

Women, at least those of a high social class, wore tubular gowns. These gowns were made of small layered rings of cloth with tassels on the bottom, and they seemed to ripple as they moved. The women’s shoulders are usually kept bare. Alethelan clothing was bright and richly coloured. Some men also wore many rings on their hands.{{book ref|sa3|103}} 

==== Writing and language ====
The Alethelan people wrote in the [[Dawnchant]]. At some point in history though, the Alethelan people adapted the original Dawnchant script to fit their own language. This process began with simple phonetic substitution, in which humans used the Dawnsingers' script to transcribe their own languages.{{book ref|sa3|i|2}} By the time of the [[Recreance]], they spoke a language known as Alethelan.{{book ref|sa3|104}}

==== Architecture ====
The Alethelan people also have their own architectural styles. A lot of things are richly and brightly coloured. They also tend to use much sandstone in their buildings, and often incorporate large pillars into their designs. The Knights Radiant and Vorinism play a large role in their decorations.{{book ref|sa1|60}}
