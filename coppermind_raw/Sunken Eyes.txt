The soldier with '''sunken eyes''' is a [[Shin]] soldier on [[Roshar]].{{book ref|sa5|38}}

== Appearance and Personality ==
As his name suggests, the soldier's eyes are notably sunken. He is rowdy, due to the lax attitudes at the soldier camp at the [[Stoneward monastery]]. He feels resentful towards those who do not subtract.{{book ref|sa5|48}}{{book ref|sa5|38}}

== History ==
One day in {{rosharan date|1149}}, after defending the coast from raiders, the soldier with sunken eyes came to the homestead of [[Neturo-son-Vallano]] and [[Zeenid-daughter-Beth]] with two other soldiers. They raided the household for wine and slaughtered [[Molli]], who was grazing nearby. As they were eating Molli's meat and drinking the wine, [[Szeth]] came over and began questioning them. The soldier with sunken eyes explained that the raiders don't expect resistance, which made fighting them off easy and made it unlikely that they would return. He and Szeth got into an argument about the necessity of those who subtract. The other two soldiers left.

The soldier with sunken eyes explained to Szeth that he slaughtered Molli to remind the shepherds of the role of the soldiers; he believed it was worth the whipping he would receive as punishment. Szeth, angered by Molli's death, tried to attack the soldier, but was largely ineffective because he was a child. The soldier held back, knowing that if he killed Szeth, he would be hanged. Szeth came at him a second time, armed with a recently unearthed stone. The soldier thought that Szeth was trying to rob him, so he started choking the child. Szeth bludgeoned him death with the stone.{{book ref|sa5|38}}

The soldier's death was used as justification for marking Szeth as one who subtracts.{{book ref|sa5|48}}
