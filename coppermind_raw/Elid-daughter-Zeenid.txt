'''Elid-daughter-Zeenid''' is a [[Shin]] [[Honorbearer]] and the sister of [[Szeth]] on [[Roshar]].{{book ref|sa5|24}}{{book ref|sa5|90}}

== Appearance and Personality ==
Elid is a little taller than Szeth, but still shorter than average. As a youth, she is not as skinny as he is, but as an adult, she grows into wiry strength. She has black or dark brown hair.{{book ref|sa5|24}}{{book ref|sa5|68}}{{book ref|sa5|75}}{{book ref|sa5|90}} As a child, she wears an orange apron as her splash, over a gray dress and white undergown.{{book ref|sa5|24}} As an adult, she keeps her hair in a ponytail and possesses keen eyes, a knowing smile, and an air of authority. She wears a necklace made of polished stone.{{book ref|sa5|68}} She carries a side sword as a symbol of her station.{{book ref|sa5|75}} As an Honorbearer, she wears grey robes. On the day she fights Szeth, she wears cloth wrappings that cover even her head. She has cut her hair short, and now sports scars on her face.{{book ref|sa5|90}}

Elid is bored by pastoral life and comes to enjoy the relative excitement of life in the [[Stoneward monastery|monastery]].{{book ref|sa5|24}}{{book ref|sa5|68}} Still, she remains rather lax, in contrast to Szeth's strict adherence and high performance.{{book ref|sa5|57}} She inherited her parents' more permissive view of [[Stone Shamanism]], and does not honestly think rocks are holy. She is known to speak directly and swear casually.{{book ref|sa5|68}} She is remarkably personable.{{book ref|sa5|57}} She cares deeply about all her family members, and attempts to comfort them whenever they experience difficulties.{{book ref|sa5|68}}{{book ref|sa5|75}}{{book ref|sa5|90}}{{book ref|sa5|36}} She can and does hold grudges, including against [[Neturo]] and Szeth, though she doesn't let this prevent her from being there for them.{{book ref|sa5|75}}

== Attributes and Abilities ==
As the [[Dustbringer]] Honorbearer, she has control over the [[Surges]] of [[Division]] and [[Abrasion]].{{book ref|sa5|90}} She is made into a [[Cognitive Shadow]] by [[Ishar]], and can possess a new human body whenever she dies, much like the [[Fused]].{{book ref|sa5|128}} 

== History ==
As a youth, Elid played the flute. She had a habit of spinning her flute with her fingers, and once broke a flute in that way. She often accompanied Szeth dancing around their family's sacred stone. By the time she was fourteen, Elid was bored of practicing with the flute.{{book ref|sa5|24}}

One day in {{Rosharan date|1149}}, after Elid played the flute for Szeth to dance to, they got into a series of arguments. It was then that Szeth found a new stone, freshly emerged from the ground.{{book ref|sa5|24}} Szeth went to fetch their father Neturo, who sent a note to their mother [[Zeenid-daughter-Beth|Zeenid]], while Elid stayed behind at the family's sacred stone.{{book ref|sa5|24}}{{book ref|sa5|27}} Elid was concerned about what to do with the new stone. When Neturo explained that the discovery of the stone might result in the Shamans forcing them to move, Elid protested at the injustice. Elid participated in the discussion about what to do with the rock and was relieved when Szeth decided to go with her parents' plan of moving it. Once Zeenid removed the rock, Elid was worried about the scrapes the shovel left.{{book ref|sa5|27}}

As they were looking at the stone, the warning horns sounded. Elid helped Neturo and Szeth round up the flock to move inland, away from the coastal raiders.{{book ref|sa5|27}}{{book ref|sa5|36}} They got the sheep up towards the mountains and in a huddle. While Szeth went to cook a meal, Elid stayed with the sheep, counting them by the light of a small lamp. She found three missing, including [[Molli]]. When Szeth finished cooking and came over to check on the sheep, Elid told him about the missing sheep. Szeth went back to the homestead to find Molli.{{book ref|sa5|36}}

When Szeth was found the next morning, holding Molli's pelt and near the dead body of a [[Sunken Eyes|soldier]], Elid was sent to stay with her cousins for a few days. While she was away, her parents decided to move the entire family to the monastery, rather than let Szeth go alone.{{book ref|sa5|48}} Elid did not like being forced to move to the monastery, and blamed Szeth for ripping her old life away from her. However, she wanted to project strength as the family endured the difficult transition, and was the first to step onto the stone at the mouth of the training yard.{{book ref|sa5|51}}

While at the soldier's camp, Elid worked as a scullery maid. She kept tabs on all her family members and tuned in to their struggles. She would let Zeenid know whenever Szeth ran laps as punishment.{{book ref|sa5|57}}

When Szeth was chosen as an acolyte of the Honorbearers and moved to [[Elsecaller monastery|Pozen's monastery]], Neturo decided to come with him, while Zeenid decided to stay in hopes of being released and becoming a shepherd again. Elid hated having to choose between two pieces of her family, but chose to follow Szeth and Neturo.{{book ref|sa5|68}} While at the Elsecaller monastery, Elid would often travel.{{book ref|sa5|73}} She trained with the sword with the monastery's shamans.{{book ref|sa5|75}}

When Elid heard that Szeth was about to leave on a pilgrimage, she told the shamans she was thinking of joining so she could gain access to the monastery's rock garden, where Szeth was meditating. She spoke with Szeth, discouraging him from allowing Neturo to continue following him. She was surprised to learn that Szeth knew that their father was having an affair with [[Sivi-daughter-Sivi]]. She feared that, if Neturo continued following Szeth from monastery to monastery, it would break up Neturo and Sivi. She told Szeth she hates him.{{book ref|sa5|75}}

Elid was turned into a Fused by Ishar. Szeth killed her using [[Nightblood]].{{book ref|sa5|141}}{{expand}}

== Relationships ==
=== Neturo ===
Elid cares deeply about Neturo, as she does all the members of her family. When he was distraught that Zeenid left him, she was there to comfort him. However, she also resents him for putting Szeth's well-being above all else, including the well-being of the other family members. She refuses to talk to him about this and dislikes when he brings her gifts.{{book ref|sa5|75}}

=== Szeth ===
When Szeth was younger, Elid felt sorry for Szeth and wanted to protect him.{{book ref|sa5|57}} But when she saw the effects that being on the soldier's camp had on her mother, as well as the effect that Zeenid being so unhappy had on her father, she began to resent him.{{book ref|sa5|75}}

=== Zeenid ===
Elid cares about Zeenid and resents the fact that she had to leave her in order to follow Szeth and Neturo. She misses her.{{book ref|sa5|75}} It is possible she spends so much time travelling while at the Elsecaller monastery in part so she can visit Zeenid.{{book ref|sa5|73}} Later, with Szeth Truthless and Neturo doing fine for himself as the [[Bondsmith]] Honorbearer, she takes time to visit her mother and is able to hold her hand as she dies.{{book ref|sa5|90}}
