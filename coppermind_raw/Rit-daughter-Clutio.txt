'''Rit-daughter-Clutio''' is the [[Order_of_Stonewards|Stoneward]] [[Honorbearer]] in [[Shinovar]]. She dies in 1175, when [[Szeth]] kills her and the other Honorbearers with [[Nightblood]].

==Appearance and Personality==
Rit is a middle-aged [[Shin]] woman who wears the grey robes of a [[Stone_Shamanism|Stone Shaman]] without any splash of color. She has grey hair which might once have had some blond in it. When corrupted by [[Ishar]], she is arrogant and brusque.{{book ref|sa5|40}} Nothing is known about her personality previous to being corrupted, though she was a [[Stone Shamanism|Stone Shaman]] and obeyed the [[Honorbearers]] with exactness.{{book ref|sa5|68}}

==Attributes and Abilities==
As the [[Stoneward]] [[Honorbearer]], she has access to the [[Surgebinding|Surges]] of [[Cohesion]] and [[Tension]]. This allows her to cause stone to flow like liquid or make rope solid as steel.{{book ref|sa5|132}} She is extraordinarily skilled with these abilities, shocking [[Szeth]] when he first fights her in [[Taln]]'s monastery.{{book ref|sa5|41}} Like all Honorbearers, she is trained in the use of all ten Surges, though Cohesion and Tension are her specialty. 

[[Ishar]], in his corruption of the [[Honorbearer]]s, made her and her colleagues into human [[Fused]], which allows her to be reborn after being killed through most means. Similar to the Fused, this requires the body of a living human, though, unlike the Fused, the process is very painful and takes several days.{{book ref|sa5|128}} Rit and the other Honorbearers are abnormally strong with their Surges,{{book ref|sa5|41}} and can access them without using any Stormlight.{{book ref|sa5|42}} This may be a power granted to them by their [[Connection]] to Ishar, similar to how the [[Herald]]s received power directly from [[Honor]].

She is a good swordsperson, though not as good with the blade as Szeth is.{{book ref|sa5|41}} 

==History==
{{rpg-canon}}
Little is known of Rit's history prior to Szeth meeting her. By 1157, while [[Szeth]] lived at the [[Stoneward monastery]], she was the head Shaman there. Given that, at that time, the [[Shin]] people did not have possession of [[Taln]]'s [[Honorblade]], this made her the highest authority in the monastery. 

After Szeth attacked the raiders from [[Steen]], the General sought the “wisdom of greater minds” to decide what to do with him.{{book ref|sa5|59}} Rit was among those whose wisdom he sought, along with three [[Honorbearers]] and the [[Farmer]]. After the Voice spoke to the three Honorbearers, intervening on Szeth’s behalf, [[Pozen-son-Nash|Pozen]] commanded Rit, the General, and the Farmer to leave.{{book ref|sa5|68}}

Sometime in 1173, Shamans under [[Ishar]]'s influence sent Rit's younger brother, [[Taszo-son-Clutio]], to recover Taln's Honorblade. Though he died in the attempt, the companions he entrusted his task to eventually succeeded.{{rpg ref|slsw|33}}{{rpg ref|slsw|14}}  Rit, as the head of Taln's monastery (or Talmut, as the Shin call him), received the blade from them and became an Honorbearer.{{book ref|sa5|42}} She ruled, maintaining Ishar’s control over her monastery and the surrounding land, until Palahanah 1175, when Szeth and [[Kaladin]] traveled to Shinovar to complete Szeth’s [[Knights_Radiant#Immortal_Words|fourth ideal]]. As their first stop, on the third of the final ten days, they went to Rit’s monastery and Szeth killed her with [[12124]].{{book ref|sa5|41}}

Over the next few days, as Szeth and Kaladin continued Szeth’s quest, she was reborn, taking an unknown Shin person’s body. By the tenth day, she was reborn and stood ready with Ishar and the other Honorbearers at the [[Bondsmith monastery]]. At Ishar's command, she and the others attacked Szeth, nearly killing him. As he was lying on the ground, injured, out of [[Stormlight]], he finally chose to accept agency, swearing his [[Szeth#The_Fifth_Ideal|fifth ideal]].{{book ref|sa5|133}} He promptly broke his oaths with [[12124]], before using [[Nightblood]] to kill the Honorbearers.{{book ref|sa5|136}} Because Nightblood consumes all aspect of things he attacks, when he killed the Honorbearers, they died permanently.{{book ref|sa5|132}} Rit died third, after [[Pozen-son-Nash|Pozen]] and [[Moss-son-Farrier|Moss]].{{book ref|sa5|141}}
