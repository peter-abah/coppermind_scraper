'''Sebarial princedom''' is one of ten [[Alethi]] princedoms in [[Alethkar]] on [[Roshar]]. The house colors are deep gold & black.{{wob ref|10274}} The [[highprince]] is [[Turinad Sebarial]] and his banner consists of the [[glyphs]] ''sebes'' and ''laial'' in the shape of a [[skyeel]].{{book ref|sa2|40}} 

== Geography ==
Sebarial princedom is located in the south of Alethkar, bordered to the north by [[Thanadal princedom|Thanadal]], to the west by [[Hatham princedom|Hatham]] and [[Bethab princedom|Bethab]], to the east by the [[Sea of Spears]] and the [[Eastern Crownlands]], and to the south by the [[Frostlands]].<ref>Note that the available maps of Alethkar do not clearly define the eastern and southern borders.</ref>{{map ref|Alethkar}} The city of [[Rashir]] is located near the western border with Bethab. The [[Deathbend River]] flows through the princedom and its tributaries appear to define parts of the eastern border.{{map ref|Alethkar}}

== History ==
The princedom was founded by one of the ten sons of [[Sadees]] the Sunmaker, who split up Alethkar after his death.{{book ref|sa3|19}} 

== Warcamp ==
Like the other princedoms, Sebarial contructs a large warcamp at the [[Shattered Plains]] during the [[War of Reckoning]]. Highprince Sebarial focuses on commerce rather than battle, and his warcamp is notably more advanced than the other camps. It has running water, shops, taverns, well-engineered housing, farms, twelve factories making various goods and supplies, and an information station with scribes and [[spanreed]]s, including at least one connected to [[Tashikk]].{{book ref|sa2|40}}{{book ref|sa3|47}} The camp's gates are open all night.{{book ref|sa3|42}} [[Kaladin]] thought Sebarial's camp looked more like a city than the other camps.{{book ref|sa2|55}}

[[Little Herdaz]] is inside the warcamp, and a number of Herdazians live there even if they are not from Sebarial princedom.{{book ref|sa2|87}} [[Lopen]] and other Herdazians disguise [[Elhokar]] and bring him into Little Herdaz to recover after an assassination attempt by [[Moash]].{{book ref|sa2|87}}

===Manor===
Sebarial and his [[Herdaz]]ian mistress [[Palona]] live in a manor in the camp. Although not as large as manors in Alethi cities, it is far more elaborate than other buildings in the warcamps. The house's exterior has classical columns and is situated behind taller buildings to avoid wind damage. The gardens have ornamental [[shalebark]], trees, and a flagstone driveway.{{book ref|sa2|40}} The interior is mostly white stone with marble, crystal, glass windows, and gold-trimmed statues. There are numerous maids and footmen.{{book ref|sa2|40}} 

When [[Shallan]] arrives at the [[Shattered Plains]], she invites herself into Highprince [[Sebarial]]'s entourage.{{book ref|sa2|38}} She moves into the manor and is surprised by the level of affluence displayed by Sebarial. Her guest quarters are white stone, with a sitting room and rich furnishings.{{book ref|sa2|40}} 

==Move to Urithiru==
Sebarial is one of the few highprinces that sends his armies to join [[Dalinar]]'s assault on the [[Parshendi]] that results in the [[Battle of Narak]].{{book ref|sa2|76}} This gains him a measure of respect with Dalinar, who names Sebarial the Highprince of Commerce in [[Urithiru]].{{book ref|sa3|2}} Sebarial is given a section of Urithiru and relocates most of the people in his warcamp to the city.{{book ref|sa3|18}} Some operations remain at the warcamp due to the need for agriculture and lumber, although Sebarial thinks this is not sustainable and expresses concern about [[Thanadal]] taking over the camps.{{book ref|sa3|104}}

Most of Sebarial's soldiers end up joining the [[House Kholin|Kholin]] army.{{book ref|sa3|107}} Shallan decides to live in Sebarial's section of Urithiru, and notes that it stays busier than the rest of the city, much like his warcamp.{{book ref|sa3|13}}{{book ref|sa3|18}}
