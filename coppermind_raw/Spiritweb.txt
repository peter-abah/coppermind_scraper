The '''spiritweb''' is the network of [[Connection]]s and [[Investiture]] which make up the soul of a creature, place, object, or planet.{{book ref|sa4|75}} Residing in the [[Spiritual Realm]], spiritwebs lack any tangible shape or size, with their borders defined by their [[Identity]]. Contained within a spiritweb are various pieces, including '''Spiritual DNA''', the '''Spiritual aspect''', and one’s '''Spiritual Ideal'''.{{wob ref|3923}} The Spiritweb is Connected to the [[Physical Realm|Physical]] and [[Cognitive Realm|Cognitive aspects]], and contains a Connection between both.{{book ref|yumi|21}}{{book ref|sa3|108}}

== Mechanics ==
=== Spiritual DNA ===
A person’s spiritweb, closely related to Spiritual DNA,{{wob ref|115}} makes up their presence in the Spiritual Realm. Measuring and quantifying a spiritweb is possible, if difficult, to do.{{wob ref|13361}} Spiritual DNA can be passed from parent to child like regular DNA,{{wob ref|5514}} yet does not function exactly the same as identical twins can have different Spiritual DNA.{{wob ref|3037}}{{wob ref|7761}}{{wob ref|16270}} Some ways of utilizing Investiture are written into Spiritual DNA. Spiritwebs can be manufactured, though the process of how is unknown.{{wob ref|15234}}

=== Damaging and Healing ===
A person's spiritweb can be damaged either through physical or emotional trauma. This creates "cracks" in the soul. It is easier for Shards to [[Invest]] in people who are damaged in this way, as Investiture can enter the soul through these "cracks". This is why [[Allomancer]]s need to undergo [[Snapping]] in order to awaken their powers, and is implied to be the reason why [[Surgebinder]]s tend to have psychological problems.{{wob ref|5469}}{{book ref|sa2|68}}{{book ref|sa4|110}} Spiritwebs can be damaged with weapons like hemalurgic spikes and Shardblades. Those wounds can then be healed with a graft of Investiture. The healing process does leave a permanent wound on a person's spiritweb.{{wob ref|126}} Investing an object or person for a long period of time will warp and change their spiritweb.{{wob ref|2690}} In humans, this process leads to [[Savant]]ism.{{wob ref|7267}}

==== Healing the Physical ====
Most forms of healing work by using the three aspects of the user's self; their Physical, Cognitive, and Spiritual aspects.{{wob ref|9441}}{{wob ref|5306}} Healing typically heals the Physical version by matching it to their Spiritual Ideal, which is filtered through the lens of their minds (the Cognitive aspect).{{wob ref|3923}} Almost all forms of healing in the [[cosmere]], even natural healing, are filtered through the perception of the user, {{wob ref|7024}}{{wob ref|9623}} which means that if a person hasn't accepted a wound as a part of themselves, they can still heal it regardless of how long they've had it.{{wob ref|12149}} However, even if a wound is recent, if the person has changed their mindset to see it as a part of themselves, they will not be able to heal from it.{{wob ref|6501}}{{book ref|sa3|i|13}} An example of this can be found when [[Kaladin]] was unable to heal his slave brand until he swore the fourth oath and recognized that it wasn't a part of him.{{book ref|sa4|110}}

=== Permanency ===
Even after death, a record of the Spiritweb of a person remains in the Spiritual Realm. It is not known if this can be interacted with by Invested Arts such as Hemalurgy.{{wob ref|3910}}

== Interactions ==
All forms of Investiture which one can use are related to the spiritweb. Some manifestations of Investiture are built into a person's Spiritual DNA, such as [[Allomancy]] and [[Feruchemy]]. Other abilities, such as [[Hemalurgy]] and [[Surgebinding]], involve manipulating Spiritual DNA. A few types of Investiture even work by manipulating or overwriting the spiritweb directly, such as [[Forgery]]. 

=== The Dor ===
==== AonDor ====
[[AonDor]] requires some innate Spiritual DNA, but the [[Shaod]] that turns someone into an [[Elantrian]] involves some sort of change to the Spiritual DNA during the transition.{{wob ref|14742}}{{wob ref|2173}}

==== Forgery ====
[[Forgery]] works by overlaying an object’s or person’s history and spiritweb with an alternative one. This may indicate that it is possible to remove the effects of a [[soulstamp]], though it is currently unknown.{{wob ref|837}}

===Canticle===
==== Heat Sharing ====
People from the planet [[Canticle]] are able to exchange portions of their spiritweb similar to breath. Unlike breath, heat can only be exchanged with people of Canticle (including by invitation).{{book ref|tsm|Thirty-Nine}} It is unclear how this would interact with Allomancy, as the strength of Allomantic power is generally proportional to the amount of [[Preservation|Preservation's]] investiture an Allomancer contains.{{Cite}}

====Cinderhearts====
When a [[cinderheart]] is inserted deeply into a person's chest it consumes the connections and identity in a person's spiritweb, and connects them to other people who host a cinderheart.{{book ref|tsm|50}}{{wob ref|16297}} People who have a cinderheart placed more shallowly become connected to other people who host a cinder heart, but still maintain their original connections and identity.{{Cite}}

=== The Metallic Arts ===
==== Allomancy ====
Multiple forms of Allomancy relate to the spiritweb. Allomantic [[zinc]] and [[brass]] will work only on creatures with a sufficient level of sapience and work better on creatures whose spiritweb has been damaged, as with Hemalurgic constructs.{{book ref|mb2|54}}{{book ref|mb3|3}}{{epigraph ref|mb3|21}} The ability to use Allomancy is passed down via Spiritual DNA.{{wob ref|12576}}

==== Feruchemy ====
Several of the Feruchemical Attributes are deeply connected to the spiritweb, especially the Spiritual metals such as [[aluminum]] and [[duralumin]], which allow for Identity and Connection, respectively, to be stored. Similarly to Allomancy, the ability to use Feruchemy is passed down through Spiritual DNA.

==== Hemalurgy ====
In Hemalurgy, portions of the spiritweb seem to be ripped off of one person and grafted onto another person's spiritweb.{{wob ref|5969}} This causes lasting damage to the spiritweb of the creature in which a Hemalurgic Spike is placed.{{wob ref|4961}}

=== Surgebinding ===
A [[Surgebinder]]'s [[Nahel bond]] involves a [[spren]] attaching themselves to a person's spiritweb, merging more fully as the [[Surgebinder]] progresses in their [[Immortal Words|Oaths]].{{wob ref|184}}{{cite|WOB on merging more as oaths progress}}

==== Bondsmithing ====
A [[Bondsmith]] that is bonded to the [[Stormfather]] is able to read a person's spiritweb.{{book ref|sa4|47}} The person's spiritweb appears to the Bondsmith as faintly glowing lines of varying thickness depending on the strength of the connection. It is currently unknown if other Bondsmiths share this ability.

==== Soulcasting ====
Although it does not directly alter the spiritweb, [[Soulcasting]] can alter a spiritweb if the Cognitive aspect stays altered for long enough.{{book ref|sa3|part=ars}}

==== Regrowth ====
[[Regrowth]] allows for healing both the Physical form of a living thing and its spiritweb, as does a sufficient amount of Stormlight. This likely functions by restoring one’s physical form to the state of their Spiritual Ideal.{{wob ref|9441}} Regrowth can even go so far as to reattach the soul of one recently deceased.{{book ref|sa2|88}}{{book ref|sa3|90}}

=== Shardblades ===
A [[Shardblade]] can be stolen with Hemalurgy because it is Connected to a person's spiritweb.{{wob ref|1153}}{{wob ref|4548}} When a Shardblade cuts through living matter its edge fuzzes, leaving the physical body uninjured.{{book ref|sa1|13}}

=== Fabrials ===
There are fabrials that are capable of accurately [[Aura fabrial|reading a person's spiritweb]]. However, these devices tend to go haywire if the person being read is heavily Invested such as a [[Returned]], [[Elantrian]], or [[yoki-hijo]].{{book ref|yumi|32}}
