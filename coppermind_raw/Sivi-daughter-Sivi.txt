'''Sivi-daughter-Sivi''' is the [[Order of Willshapers|Willshaper]] [[Honorbearer]] in [[Shinovar]]. She dies in 1175, after refusing [[Ishar]]’s offer to make her into a human [[Fused]].

== Appearance and Personality ==
{{quote
|I’m merely halfway holy, Szeth.
|Sivi{{book ref|sa5|73}}
}}
Sivi is a tall [[Shin]] woman who wears the colorful robes of a [[Stone Shamanism|Stone Shaman]]. She has short, dark hair, which she keeps spiked up in a style [[Szeth]] describes as “flagrant.”{{book ref|sa5|73}} She has deep violet eyes.{{book ref|sa5|57}}

Sivi is wise and insightful, but also quick to smile and laugh. She usually doesn’t take herself seriously, and doesn’t consider her position worth reverence. Despite her nonchalant countenance, she does care about Szeth and the success of the Shin people, guiding him towards his [[Honorbearer#Induction|pilgrimage]], encouraging him to accept nuance, and warning him about [[Pozen]]’s intentions.{{book ref|sa5|73}}

She enjoys drinking, and doesn’t consider it an impure act, despite her station’s social norms.

== Attributes and Abilities ==
As the [[Willshaper]] [[Honorbearer]], she has access to the [[Surge]]s of [[Cohesion]] and [[Transportation]]. This allows her to reshape stone with her hands and travel between the Physical and Cognitive Realms. She can bring others with her when Elsecalling,{{book ref|sa5|53}} and can also see into [[Shadesmar]] without fully entering.{{book ref|sa4|11}} Like all Honorbearers, she is trained in the use of all ten Surges, though Cohesion and Transportation are her specialty. 

== History ==
At some point before 1152, Sivi went on her pilgrimage and became the Willshaper Honorbearer, settling in the [[Willshaper monastery]]. In 1152, she visited the [[Stoneward monastery]] with Pozen to evaluate [[Neturo]]’s leadership. [[Szeth]] took the opportunity, seeing the Honorbearers, to ask about the morality of hurting other people. Pozen and Sivi agreed that he needed to be used to death in order to be a good soldier, and assigned him to work in the slaughterhouses.{{book ref|sa5|57}}

Somewhere between 1152 and 1159 - likely after 1157{{book ref|sa5|68}} - Sivi developed a romantic relationship with Neturo. She began visiting the Stoneward monastery frequently, both to keep an eye on Szeth, who was being considered as a future Honorbearer, and to visit with Neturo. In 1157, she told Neturo much about the Honorbearers, including the nature of [[Ishar|the Voice]], and began training him to be an Honorbearer in secret.{{book ref|sa5|143}} In 1159, she traveled to Mokdown, the city next to the [[Elsecaller monastery]], where she saw Szeth working on riddles assigned to him by Pozen. She cut him off, handing him the solutions to the riddles and inviting him to get a drink with her. They discussed the nature of nuance, Szeth’s future as an Honorbearer, and the intentions behind Pozen preparing Szeth to remove [[Tuko-son-Tuko]].{{book ref|sa5|73}} Later that year, Szeth left for Sivi's monastery in order to practice the Willshaper surges with her.{{book ref|sa5|75}}

In late 1165, she was present as Szeth completed his pilgrimage by killing Tuko.{{book ref|sa5|84}} After he went on to discover the truth about [[Ishar|the Voice]], he wrote her a letter seeking her help, though she ignored it. She then visited Pozen to discuss her fears regarding Szeth and plan what to do. When Szeth arrived to assassinate Pozen, she was still there, and Szeth found he couldn’t kill the women his father had loved. Then, faced with two Honorbearers instead of one, he fled.{{book ref|sa5|102}} Six months later, as Szeth and his army fought against Pozen and his outside of the Stoneward monastery, multiple Honorbearers came to attempt to take down Szeth. Moss went first, and Sivi followed. Because Szeth limited his use of [[Surge]]s out of a sense of honor, their fight lasted five minutes before he disarmed her.{{book ref|sa5|111}} After he was confronted by his father and gave up the rebellion, Sivi helped the other Honorbearers decide what to do with him.{{book ref|sa5|117}}

Years later, as a part of [[Ishar]]’s plans to defeat [[Odium]], the Honorbearers were made into human [[Fused]]. Sivi rejected this step, turning away from Ishar’s influence and refusing to follow further. Her defiance protected the people living in and near her monastery from Ishar’s corruption. Eventually, at some time before 1175, she died in her monastery by unknown means. She etched her last words into the stone: "I will not bow to him." On the fourth of the [[contest_of_champions|final ten days]], Szeth and [[Kaladin]] found her body and took from it [[Kalak]]'s [[Honorblade|Blade]].{{book ref|sa5|45}}
