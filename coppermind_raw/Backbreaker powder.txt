'''Backbreaker powder''' is a very strong poison used on [[Roshar]] during the [[Era of Solitude]].{{book ref|sa1|50}}

== Usage ==
Backbreaker powder is a very lethal, tasteless, white powder. Even breathing it in can be fatal. There is an antidote for the poison that is capable of counteracting all of its effects.{{book ref|sa1|50}} A small amount of backbreaker powder is capable of killing the person that ingests it in mere seconds unless the antidote is taken.{{book ref|sa1|48}}

When dusted over bread, backbreaker powder can be disguised as flour.{{book ref|sa1|50}}

== History ==
In {{Rosharan date|1173}}, [[Ardent]] [[Kabsal]], a member of the [[Ghostblood]]s, tried to assassinate Princess [[Jasnah Kholin]] with backbreaker powder during her visit to the [[Palanaeum]] in [[Kharbranth]] to stop her from attaining her goals.{{book ref|sa1|50}} He sprinkled it over [[Thaylen]] bread like flour and put the antidote in the jam,{{book ref|sa1|50}} which he knew Jasnah disliked.{{book ref|sa1|48}} For months, he visited Jasnah with a basket of bread and jam using the excuse of visiting for the company of her new ward, [[Shallan Davar]]. Each time he visited, he attempted to convince Jasnah to try the bread, putting emphasis on how different it was than the breads found in western [[Roshar]] and how light and fluffy it was.{{book ref|sa1|33}}{{book ref|sa1|50}} However, he only succeeded when visiting Shallan after she was hospitalized following her first [[Soulcasting]], when Shallan herself convinced Jasnah to try the poisoned bread.{{book ref|sa1|48}}

Jasnah was able to avoid ingesting the poison by Soulcasting the bread into smoke as she consumed it.{{book ref|sa1|70}} She also attempted to use Soulcasting to replace the contents of the jam jar with identical jam, as an extra precaution to remove unknown ingredients. This inadvertently removed the antidote from the jam, and as Jasnah was unskilled in Soulcasting organic material, she created a rotten-smelling substance. Because the antidote was no longer in the jam, Kabsal's plan went awry; instead of Jasnah being poisoned by the bread, he and Shallan were.{{book ref|sa1|48}} Kabsal attempted to remedy the situation by eating a large amount of the rotten jam, and he tried unsuccessfully to convince Shallan to eat some of it as well.{{book ref|sa1|48}}{{book ref|sa1|70}} Jasnah saved Shallan from the effects of the backbreaker powder by Soulcasting the contents of her veins into blood to remove the poison,{{book ref|sa1|48}} but she was to late to help Kabsal, who died.{{book ref|sa1|50}}
