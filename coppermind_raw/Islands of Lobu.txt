The '''Islands of Lobu''' are a group of islands in the [[Sapphire Sea]] on [[Lumar]].{{book ref|tress|15}}

== Culture ==
[[Fort]] and [[Salay]] hail from the islands, and are the same ethnicity.{{book ref|tress|15}} Salay has delicate features, with dark skin and curly black hair.{{book ref|tress|15}}{{book ref|tress|30}}{{book ref|tress|45}}

=== Hunters ===
{{quote
|I am a hunter by profession. It is a mark of pride among my people, and my family in particular, to execute an excellent hunt.
|Fort {{book ref|tress|40}}
}}
The islands have a notable culture of people called '''hunters'''. Their tenet is that they must search for a worthy hunt. The definition of a "hunt" has broadened through the years, since a society comprised solely of hunters cannot thrive; modern hunts can therefore be more figurative. When a person comes of age, they choose their hunt. The hunt can include almost anything, like baking great bread or planning perfect weddings. If someone is off the islands, they send letters to people talking about their hunt, to be recorded and hung on the wall.{{book ref|totes|40}} A person's chosen hunt does not necessarily prevent them from pursuing an even more glorious hunt.{{book ref|tress|62}} Fort's hunt is trading, the same as his mother.{{book ref|totes|40}} Salay does not specifically mention this culture, so it is unclear if all of the residents of the islands are part of it.
