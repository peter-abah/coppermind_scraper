'''Hordaland''' is the homeland of the '''Hordamen'''. It is east of [[Weswara]] across the ocean, and near [[Norweg]], [[Dansic]], and the lands of the [[Geats]]{{book ref|frugal|13}}.

John West recognizes the Hordamen as the equivalent to Vikings{{book ref|frugal|13}}, so Hordaland is presumably equivalent to some part of Scandinavia.

Hordamen warriors are disciplined fighters, not "thoughtless barbarians", according to John West.

== History == 
The Hordamen conquered Norweg and Dansic and pushed the [[Weswarans]] into their current land of Weswara{{book ref|frugal|11}}. At the time of [[John West]]'s arrival on the alternate medieval Earth, they are raiding Weswara, and their raids are becoming very threatening{{book ref|frugal|12}}{{book ref|frugal|31}}. [[Woden]] has abandoned the Weswarans in favor of the Hordamen by this point{{book ref|frugal|13}}. 

Shortly afterward, Woden incites a major invasion of Hordamen intended to destroy the Weswarans{{book ref|frugal|33}} in order to eliminate the "outsiders" from another dimension{{book ref|frugal|35}} and to serve as an example to intimidate his other worshippers{{book ref|frugal|37}}. This invasion fails due to Sefawynn stirring up the wights against the Hordamen{{book ref|frugal|35}}{{book ref|frugal|36}}.

==Abilities and Technology ==

The Hordamen have [[skop]]s, who they call skalds{{book ref|frugal|14}}. On raids, Hordamen skops use boasts to intimidate the [[landswight]]s, removing the protective effect of the defenders' [[runestone]]s. The raid first encountered by John, [[Sefawynn]], and [[Ealstan]] has one skop per ship{{book ref|frugal|13}}.

Hordamen raids use Viking-style longships with dragon figureheads. These ships each have a dozen or so rowers on each side{{book ref|frugal|13}}. Hordaland has a large number of ships available; the major invasion involves hundreds of ships{{book ref|frugal|33}}.

Hordamen warriors fight in ranks and use leather or chain shirts, helms, shields, axes{{book ref|frugal|33}}, and swords{{book ref|frugal|35}}. On raiding ships, only the captain of each ship has a chain shirt; others rely on shields{{book ref|frugal|13}}.
