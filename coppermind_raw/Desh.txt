'''Desh''' is a kingdom in southwestern [[Roshar]] during the [[Era of Solitude]]{{map ref|Roshar}} and a member state of the [[Azir|Azish empire]].{{book ref|sa3|65}} The people of Desh are referred to as Deshi.{{book ref|sa3.5|12}}

== Geography ==
Desh is a small nation that is situated at the foot of a mountainous area in western Roshar. It borders [[Azir]] to the east and [[Yezier]] to the south. [[Shinovar]] lies to the west and [[Babatharnam]] to the north, but they are separated by the mountains.{{map ref|Roshar}} During the [[Silver Kingdoms]] era, Desh was a part of [[Makabakam]].{{map ref|Silver Kingdoms}} Its strategic position against Roshar’s western mountains provides a natural defensive border and a series of labyrinthine caves for storing grain and war fabrials.{{rpg ref|slwg|42}}

In the eastern region of Desh, vast steppes contain unusually pure sandstone that’s ideal for making glass for spheres. Here, military reserves cart the crushed sandstone to the Azish border. It’s then sold and transported to Jah Keved, where it’s heated and mixed with minerals from the Bavland mines to produce finished sphere glass.{{rpg ref|slwg|42}}

== Politics ==
As a member of the Azish empire, Desh formally recognizes the [[Prime Aqasix]] as its emperor.{{book ref|sa3|65}} In practice, the member states of the empire are generally self-governing, although they follow Azir's lead in international matters and maintain deference to the emperor through certain cultural customs.{{book ref|sa3|12}}{{book ref|sa3|122}} Desh is a quiet sentinel of military power and emergency provisions. The kingdom is ruled by a council of Deshi royalty and retired Azish generals. Although Desh sends ambassadors to nations near and far, they typically serve more as spies than diplomats.{{rpg ref|slwg|42}}

Although soldiers in Desh are commanded by the royal council, soldiers consider themselves to be in service of their goddess Vedahha and all Talga. All adults with able bodies and the spirits of guardians are conscripted to military service as active-duty soldiers or reserves. The Deshi sand runners are a testament to Deshi efficiency, as the kingdom’s soldiers help fund its robust military, along with subsidies from Azir.{{rpg ref|slwg|42}}

== Culture ==
People from Desh were common visitors to the [[Tashikk]]i city of [[Yeddaw]].{{book ref|sa2.5|4}} [[Lift]] characterizes their clothing as "rural" in appearance with lighter colors, similar to clothing worn in [[Alm]].{{book ref|sa2.5|4}} In Desh, children are treated with the utmost courtesy. An honorific, Talga, is given to all who can’t fight due to youth, old age, illness, or disability. Deshi people typically eat stewed grains, fried vines, roasted goat meat and various preparations of soft-shelled cremlings. On holidays, some enjoy a rare treat of sparkling fruit liquor from Alm.{{rpg ref|slwg|42}}

The people with temperaments too gentle for war become surgeons, apothecaries, farmers, priests, scribes, artifabrians, teachers, and merchants. Some become nomads and keep to the mountains, building obelisks to designate waystops. The Deshi people worship a goddess named [[Vedahha]].{{rpg ref|slwg|42}}

== History ==
During the Silver Kingdoms Period, the land that would come to be known as Desh was part of the kingdom of [[Makabakam]].{{map ref|Silver Kingdoms}} Though Makabakam eventually lost authority and collapsed, causing various cultural groups and nations to form in its ruins. At some point during or after this event, Desh was formed.

At some point prior to 1174, Desh came to be a member state of the Azish Empire.{{book ref|sa3|65}} 

Lift visited Desh in her past and enjoyed it, as few people had tried to kill her during her time there.{{book ref|sa2.5|4}}

One of Desh's ambassadors was in [[Azimir]] when [[Noura]] told [[Dalinar]] that the [[Makabak]] empire would join his coalition against [[Odium]].{{book ref|sa3|65}} Decades have passed since Desh mobilized its impressive armies, but times of peace haven’t eroded its prowess.{{rpg ref|slwg|42}} During the True Desolation, the Azish started preparing the necessary paperwork to take full control of the Deshi reserve armies. However, the royal family is reluctant to relinquish control to the Bronze Palace until the situation intensifies.{{rpg ref|slwg|43}}
