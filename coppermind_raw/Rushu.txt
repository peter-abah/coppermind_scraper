'''Rushu''' is an [[ardent]] that works as scholar affiliated with the [[Kholin princedom]].{{book ref|sa2|4}} Rushu is interested in the study of handwriting{{book ref|sa2|4}} and [[fabrial]]s,{{book ref|sa2|35}} and does research that revolutionizes [[artifabrian]]s' understanding of how [[Shardblade]]s function.{{book ref|sa2|67}}

== Appearance and Personality ==
Rushu is a very attractive young person. Like many ardents, they{{wob ref|16685}} keep their head shaved.{{book ref|sa2|35}} Their face has delicate features, with long lush eyelashes and button-like lips.{{book ref|sa2|35}}{{book ref|sa2|4}} They are seemingly implied to have a non-binary gender identity.{{book ref|sa5|23}}{{wob ref|16685}}{{book ref|sa2|35}}

Rushu is a brilliant scholar,{{book ref|sa2|35}} but occasionally somewhat oblivious to their surroundings.{{book ref|sa2|35}}{{book ref|sa2|4}} They do not notice hints or implied commands in others' words,{{book ref|sa2|35}} it often requires a direct command and multiple promptings to get their attention and make them realize what they are supposed to do.{{book ref|sa2|35}}{{book ref|sa2|4}} Rushu often gets distracted as well, reading scientific reports when they should be helping with an experiment.{{book ref|sa2|35}} They have trouble focusing, often wandering off on tangents and random thought experiments in conversation,{{book ref|sa2|35}} or getting very excited.{{book ref|sa3|50}} They joined the ardentia to escape the advances of men, but this did not work particularly well, as they still receive attention from many of the male ardents. They are particularly interested in [[spren]] research{{book ref|sa2|35}} and the study of handwriting, among other things.{{book ref|sa2|4}} Rushu is also a courageous person that is willing to risk their safety for their studies, as evidenced by their desire to ride on the [[fabrial]] parapet as it ascended.{{book ref|sa2|35}}

==Attributes and Abilities==
Rushu is a skilled artifabrian, who devised a hovering seat for [[Rysn Ftori]].{{book ref|sa3.5|10}} They are also trained in the use of a [[Soulcaster]], and have one during the expedition to [[Aimia]].{{book ref|sa3.5|15}}

== History ==
=== Early Life ===
Much about Rushu's early life is unknown, but they joined the ardentia at some point in their past to escape the advances of men, due to their extremely attractive appearance.{{book ref|sa2|35}} They became one of the Kholin scholars and worked often with Brightlady [[Navani Kholin]], who became fond of them and developed a high regard for Rushu's intelligence.

=== Research on the Shattered Plains ===
{{image|Zahel and Rushu by Marie Seeberger.jpg|side=right|width=300px|Rushu and [[Zahel]]}}

Rushu was summoned after the [[glyph]]s counting down to the arrival of the [[Everstorm]] were discovered for the first time. Rushu observed that the glyphs were poorly drawn, asymmetrical and uneven, as well as vague in their meaning. They were charged by Navani to make a copy of those glyphs and not to speak of them to anyone, then were sent away so that Navani could speak with [[Highprince]] [[Dalinar Kholin]] privately.{{book ref|sa2|4}}

Rushu was also present at Navani's test of the floating parapet made with the use of reverser [[fabrial]]s. They were initially distracted, reading about [[Geranid]]'s experiments with [[spren#Flamespren|flamespren]], but soon focused on the project at hand. Rushu was excited that the parapet seemed to be working, but wished that they could have been on the platform itself to check the gemstones for cracks. Navani sent Rushu to inspect the gems with the other ardents, so that she could speak to her nephew Brightlord [[Adolin]] Kholin privately.{{book ref|sa2|35}}

Rushu also later questioned why [[Shardblade]]s could be summoned or dismissed even if the gemstone on their hilt had gone dun. They spent several weeks in contact with the ardents in [[Kharbranth]] via one of the [[Tashikk]]i relay stations to do some research in the [[Palanaeum]] on the subject. They reported to Rushu that they had found accounts that the Blades initially were not able to be summoned or dismissed until ornamental gemstones were added to their hilts. They also found stories of the hilts actually changing shape to accept the gemstones. This knowledge that Rushu provided was a breakthrough for artifabrians, helping them to realize that the only function of the gemstone was to aid in bonding the Blade; therefore, it was not part of a fabrial that made the sword supernaturally light and sharp.{{book ref|sa2|67}}

=== Service in Urithiru ===
Shortly after arriving in [[Urithiru]], Rushu assisted [[Adolin Kholin]] in directing workers to unload their wagons at the right place in the massive city according to Navani's layout.{{book ref|sa3|2}}

When Dalinar and Navani considered getting married, Navani asked several ardents, including Rushu, to marry them. However, like all of the other ardents, Rushu turned them down, as [[Vorinism]] considered them siblings by way of Navani's marriage to [[Gavilar Kholin]]; they even squeaked and literally ran away.{{book ref|sa3|4}}

Later, Rushu joined King [[Elhokar Kholin]], Dalinar, and Navani on a trip to the Alethi warcamps on the [[Shattered Plains]]. The group traveled to the Shattered Plains via the [[Oathgate]], and then were flown to the warcamps by [[Kaladin]] and his [[Windrunners]], which Rushu found exhilarating. Once they arrived, Rushu discussed sketches for a flying ship with Navani and commented that the math wasn't right, though they had trouble focusing, as they were still excited about the experience of flying.{{book ref|sa3|50}}

=== Expedition to Aimia ===
[[Navani]] selected Rushu to join [[Rysn]]'s [[expedition to Akinah]]. They got secret details on the mission and briefed [[Lopen]], [[Huio]], and [[Cord]] when they joined.{{book ref|sa3.5|3}} A few weeks into the voyage, Rushu started experimenting with their fabrials. In the meantime, they reprimanded Lopen for making inappropriate jokes near Rysn. Using a pair of silvery hoops with ruby, they explained conjoining fabrials that move in opposite directions to Rysn. They discussed why spanreeds didn't work on a ship while those other fabrials did, an answer that was based on perception. Rushu proposed to Rysn that they could make Rysn's seat hover in the air, although she would not be able to move it. When putting the fabrials away, Rysn spotted sheets of [[aluminum]]. The ardent explained that aluminum could block spanreeds and that Navani wanted them to experiment with the sheets.{{book ref|sa3.5|5}}

When Huio flew to some nearby land to send and receive messages, Rushu gave him some aluminum plates to use in experiments when writing to them on the ship.{{book ref|sa3.5|7}} Later, the expedition camped a night in a small cove. Rushu went to Rysn to experiment with her chair, without waiting for Rysn's permission. They explained that when Huio was playing with their spanreeds he had made an important discovery: if aluminum was placed in the spanreed in the right way, only movement along one plane of motion was transferred to the other reed. They demonstrated the effect by making Rysn's chair hover, then pushing the chair around, something thought to be impossible before. At [[Nikliasorm|Nikli]]'s suggestion, Rysn asked Rushu to report the discovery back to Urithiru immediately.{{book ref|sa3.5|9}} Later, Rushu and Huio installed a mechanism on Rysn's seat that allowed her to hover and rotate her seat.{{book ref|sa3.5|10}}

When the expedition reached Aimia, Rushu joined Huio and Lopen in a boat going to shore.{{book ref|sa3.5|12}} They arrived on a beach full of gemstones. When one of sailors asked if the treasure was cursed, Rushu answered that they were there to loot the place. Rushu made some sketches of the skeletons on the beach before joining Lopen and several guards to move inwards on the island.{{book ref|sa3.5|13}}

When they reached the outskirts of the city, Rushu sketched some of the buildings while Lopen flew into the air to look ahead. They moved on and surveyed the city, noting how much it had fallen to rubble. Rushu noted how important this place would be for historians to help understand the past. Lopen asked where to find the [[Oathgate]], and they replied that most were situated to provide easy access for the ruling class. One of the sailors brought a note from Rysn, warning them that something felt off, as it all looked too perfect. They asked Lopen for one of the gemstones from the beach and inspected it with a jeweler's loupe. It was fake. They wondered why someone would set up such an elaborate deception. After a short time they reached the location of the Oathgate, which was very decayed and without a place to put a Shardblade. They concluded that it was fake as well. The ardent asked the sailors to look for stairs or a trapdoor that could lead down. They quickly found it, and Rushu used a Soulcaster given to them by Navani to remove the wall blocking the entrance to the cavern. They remarked that it would have taken an extraordinary effort to hide the Oathgate down in the cavern. Rushu was proud they managed to crack the secret, but Lopen was worried about the response from whoever was watching the place. Lopen left for the beach and told Rushu to gather the sailors and not to get killed.{{book ref|sa3.5|15}}

After the battle against the [[Sleepless]] hordelings, they made a few more rounds through the city to sketch it. Lopen was talking to some rocks, and the ardent asked him if he could be less weird. They moved to the rowboats to get back to the ''[[Wandersail (1174 ship)|Wandersail]]'', and Lopen praised Rysn's negotiation skills, saying he couldn't believe they survived. Rushu concurred that there had been something strange about the situation.{{book ref|sa3.5|epilogue}}
