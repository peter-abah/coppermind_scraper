'''Vedel''', also known as '''Vedeledev''' in [[Vorinism]], is one of the ten [[Herald]]s of the [[Almighty]] on [[Roshar]], and the patron Herald of the [[Order of Edgedancers]].{{book ref|sa3|part=ars}} Like the other Heralds, Vedel protected mankind during the Cycle of [[Desolation]]s before abandoning them at [[Aharietiam]].{{book ref|sa1|part=prelude}}

== Appearance and Personality ==
{{image|Vedel by Esther Schrader.jpg|side=left|width=200px}}

{{for|/Gallery|more images}}

Vedel would appear as [[Alethi]] or [[Veden]] to modern Rosharans, though her skin appears paler than average of said ethnic groups, and some would interpret her as being from the [[Horneater Peaks]].{{book ref|sa5|62}}{{wob ref|11325}} Her hair is long and dark, with slight curl.{{book ref|sa5|62}}

== Attributes and Abilities ==
{{image|Vedel by Caio Santos.jpg|side=right|width=200px}}

{{for|Herald#Abilities|more information about Heralds' powers}}

Vedel is a [[Herald]], making her a [[Cognitive Shadow]] and thus immortal. When she dies, she returns to [[Braize]] to be tortured.{{wob ref|8942}}{{book ref|sa3|38}} Until [[Aharietiam]], she wielded a [[Honorblade]], which gave her access to the [[Surge]]s of [[Abrasion]] and [[Progression]], and she was a capable swordswoman.

After losing her people, and before becoming a Herald, she served as a healer. She possesses vast medical knowledge, and trained new surgeons before a Desolation.{{book ref|sa2|1|7}} It's likely that a lot of the Heralds' teachings regarding medicine -- such as the mandate to always wash hands before an operation -- came from her.{{book ref|sa1|10}} 

By using the Herald's Connection to Honor, she was able to tap into the constantly rejuvenating Investiture of the Spiritual Realm, allowing it to flow into the Heralds and seal their souls at their current age, thus granting them immortality. This includes herself, thus granting herself eternal life.{{book ref|sa5|64}}{{book ref|sa5|65}}

==History ==

=== Life on Alaswha ===
She was born on Alaswha and became the queen of a city-state on the war-torn planet. She appears to have been given Surges before the scouring, given to her by Odium.{{book ref|sa5|58}} These Surges slowed down her rate of aging.{{book ref|sa5|58}} She fought for [[Odium's empire]] to expand its hegemony.{{book ref|sa5|58}}

At the time of the scouring of Ashyn, seven thousand one hundred years before the [[Night of Sorrows]], she was visiting Odium's empire.{{book ref|sa5|63}} It appears her entire nation died in the scouring, though she survived by passing through an Elsegate to [[Roshar]].{{book ref|sa5|63}} Following the loss of her people, she became a skilled healer.{{book ref|sa5|63}}

=== Herald of the Almighty ===
Along with the others that would come to be Heralds, she approached [[Honor]] and formed the [[Oathpact]]. She somehow sealed the immortality of the Heralds, granting them a constant influx of Investiture from Honor through their Connection to him.{{book ref|sa5|65}} Whenever a Desolation ended, she'd travel along with them to [[Braize]], where she suffered torture until one of them broke. She broke at least once, starting a new Desolation.{{book ref|sa3|38}} 

While on Roshar, she trained surgeons in anticipation of the upcoming Desolation.{{book ref|sa2|i|7}} Once the [[Knights Radiant]] formed, she admitted patronage of the [[Order of Edgedancers]].

Over time, she would begin to draw upon the powers of Roshar itself. What this means, however, is currently unknown.{{book ref|sa5|120}}

=== Aharietiam ===
At the time of the [[Last Desolation]], four thousand five hundred years before the Night of Sorrows, Vedel decided with the other Heralds to give up the Oathpact and leave [[Taln]] alone on Braize.{{book ref|sa3|38}} 

=== Reforging of the Oathpact ===
She returned to the Heralds for the making of the new Oathpact.{{book ref|sa5|144}} When creating this new Oathpact, Ishar claimed that he was using her method, involving Surgebinding from before Honor's restrictions and Stormlight itself.{{book ref|sa5|144}}

== Culture ==
{{image|Vedel.jpg|Vedel's chapter icon|side=left|width=150px}}

Like all the Heralds, Vedel's name has become part of culture. In [[Vorinism]], she is called '''Vedeledev''', and swearing by Vedeledev's golden keys is used to express astonishment.{{book ref|sa1|7}} "Vedeledev knows" is also used.{{book ref|sa2|67}} The name is sometimes shortened to '''Vev''' -- this is used both as an expression of its own, and as the Vorin number 4.{{book ref|sa3|68}} Names [[Vedelliar]] and [[Vevidar]] are probably derived from Vedel.

She is named the Herald of Healing, who is a master of the mind and soul and held the keys of immortality.{{book ref|sa5|55}}{{book ref|sa5|62}}

Within the context of the [[Ten Essences]], Vedel is traditionally associated with the Loving and Healing Divine Attributes, the [[Polestone|gemstone]] diamond, and the Essence of lucentia.{{book ref|sa1|part=ars}}

The golden keys that people swear by and that are present in some artwork of her{{file ref|Vedel Endpage.jpg|Vedel Endpage}} are derived from a Rosharan myth; though they have really existed, they are more symbolic than actually imbued with any power.{{wob ref|3247}} This myth calls them the "keys of immortality."{{book ref|sa5|55}}{{book ref|sa5|62}}
