'''Wayne's mother''' is an unnamed woman from the [[Roughs]] on [[Scadrial]] who raises [[Wayne]] as a child. She works as a miner in [[Tinweight Settlement]].

== Appearance and Personality ==
Wayne's mother is a woman from the Roughs. Her exact age is unknown, but she is presumably around her thirties to forties while raising Wayne. Due to her mining profession, she usually wears trousers, a buttoned shirt and boots that are consistently covered in soot. She usually wears her hair up. Due to her career in the mines, her face has a "hollowed-out" look. 

{{sidequote|Oh, Wayne. You’re so precious.|Wayne’s mother{{book ref|mb7|prologue}}|side=right|size=300px}}

As his mother, she has a great relationship with Wayne. He is able to put a genuine smile on her face even after a long and hard day. Likewise, she often tells him stories about the Roughs, which he really enjoys. She is also a little concerned about Wayne's behaviour, such as his habit of stealing and his swearing. Secretly, she worries that she isn’t a good parent to Wayne. Hearing silly voices makes her happy, and both her husband and son were good at making silly voices. She also has somewhat of a Roughs’ dialect, often using was instead of were and what instead of that.{{book ref|mb7|prologue}}

== History ==
At an unknown date, Wayne's mother was born and then met Wayne's father; together, the pair eventually conceived [[Wayne]]. Originally Wayne's father had worked in the mines of the Tinweight Settlement whilst his mother did laundry back at home, but this changed when he died in a mine collapse. Wayne's uncle, [[Gregr]], came to live with the family and worked at the mines instead. Gregr built a variety of furniture for the family to use in order to save money, but Wayne grew to greatly dislike him; he felt that his uncle didn't care about him or his mother, and it's implied that he was abusive. Gregr also died in a mining accident, leaving Wayne's mother to have to work in the mines herself. Wayne took up doing the laundry in her stead to make additional money.{{book ref|mb7|prologue}}

{{sidequote|You’re the wind. You’re the stars. You are all endless things.|A part of her favorite poem{{book ref|mb7|prologue}}|side=right|size=300px}}

One night in {{date|319|PC}}, Wayne's mother returned home to find her son having stayed up at night again. She was sad to discover that Wayne had been stealing again, and after telling him that it was not alright, she told him a story about [[Allomancer Jak]]. This was the last good memory Wayne had of his mom, as the next day another collapse happened at the mine, killing her.{{book ref|mb7|prologue}}
