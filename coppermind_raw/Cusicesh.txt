'''Cusicesh, the Protector''' is an extremely large magical entity in a bay neighboring the city of [[Kasitor]] in [[Iri]] on [[Roshar]].{{book ref|sa1|i|5}} Although Rosharans generally refer to him as a [[spren]],{{book ref|sa1|i|5}}{{book ref|sa5|i|11}} he is actually a similar spren-adjacent being, and is presumably a species of [[Splinter]].{{wob ref|16876}}

== Attributes and Abilities ==
Cusicesh is made up of more [[Investiture]] than a typical spren, although he does not have the same amount of Investiture as beings such as the [[Stormfather]] or the [[Nightwatcher]].{{wob ref|174}}{{ref|group=fn|text=Cusicesh is a Splinter,{{wob ref|16876}} but it is not known which [[Shard]] or Shards he is related to.}} He is not capable of forming a [[Nahel bond]], for reasons unrelated to his level of Investiture.{{wob ref|14475}} He is sapient and able to speak.{{book ref|sa1|i|5}}

=== Daily Appearance ===
{{image|Cusicesh the Protector by Jordi Rapture.jpeg|side=right|width=400px|Above the harbor}}
At exactly seven forty-six in the morning every day, Cusicesh rises from the waters of the bay, sending out an illusory wake. This happens without fail such that the locals can set timepieces by his occurrence. He takes the form of a hundred-foot tall column-shaped jet of water, with four long arms that extend downward from the column and form fingers and thumbs. The arms always form in the same places, and the people in the city have placed large golden pedestals where the hands come to rest. He is a translucent sea-blue color, ranging from deep blue in the center to a lighter blue on the surface. He forms a face on a stumplike neck that always looks east toward the [[Origin]]; the face shifts very rapidly into different human faces, both male and female. The faces continue to cycle for ten minutes, and Cusicesh then retreats into the water, leaving another illusory wake. Cusicesh often leaves people who witness his daily appearance with a feeling of being drained.{{book ref|sa1|i|5}}

=== Perpendicularity ===
When the time comes for the [[Iriali]] to leave Roshar, Cusicesh is able to open a [[perpendicularity]] for them; specifically, he appears to open [[Honor's Perpendicularity]]. The means by which he does so is not known, and the perpendicularity exhibits unusual behavior. It is also unclear how Cusicesh knew that the time had come for the Iriali to leave Roshar, but the [[Seventeenth Shard]] was able to predict his emergence with a high level of precision.{{book ref|sa5|i|11}}

== History ==
Cusicesh has been present in the bay for a very long time.{{wob ref|9510}} He is ingrained into Kasitor society to the point that he dictates the local system of timekeeping.{{wob ref|14638}} The people of Kasitor have constructed a viewing platform on the bay in order to provide better views of Cusicesh, and the platform can get crowded when he makes his daily appearance. Most Iriali consider Cusicesh to simply be a part of the city, although some people worship him as a god.{{book ref|sa1|i|5}}

Cusicesh's remarkable attributes gave him a level of renown in other parts of Roshar.{{book ref|sa2|24}} [[Axies the Collector]], an avid observer of spren, made a trip to Kasitor to see Cusicesh. He was fascinated by Cusicesh and was not able to categorize him with any other spren that he had encountered in his travels.{{ref|group=fn|text=Axies would have ended up correctly grouping Cusicesh with other Splinters ("spren-adjacent entities") such as [[seon]]s.{{wob ref|16876}}}} He took notes on several of Cusicesh's unique qualities.{{book ref|sa1|i|5}}

=== Long Trail ===
Shortly before [[Stormfall]], Cusicesh appeared at a different time than usual, faced his palms towards the city, and spoke directly to the Iriali in a sonorous voice. This appeared to be the first time he had exhibited any of these behaviors in the history of Iri, which by that point had existed for at least several millennia. He informed them that he would be the guide for the Fifth Journey in the [[Long Trail]], a reference to the Iriali belief that their people would need to visit seven lands before becoming [[One]].{{book ref|sa5|i|11}}

He opened a perpendicularity that looked like a column of light; [[Dyel's mother]] referred to it as "[[Honor's Perpendicularity|Honor's Gateway]]". Cusicesh instructed the denizens of Kasitor that it was time to seek the Fifth Land, and the Iriali quickly prepared to leave Roshar forever. [[Baon]], [[Galladon]], and [[Demoux]] of the [[Seventeenth Shard]] were in the city to observe; Demoux was overheard saying that the perpendicularity "behaved oddly" for one of its type.{{book ref|sa5|i|11}}

== Origin and Role ==
{{theory}}
Cusicesh accompanied the Iriali in the Fourth Land (Roshar){{book ref|sa5|i|11}} and is confirmed to be their guide specifically for the Fifth Journey.{{wob ref|16876}} This is likely the origin of his nickname as "the Protector". Cusicesh is not classified as a spren, is unlike any other being on Roshar, and possesses the ability to open a perpendicularity. It is therefore plausible that he came to Roshar from another world, like the Iriali themselves.{{wob ref|9551}} 

Golden haired people are later seen on [[Scadrial]],{{book ref|mb7|32}} implying that it may be the Fifth Land.

== Trivia ==
*When [[Shallan Davar]] first learned of the name "[[Odium]]" from [[Pattern]], she wondered if it was another unique spren like Cusicesh or the Nightwatcher.{{book ref|sa2|24}}
*In facing east towards the [[Origin]], Cusicesh must look across the continent of Roshar.{{map ref|Roshar}} The fact that observers in Kasitor can see Cusicesh's changing faces implies that at least part of the city is situated to the east of the bay.
