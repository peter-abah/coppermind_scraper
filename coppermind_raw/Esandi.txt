'''Esandi''' is a [[Dhatri]]an [[aetherbound]] who serves the [[Malwish Empire]].{{book ref|iote|47}}{{book ref|iote|48}}

==Appearance and Personality==
She has light brown skin and appears to be ethnically Dhatrian. However, she wears a Malwish uniform and appears to be a member of their military.{{book ref|iote|48}}

==Attributes and Abilities==
She is able to speak Malwish, with a noticeable accent.{{book ref|iote|48}}

One of her palms has a visible living aether bud of an unknown variety.{{book ref|iote|48}} She serves the Malwish despite some sort of restrictions on aetherbound serving [[Scadrian]]s.{{book ref|iote|48}} As of the discovery of the [[Dakwara]], she is the only aetherbound who serves the Malwish.{{book ref|iote|47}}

==History==
It is unknown how Esandi came to live on Scadrial and serve the Malwish.{{book ref|iote|47}} She was familiar with Colonel [[Dajer]], and owed him a favor related to unspecified past events.{{book ref|iote|48}}

During Dajer's mission to find a [[perpendicularity]] in the [[Drominad System]], he contacted Esandi via datapad seeking to verify information about the capabilities of an aetherbound with a cracked or disconnected bud. He also asked Esandi to show [[Illistandrista|Starling]] the living aether bud on her palm. Although Esandi cautioned Dajer that doing so revealed closely guarded state secrets, she acquiesced.{{book ref|iote|48}}

Dajer's ulterior motive in revealing Esandi's abilities was to intimidate and/or impress Starling by confirming that the Malwish had an aetherbound in their service.{{book ref|iote|47}}{{book ref|iote|48}} Starling was indeed surprised that an aetherbound could serve [[Scadrian]]s; she had previously discussed this possibility with her crew (including [[Ed]], [[Leonore]], and [[Aditil]]) and they had concluded that such service was forbidden,{{book ref|iote|46}}{{book ref|iote|48}} presumably by the [[Aether#Primal_Aethers|primal aethers]]. When Starling asked how the aethers allowed Esandi to serve the Malwish, she simply responded that there were ways around the restriction.{{book ref|iote|48}}
