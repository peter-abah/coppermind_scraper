The '''Eelakin''' are an ethnic group on [[First of the Sun]], indigenous to the [[homeisles]]. They originally came from another planet, and travelled through the [[Cognitive Realm]], before finding and settling First of the Sun.{{book ref|iote|52}}

== Culture ==
=== Ethnicity and Customs ===
The Eelakin people generally have brown skin, although the exact shade of brown differs between islands, with men from the outer isles being slightly more brown then those from the inner isles.{{book ref|iote|3}} Most Eelakin have black hair.{{book ref|iote|1}} Early Eelakin were around five and a half foot tall. {{book ref|iote|52}}

As society changed, so did the Eelakin. Homeislers began asking more questions, feeling the need to explain the world around them.{{book ref|iote|2}}  They became more and more educated, which in turn led to greater advances in science. The Eelakin naming traditions also progressed. People used to either be called one of the ancients' names, like [[Yaalani]] or [[Cakoban]], or be named after their time of birth and the number of children their parents had. For example, the seventh child of a family, who is born during the morning, might be called 'Seventh of the Morning'. 

However, names changed, with most names being regular, one worded names. Some people are still named in reference to their birth order, but instead of the time they were born, something else important, which led to names such as 'First of the Sky' and 'Second of Saplings'.{{book ref|iote|6}} Families used to be quite large, with some people having eleven or twelve children.{{book ref|iote|8}} [[Aviar]] also play a large role in Eelakin society, with almost everyone having bonded one.{{book ref|iote|7}} Many Eelakin gain Aviar as small kids.{{book ref|iote|1}} It is considered to be very weird for people to let their Aviar ride on their heads.{{book ref|iote|8}} The Eelakin also consider bowing to one another a way of showing deference.{{book ref|iote|20}} Some of the youth on the homeisles surf.{{book ref|iote|31}} It seems that at least some Eelakin are buried at sea.{{book ref|iote|9}}

=== Fashion ===
Eelakin fashion is generally quite colorful.  Historically, they have been known to wear bright dresses, wraps, skirts{{book ref|iote|1}} and loincloths.{{book ref|iote|52}} They also wore bandanas and clothed themselves after the [[Saoa]] style.{{book ref|iote|13}} Clothes were sometimes striped or had Eelakin patterns on them.{{book ref|iote|7}} Clothing was often dyed to carry the colors of the owner's village.{{book ref|iote|13}} 

However, after the arrival of the [[Malwish Empire|Ones Above]], fashion changed. Eelakin clothing began to approximate the cut and style of these Ones Above, with more businesslike blouses, jackets and slacks.{{book ref|iote|7}} As clothing became better tailored, people began wearing cargo pants, tight buttoned shirts and rugged jackets.{{book ref|iote|20}} However, a decent number of Eelakin kept wearing the wraps of their past. Due to the presence of [[Aviar]] in the Eelakin culture, many people wear shoulder pads, so that the Aviar can grip their shoulders without hurting the owner.{{book ref|iote|13}}

=== Language ===
The Eelakin also speak their own language, and as one might expect, it has undergone significant changes throughout its history. The old version of the language had similar sounds but slightly different words. Dream was said more like drem, having once rhymed with the word 'them'. In addition to this, the word 'them' was a noun you could count, like a group, whereas 'they' used to mean whatever it means right now.{{book ref|iote|24}}

While they do have a regular script, most of the Eelakin histories are recorded by the [[loremother]]s. They consider this to be more accurate, as there is a check on the loremothers' teachings, whereas anyone can write whatever they want.{{book ref|iote|47}} The ancient Eelakin also had stories of [[Daccwaga]]s.{{book ref|iote|52}} People from backwater isles are known to have slight accents.{{book ref|iote|7}} The Eelakin also have stories of people who died of dehydration after being unable to recover from a capsize.{{book ref|iote|29}}

=== Religion ===
The Eelakin worship the various islands of the [[Pantheon]]. The loremothers teach that the god [[Patji (being)|Patji]] created the world, and gave power to the [[Dakwara]], who in turn created the homeisles. After everything was good, Patji and the other gods came down from the heavens and became islands.{{book ref|iote|38}} The Eelakin burn offerings to the various islands.{{book ref|iote|2}} The loremothers are in charge of teaching religion, and they also talk of stories, such as the stories of Cakoban, Yaalani or Linyi.{{book ref|iote|3}} At least some Eelakin also view the [[glowing butterflies]] as being holy.{{book ref|iote|21}} The Eelakin have several poses to honor the gods, such as the rain acceptance pose, where one sits on the ground with their hands out to the sides and their palms up.

=== Cuisine ===
The Eelakin eat lots of fish, as it is a good way to get protein. They practice both spearfishing and regular fishing.{{book ref|iote|2}} They've even hunted deep-sea fish.{{book ref|iote|31}} They also eat a lot of pork{{book ref|iote|33}} and nuts.{{book ref|iote|44}} First of the Sun is also home to a number of fruits common to the cosmere, like [[kalofruit]].{{book ref|iote|38}} The Eelakin have been known to eat certain species of snake.{{book ref|iote|38}} While in Shadesmar, they eat skullsnake meat, which tastes somewhat similar to pork.{{book ref|iote|33}}

=== Technology ===
==== Traditions ====
While Eelakin society has changed incredibly fast, tradition is still important to them, and many people still remember how life used to be. Sailing and navigating was incredibly important to the Eelakin, and there are a number of people still practicing the old arts.{{book ref|iote|11}} They generally sailed the seas on outrigger canoes,{{book ref|iote|2}} or catamarans.{{book ref|iote|52}} 

Ancient Eelakin navigated the seas using devices such as sextants, compasses and maps. They were also trained to feel ocean currents and waves to judge their position and the location of nearby islands. An Eelakin sailor usually had a book with him, writing down his observations, such as the rising and setting of the sun, the locations of constellations and the presence of fish and the direction they swim. Some Eelakin sailors would be so busy that they'd barely sleep for almost a week, writing everything down, like the distance they've traveled.{{book ref|iote|2}} The Eelakin also use a sort of North Star for navigation, although this star isn’t in the north.{{book ref|iote|45}}

The Eelakin also used the presence of birds to find their way, as birds generally return to islands at night. They even navigated using clouds, which can have green reflections, indicating the presence of land. They made their maps from string, which was bound around wooden sticks on a board. Sailors often clip their Aviar's wings so they don't fly away.{{book ref|iote|2}}

==== Technological progress ====
While many would assume that the Eelakin only started progressing after the arrival of the Ones Above, this isn't correct. The Eelakin were already inventing new technologies at a fast pace, and the arrival of the Malwish boosted this to incredible levels. They developed new subway systems{{book ref|iote|5}} shortly before the Ones Above arrived. The Malwish gave the Eelakin people many technologies, such as cars,{{book ref|iote|1}}, highly effecient fish farms, new medicines and machines that can locate Aviar.{{book ref|iote|7}} However they refuse to be tricked into using technologies far too advanced for them to understand. Instead, they build infrastructure and progress at a pace they can comprehend.

== Politics and Government ==
=== Government ===
The Eelakin government consists of two different houses, the elected house and the traditional house. The elected house, the senate, is made up of senators,{{book ref|iote|1}} who are appointed during votes by the people. The traditional house is made up of a hundred and twenty chiefs and an unknown number of [[kingmaker]]s. The traditional house has the ability to overrule the senate.{{book ref|iote|10}}

Some duties, such as government administration, are headed by a public company, such as the [[First Company]]. The head of such a company, as decided by multiple vice presidents, also serves as the head of the government. The effort to take over government duties is a long and public process, and the people are charged with voting a company into office. It appears that the generals heading the militaries of the leading company also serve as the Eelakin's main generals.{{book ref|iote|7}} For a vote of no confidence to occur, both the traditional and elected houses need to agree.{{book ref|iote|61}} Eelakin laws regarding challenges to a man's life are fairly strict, and being challenged isn't enough reason to be allowed to kill a man.{{book ref|iote|7}} 

=== Foreign policy ===
==== Ones Above ====
The Eelakin have established extensive relations with the [[Malwish Empire]], who they call the Ones Above. The Malwish just appeared in the sky one day, and revealed themselves to the Eelakin. The two nations pretend to like each other, but both of them view the other with distaste; The Eelakin being viewed as unimportant primitives and the Malwish being viewed as evil tyrants.{{book ref|iote|20}} Before the Ones Above revealed themselves to be humans, artist renditions showed them as having black pits as faces.{{book ref|iote|3}}

The Ones Above came with aim of controling the planet First of the Sun for their war effort against the [[Roshar]]ans. This includes its profitable [[Aviar]], the rumored [[perpendicularity]] with immense strategic importence as well as its Eelakin inhabitants. However, they cannot directly conquer the planet without violating inter-galactic treaties and risking [[Shard of Adonalsium|Shardic]] involvement. To exploit the loophole in the law, the Malwish have two methods. One method is to trick them into advancing technology too rapidly, thus posing a threat, allowing them to be conquered; the other method is coaxing them into signing a deal. The Eelakin were wise of the situation and stalled their plots for 8 years, but were slowly giving in by becoming dependant on energy and medicine provided by the Malwish.{{book ref|iote|20}} Eventually, with the foundation of the Eelakin [[Navigator]]s, the Malwish were forced to stop their efforts and would need to pay for the use of the Navigators and perpendicularity fairly.{{book ref|iote|61}}

==== Rosharans ====

Like the Ones Above, the Rosharans seek to control First of the Sun and use its resources for their war effort. The Eelakin don't have extensive relations with the various Rosharan nations, but they met a [[Knights Radiant|Knight Radiant]] shortly before the Drominad perpendicularity was made public. 

This Radiant also gave them an offer: join them, and keep one out of every hundred birds born, or do not and become an enemy of Roshar.{{book ref|iote|7}} Like the other cosmere powers, the Rosharans were forced to abandon any ideas of conquest or control of the planet after the foundation of the Navigators and the binding of the [[Dakwara]].{{book ref|iote|61}}

== History ==
The Eelakin people originated on an unknown world in the cosmere, but after a time they left, travelling through Shadesmar until they found First of the Sun. The Eelakin have stories of empires, vast cities and wars with no place they could have happened on First of the Sun. It appears that a man named [[Cakoban]] was their chief or guide to this place. They entered through the perpendicularity, and likely left the Pantheon for the homeisles fairly quickly.  A few Eelakin left the rest to undertake new journeys through Shadesmar.{{book ref|iote|52}} It appears that a few Eelakin stayed behind and became [[trapper]]s.{{book ref|iote|2}}

The Eelakin lived largely uneventful lives, at least on cosmere-wide scale, on the homeisles for the next few thousand years. They started progressing technologically, inventing steam power and trains, and more modern companies. At some point, shortly after these discoveries, the Ones Above appeared. The Ones Above gave them plenty of new technologies, and Eelakin society began to change even more rapidly.{{book ref|iote|5}} The [[Northern Interests Trading Company]] was the first to make a large expedition to the Pantheon islands, seeking to find out what makes Aviar so special. During this trip, they figured out that the Ones Above were trying to get them to advance incredibly fast, so that they could conquer the Eelakin.{{book ref|iote|20}}

The next few years saw the First Company, formerly known as the Northern Interests Trading Company, rise to power, taking over government administration and power from another institution. Its president was a woman named [[Vathi]]. She led the negotiations with the Ones Above, who wanted a facility to farm the Aviar, stalling as long as she could. Eventually, she had no choice but to agree to it, with the contract giving her two months time to think of an escape.{{book ref|iote|7}} As a last measure, she granted one of the last remaining trappers, a man named [[Sixth of the Dusk]], the chance to go through [[Patji's Eye]] and seek a way out.{{book ref|iote|11}} During the next two months, while Vathi waited, government officials in both houses slowly turned against her, seeing her do nothing.{{book ref|iote|61}}

After the deadline was nearly up, Dusk returned through the perpendicularity, bringing an offworlder with him.{{book ref|iote|61}} He had bound the Dakwara, a large [[Evil|type 1-6 Threnodite entity]] and had rediscovered their ability to [[Navigator|Navigate]] Shadesmar. The First Company made a deal with the dragon [[Xisisrefliel]], who made the presence of the Navigators and the Drominad perpendicularity known throughout the cosmere. Xixis in turn gaining right of first negotiation for reduced tariffs. This led to nearly every great power taking an interest in the Eelakin, which also had the effect of forcing these great powers to pay fairly for the use of these services.{{book ref|iote|epilogue}}
