The '''Skybreaker monastery''' is a [[Monastery of Truth]] dedicated to the [[Herald]] [[Nale|Nin]] and associated with the [[Order of Skybreakers]]. It is located in the east of [[Shinovar]].{{file ref|Map_of_Shin_Monasteries.jpg}}

== Location ==
The Skybreaker monastery is located in the east of Shinovar, at the base of the [[Misted Mountains]].{{file ref|Map_of_Shin_Monasteries.jpg}} The terrain near the monastery is hilly, the windswept ground covered in scrub grasses whose petals spread out to the sides. The town where the monastery is located lies on a ridge; the ground surrounding the village walls is barren for miles. The monastery has a broken roof and a collapsed chimney, but its walls remain standing.{{book ref|sa5|108}}

== History ==
[[Szeth]] and [[Kaladin]] visited the Skybreaker monastery on their journey to [[Cleansing of Shinovar|cleanse Shinovar]] in 1175. As they approached the monastery, Kaladin told Szeth that he wants him to choose for himself, not to simply follow another's orders. Szeth realized that he could ''choose'' to fix himself.{{book ref|sa5|108}}

The two of them then entered the town containing the monastery. [[Syl]] led them into the monastery; Nin, who was also the Skybreaker [[Honorbearer]], was waiting in the entryway, hands on his [[Shardblade]]. Nin demanded that since Szeth would never be able to survive a fight against him, he should instead follow Nin's every command in the [[Bondsmith monastery]], Szeth's next and final test. Szeth said that he deserved to choose for himself. Because Szeth refused Nin's oath, Nin decided that Szeth would instead need to fight him. Szeth was determined not to fight, and he dropped his Shardblade.{{book ref|sa5|108}}

Kaladin intercepted Nin's strike with Syl, who was in the form of a spear, making himself the fighter in Szeth's place. He strategically decided to forgo using [[Stormlight]] if Nin would do the same, as all the Heralds were experts in their own [[Surgebinding|Surges]].{{book ref|sa5|108}} Over the course of several minutes, Kaladin took several hits on his [[Shardplate|Plate]], and while he was able to keep Nin at bay, he scored no hits on the Herald. As a last-ditch attempt, he tried to get Nin to remember, to care about all the innocents he killed. Nin then shattered his armor and knocked him to the ground, but just before Nin was about to strike, the [[Wind]] returned Kaladin's song.{{book ref|sa5|109}} With the Wind's help, Kaladin finally got through to Nin by telling him the story of [[Derethil]] and the ''[[Wandersail]]'', telling him that when people uphold the law, they retain responsibility for it. Nin collapsed to the ground with the realization that he could no longer -- and had always been able -- to use the law as a shield from culpability.{{book ref|sa5|110}}

After the fight, Kaladin cooked dinner for Szeth while Nin accepted the full weight of his failure. Nin told Kaladin and Szeth that [[Ishar]] was waiting at the final monastery, and the three of them set off for it.
