'''Silverlight''' is a city state in the [[cosmere]], located in the [[Cognitive Realm]].{{wob ref|2555}}{{wob ref|2520}} It is an ancient city that existed in some form long before notable cosmere events such as the [[Reod]] and the [[Catacendre]].{{wob ref|10972}} The city was once a collection of [[dragon (cosmere)|dragon]] palaces and many dragons still have a home base there, including [[Xisisrefliel]].{{book ref|iote|30}}{{wob ref|16187}} People from Silverlight are called Lighters.{{book ref|iote|30}}

It is a meeting point for [[worldhoppers]] and [[Realmatic Theory|Realmatically aware]] experts,{{wob ref|190}} and it is the base of operations for the [[Seventeenth Shard]].{{wob ref|12508}}

== Etymology ==
The name ''Silverlight'' is derived from the ''Silverlight Nexus'', the city’s greatest landmark.{{book ref|iote|30}} Similarly, the demonym ''Lighters'', seems to be derived from the city’s name.

== Geography and Appearance ==
{{image|Silverlight by Hugh Hammons.jpg|side=right|width=400px|}}
Silverlight is located entirely in the [[Cognitive Realm]]. Not much is known about its location, but the ground near the city is glassy and is known to have plants growing on it.{{book ref|iote|30}}{{book ref|iote|47}}

=== Universities ===
Silverlight is famous for its universities. Their Arcanists are aware of many esoteric facts about various planetary systems in the cosmere. This includes manifestations of [[Investiture]], the locations of [[perpendicularity|perpendicularities]], the existence of [[Splinter]]s, and the history and current status of [[Shard]]s.{{au ref|roshar}}{{au ref|scadrial}}{{au ref|sel}} They seek to learn more about cosmere mysteries that they cannot fully explain, such as the existence of a perpendicularity on [[First of the Sun]]{{au ref|drominad}} or the nature of the [[Evil]] on [[Threnody]].{{au ref|threnody}} As part of their research, they travel throughout the cosmere investigating unusual events and situations,{{au ref|drominad}} although some people prefer to avoid the more dangerous fields of study.{{wob ref|14129}} The universities are in contact with the [[Ire]], which was for a long time one of the most cosmere-knowledgeable groups in existence, but the Ire are not always willing to collaborate with Silverlight's scholars.{{au ref|Sel}} Khriss was involved in the founding of at least one of them.{{wob ref|8342}} 

Silverlight has produced a number of guide books for the various worlds in the cosmere. These were often written with great difficulty due to how hard it was to get from world-to-world before space travel. Among these is a survey book that has the descriptions of a number of worlds in one volume. One such survey book was in the possession of a [[Thaylenah|Thaylen]] worldhopper who was killed by the [[Cinder King]] on [[Canticle]].{{book ref|tsm|16}} Arcanists sign a contract with the universities, which makes their research property of the university.{{book ref|iote|30}} Meanwhile, non-researchers have also been known to sign a contract with the universities. They fund them, and in turn, they get first rights to everything created or discovered by the Arcanists.{{book ref|iote|32}}

=== Silverlight Nexus ===
Silverlight has its own [[perpendicularity]], known as the Silverlight Nexus. In the Cognitive Realm, the perpendicularity appears like a sun. It is colored a frosty white, and hangs in the void beneath the city, lighting everything from underneath with a calm, white glow. The Nexus is about as large as the city itself, making it the largest perpendicularity by far. It is also unique among perpendicularities in that it leads to three different places in the Physical Realm, rather then one. One of these is the greatstar of [[Yolen]]. The powerful Nexus of Silverlight is also the cause of the time dilation in Silverlight. Each day spent in the city passes as ten for those on the outside, which can cause problems with relationships if one stays in Silverlight too long. The perpendicularity is available for travelers through holes in the glassy ground.{{book ref|iote|30}}

== Culture ==
=== Lighters ===
The inhabitants of Silverlight are called ''Lighters''. Most Lighters have lived in Silverlight for generations, but many of them still retain some part of their ancestry. The city is home to people from dozens of planets, and many of them aren’t human.{{book ref|iote|30}}{{book ref|iote|32}}{{book ref|iote|28}} Residents of Silverlight are known to use [[Silver#Threnodite_Chains|silver chains]] that originate on Threnody,{{wob ref|12843}} possibly to anchor themselves through [[Cognitive anomaly|Cognitive anomalies]] while traveling to different planetary systems.{{book ref|sa4|64}} Due to the diverse knowledge of its residents, Silverlight has the best parties in the cosmere.{{wob ref|14599}} Silverlight has its own recognizable accent.{{book ref|mb7|epilogue|1}}

=== Architecture ===
Like any settlement that has existed as long as it has, Silverlight has experienced many different architectural styles. By the Space Age, the city’s architectural aesthetic is to build tall instead of wide: with many willowy skyscrapers of a limber design, using [[dragonsteel (metal)|dragonsteel]] supports—ultralight and extremely strong, allowing for constructions that go much higher than otherwise would have been possible. Walkways crisscross them, and many have hovering pods as rooms, hanging out over the expanse.{{book ref|iote|30}} Many of the buildings owned by [[dragon (cosmere)|dragons]] started as dens, then became palaces and ended up as skyscrapers.{{book ref|iote|32}}

=== Laws ===
Silverlight appears to have its own jurisdiction and laws. They are known to have played a large role in the development of interplanetary laws and treaties, such as the [[Silverlight Codes of Interplanetary Conduct]]. These codes include the Refugee and Lost Expatriate Bill, which forces interplanetary travelers to grant asylum under unspecified circumstances.{{book ref|tsm|43}} The Silverlight courts are considered to be just. [[Hoid|Cephandrius Maxtori]] is a recognised outlaw by Silverlight laws.{{book ref|iote|28}} Slavery is outlawed in Silverlight.{{book ref|iote|32}} There are also a number of accords about the treatment of prisoners called the ''Silverlight Accords''. They do not protect the dead.{{book ref|iote|48}} By law some artifacts, like those of the Iriali describing their travels, belong to the people who created them themselves, and any sale or transfer of such an artifact is forbidden. It can only be directly gifted to another.{{book ref|iote|30}}

=== Economy ===
[[File:Silverlight_Mercantile.svg|thumb|right|100px]]
Silverlight has a large and well running economy. They have a mercantile company, known as [[Silverlight Mercantile]], that is known to act as an interplanetary postal service, making use of mysterious [[Messengers|messengers]] that can trace [[Connection]] to find anyone, no matter where they are.{{book ref|mb7|epilogue|6}} Little more is known about it beyond this function. [[Harmony]] used them in order to send [[MeLaan]] a message concerning [[Wayne]]'s passing.{{book ref|mb7|epilogue|6}}

Silverlight also has its own film industry which uses false facades to imitate building fronts.{{book ref|tsm|35}} The city is also a hub for the trading of information.{{book ref|iote|32}} Silverlight is relatively safe, but it is still a big city and docking fees include only minimal security. For that reason, many people choose to leave a guard behind on their ship.{{book ref|iote|30}} Agriculture is a little different in Silverlight. Plants here grow by the light of the Silverlight Nexus, the city’s perpendicularity. Thus, they need to be exposed to the Nexus’ light and tones.{{book ref|iote|47}} The city is a metropolis, and traffic can be a mess. Generally though, flight control keeps it organized.{{book ref|iote|30}}

== History ==
Silverlight has a long history, going back millennia before the Space Age. The city started out as a bunch of dragon palaces. However, it soon became one of the first multicultural settlements in the cosmere. A few decades before the discovery of [[Patji's Eye]], the dragons revealed that Yolen was the homeworld of the [[Shard]]s, and that it was accessible via Silverlight’s perpendicularity.{{book ref|iote|30}} This caused people to want to visit the place, and soon there were long waitlists.{{book ref|iote|25}} Silverlight is a politically strong and important power, and even the Malwish don’t dare bully them.{{book ref|iote|23}}
