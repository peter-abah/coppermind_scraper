'''Rall Elorim''' is a city in the mountains of northern [[Iri]] on Roshar{{map ref|Roshar}} and the most populous city in the nation.{{book ref|sa1|i|5}} It is also called the '''City of Shadows''' because of its location under a mountainous overhang, which ensures that the city lies shrouded in shadow for most of the day. Due to the [[Iriali]] tendency to congregate, the city is considered to be of a fairly impressive size for such a small kingdom.{{rpg ref|slwg|154}} Rall Elorim also contains an [[Oathgate]].{{book ref|sa3|8}} 

==Geography==
====Location====
The city is located in the extreme northwest of Roshar. It is situated at the north end of a small mountain range, on a peninsula between the [[Reshi Sea]] and the [[Northern Depths]].{{map ref|Roshar}} The city also looks to lay next to a river, leading north into the ocean.{{rpg ref|slwg|156}} [[Feverstone Keep]] was located near Rall Elorim.{{epigraph ref|sa3|84}}{{rpg ref|slwg|155}} Perhaps due to the city's remote location, people from other parts of Roshar, such as [[Gawx]] and [[Kaladin]], have heard tales that characterize it as an exotic, mysterious land.{{book ref|sa2|I|9}}{{book ref|sa1|16}}

====Climate and Ecology====
Like the rest of Iri, Rall Elorim has a warm climate, and enjoys a lot of rain.{{map ref|Roshar}} The mountainous overhang above the city and the spray from the waterfalls create a rich environment for plant life, leaving most of the city draped in vines and slick with algae.{{rpg ref|slwg|154}} The citizens of Rall Elorim farm some of the banks along the canals, and groves of rare plants grow on top of the [[Stormfalls]]. These canals are also home to [[Khornak]]s. The seas north of Rall Elorim are filled with [[greatshell]]s.{{rpg ref|slwg|155}}

==Structure==
===Architectural Styles===
Rall Elorim is truly ancient, originally built as one of the [[Dawncities]]. It has changed hands many times during the Desolations, which has given the city uniquely varied architecture tracing back to both humans and singers. After the Iriali took up residence in Rall Elorim, the city became known for its myriad shrines and temples to celebrate the [[Iriali#Religion|One]].{{rpg ref|slwg|154}} The inhabitants of Rall Elorim prefer ceramic sphere lamps to light their city, as they give off a much calmer light then their glass equivalent.{{rpg ref|slwg|157}} The city also has a boat lift to lower boats into the river upon which it lies.{{rpg ref|slwg|156}}

Most striking are perhaps the thousands of prayer statues along every street and canal, each celebrating a unique facet of human or even singer experience.{{rpg ref|slwg|154}} These statues are mostly human, but some of them also resemble animals or spren. They are rarely painted; most of them are of the same cremlike color, which supposedly alludes to their One-ness.{{rpg ref|slwg|157}} The statues are made by sculptors, who at times ask strangers to put their personal touch onto one, likely to make the statue a unique experience.{{rpg ref|slwg|155}}

===Landmarks===
====Dirgehollow====
At the base of the overhanging mountain where the streets are in near-constant darkness, the mostly lawless district known as the Dirgehollow collects the poor and downtrodden. The district earns its name and reputation from countless stories of hauntings and terrible visions. Enigmatic [[shadowspren]], wailing songs, and wandering figures all feature in these tales. Many beggars and urchins are afraid to stay too long on the streets of the Hollow, but they often have little choice. The stories reflect a tragic truth: The crypts beneath the Dirgehollow host the [[gemheart]]s of [[shadowform]] singers created by [[Ba-Ado-Mishram]] during the [[False Desolation]]. Trapped between the Cognitive and Physical Realms, these incorporeal singers have suffered imprisonment for millennia. In the Dirgehollow, the anti-Fused resistance enjoyed relatively free rein,{{rpg ref|slwg|155}} until they were driven out of their safehouse by [[Dymab]].{{rpg ref|slwg|156}}

====Elorim Reservoir====
At the heart of Rall Elorim, a perfectly circular reservoir collects water from the [[Stormfalls]], ready for distribution to the region’s canals. A stone bridge runs to the reservoir’s center, where a weathered temple honors the One. This central platform is the site of Rall Elorim’s [[Oathgate]], and the temple is its control building. The reservoir also plays a large role in the [[Vaporlight Festival]]. The city dwellers board rafts, sing and dance together while donning elaborate vaporspren costumes in myriad colors.{{rpg ref|slwg|155}}

====Feverstone Road====
The city is also home to Feverstone Road, which is named after a [[Feverstone Keep|nearby keep]]. Feverstone Road once cut a clean pass through the mountains, however, since the Recreance, Feverstone Road has gradually fallen to ruin, lost to memory and long buried by crem. Nevertheless, Iriali archaeologists argue over cryptic historical references to a “betrayer’s path” beginning somewhere in Rall Elorim. Daring treasure hunters also sometimes launch expeditions to map the road, chasing rumors of buried Shards. However, many claim the pursuit is foolish, and some stories suggest that any Shards are kept in the clutches of jealous [[Spren#deathspren|deathspren]].{{rpg ref|slwg|155}}

====Stormfalls====
Rall Elorim is home to the [[Stormfalls]], a number of waterfalls created by the mountain situated above the city. The Stormfalls have a great impact on the culture and ecology of the city. Climbing the crest of the Stormfalls is said to be notoriously dangerous, especially with the unpredictable storm surges. However, weary tidereaders and Truthwatchers have been known to seek peaceful meditation at the brink of the roaring waterfalls.{{rpg ref|slwg|155}}

====The Trail of Stars====
The Trail of Stars is a carefully kept oral tradition spoken among many of Rall Elorim’s shoemakers. Its countless stories teach the importance of taking the correct steps along your unique life’s journey, keeping vigilant for signs along the path, and finding the proper footwear for the next leg of the Trail. Elements of the Trail’s eclectic tales have become embedded throughout the architecture of the city for those who know where to look. Out-of-place prayer statues, glyphs with strange flourishes, and arches adorned in peculiar metals might all belong to the Trail.{{rpg ref|slwg|155}}

====The Vaults====
Below the Dirgehollow district lies a maze of tunnels and caves. They host hundreds of tombs and mausoleums dating back all the way to the earliest settlement of the city. Though a haven for historians, the tunnels are difficult to navigate and cave-ins are common. Somewhere in the deepest reaches, the Herald [[Pailiah]] keeps a vault of treasured artifacts,{{rpg ref|slwg|155}} and her [[Eyes of Pala|Eyes]] have made their base.{{rpg ref|slsw|5}}

==Government==
===Ruling council===
For most of its history, under Iriali rule, the city’s royal palace hosted the [[Iri#Government|Iriali Triumvirate]]. They also ruled the city, with each of the monarchs having different things to manage. During the [[True Desolation]], the Iriali Triumvirate consisted of two kings and a queen. These monarchs made the difficult decision of making peace with Odium. As part of this peace, the monarchs have been joined by one Regal singer, named [[Imalei]], and two Fused, forming a ruling council. These singers also hold royal titles, but to maintain existing relationships, the human monarchs handle foreign relations.{{rpg ref|slwg|154}}

The Regal singer, Imalei, serves mostly as a public speaker and go-between for the Fused.{{rpg ref|slwg|154}} The other two singer monarchs are a malen [[Fused|Deepest One]] named [[Dymab]], who has made a mission of eliminating the resistance in “his” city, and [[Lilinum]], a fierce femalen [[Fused|Devastating One]] with her own agenda.{{rpg ref|slwg|155}}

==Citizens==
It is not entirely clear what people founded and first inhabited Rall Elorim, but it is believed to have been the [[Dawnsinger]]s. However, they were at some point driven from Rall Elorim, and the city was inhabited by another people, who were there before the Iriali. This could be the people from the Iri Plains, a separate ethnic group inside the nation of Iri.{{book ref|sa2|54}} After their arrival however, the Iriali rose to become the nation’s, and the city’s, most populous ethnicity.{{rpg ref|slwg|154}} The city has also often been inhabited by singers, during the [[Desolation]]s, the city changed hands numerous times and during the [[Era of Solitude]] the Iriali kept Parshmen slaves.{{rpg ref|slwg|155}} 

During the True Desolation, many Iriali welcomed the singers and [[Fused]], and the government made peace with Odium. Not all Iriali liked having made peace with the Fused however, and several resistance groups formed, the most prominent of which was located in Rall Elorim itself and consisted of both humans and singers.{{rpg ref|slwg|154}} The Iriali inhabitants celebrate the [[Vaporlight Festival]].{{rpg ref|slwg|155}}

==History==
===Early history===
Rall Elorim was founded very early on in Roshar’s known history. It is not entirely clear, but as a [[Dawncity]], it is thought that Rall Elorim was founded and first inhabited by the [[Dawnsinger]]s.{{rpg ref|slwg|154}}{{book ref|sa1|54}} At some point in the city’s history, the singers were driven away and another people took up residence here. It is unclear what people this was, although the separate ethnic group from the Iri plains is a likely candidate,{{book ref|sa2|54}} Some Riran historians believe that it were their ancestors who came to inhabite the city.{{rpg ref|slwg|53}} Rall Elorim changed hands many times during the Desolations, which has had its effect on the city’s architecture and culture.{{rpg ref|slwg|154}} At some point during the Desolations, the Iriali arrived on Roshar and took up residence in Rall Elorim, crowding out the other peoples.{{book ref|sa3|107}}

===Era of Solitude===
Rall Elorim flourished during the Era of Solitude, becoming one of Roshar’s greatest cities, and stories of Rall Elorim spread across the continent.{{book ref|sa1|16}} During the [[False Desolation]], the [[singers]], under [[Ba-Ado-Mishram]], were interested in either controlling the city itself, or something near Rall Elorim,{{epigraph ref|sa3|84}} and there was a major offensive near [[Feverstone Keep]], which was in turn not far from Rall Elorim. The singers were defeated however, and the Radiants abandoned their oaths shortly after, a number of them not far from Rall Elorim.{{book ref|sa1|52}} At some point near the tail end of the Era of Solitude, a new Iriali Triumvirate was appointed, with two kings and a single queen.{{rpg ref|slwg|154}} [[Lift]] also grew up in Rall Elorim, and recalled terrible memories of her time there.{{book ref|sa2|I|9}}

===True Desolation===
After the Everstorm was summoned, the rulers of Rall Elorim agreed to a peace deal with Odium. Immediately, three new monarchs were appointed, bringing the total up to six. Shortly after, a resistance of both singers and humans formed, trying to remove the Fused from the city.{{rpg ref|slwg|154}} During the True Desolation, the Iriali rediscovered a chamber containing nine bas-reliefs, made of stone with inlaid obsidian and gemstones, each picturing a different [[Unmade]]. The gemstones, once infused with [[Stormlight]], light up after a similar style to some bas-reliefs found in [[Urithiru]].{{rpg ref|slwg|96}}

Rall Elorim’s treaty with Odium deeply concerned the Coalition of monarchs as the city's Oathgate had a great deal of strategic value.{{book ref|sa3|107}} Meanwhile, in the city, the singer King Dymab drove the resistance out of their Dirgehollow hideaway, and while they escaped, the resistance needed a new safehouse, ideally near the palace.{{rpg ref|slwg|156}} It is likely that during the continuation of the Iriali Long Trail in 1175, a large portal was created near Rall Elorim. [[Cusicesh the Protector]] guided the Iriali through it, and towards another planet. It is unknown if only the Iriali themselves came, or if some singers from Iri also joined them.{{book ref|sa5|i|11}}
