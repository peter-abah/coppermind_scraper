General '''Kinal''' is the ranking officer at the [[Stoneward monastery]] in southern [[Shinovar]] on [[Roshar]].{{book ref|sa5|57}}

== Appearance and Personality ==
Kinal is a large man with darker skin. He is likely middle-aged, as he has a graying beard. He is generally impassive and does not speak much, giving him a dangerous air.{{book ref|sa5|57}}

== History ==
In {{Rosharan date|1152}}, [[Szeth]] was at the the [[Stoneward]] monastery training camp, and General Kinal was its leader. Szeth trusted Kinal and believed that he knew what he was doing. [[Neturo]] was also at the camp, and had risen through the ranks to become Kinal's aide-de-camp, implementing new policies that significantly increased morale. Kinal reported these improvements to the [[Honorbearer]]s, prompting a visit from [[Pozen-son-Nash]] and [[Sivi-daughter-Sivi]]. Kinal was unusually animated while speaking with them about Neturo, and he also praised Szeth. After speaking with Szeth about the internal conflict that he felt when fighting, the Honorbearers instructed Kinal to assign Szeth to defend against the next raid, keeping him in the rear but giving him experience seeing violence and death.{{book ref|sa5|57}}

By {{Rosharan date|1157}}, Szeth had become a seasoned warrior. He still followed the General's orders, although he was also being strongly influenced by [[Ishar|the Voice]]. While defending against stonewalker raiders from [[Steen]], he had his men burn their ships and he personally stabbed two captives. Szeth was chastised by Kinal and Neturo for going too far, and Kinal planned to consult his superiors to decide on a punishment. Szeth was left confused, since the Voice had counseled him that he was simply taking initiative while technically following his orders.{{book ref|sa5|59}} 

A short time later, Kinal was part of a tribunal that included Neturo, [[Rit-daughter-Clutio]], the [[Farmer]], and several Honorbearers. Kinal regretfully announced that Szeth was to be decommissioned and sent to work hard labor in the mines. However, he was overruled by the Farmer, who planned to give Szeth a much lighter sentence. The Honorbearers then intervened on Szeth's behalf, spurred to action by the Voice. When Pozen announced that Szeth was actually a hero and would be trained as an acolyte, Kinal was stunned and confused.{{book ref|sa5|68}}
