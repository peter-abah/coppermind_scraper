'''Ealstan''' is the [[thegn]] of [[Stenford]],{{book ref|frugal|5}} and after defeating [[Ulric Stromfin]],{{book ref|fwh|36}}, he becomes the new earl of [[Maelport]].{{book ref|fwh|epilogue}}

== Appearance and Personality ==
{{quote|But man, he looked like he could have gotten into a boxing match with a boulder, and won.|John's opinion of Ealstan{{book ref|fwh|4}}}}
Ealstan looks as if he is in his midforties, and at around six-foot-four in height, he towers over others. He is very muscular, and his hair is streaked with grey, as is his beard. He has all of his teeth, and for someone living in a time before dentists, [[John West]] believes Ealstan's smile isn't half bad.{{book ref|fwh|4}} Ealstan wears a tunic with loose, baggy trousers, along with a vibrant orange cloak.{{book ref|fwh|1}}

Ealstan is a man of honor and integrity, with a deep sense of duty.{{book ref|fwh|10}} He cares strongly for the people under his protection, and would give his life to protect them if needed.{{book ref|fwh|33}} While he is naive about the types of modern language and technology used by John West and the other [[Ulric Stromfin|outlanders]],{{book ref|fwh|4}}{{book ref|fwh|12}} he is not afraid of them.{{book ref|fwh|36}} Ealstan hates bows, and prefers hand-to-hand combat, with his axe being his weapon of choice.{{book ref|fwh|12}}{{book ref|fwh|30}}

== History == 
===Ulric Visits Stenford===
{{quote|You forget your place. I will send word to the earl. You personally do not need to be informed of anything.|Ealstan to Ulric{{book ref|fwh|7}}}}
Ealstan was among the first to inspect the burn mark left in a field outside of [[Stenford]], after [[John West]] arrived in the [[Earth (Frugal Wizard)|dimension]]. Unsure of what caused the mark, Ealstan ordered [[Hild]] to fetch [[Sefawynn]] as he believed they would be in need of her services as a [[skop]] that night.{{book ref|fwh|1}}

After [[Ulric Stromfin]] and [[Quinn Jericho]] arrived in Stenford later that evening, Ealstan took the pair to inspect the burn mark in the field.{{book ref|fwh|3}} Upon returning, he was fetched by [[Wyrm]] and brought to where he and [[Sefawynn]] were holding [[John West]] hostage. After deciding that John must be an [[aelv]], Ealstan and Wyrm retreated to the meeting hall to talk some more with Ulric and Quinn.{{book ref|fwh|4}} After they finished their conversation, Ulric and Quinn started to leave, and they asked Ealstan to let them know if he saw or heard about any more strange occurrences. Ealstan wasn't impressed by the two strangers giving him orders, and told Ulric to remember his place. This incensed Ulric, who took the opportunity to demonstrate his might by shooting [[Oswald]] and killing him. All that was left where Oswald had been standing were his booted feet. Ulric then indicated to Quinn to take Wyrm [[Bearn-gisel|hostage]], as Ulric had mistaken him as being Ealstan's son. The two men then left with Wyrm, and headed to [[Wellbury]].{{book ref|fwh|7}}

Ealstan readied some horses for the mission to rescue Wyrm. He felt a duty to accompany Sefawynn on the journey, as Wyrm had been taken under his watch, and Ealstan was Stenford's best soldier. After [[Rowena]] brought Ealstan his shield and axe, he allowed Sefawynn to lead the way, and they set out from Stenford.{{book ref|fwh|10}}

===The Rescue of Wyrm===
Ealstan accompanied [[Sefawynn]] and [[John West]] on the mission to rescue [[Wyrm]].{{book ref|fwh|11}} After a brief detour, and a tense encounter with some [[Hordamen]], the three unexpectedly rescued [[Yazad (Frugal Wizard)|Yazad]]. Yazad invited them back to his preserve to rest and recover,{{book ref|fwh|15}} before they set out for [[Wellbury]] the next day.{{book ref|fwh|19}}

Once in Wellbury, while John was distracting [[Wealdsig]], Ealstan and Sefawynn snuck in and rescued Wyrm from the pit. However, they were spotted, and the soldiers of Wellbury began to attack Ealstan. He kept Sefawynn and Wyrm behind him, as he fended off soldiers with his axe. An archer shot Ealstan in the gut, the arrow lodging itself deep inside, and Ealstan stumbled as blood started to pour out of the wound. John used his augments to try to intimidate and convince Wealdsig and his soldiers to back down. However, Wealdsig was unfazed, so John instead cut his palm and transferred some of his [[nanites]] to Ealstan's wound. Ealstan was healed, which cowed and awed Wealdsig. The [[midfather]] was then convinced to work with the rescue party against Ulric, and he let them go free with Wyrm.{{book ref|fwh|22}} 

===Hunting Ulric===
{{quote|They didn't have bows.|Ealstan, on how he managed to beat two fully armed men with plating augments{{book ref|fwh|36}}}}
After leaving [[Wyrm]] in the care of [[Yazad (Frugal Wizard)|Yazad]] at the preserve, Ealstan, [[Sefawynn]], [[John West|John]], and [[Thokk]] set out for [[Maelport]] to find and stop [[Ulric Stromfin|Ulric]]. Along the way, the encountered [[Ryan Chu]] and his group of rebel fighters.{{book ref|fwh|24}} Ryan agreed to work with them, and together they made their way to the earl's seat of Maelport.{{book ref|fwh|30}}

In Maelport, they found Ulric. He had killed [[Alwin|the earl]] before they arrived. The town was also being attacked by [[Hordamen]]. While there, Ealstan managed to take down two fully armed men with plating augments, dismember one of their arms, use the arm to activate that man's gun, and he killed Ulric with it.{{book ref|fwh|36}} He became known as an [[aelv|aelv-slayer]], and after the Hordamen were defeated, he was made the new earl of Maelport.{{book ref|fwh|epilogue}}

== Relationships ==
===Rowena===
{{quote|I like my wife. It makes perfect sense to use her name for something else I like.|Ealstan admits he named his axe after his wife{{book ref|fwh|30}}}}
Ealstan has a warm and affectionate relationship with his wife, Rowena.{{book ref|fwh|10}} He is protective of her,{{book ref|fwh|7}} and he isn't shy to admit that he likes her so much, he named his axe after her.{{book ref|fwh|30}} He also trusts her with the defence of Stenford in his absence.{{book ref|fwh|13}}
