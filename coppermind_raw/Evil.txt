The '''Evil''', also referred to as a '''Type 1-6 Threnodite entity''', '''Nephilim''', or '''Eidolith''', are [[Splinter]]s of [[Ambition]] that live on [[Threnody]].{{book ref|iote|39}}

== Appearance and Characteristics ==
{{quote
|You don’t just want me dead. You want me broken and terrified first. Nazh is right—and for once, Ed is wrong. You’re not just some force of nature… You’re evil.
|[[Starling]] on the Evil{{book ref|iote|27}}
}}

The Evil is formed by entities of almost pure [[Investiture#Anti-Investiture|anti-Investiture]], caused by the Splintering of Ambition.{{book ref|iote|25}} They can be influenced by the thoughts of mortals, though this functions differently when they are close to the [[Grand Knell]].{{book ref|iote|25}}{{book ref|iote|54}} Entities tend to stay away from populated areas to avoid this.{{book ref|iote|25}}{{book ref|iote|35}}

They are attracted to Investiture and seek it out, apparently as prey.{{book ref|iote|52}}{{book ref|iote|25}} They appear in Shadesmar as large translucent white beings, moving in a strange juddering manner and leaving afterimages behind as they move.{{book ref|iote|26}}{{book ref|tsm|15}} They are vaguely humanoid, with many limbs and lacking eyes.{{book ref|iote|27}} They tend to stay close to the Threnodite subastral, near the [[Grand Knell]].{{book ref|iote|25}}

== Powers and Abilities ==
Due to their anti-Investiture nature, the Evil are capable of killing even highly [[Investiture|Invested]] beings such as [[Dragon (cosmere)|dragons]].{{book ref|iote|25}}{{book ref|iote|26}} When touching others, they will quickly dissolve into a black mist.{{book ref|iote|54}} They are able to grow hundreds of extra arms when needed.{{book ref|iote|26}} They are able to show people visions when others look into their eye sockets.{{book ref|iote|27}}

Certain manifestations of Investiture, as well as some technology, tend to fail when entities are nearby, such as [[Feruchemy|Feruchemical]] healing or wireless signals.{{book ref|iote|39}}{{book ref|iote|43}} They are resistant to most conventional and Invested weapons.{{book ref|iote|26}}{{book ref|iote|54}} Entities are strong enough to easily break through [[Allomancy|Allomantic]] bullet shields, [[Awakening|Awakened]] deflection shields, or [[AonDor|Aonic]] force fields.{{book ref|iote|39}} However, some Arcanists have had luck overfeeding the things by hitting them with Investiture.{{book ref|iote|40}}

== History ==

Before the Splintering of [[Ambition]], over 7700 standard years before the [[True Desolation]],{{book ref|sa4|14}}{{book ref|sa4|76}}{{wob ref|8928}} the Evil did not exist in the state it would later become; as it was warped by the clash between [[Odium]] and Ambition.{{book ref|iote|25}}{{book ref|iote|54}}{{wob ref|16584}}

Sometime after the people of Threnody discovered the continent that contained the [[Forests of Hell]], an unknown cataclysm occurred in their Homeland.{{book ref|sfs}} A being or force referred to only as the Evil appeared and somehow managed to kill the majority of people living on their continent. The survivors were desperate enough to flee across the ocean to the Forests that they had once believed to be hell, where the [[Forescout]]s had already begun to settle. The Forescouts helped care for the refugees and acclimatize them to their new home, coexisting with the [[shade]]s and following the [[Simple Rules]]. Much of the government, cultures, and technology of the old continent, what was now called the [[Fallen World]] was lost. Ideals such as justice, kindness, or moral living became things of the past, worship of the [[God Beyond]] became less common, and much knowledge, such as the use of gunpowder, was nearly forgotten in the Forests. The people who survived in the Forests were a harsh, hardy people that focused more on practicalities and less on things such as love and family.

Despite the fact that less than a century had passed since the Evil made its appearance on Threnody, the true nature of the Evil had already become surrounded by myths and legends by the time that [[Silence Montane]] ran her [[Silence's waystop|waystop]].{{book ref|sfs}} Some of the [[Forescout]]s, such as [[Silence's grandmother]] believed that one day they would be the ones to destroy the Evil. Others, who told stories about the [[White Fox]], said that he had been to the Fallen World and shaken hands with the Evil there.

As of the rebellion against the [[Cinder King]], the Evil was still on Threnody,{{book ref|tsm|15}} though others have long since started wandering the Cognitive Realm.{{book ref|iote|27}}{{book ref|iote|52}}
