'''Marat''' is a small kingdom in southern [[Roshar]].{{map ref|Roshar}} It is part of the [[Makabak]] region.{{book ref|sa3|65}}

== Geography and Ecology ==
Marat borders the [[Southern Depths]] to the south, [[Tukar]] to the west, [[Emul]] to the northwest, and [[Greater Hexi]] to the east. The northernmost part of Marat includes the southernmost part of mountain range in the center of the continent. A river separates the border between Marat and Tukar.{{map ref|Roshar}} A grain similar to wheat that pulls down into burrows grows in Marat. Disc-like creatures with claws eat the grain then leave droppings with seeds to the east. The seeds grow into polyps, then blow back west to grow into grain.{{book ref|sa3|106}} 

Despite its low population,{{book ref|sa3|27}} Marat has a few notable settlements. Along the Southern Depths is the city of [[Rossen Dar]] and the towns of [[Jabom]] and [[Ja Dran]]. Near Tukar's border, there is the town of [[Khrishji]], and close to Hexi there is the town of [[Mikhan]].{{map ref|Eastern Makabak}}

Southern Marat has a notably low population, even compared to the rest of the area.{{book ref|sa3|27}}

It has an area of around 524,000km<sup>2</sup>.{{url ref|url=https://www.17thshard.com/forum/blogs/entry/634-roshar-physical-characteristics-and-areas/|text=Roshar Physical Characteristics And Areas|site=17th Shard}}

== History ==
=== Silver Kingdoms Period ===
During the Silver Kingdoms period of Roshar, the territory that would come to be known as Marat was part of [[Makabakam]].

Eventually, Makabakam collapsed and, at an unknown point, Marat formed within its former territory.  

=== Age of Solitude ===
Over the Age of Solitude, Marat became famous for its furniture, rugs, and bazaars. Due to this, they became a center of lucrative trade and commonly exported their products to nations across Roshar, including Alethkar.{{book ref|sa3.5|6}}{{book ref|sa1|26}}{{book ref|sa3|i|12}}{{book ref|sa3|122}}

[[Szeth]] visited the country during his exile.{{book ref|sa3|106}}

=== True Desolation ===
During the [[True Desolation]], one of the first moves of the [[Fused]] was to gather a large force of [[singer]]s in southern Marat.{{book ref|sa3|27}} During their conquest, the singers pressed any survivors into labor at docks but allowed them to burn their dead.{{book ref|sa3|106}} The Fused amassed a large naval fleet of over a hundred ships in the Marat ports and coves in preparation to launch an attack at [[Thaylen City]].{{book ref|sa3|109}}{{book ref|sa3|111}} In the week before the assault, [[Venli]] lived in a cave and told the story of the [[listener]]s to the singers to prepare them to fight.{{book ref|sa3|i|12}} The fleet left for Thaylen City during the surprise [[Everstorm]] and returned after Odium's defeat.{{book ref|sa3|121}}

[[Nale]] brought [[Szeth]] and the other [[Skybreakers]] there to tell them the truth of the [[First Desolation]].{{book ref|sa3|106}}

== Politics ==
Marat is a tribal nation. Each city in the country is run by a leader referred to as elder brother, analogous to the [[highprince]]s of eastern nations. Marat's only contribution in global politics is its role as a waystop between [[Vorin]] and [[Makabaki]] kingdoms.{{book ref|sa3|106}} Marat has a low population compared to other countries, especially in its southern reaches.{{book ref|sa3|27}} Some people in the east, including [[Taravangian]], consider the Marati people to be barbarians.{{book ref|sa3|24}} 

=== International Relationships ===
==== Alethkar ====
The Marati people have a stable trade relationship with Alethkar, exporting furniture and rugs.{{book ref|sa1|26}}{{book ref|sa3|i|12}}{{book ref|sa3|122}} 

==== Tukar ====
They appear to have a tense relationship with Tukar, with [[Szeth]] speculating that the Tukari might abandon their war on [[Emul]] to attack Marat.{{book ref|sa3|106}} The nature of this relationship, beyond the tension within it, is currently unknown.

== Culture ==
=== People ===
West of Marat, women's hands are not covered for modesty.{{book ref|sa2|31}} Their clothes have colours similar to Azish patterns. The cloth is thicker and coarser then Azish clothing. Their outfits are also more enveloping then Azish clothing and have braided tassels that hang low from the waist. This style is common in southeastern Makabak.{{book ref|sa4|30}}

The Marati are described as a rather proud people, like you would find in any other nation across Roshar.{{book ref|sa3|106}} Marati people are typically brown-skinned with black hair, much like the nearby Tukari.{{book ref|sa3|27}}{{book ref|sa4|30}} They have many customs, some of which are tribe- specific, while others are universal.{{rpg ref|slwg|42}}

=== Economy ===
Marat has grand bazaars that attract merchants looking for lucrative trade.{{book ref|sa3.5|6}} Some of Marat's exports are fine textiles fine furniture and rugs.{{rpg ref|slwg|42}}{{book ref|sa1|26}}{{book ref|sa3|i|12}}{{book ref|sa3|122}} [[Highprince]] [[Dalinar Kholin]] used some to decorate his barracks.{{book ref|sa1|26}} [[Queen Fen]] gave [[Shallan]] a fine Marati rug as a wedding gift.{{book ref|sa3|122}} [[Mraize]] owns rugs from Marat.{{book ref|sa3|i|12}} Families pass down auspicious patterns that become more intricate with each generation. If a family includes rugmakers or carpenters, they integrate their ancestral pattern into each piece they make.{{rpg ref|slwg|42}}
