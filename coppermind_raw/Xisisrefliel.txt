'''Xisisrefliel''', also known as '''Xisis''' or '''Foil''' for short, is a [[dragon (cosmere)|dragon]], originally from [[Yolen]], who for a time lived on [[Lumar]] in the [[Crimson Sea]].{{wob ref|16606}}{{wob ref|14133}}{{book ref|tress|31}}{{wob ref|16182}} Because of this, the sea became known as 'the domain of the dragon.'{{book ref|tress|23}} Despite the official agreement of dragons not to meddle in [[cosmere]] events, Xisis maintains a significant involvement,{{book ref|iote|40}} and often makes servants of powerful people from other planets, in exchange for extending their lifespans.{{book ref|iote|32}}

== Appearance and Personality ==
=== Appearance ===
==== Human ====
Like all dragons, Xisisrefliel has two main bodies, his draconic one, and his human one. In his human body, he has ebony skin, with a cool and polished sheen to it. He is bald, rightly believing that it matches well with his well tailored business suits. His eyes are silvery, chosen deliberately because of his heritage. Despite being older than the Shattering, and over ten thousand years old during the Space Age, Xisis doesn't appear like an old man.{{book ref|iote|32}}

{{image|Xisis by ohnoesaxel.jpg|side=left|width=300px}}

==== Dragon ====
In his draconic form, Xisis has black scales, silvery hair, and [[Dragonsteel (metal)|dragonsteel]] ridges, claws and horns.{{book ref|tress|51}}{{book ref|tress|34}} He is very large, around the size of four humans standing on top of one another.{{book ref|tress|51}}

=== Personality ===
Xisisrefliel is an incredibly old dragon, over ten thousand years old during the Space Age. This has had its effect on him; he's an incredibly cunning dragon, and is accustomed to looking ahead, generally further then anyone else.{{book ref|iote|epilogue}} It has also made him fairly grouchy, although the dragon [[Illistandrista]] believes that he acts worse than he actually is.{{book ref|iote|28}} He is considered to be a terrible enemy, willing to chase someone across the entire cosmere for a slight.{{book ref|iote|30}}{{book ref|iote|32}} He also considers making people live in misery far more entertaining than killing them.{{book ref|iote|32}}

Xisis rejects the 'old school dragons', who spend all their time acting as gods. Rather, he is one of the dragons who wear suits, and run governments. That is not to say that he doesn't like being viewed as a god, he even has at least one whole planet worshipping him. Xisis often makes remarks about eating mortals, but he doesn't actually. He likes that there is a lot of misinformation about dragons.{{book ref|iote|32}}

As a primary patron of the [[Silverlight|Silverlight University]], Xisis is very interested in science, and he often goes to listen to the presentations given at the university.{{book ref|iote|32}} He is also somewhat of a researcher himself. Xisis is particularly interested in the decomposition cycle of the [[Aether]]s that engender the seethe on Lumar. This led him to travel to the planet, setting up a home base there to do research.{{wob ref|16142}}{{wob ref|16187}} While there, he also attempted to master the aethers;{{book ref|sa4|part=ars}} something he has likely achieved, as he is able to control the crimson spores that make up the sea in which he made his domain{{book ref|tress|50}} and to temporarily cure people of their [[spore eater]] affliction.{{book ref|tress|31}}{{book ref|tress|52}}

As a dragon, Xisis is nearly immortal. There are a few creatures in the cosmere whom he fears however, such as an [[Elantrian]] named [[Riina]].{{book ref|totes|52}} He also likes drinking ruby wine, and, like many others of his kind, he dislikes [[Hoid]].{{book ref|iote|32}} Over his lifespan, Xisis has acquired a lot of servants. He does not like putting too much effort into training his servants, and prefers they be well-liked with a good temperament.{{book ref|tress|52}}{{wob ref|16142}} He is rather honest, feeling no need to lie; however, he is rather imperious, and the exact truth of his statements can be warped by his own self-image.{{wob ref|16142}} He is rather honest about the way he personally feels he treats his slaves, believing that he treats them well.{{wob ref|16142}}{{book ref|tress|52}}

== Attributes and Abilities ==
As a [[dragon (cosmere)|dragon]], he possesses an extremely long lifespan,{{wob ref|2655}}{{wob ref|5730}}{{wob ref|15310}} and the ability to shapeshift between a chosen humanoid form and his draconic self.{{book ref|tress|51}}{{wob ref|12076}}{{wob ref|7171}}{{wob ref|148}}{{wob ref|16187}}
{{image|Xisis by BrightWatcher.png|side=right|width=300px}}

Xisis has the ability to cure diseases including that of [[spore eater]]s.{{book ref|tress|31}}{{book ref|tress|52}} He claims that this is temporary, only lasting a year or two, unless the person remains in his vicinity.{{book ref|tress|52}} He can also extend the lifespans of mortals with ease, through unknown mechanics.{{book ref|iote|32}}

Xisis is also seemingly a Tenth Heightening [[Awakener]], able to Command Awakened cloth without words.{{book ref|tress|52}}{{book ref|wb|part=ars}}

Through unknown means he is also able to control and shape the spores of the Crimson Sea to his whim, allowing him to form tunnels to his base of operations at the bottom of the ocean. This base is itself a series of tunnels made out of the crimson spores.{{book ref|tress|50}}

He is an accomplished pianist, able to improvise works at a level comparable to those of great composers. He uses his aptitude as a negotiating tool to manipulate his quarry's emotions.{{book ref|iote|32}}

== History ==
=== Lumar ===
Xisis lived on [[Lumar]] in a palace at the bottom of the [[Crimson Sea]] for hundreds of years before the events of the exodus of the [[Sorceress]].{{book ref|tress|31}} His arrival had some connection to the departure of the [[Iriali]], but the exact nature of this relation is unknown.{{wob ref|16140}} During this time he researched the ecosystem at the bottom of the spore seas,{{book ref|tress|51}} as well as the water cycle and the decomposition of spores.{{wob ref|16142}} 

To help with his chores and research, he had been making trades, taking a slave in exchange for a boon.{{book ref|tress|34}} [[Captain Crow]] took the ''[[Crow's Song]]'' above his palace and was able to get an audience with him in order to sell [[Tress]] to him. Tress, however, was able to convince him to take Captain Crow instead.{{book ref|tress|52}} When he offered Tress a boon, she asked if he could grant three lesser wishes. When he agreed, she asked for a new tablet for [[Fort]], glasses for [[Ann]], and information to track [[Salay's father]].{{book ref|totes|53}}

Like many [[dragon (cosmere)|dragon]]s, his home base is in [[Silverlight]].{{wob ref|16187}}

=== The Discovery of Patji's Eye === 
After leaving Lumar, Xisis was given charge of a ship called the [[Dynamic Storyteller's Incredible Conveyance]].{{book ref|iote|25}} He inherited the ship and its debts after the disappearance of [[Hoid]]. He typically had the crew do merchant runs through Shadesmar, and installed Crow as the ship's captain.{{book ref|iote|22}} [[Illistandrista|Starling]] visited Xisis to broker a deal with him, following a mutiny against Captain Crow. Xisis' guard, a [[peakspren]] named [[Spring]], had been instructed to tell Starling that Xisis was away, but Starling knew that he would be in Silverlight to listen to a lecture from [[Professor Lake]].{{book ref|iote|32}} By this point, Xisis had already heard of the mutiny from Crow herself.

Xisis' skyscraper in Silverlight has a special entrance for dragons, but due to her manacles, Starling was unable to fly up to see him, and Xisis made her wait for over half an hour before agreeing to speak to her. He considered her care for the other crew members to be silly, unbecoming of a dragon, and told her as such. They argued about whether or not [[Frost]] was still alive, and then Xisis began to play the piano. Xisis wanted to disband the crew of the ''Dynamic'' and send Starling away, but agreed to leave the crew intact and give Starling full ownership of the ''Dynamic'' if she uncovered proof of the [[Drominad]] [[Perpendicularity]].{{book ref|iote|32}}

Xisis later entered negotiations with [[Vathi]] and the [[Eelakin]] government, following the discovery of [[Patji's Eye]]. He agreed to publicize news of the [[Navigator|Navigators]], in exchange for better tariffs when the perpendicularity was eventually opened for trade.{{book ref|iote|epilogue}}
