== Background ==
The '''Emuli offensive''' is a military operation conducted by the [[coalition of monarchs]] to liberate [[Emul]] from the control of [[Odium]]'s forces.{{book ref|sa4|19}}. Raised as an alternative by The [[Dieno|Mink]] to [[Dalinar]]'s desire to retake [[Alethkar]] as a strong offensive of the [[Coalition of monarchs]], the '''Emuli offensive''' presents a much stronger advantage to the [[coalition of monarchs]] than the liberation of [[Alethkar]], along with being a major strategic goal as a staging ground for the eventual liberation of [[Alethkar]]{{book ref|sa4|17}}{{book ref|sa4|19}}. Despite [[Veden]] betrayal at the hands of [[Odium]] carried out by the orders of [[Taravangian]]{{book ref|sa4|I-6}}, the offensive is successful in liberating [[Emul]]{{book ref|sa4|99}} employing a "hammer-and-anvil"" approach to push the [[singer]] army into the forces of the mad herald [[Ishar]].{{book ref|sa4|101}}. [[Raboniel]] uses the offensive as an opportunity to lead an invasion of [[singers]] to [[Urithiru]] while the only [[Knights Radiant]] who can resist the [[Suppression fabrial]] at [[Urithiru]]: [[Dalinar]] and [[Jasnah]] at [[Urithiru]] are occupied with the offensive {{book ref|sa4|14}}.

== Major  Events ==

Ensuring  the invasion of [[Urithiru]] wasn't not interrupted by [[Jasnah]] or [[Dalinar]], [[Odium]] set in motion a distraction to prevent them from investigating the disturbances at [[Urithiru]]{{book ref|sa4|14}}. A simultaneous assault of the [[Skybreakers]] and betrayal from the [[Veden]] forces was orchestrated when [[Taravangian]] was instructed to, fulfill his agreement with [[Odium]] to spare [[Kharbranth]],  order his troops to betray the [[Coalition of monarchs|coalition]] force's {{book ref|sa4|I-3}}. The attack was short lived and [[Taravangian]] was arrested just 15 minutes after sending the order{{book ref|sa4|I-6}}. 

The Veden army was soon after was almost destroyed in its entirety, even though they were reinforced by a small force of singers and Skybreakers.{{book ref|sa4|47}} After this battle, the Coalition would decide to leave Dalinar and the Alethi in charge. At the meeting where this was decided, Jasnah dueled the [[Highprince]] [[Ruthar]], who was the last holdout of [[Torol_Sadeas|Sadeas]]’s faction, and this led to Ruthar’s ‘death’, though he was healed by [[Renarin]], and his removal as a Highprince. Jasnah would use this event to outlaw honor duels in Alethkar.{{book ref|sa4|60}} 

Some time later Jasnah herself would go onto the battlefield disguised as a regular Shardbearer by having her [[Shardplate]] appear as regular dead Plate. This was one of a series of battles that led to the Coalition’s victory.{{book ref|sa4|64}} During the campaign, Dalinar grew curious about his powers and decided to seek out the Herald [[Ishar]], who was leading nearby [[Tukar]] as Tezim, and they would meet briefly in Tukar towards the end of the campaign.{{book ref|sa4|111}} After this meeting, Dalinar would encounter Odium and would enter the contract for the Contest of champions, which had been drafted by [[Hoid|Wit]] during the campaign.{{book ref|sa4|112}} 

After the contract was made, Taravangian was killed by [[Szeth]] while Odium was present. This allowed Taravangian to use the Cognitive aspect of Nightblood to kill Rayse, allowing him to pick up the Shard.{{book ref|sa4|113}}

After the Offensive ended, the [[Order of Stonewards|Stoneward]] [[Dami]] would swear the Fourth Ideal, unlocking his Shardplate and making him the third [[Knights Radiant|Radiant]] to so after Jasnah and Kaladin.{{book ref|sa5|6}}
