'''Torio''' is a kingdom on [[Komashi]]{{book ref|yumi|part=anotherepilogue}} that was destroyed by the [[father machine]];{{book ref|yumi|39}} it continued to exist as a simulation made from Investiture in several localized areas to serve as prisons for the [[yoki-hijo]] who survived the great reaping of souls that occurred upon the machine's activation.{{book ref|yumi|38}} It is relatively small by modern [[cosmere]] standards, measuring less than fifty miles across.{{book ref|yumi|34}}

== Geography and Ecology ==
=== Environment ===
{{sidequote|Plants on Yumi’s world don’t really defy physics so much as they sneak past while physics is distracted by a nice drama on the viewer. Probably something involving pendulums. Physics loves those things.|[[Hoid]] discussing [[Painter]]'s discovery that the trees are lighter than they should be.{{book ref|yumi|23}}|side=right|size=350px}}
Torio’s environment is characterized by extreme heat emanating from the ground.{{book ref|yumi|2}}{{book ref|yumi|4}} Water is not rare there, but it is concentrated in only a few locations.{{book ref|yumi|4}} Villages are often built around a steamwell, which serves as a source of humidity and water.{{book ref|yumi|4}} The ground near a village is hot enough to hurt but not lethal, whereas the surrounding wastelands--referred to as the searing barrens--are hot enough to ignite wooden clogs.{{book ref|yumi|4}} Still, these lands can be traversed at night in hovering wagons pulled by flying devices.{{book ref|yumi|4}} The extreme ground heat also causes thermal updrafts throughout the region.{{book ref|yumi|4}} Up high, beyond the scorching barrens, there exist cold wastes,{{book ref|yumi|4}} where stone gives way to soil.{{book ref|yumi|19}}

Plant life has adapted to these conditions by floating on thermal updrafts.{{book ref|yumi|2}} Small flowers have wide leaves to collect rain and ride the thermals, with fine dangling roots to absorb humidity from the air.{{book ref|yumi|2}} Stocky trees--measuring about eight feet from the bottom of their wide, webbed roots to their tops--are often chained outside homes in villages.{{book ref|yumi|4}} Other trees have curled, finger-like roots at the bottom.{{book ref|yumi|24}} Made of heavy wood, these trees rely on local adaptations{{book ref|yumi|4}} that somehow render them light enough to float on the thermals.{{book ref|yumi|23}} Soil-bound plants such as bamboo also exist in Torio, growing in the cold wastes or near cold springs.{{book ref|yumi|19}}

Animal life in Torio includes field mice that inhabit floating plants,{{book ref|yumi|23}} giant crows roughly the size of a seven- or eight-year-old child–looked after by [[corvider]]s{{book ref|yumi|6}}–and giant hawks,{{book ref|yumi|23}} the largest of which can be fifteen feet across.{{book ref|yumi|25}}

=== Location ===
Torio is located somewhere on the planet [[Komashi]] in the [[UTol system]] in the [[cosmere]].{{book ref|yumi|part=anotherepilogue}} Being less than fifty miles across, its size is primarily limited by the dispersal of the steamwells.{{book ref|yumi|34}} It is unknown if there are other nations near Torio.

== History ==
{{sidequote|One thousand seven hundred and sixty-three years. Yes…festival day. The day we would create power for our people from the spirits themselves.|One of the [[Torish]] scholars from the [[Institute of Mechanical Solutions]] discussing the day the [[father machine]] was activated.{{book ref|yumi|38}}|side=left|size=350px}}
It is unknown how long the kingdom of Torio existed prior to its destruction. The yoki-hijo reform movement is a couple of hundred years old by the time the father machine is activated,{{book ref|yumi|22}} and the kingdom has an established monarchy with the capital, [[Torio City]], serving as seat of the queen. The [[yoki-hijo]]'s art is ancient,{{book ref|yumi|4}} though this does not necessarily mean that the kingdom of Torio is also ancient.

Torio was destroyed by the father machine during the annual [[Festival of Reveals]] in Torio City, where scholars intended to demonstrate a revolutionary means of harnessing spiritual power.{{book ref|yumi|38}} Upon its activation, due to poorly created [[Command|Commands]], it conflated [[hijo]] and the souls of men, absorbing both all across the planet and leading to the genocide of all of Torio, as well as the creation of the [[shroud]] covering all of Komashi.{{book ref|yumi|39}} The only survivors were the nomads who lived at the edge of civilization{{book ref|yumi|39}} and a few yoki-hijo, who were too heavily Invested to be controlled by the machine.{{book ref|yumi|38}}

The father machine's genocide ended all rule in Torio and left only small groups of survivors{{book ref|yumi|39}}. For the next one-thousand seven-hundred and sixty-three years,{{book ref|yumi|38}} the kingdom continued to exist in some sense as Investiture-based simulations in localized areas hidden in the shroud,{{book ref|yumi|38}} where the yoki-hijo were made to repeat the same day ad infinitum, constantly having their memory of the day erased.{{book ref|yumi|36}} The destruction of the father machine and removal of the shroud reveals that crumbling ruins are all that remain of the kingdom of Torio.{{book ref|yumi|40}}{{book ref|yumi|epilogue}}

== Culture ==
=== Peoples ===
{{sidequote|I consider myself a reasonably adroit person, but Torish clogs--they call them getuk--feel like bricks tied to my feet. They aren’t necessarily hard to balance in--they’re only six inches tall--but they grant most outsiders the graceful poise of a drunk chull.|[[Hoid]] discussing [[Torish]] clogs from an off-worlder's perspective.{{book ref|yumi|2}}|side=right|size=350px}}
The people of Torio wear bright clothing similar in color to the formal dresses and wraps worn at weddings in modern [[Nagadan]].{{book ref|yumi|10}} These outfits can be quite bulky, especially for women, who often wear bell-shaped dresses{{book ref|yumi|10}} designed to avoid rising too high when caught in the thermal updrafts common to the region.{{book ref|yumi|2}} Men's attire is loose and baggy--often pastel in color with a soft, watercolor style--and tied closed at the ankles.{{book ref|yumi|10}} They will occasionally accent their outfits with black hats, and often wear short, neat beards.{{book ref|yumi|10}}

All genders wear clogs known as getuk--wooden footwear about six inches tall--designed to keep their feet high enough off the ground to prevent burning.{{book ref|yumi|2}}{{book ref|yumi|10}} While locals grow adept at walking in them, off-worlders find the clogs difficult to move in, comparable to having bricks tied to their feet.{{book ref|yumi|2}}

Food in Torio can be cooked directly on the ground, and is served with spoons and maipon sticks.{{book ref|yumi|2}}{{book ref|yumi|11}} The Torish diet includes stews{{book ref|yumi|2}} and a deep blue-purple grain called mingo, similar to [[Scadrian]] rice.{{book ref|yumi|6}} Nuts are harvested in orchards of floating trees.{{book ref|yumi|23}} Meals can be accompanied by smaller bowls of savory sides, each containing only a bite or two.{{book ref|yumi|22}}

Torish people bathe at cool springs--typically located in high ground{{book ref|yumi|2}}--or in bathhouses if their town does not have a spring.{{book ref|yumi|13}}

Weeks in Torio are eleven days long, and it is common to mark out lengths of time in these eleven-day “weeks.”{{book ref|yumi|20}} 

=== Religion ===
{{sidequote|Each yoki-hijo trained in an ancient and powerful art. A deliberate, wondrous artistry requiring the full synergy of body and mind. Geological reorganization on the microscale, requiring acute understanding of gravitational equilibrium.<br />In other words, they stacked rocks.|[[Hoid]] explaining the [[yoki-hijo]]'s art to his listeners.{{book ref|yumi|4}}|side=right|size=350px}}
Religious devotion in Torio centers on the [[Hijo]]--spirits (or [[Splinter]]s left behind by [[Virtuosity]]) that naturally reside underground.{{book ref|yumi|17}} Torish people will pray to these spirits,{{book ref|yumi|36}} and build dedicated shrines in their villages where they can seek the spirits' blessings.{{book ref|yumi|2}} They also believe in an afterlife, holding that the souls of the unworthy drift in the cold sky--forever forbidden the warmth of the ground.{{book ref|yumi|11}}

At the heart of Torish religion are the yoki-hijo, which translates to "Girl of Commanding Primal Spirits."{{book ref|yumi|2}} These individuals are chosen at birth and highly Invested by the spirits,{{book ref|yumi|39}} marked by the appearance of a falling star in the sky.{{book ref|yumi|2}} They travel among Torio's villages, summoning spirits through the creation of sophisticated sculptures of stacked rocks{{book ref|yumi|4}}. Once summoned, the spirits willingly transform into devices--ranging from simple lights to repelling pairs that can lift objects or entire buildings--in exchange for receiving the gift of the sculpture.{{book ref|yumi|4}}

Torish culture deeply values the role of the yoki-hijo, as they provide many essential devices for survival and prosperity.{{book ref|yumi|2}}{{book ref|yumi|4}} They are also regarded as major religious figures and are deeply revered by the people.{{book ref|yumi|4}}

In the final two hundred years of Torio's society, a schism arises among the yoki-hijo.{{book ref|yumi|22}} By the time of Torio's destruction, twelve out of the fourteen active yoki-hijo have adopted a reform movement, opting for more personal freedom by easing strict traditions and rituals tied to their station.{{book ref|yumi|22}} These reformed yoki-hijo feed and dress themselves and take days off when they feel it necessary,{{book ref|yumi|22}} yet they have not abandoned their station; even the most liberal among them continues serving the people.{{book ref|yumi|23}} Both of the remaining orthodox yoki-hijo remain unaware of the reform movement.{{book ref|yumi|22}}

=== Language ===
No curses or swears exist in Torio’s language.{{book ref|yumi|2}} Instead, to convey derision or praise, speakers will add modifiers to words or conjugate their sentences in higher or lower forms.{{book ref|yumi|2}} Torio's language shares a common root with that of Nagadan,{{book ref|yumi|2}} and its script appears familiar to modern Nagadan citizens;{{book ref|yumi|31}} however, it remains unintelligible to them in practice.{{book ref|yumi|41}}

=== Economy ===
Little is known about the economic system of Torio outside of how it relates to the spirits. Many devices necessary for daily life come from spirits summoned by the yoki-hijo and are distributed according to need.{{book ref|yumi|4}} Other necessities--such as water for cooking and bathing--appear to be communal.{{book ref|yumi|4}} Water that does not escape as steam from the town geysers is collected by concentric rings of bronze plates.{{book ref|yumi|4}} Even so, typical Torish citizens do use money to buy other goods.{{book ref|yumi|15}}
