'''Paalm''' is a female [[kandra]] of the Third Generation. She is an agent of [[Harmony]] sent to protect [[Waxillium Ladrian]] in the [[Roughs]], under the guise of a human woman, '''Lessie'''.{{book ref|mb5|26}} 

Sometime after Lessie's supposed death, she resolved to “free” people from Harmony’s control and bring chaos to the city of [[Elendel]]. She went by the alias Bleeder and colluded with the [[Set]], who provided her [[Hemalurgy|Hemalurgic spikes]] made from [[trellium]] that would allow her to hide from Harmony and steal [[Allomantic]] and [[Feruchemical]] powers.{{book ref|mb5|epilogue}} Using this single spike (as opposed to the standard two for kandra blessings) allowed her to prevent Harmony from knowing where she is or taking control of her. {{book ref|mb5|7}}

She ultimately committed suicide to avoid being taken control of by Harmony after she was shot by Wax with a second Hemalurgic spike.{{book ref|mb5|26}}

== Appearance and Personality ==
{{for|/Gallery|more images}}
As a [[kandra]], Paalm can shapeshift to give herself the appearance of any corpse she’s digested. However, as Lessie (and Bleeder), her appearance is lean with a squarish face.{{book ref|mb5|prologue}}

In the days of the Final Empire, Paalm was dedicated to whatever role she was ordered to play by the [[Lord Ruler]]. As such, she didn’t have much personality beyond her facade and an underlying fanaticism to her master.{{book ref|mb5|13}}

As Lessie she was rougher around the edges, constantly criticizing Wax and his inexperience in the Roughs.{{book ref|mb5|prologue}} However, she wanted him to be happy and stay in the Roughs, despite Harmony’s plans.{{book ref|mb5|26}}

As Bleeder, she became obsessed with freedom. While her motives may have been psychotic, she was always meticulous when enacting her plan.{{book ref|mb5|20}} Additionally, she at times showed remorse for the pain she caused, but believed it to be absolutely necessary.{{book ref|mb5|16}} Above all, she has a deep hatred for Harmony.{{book ref|mb5|26}}

== Abilities ==
As a kandra of the Third Generation, Paalm excels at creating bodies; of all the kandra, only [[Tensoon]] is more skilled.{{book ref|mb5|13}} Her considerable expertise comes from her history of service to the Lord Ruler, and the time she spent crushing entire kingdoms and rebellions for him. She also knows a great deal about Hemalurgy, having learned how to grant herself Allomantic and Feruchemical abilities with spikes of [[trellium]].{{wob ref|7038}}{{wob ref|7776}} She is proficient at using her stolen abilities, although she seems to prefer Feruchemical [[steel]]. She knows how to create at least one new kind of Hemalurgic construct using only a single spike in the creation. She is able to hide herself from Harmony's sight by spiking herself with a trellium spike.{{book ref|mb5|7}}{{book ref|mb5|13}}{{wob ref|7773}}{{book ref|mb5|21}}{{book ref|mb5|epilogue}}

== History ==
=== The Final Empire ===
As per the agreement between the kandra and the [[Lord Ruler]] Paalm was to serve him directly.{{book ref|mb5|13}}{{book ref|mb3|7}} While under his control she was almost constantly sent on missions and was used to stop rebellions and topple kingdoms, some other kandra even speculated that she had pretended to be an [[Inquisitor]] at one stage. Due to her work, she did not often visit the homeland, and it was suggested that she became familiar with the workings of Hemalurgy. Her loyalty lasted until the [[Final Ascension]], where it then switched to [[Harmony]], again to the point of fanaticism.{{book ref|mb5|13}}

=== Lessie ===
{{sidequote
|I think I loved you even on that day... Lawman for hire. So ridiculous... but so earnest. You didn't try to shelter me, but seemed so eager to impress... A lord with a purpose."
|Bleeder to Wax.{{book ref|mb5|25}}
|side=right|size=300px
}}

While grooming [[Wax]] to become his sword, Harmony decided that he required a bodyguard. Paalm showed herself to be enthusiastic for the role, and Harmony agreed to send her, thinking that the pair would be good for each other out in the [[Roughs]].{{book ref|sos|26}}

Wax first encountered Paalm, disguised as 'Lessie', at The Weeping Bull, where she was disguised as a dancer. Wax formally met her during their second encounter while hunting [[Granite Joe]]. She claimed to be hunting him as well, but was actually in Granite Joe's employ. Lessie seemingly had a change of heart when Wax told her that he still thought her legs were pretty, and she shot Granite Joe in the neck with her crossbow, killing him.{{book ref|mb5|prologue}} After that, Lessie became a law-woman alongside Wax and Wayne. Originally, the role was meant to have no romantic nature to it, but Paalm suggested adding that element to their relationship with Harmony agreeing.{{book ref|sos|26}} The two eventually married with a simple [[Pathian]] ceremony, while being chased by a mob on horseback.{{wob ref|3970}}

When the time approached for Wax to move back to [[Elendel]], Paalm resisted the idea, deciding that Wax was too happy in the Roughs to leave, even with the idea that she could accompany him to Elendel.{{book ref|sos|25}}{{wob ref|2697}} While hunting [[Bloody Tan]] in his lair in the town of [[Feltrel]], 'Lessie' and Wax split up. At some point, she was taken hostage. Wax attempted to shoot Bloody Tan, who was using 'Lessie' as a human shield, but he pulled her into the path of Wax's bullet, making her take the shot to the head and seemingly killing her.{{book ref|mb4|prologue}} As part of Harmony's plan, Wax moved back to Elendel, and Paalm's time as Lessie was over. Some time afterward, she went on a murderous rampage and broke free of Harmony's influence, disobeying her contract with him.{{book ref|mb5|26}}{{book ref|mb5|7}}{{book ref|mb5|13}}

=== Bleeder ===
{{quote
|Bleeder is ancient, older than the destruction of the world- almost as old as the Final Empire. Indeed, she is older than I am, though not older than my powers. She is crafty, careful, and brilliant. And I'm afraid that she might have gone mad.
|Harmony talking to Wax.{{book ref|mb5|7}}
}}

Paalm took the name Bleeder and set about a plan to free the people of Elendel from Harmony's influence.{{wob ref|309}} At some point, she allied with the Set to achieve her goals{{book ref|sos|17}} and learned how to steal abilities with trellium.{{wob ref|7038}}{{wob ref|7776}} She killed [[Idashwy]] with a spike to steal her Feruchemical power{{book ref|mb5|6}}{{book ref|mb5|11}}{{book ref|mb5|17}} and killed Governor [[Replar Innate]] and then impersonated them.{{book ref|sos|22}} She also impersonated a man named [[Johnst]] who blew up a dam, causing food shortages and economic instability.{{book ref|sos|14}}

{{image|The Lawman and the Kandra by Ingvild Schage.jpg|side=right|height=250px|Wax holding Paalm as she died}}

Using her newly acquired power and appearance, Bleeder disrupted [[Winsting Innate's auction]] for his Senate vote, firing several shots at the guests to get them to begin attacking one another. She then entered Winsting's saferoom ahead of him, murdering him inside and cutting out his tongue in a symbolic gesture, removing Harmony's voice. As she left, she killed his bodyguards outside the saferoom.{{book ref|mb5|prologue}}{{book ref|mb5|6}}{{book ref|mb5|4}}{{book ref|mb5|5}}{{book ref|mb5|23}}

Around the same time, she impersonated [[Larskpur]] and killed [[Father Bin]] inside a church by driving spikes into his eyes—another symbolic gesture, removing Harmony's eyes{{book ref|mb5|23}}—to turn the [[Church of the Survivor|Survivorists]] against [[the Path]]ians. When the people grew aggressive, she exposed the kandra who attempted to calm the violence and pushed harder for violent rebellion against Innate. Exposing the kandra was symbolic for deafening Harmony, as they were his ears.{{book ref|sos|20}}

When Wax confronted her, she attempted to flee by using a spike granting herself Allomantic steel. Wax gave chase, stopping her at the [[Eastbridge]]. In that confrontation, Bleeder attempted to convince him that she was Lessie, finally succeeding when he shot her with his Pathian earring, reforged as a bullet. Before Harmony could fully take control of her, she committed suicide.{{book ref|mb5|23}}{{book ref|mb5|24}}{{book ref|mb5|25}} After death, she asked Harmony to look after Wax.{{book ref|mb6|28}} Wax later gave her spikes to the kandra.{{book ref|mb7|15}}

== Trivia == 
* The name "Bleeder" is possibly derived from the doctors of antiquity, who would force patients to bleed profusely, in an effort to purge sickness and disease. This is comparable to Paalm's actions in ''[[Shadows of Self]]'' in which she causes great harm to Wax and the Basin, to cleanse them of Harmony's control.
* According to Brandon, writing her character was "probably the darkest I've gone in one of my books, ... but it was also somewhere I hadn't explored before, and so it was really interesting to me."{{wob ref|12143}}
* During the initial draft of Alloy of Law, Brandon didn’t plan for Lessie to be a kandra. However, it was included during revisions after the plotline of the entire series had been finalized. {{wob ref|321}}
