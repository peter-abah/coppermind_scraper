'''Attica Smedry''' is an [[Oculator]] and the heir to [[House Smedry]].{{book ref|a1|20}}

He is married to [[Shasta Smedry|Shasta]] and the father of [[Alcatraz Smedry|Alcatraz]]. His [[Smedry Talent|Talent]] is losing things.

== Appearance and Personality == 
Attica is described as being a tall man with hawkish features and messy dark brown hair.{{book ref|a2|20}}{{book ref|a3|2}} He usually wears an antiquated suit or dark robes. After his consciousness was transferred to the Incarnate lenses (The blood-forged lenses that help the user control the power of the Incarna), His appearance is now  a set of slate-gray lensed glasses. Attica is charming and well-liked by the people of [[Nalhalla]].

His son [[Alcatraz Smedry|Alcatraz]] describes him as a terrible father, and his father [[Leavenworth Smedry|Leavenworth]] comments that he does not cope with fame too well. Attica himself admits to being terrible at saying goodbye.{{book ref|a4|6}} However, while he may not always pay attention to Alcatraz, he does love him, as was proved when Alcatraz looked through the Shaper’s Lenses: he wants to be universally adored, but he also wants his family there with him.{{book ref|a5|18}} He also sacrifices his life for Alcatraz.

Attica's motives are always unknown, as he seems to be four steps ahead of everyone else. Even when his form became the Incarnate lens, he seemed to have decided it was unavoidable, and in fact, critical for the world, so that the Incarnate energy could be released through the Worldspire. Nothing seemed to faze him, which is why he never seemed to care about Alcatraz. Ultimately, he wanted the best for everyone, and with his attention divided, he could never show enough of it to where it was most needed.

== Attributes and Abilities ==
Attica has the ability to lose things, which is a space talent on the [[Smedry Talent|Incarnate Wheel]]. Though he is never seen using his Talent, he is stated to have great control over it.{{book ref|a3|1}} Attica is also a skilled [[Oculator]] and can use the different Oculator's [[Lens]]es.

Other than his magical abilities, Attica is a renowned scholar and researcher in the Free Kingdoms. His achievements include discovering how Transporter's Glass works and devising a method for collecting the Sands of Rashid. He is incredibly intelligent, as he found a loophole in the rules of the Library of Alexandria -- which allowed Alcatraz to free him from the library{{book ref|a2|20}} -- and he was able to find a way to infiltrate the [[Highbrary]].{{book ref|a4|21}}

Once his consciousness moved to the Incarnate lens, Attica could perceive the power of the Incarnate wheel, and, with his research, was able to help Alcatraz spread the talents to all of humanity to effectively dilute it, though the power of the Smedry talents remained at their usual force.

== History ==
Attica married Shasta ignoring the objections of his father, who believed Shasta was only manipulating him in order to gain his Talent.{{book ref|a1|20}} Together, they researched the origins of Smedry Talents and the [[Sands of Rashid]], which Attica managed to collect. Shortly after the birth of Alcatraz, Attica and Shasta separated, and Attica disappeared from the [[Free Kingdoms]].

Thirteen years later, Attica allowed himself to be captured in the [[Library of Alexandria]] and turned into a Curator. He left a message bequeathing his soul to Alcatraz, which allowed his son to free him. Before he was freed, he used his Curator status to gather knowledge about the [[Incarna]] and the Talents.{{book ref|a2|20}}

Upon returning to the Free Kingdoms, Attica spent a week alternately socializing with Free Kingdom nobility and feverishly recording knowledge gained from the Library of Alexandria. He then announced his intention to develop a way to give everyone Talents using the knowledge he gained during his imprisonment.{{book ref|a3|Royal Epilogue (Not a Chapter)}}

After this, he went to the [[Highbrary]] to read its Forgotten Language books in order to gain knowledge on how to give everyone Smedry Talents. He was eventually captured by Biblioden and sacrificed on the altar of outdated encyclopedias to create a Blood-forged Lens.{{book ref|a5|20}}{{book ref|a5|21}}

Once Biblioden was defeated, it was discovered that Attica's consciousness resided in the Incarnate lens forged by his sacrifice, and it is believed it will be trapped in there for the remainder of his days, as he hadn't been freed by the time of Bastille's writing of the book, which was most likely being finished not long after the marriage of Alcatraz and Bastille.
