The '''Library of Alexandria''' is a repository of all knowledge ever recorded. It was originally located in the [[Hushlands]], though the [[Curators of Alexandria|Curators]] moved it after it outgrew its location.{{book ref|a2|7}} It now resides beneath a small hut.

[[Wikipedia:Library of Alexandria|The library is still known in the Hushlands]], though the [[Librarian]]s have covered up its continued existence by claiming it was destroyed by the Roman Empire.{{book ref|a2|7}}

== Curators ==
{{for|Curators of Alexandria}}
The library is protected by a curse wherein anyone who reads from or moves a book forfeits their soul and becomes one of the library's curators.{{book ref|a2|7}}

The Curators try to get visitors to check out a book so that they can claim their soul.{{book ref|a2|9}} The library is laid out so that one will get hopelessly lost, which the Curators hope will make a visitor just give up and ask for a map, which gives the Curators that person's soul. Traps are everywhere as well, with the information on how to get out of them written on books that can be checked out.{{book ref|a2|11}}

== Tomb of Alcatraz the First ==
{{for|Allekatrase}}
The center of the library is a tomb for [[Allekatrase|Alcatraz Smedry the First]]. Alcatraz the First broke time around his tomb, meaning no decay occurred within the bubble of frozen time. The tomb is the oldest part of the Library. Within the frozen bubble of time, there was a Truthfinder's Lens that could only be obtained by one who possessed the Breaking Talent. [[Alcatraz Smedry]], his descendent, claimed the Lens when he visited the Library.
