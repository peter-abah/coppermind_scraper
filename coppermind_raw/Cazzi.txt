The '''Cazzi''' are a people that lived in [[Classical Scadrial]], known for their culture centered around death and mortality.{{book ref|mb1|11}}{{book ref|mb3|4}} The Cazzi people and their religion, like all others, were erased by the Lord Ruler as he assimilated them into the Final Empire and the imperial faith.

== Religion and Culture ==
=== Culture ===
The Cazzi people deeply valued the importance of death and mortality.{{book ref|mb1|11}}{{book ref|mb3|4}} 

They kept a caste of slaves, on which the Lord Ruler based the lives of the skaa.{{epigraph ref|mb3|27}} Whom they kept as slaves and how the system was systemically justified is currently unknown.

=== Religion ===
The Cazzi are a people familiar with death. They teach of the beauty of mortality, the importance of death, and the vital function of the human body as a partaker of the greater divine whole.{{book ref|mb1|11}}{{book ref|mb3|4}} '''Death chants''' are among their practices. These chants are believed by the Cazzi to help free the spirits of the dead from their corpses, allowing them to return to the "'''mountain of souls'''".{{book ref|mb1|11}} 

They believe that all beings are part of a singular divine whole, and each body is a work of art created by a spirit that lives within the world. It is further taught that those with mental or physical disabilities have such disabilities due to being punished by the spirit for their evil.{{book ref|mb3|4}} They believe in a form of body-soul dualism, teaching of a spirit which exists within the body and is capable of being separated from it. However, it appears that it does not naturally leave the body, and requires a death chant in order to free it from the flesh.{{book ref|mb1|11}} 

As they valued the vital functions of the body as a partaker in the divine whole,{{book ref|mb3|4}} they kept detailed diagrams and descriptions of the human body.{{book ref|mb3|82}} 

Sazed used Cazzi teachings about the body to restore humans to their pre-ascension form.{{book ref|mb3|82}}

== Trivia ==
* In older versions of ''[[The Hero of Ages]]'', they were referred to as the Canzi.
