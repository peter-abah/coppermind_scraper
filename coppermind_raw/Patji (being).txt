'''Patji''' is an avatar of [[Autonomy]] located on the planet [[First of the Sun]] in the [[Drominad system]].{{rpg ref|slwg|84}}{{wob ref|9385}} They typically speak with a masculine voice. As an avatar of Autonomy, Patji is strongly aligned with its intent, and believes that people must prove themselves. [[Navigator|Navigation]] is mentioned to be of Patji, and they can speak to Navigators.{{book ref|iote|33}}

== History ==
At some point Patji was a being of its own, with unknown amounts and origins of [[Investiture]], somehow related to the [[Shard]] Autonomy.{{wob ref|8902}} Autonomy was not always aware of Patji's existence, but at some point sensed it and was consciously and directly involved in shaping it into an avatar.{{wob ref|9385}}{{wob ref|13220}} The relationship between a Shard and avatars is poorly understood, but Patji remains strongly connected to Autonomy.{{wob ref|9385}}

Patji has close ties to the planet First of the Sun, and presumably lends its name to the island of [[Patji (island)|Patji]] and its [[perpendicularity]] known as [[Patji's Eye]]. The humans who live on First of the Sun pray to Patji and sometimes refer to it as the '''Father''', or the '''Father island''', although they also personify other islands.{{book ref|iote|2}}{{book ref|iote|1}} Patji is not thought to permanently "reside" on First of the Sun, but the concept of residence is blurry for beings that reside in the [[Spiritual Realm]].{{wob ref|9385}} The [[Eelakin]] believe that their world was created by Patji, who gave power to their servant the Dakwara. She birthed the [[Homeisles]], and then the other gods came down and became islands to join Patji.{{book ref|iote|38}}

=== Letter to Hoid ===
Patji was the author of a notable [[Letters#Second_Oathbringer_Letter|letter]] to [[Hoid]].{{wob ref|12761}} In the letter, Patji referred to itself using plural pronouns such as "we" and "us" and mentioned an "avatar of our being", implying that in some ways it was speaking for Autonomy as a collective. The letter established a previously existing relationship with Hoid and discussed the fact that he had approached an unknown avatar with a plea for help. Patji seemed impressed that Hoid was even able to find the avatar in question, as it was thought to be well hidden, but it was unable to respond to him and would not have been receptive to his pleas anyway. Patji went on to say that it was not concerned about [[Rayse]] and wanted to be left alone. It also instructed Hoid to stay away from [[Obrodai]] and the young avatar located there. The letter included several sea-related metaphors and mentioned that Hoid would need to visit their waters and overcome a set of unknown tests in order to gain their respect and reopen the discussion.{{epigraph ref|sa3|42}}
