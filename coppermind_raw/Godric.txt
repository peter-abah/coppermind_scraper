'''Godric''' is a fallen lord, and leader of a group of bandits working for [[Ryan Chu]].{{book ref|fwh|25}}{{book ref|frugal|31}}

== Appearance and Personality ==
{{sidequote|What was it with these thugs who looked like they spent half of their time in a salon?|John's first impression of Godric{{book ref|fwh|25}}|side=left|size=300px}}

Godric is notable for his beard, trimmed almost to a point. He also has full, long dark hair that [[John West]] deems as perfectly styled. Godric wears a well kept tunic and trousers, along with a deep red cloak, fastened with a silver clasp. He wears gloves, and his belt buckle is also silver. He appears more refined than the other bandits in his group.{{book ref|fwh|25}}

Godric is a desperate, terrified man with nothing to lose.{{book ref|fwh|25}} He feels terrible about himself, and about abandoning his duty as [[thegn]], though he felt he had no choice but to move to a life of crime after [[Alwin|the earl]] stopped protecting his village from attacks, and started buddying up to strangers.{{book ref|fwh|25}}{{book ref|fwh|28}}{{book ref|fwh|29}} He has a deep respect for [[skop]]s, he believes in [[aelv]]s, and he makes offerings to [[wight]]s.{{book ref|fwh|25}}{{book ref|fwh|29}} He also doesn't like sugar.{{book ref|fwh|29}} Despite being a fallen lord, Godric is a good man, and he is willing to fight to protect his homeland, even putting aside local disputes to fight together against the [[Hordamen]].{{book ref|fwh|29}}{{book ref|fwh|33}} 

== History ==
In the past, Godric was the [[thegn]] of a village.{{book ref|fwh|25}} However, after [[Alwin|the earl]] stopped protecting his village from attacks, no longer defending the roads,{{book ref|fwh|25}} and started cozying up to strangers,{{book ref|fwh|29}} Godric abandoned his duty to the village, and turned to a life of crime, becoming leader of a group of bandits.{{book ref|fwh|25}}

At some point, he and his group of bandits started working with [[Ryan Chu]]. Godric believed him to be an [[aelv]], given Ryan's Chinese appearance, and the abilities afforded to him via his augments.{{book ref|fwh|27}} Ryan gave Godric his last blackout grenade, and instructed him to use it on anyone who had the same accent as Ryan did.{{book ref|fwh|26}}{{book ref|fwh|27}}{{book ref|fwh|29}}

{{sidequote|This is a very fine tree. Would you not say so?|Godric to the travellers{{book ref|fwh|25}}|side=right|size=300px}}

Shortly after joining forces with Ryan, Godric and a group of eleven of his bandits set a trap on a road through the woods, for passersby on their way to [[Maelport]]. As [[John West]], [[Ealstan]], [[Sefawynn]], and [[Thokk]] made their way down that road, the bandits set their plan into motion. They moved through the trees, and quickly surrounded the four travellers before they had a chance to escape.{{book ref|fwh|24}} 
Godric walked in a circle around the travellers, before stopping beside a notably large tree. He proclaimed that it was a mighty fine tree, grown from the seed of the world tree, and that he and his men were the only force guarding the tree. Ealstan and John seemed confused as to what Godric was implying, but Sefawynn quickly understood that Godric was in fact, demanding a toll to be paid. Ealstan seemed to recognise that Godric had once been a thegn, and began to berate him for taking advantage of the situation created by [[Alwin|the earl]] no longer defending the area, comparing Godric to a wolf. This only caused Godric to demand a double toll from Ealstan. Sefawynn implored Ealstan to not taunt the men any further, as she was sure that the bandits already knew how people felt about them. Godric recognised Sefawynn as an intelligent woman, and when he learned that she was a [[skop]], he let her pass without paying a toll. He also allowed the travellers to keep one of their horses for her to ride. However, he took their three other horses as payment for their toll. Godric and the bandits let the travellers move their belongings to the packhorse which they had kept for Sefawynn, leaving them with just enough that they were capable of continuing on their journey, so that the bandits might have the opportunity to rob them again in future. As the travellers were about to leave, Godric asked Thokk if she was a skop too. She denied that she was, and told Godric that John was her mute nephew. The four started to continue on their way, but John spoke.{{book ref|fwh|25}} 
Godric immediately recognised John's accent as being the same as Ryan's, and hit him in the back with the blackout grenade, knocking John unconscious.{{book ref|fwh|25}}{{book ref|fwh|27}}

{{sidequote|My fault. Used the aelv stunning device on you. Wasn't supposed to knock you out that long, though.|Godric to John{{book ref|fwh|27}}|side=left|size=300px}}

Godric and the bandits then escorted Ealstan, Sefawynn, Thokk, and the unconscious John back to their camp.{{book ref|fwh|27}} They put John on a mat in what appeared to be a small tool shack abutting the forest, while they waited for him to wake up.{{book ref|fwh|26}} In the meantime, Godric and the bandits, along with Ryan, chatted and laughed amiably by the fire with Ealstan, Sefawynn, and Thokk. Ealstan, Sefawynn, and Thokk were also given some soup to eat.{{book ref|fwh|26}}{{book ref|fwh|27}}

After John regained consciousness, he snuck out of the shack and dashed toward the fire. He pulled up short when he recognised Ryan.{{book ref|fwh|26}} John made a sheepish comment about having been unconscious for a while, and Godric admitted that he was the one who had shot him with the blackout grenade, though he hadn't expected it to knock John out for so long.{{book ref|fwh|27}}

On [[Woden|Wodensday]], Godric and his bandits helped cause a distraction while Ryan tried to stop [[Ulric Stromfin]].{{book ref|fwh|32}} He then joined with other soldiers in [[Maelport]] to fight against the [[Hordamen]].{{book ref|fwh|33}}
